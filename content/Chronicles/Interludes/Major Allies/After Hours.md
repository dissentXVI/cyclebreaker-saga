# After Hours

*Late at the Sailor's Whimsy after closing, Mireya works up the nerve to join M'ylaena at her research table. Their conversation about goblin religion drifts toward Aspiria — and Mireya reveals that she may have heard a voice.*

---

The door clicked shut behind Jendrick and left the Sailor's Whimsy in the quiet aftermath of another night. Behind the bar Mireya went about the routine of finishing up the last few dishes from the evening's dinner service. Since the Lionstooth opened its doors, competition had grown fierce and drew away many once-loyal patrons. Even on their busiest nights, the aftermath now required only a few minutes to restore order. She placed the final wooden plate back on the shelf, letting out a sigh as she surveyed the emptiness of the room–except for the half-elf immersed in her studies there at the corner. 

M’ylaena had scarcely moved for the past hour, the last time she had poured herself a cup. It sat mostly untouched in that time; the first few nights Mireya had thought it the quality of the brew, but she knew better now. Whatever drove M’ylaena had less use for food and drink than she did. 

Picking at her fingernails nervously, Mireya worked up the courage to join M’ylaena. She took a seat across from the scholar, glancing at the tomes that surrounded her. There was a common theme among them, and Mireya couldn't help but ask, "Goblins and their rampages keeping you up? You've got quite the collection spread out tonight."

It took wading a stretch of silence to make it to the shores of M’ylaena’s attention. "Hardly a rampage. Of late their activity has become more organized–and more interesting beside, our mutual friends have brought hidden patterns to light.”

Mireya nodded, her curiosity evident. "How do you mean organized? They still seem as random as ever.”

M'ylaena took a sip of her tea  before answering with a brief flicker of disappointment at the temperature, "Their attacks have always had a strategy behind them, but more recently... Considering our friends’ discoveries, their end goal goes beyond short term supplies. I've been delving into their culture and religion to see if there's a pattern. It's fascinating."

Raising an eyebrow, Mireya pressed further, "Fascinating how?"

M'ylaena spread her hands ever so slightly.  "A people once believed too simple for culture, now discovered to have a rich one? Written language, a god? Novel recipes? They have been steeping there in the woods, but something has stirred them from their home. I’m trying to piece together why. All evidence points to a nature deity, not an imperialist. But no matter the schism they appear to be going through–And I do suspect it is a structured enough religion to call it a schism–I do believe this to be a guardian spirit, or at the very worst one inclined to natural cycles as it comes to destruction and creation. Who knew goblins would prove so fascinating?"

Leaning in, Mireya shifted the conversation. "Deity? You mean like our Twelve? Most of the locals around here worshiped Tyrachon, and I grew up following Petra, of course.” She paused and considered her words carefully but decided to forge ahead: "And well, I’m wondering about Aspiria. Do you think the old stories are true? Is Aspiria really dead, or is there something more to it?"

The question came suddenly and seemed to throw the half-elf off balance. She leaned back in her chair, away from the tomes, and considered the innkeeper before her. "There are theories within some sects… most believe the Titan is dead and the world is built upon Her corpse. Some small number, typically those from Demeria’s flock, sects whose beliefs survive only by oral tradition, who think it's the only living part of Her left, and the planet itself may still hold some remnants of Her once immense power. If one were to consider pneuma a ‘life force’, it stands to reason, but the idea seems… effectless? An interesting thought experiment without a doubt."

Mireya pondered with curiosity. "Makes you wonder about the secrets hidden beneath our feet.” Mireya dropped her eyes to her fidgety hands, “Makes you wonder what could it mean if a voice claiming to be Aspiria spoke to someone."

“I meant more along the lines of confirming theoretical pneuma channeling equations. Atmospheric concentrations defy the expected–oh,” M’Ylaena pauses herself. If the implication were not so interesting, she might have exercised some satisfaction at having picked up on the implication at all. “Oh. Tell me more.”
