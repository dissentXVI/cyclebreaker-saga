# Update Letter — Aqualonde

*A letter from Mireya delivered to the party in Aqualonde. Laconia is holding, the newcomers are adapting, but Tarwa'kem loggers are pushing into the forest and tensions are rising.*

---

The following letter has been delivered to the party in Aqualonde, having been sealed with a crescent moon

Dearest friends,

I hope your travels around Halinor are going well. I've last heard your direction aims for Carinthia first, so hopefully this letter finds you fresh off one victory and before an imminent second. And you will enjoy warmer climes after seeing the fortress of the north!

Laconia is doing well in your absence, though I know the locals miss you all. Helen and Bella have returned but briefly–they have some supplies and concoctions to gather for their noble patrons. Who might have imagined something like that before your arrival? Diazana and Jendrick have written from the field. They believe they have found the site they were looking for and Sylviane is looking forward to exploring. I think she misses her brother, for as much as she ‘complains’ about him, Jendrick claims it is never especially sincere. 

The newcomers are acclimating well. Our D'yadyan friends have been too long in drier lands and marvel at the rains we get. It's been some trouble for some of their staple crops, though, and while we've had some luck adapting with our farmers’ know-how, we’ll be glad to pick Helen’s brain while she's here. The goblins have interspersed well, and both they and the tieflings have good protection with Zeyn. I do wish he would take a break and relax from the constant drilling and training, but I suppose Laconia will not regret having a most capable corps of guards. 

Particularly not when the forest has become more contested of late. Loggers from Tarwa’kem are staking claims and setting patrols with a little more desperation than before. No fights have broken out yet, but Zeyn seems to think it will be inevitable. 

The town will hold and our mission, as we've discussed before, will continue. Yenden believes he has found some reports from the south that might bear investigation–increased talk of savagery in Gragato, thanks to some contacts he's made around Calden’s Keep. Nothing to draw you away, of course–Jendrick is closer, after all, and can break away from D and S to investigate first hand. 

I know it will be tough to get back to Laconia until after Avosa, but if you find the time, a warm welcome is always found here. Laena, thank you for the supplies from Tarwa’kem! Also we have some good news from Gothmog’s friends. Mission success, and they will be heading back here soon. More reason to come visit?

Your friend, 

Mireya L
