# It Belongs Out of a Museum

*After Dagozog's fall, Theo and Ylaena return to Shurevaire on a hunch from Diazana — to recover the second piece of Velethoui's staff from the Hall of Antiquities. The security has been massively upgraded. The piece will need to be stolen.*

---

It Belongs Out of a Museum!

*This story takes place after the fall of Dagozog, during the adventures of the party in Tarwa’kem, Carinthia, and Aqualonde. The travel to Shurevaire is a long one, but based on a hunch that Diazana had about Velethoiu’s staff and on behalf of a request from Gothmog, Theo and Ylaena have left Laconia to recover the second part of the archmage’s staff from Shurevaire’s vaults. They encounter massively enhanced security and the conclusion that this staff piece will need to be stolen, for without it the Paragon problem may grow too great to handle.*

	“You don’t miss it a little?” Theo asked as the pair walked through the courtyard paths to the Hall of Antiquities. There was the sort of hustle and bustle unique to Shurevaire–many people going many ways, but more often than not quietly absorbed in their own business and not infrequently looking at a book or scroll as they went.

	“I’m not ungrateful for my time,” the woman next to him claimed as they strode purposefully toward the archives. The first few checkpoints were simple–they merely lifted their hands, displayed their rings of station, and passed on through.

	“But you enjoy being out in the world more,” Theo surmised for her.

	She wore something approaching a smirk as she said, “I appreciate the freedom fieldwork brings. Here there is always some… someone else checking over your work. And they always have opinions about what you’re doing, how you’re doing it, whether it should be done at all. I see why Beira left it.”

	“Your old tea–her,” Theo answered simply, lightly; it was both a sore subject for her and an ill-timed one for them both. The Restricted Section stood before them, and before it loomed one of the Arbiters. 

	“Paperwork, scholars, and business plainly stated,” intoned the robed figure, the orb-adorned staff in their hand coming to glow. 

	Theo and Ylaena both carried missives from Yenden, and the arcane marks glowed truthfully as the Arbiter waved the light from their staff over it. 

	“We’ve come on behalf of Scholarus Medius Clearlade,” Ylaena explained simply, “For matters related to his Thesis. References to Veleth–”

	The Arbiter frowned and shook his head. “Due to recent… events, access to this area is barred. Given your fieldwork placement, you might not have heard.”

	Ylaena bowed her head and supplied in turn, “We know full well, Arbiter, and were attempting to spare mention of your order’s… oversight. The spell of witness, after all,” she added as her eyes rose to the orb aglow in the Arbiter’s staff.

	“The incident is resolved, Arbiter; we are actually on a mission of reunification,” Theo added helpfully, and when the robed figure turned their sight toward the elf, he gave a good-natured smile and pat of his bag. “Unfortunately the perpetrators are not fit to receive punishment, but I think your order will be satisfied with the current, ah, state of their being.”

	The silence hung in the air as the robed figure waited for the staff’s light to waver, but it shone true. The Arbiter spoke at last, “Very well. You will be permitted to pass. Relics are not permitted to leave the grounds. Prepare for search prior to departure.” The staff’s light died as it pulled back toward its bearer, permitting the two students to enter the maze of the Hall of Antiquities. 

	

	The pair walked on into the depths of the archives, whispering amongst themselves as they went–Theo a touch more worried as he pointed out the flaws of their plan.

	“Arcane Eyes, Arbiters, search on exit–I can speak around the truth as well as you, Miss Sharie, but I sincerely hope you have more to it than that when it comes to our escape,” Theo hissed. One of the Eyes hummed with strain as it bobbed in the air among them, watching for nefarious activity. It was itself not focused on them, of course, but those floating sentinels would be a problem if they were seriously going to grab the base of the staff.

	“Did you know your problem is actually the same as Shurevaire’s own? At least when it comes to defending against the vast possibilities of mischief that may come?” wondered the dreary young woman walking at Theo’s side.

	“It is certainly not with the divination or abjuration side of things,” he opined quietly as the two of them moved. “I assure you, there’s no slacking when it comes to–”

	“Imagination, Theo,” comes the slight sigh that passed for a laugh with Ylaena. “You’re both too earnest to comprehend the depths a desperate soul might go through to obtain power.”
	His own lips twitched amusedly as they entered the catalogues relevant to their current interests. “That’s not worrisome at all. Explain to me then, O Practiced Thief, how we’re going to do this?”

	“You’re going to eat the staff. I’ll swap out a relic of comparable power to trick the Eyes, and the weave of your body’s own pneuma will keep them from sussing out most of the magic shrinking the staff,” Ylaena explained as they drew open the drawer. Her hand was in Theo’s satchel  as she withdrew the lightweight piece of Navarresean architecture. The spell on it was binding, keeping energies in, and as she had already planned, it had a similar philosophy of Weaving to those inlaid in the staff before them.

	Velethoui’s staff, the second piece of the broken relic, sat in a broad but shallow shelf on a pillow of velvet. It seemed to still the air as they drew it out from storage, quieting even the hum of the Eyes on their patrols outside the small alcove where the students stood. It was white with the appearance of wood, but frankly the thing was likely more pneuma made material than it was truly natural. Neither of them had studied it first hand; frankly it had never been truly relevant before, not worth putting the eyes of Shurevaire on them for seeking it out. Ylaena had the honors; she lifted it, replacing the staff with a reconfiguring of the Navarresean stone.

	“Well, pocket it in my mouth anyway,” Theo remarked after the silence stood between them for a moment. He had to admit, it was well thought out. “The whiff of Transmutation they’ll gather will be blamed on my own academic focus… the missing weight from my bag, the collective weight of Yenden’s and our own good standing, the empty hands they’ll come away with on the search… It will delay their discovery of the theft, but I fear it will still come to light.”

	His eyes were on her now, weighing up some internal calculus that her own stare returned. Neither bespectacled glance betrayed their inner thoughts; but it was in unison the pair nodded. It was a sacrifice of their own future in Shurevaire, but one that might pay off for broader Halinor. 

	“The paperwork alone would take months to reach the Archdean’s desk,” Ylaena pointed out to the tall elf beside her. His look registered no surprise, only agreement.

	He gave in turn, “And he and the other deans are too concerned with other projects. I’m surprised we are not recalled from Laconia ourselves. No,” he answered with a sigh, “Waiting for approval would set back research for too many moons. The robed men I saw… their machinations are in motion already. We must consider the possibility that every second counts.”

	Ylaena’s own head dipped in ready agreement. “I have studied outside this island before. It would not break anything to return to independence.”

	“Not quite so isolated an independent elective,” Theo answered Ylaena with a reassuring hand on her shoulder. “Besides, when we and our intrepid, courageous, and in one case dashing friends put to work what we discover with Velethoui’s staff, our own scholastic fame will be unimpeachable.”

	That drew a smirk from the dark-haired half-elf. Theo continued, “I do fear Yenden and Gothmog might be implicated in this. At least Gothmog will have an alibi…”

	“And your forgery with the letter might spare Yenden in the crossfire,” Ylaena assured Theo as she handed him the shrunken staff. “Now come along; I want to be across the sea by the time they discover this. Unimaginative they may be in crime, but I doubt that holds true for punishment.”
