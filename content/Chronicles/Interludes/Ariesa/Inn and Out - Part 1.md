# Inn and Out — Part 1

*Ariesa, traveling alone toward Laconia, clears a rat nest from the Merry Mountain Inn's cellar — and is quietly shuffled out the back door. On returning she finds a Tarwa'kin noblewoman's carriage and a face she recognizes.*

---

"And I do thankee kindly for your help, miss," the Innkeeper whispered as he shoved a bag of coins into Ariesa's hand. "You'll find the promised fee there, plus a little extra for your discretion."

Ariesa gave the jittery little man a full-toothed grin and a conspiratorial wink. "I'd never slay and tell, my friend, but I understand your concern. Rest easy that none of your regulars will hear any details from me." She referred, of course, to the dire rat nest that she had just removed from the cellar of the Merry Mountain Inn. It hadn't been a particularly glorious battle, but she needed the money to continue her journey to Laconia.

"Details? I don't…" The man blinked at her, confused.

An angry flush climbed up Ariesa's neck. That explained why he had hurried her to the back door when she'd finished the task. He wasn't worried about her gossiping about rat-infested sacks of potatoes. He was worried that the travelers who frequented his Inn would learn he had employed someone like her.

"I personally don't mind Tieflings myself at all," He stammered out, "But we don't get many around these parts, and you know how people are, especially nowadays: superstitious and small-minded. Folks might avoid the Inn, trying to skirt bad luck," He kept up his nervous chatter while slowly closing the door in her face. "There's a nice, clear creek to the northwest, not ten minutes' walk if you want to wash up. You're rather dusty and there are some rat…well, I really am grateful to you. Best of luck. Good-bye."

With a thud and a click, Ariesa was shut out of the Inn, left alone in a neatly-tended kitchen garden. She cursed, gathered her strength, and gave the door a kick. The offending wood barely rattled in its frame. She kicked it once more for good measure then stomped off toward the creek to clean the rat guts out of her hair.

At the creek, she lathered her hair and scrubbed her skin raw. The water was fresh but icy with the early spring thaw, so she wasn't tempted to linger. It took her a few hours longer to give her clothes a thorough cleaning and even more time to plait her thick, curling hair into a braid. When she was finished, her small, cloudy looking glass told her that she looked more like a dignified traveler and less like a vagrant. Maybe she even looked too dignified. She re-braided her hair so that it fell over one shoulder. It looked less severe, more spunky, still respectable.

"Don't get many Tieflings around these parts, huh?" Ariesa gave her reflection a smile sharpened by the steel in her spine. "Sounds like your patrons are overdue for some healthy exposure."

The sky was fading into the rosy tints of dusk when Ariesa made it back to the Inn. All in nature was calming down for the night, but the people in the area obviously hadn't gotten the message. Employees in rough homespun darted around the front door like confused ants pouring from a kicked hill. The Innkeeper was wringing his hands, standing before a group of people right out of a romance. Roughly a dozen armored humans surrounded a lavish carriage. The carriage was a deep night-sky blue with gold embellishments. A golden hare, raised to attention on its back legs, was emblazoned on the side. The polished armor of the guards also sported an engraved rabbit on each breastplate.

Ariesa caught the eye of an Inn employee scurrying by carrying an armful of sheets. "Hey, what's going on?"

The woman's eyes drifted to Ariesa's horns, but she dipped a small curtsy and gave her a half-smile. "Some Tarwa'kin noble can't get to town tonight and needs a room. Now, somehow, we're meant to turn the Merry Mountain into a palace. If you'll excuse me, ma'am," The employee all but sprinted away before Ariesa could offer a helping hand. She turned back to the armored guards, satisfied with watching the spectacle unfold and waiting until she could enter the dining room without the risk of being bowled over by a zealous employee or skewered by a zealous guard.

The guards began to enter the Inn in groups of three and returning to their commanding officer to report that there were no assassins lurking behind curtains or underneath tables. At least, that's the report Ariesa assumed they gave since eventually the guards formed two lines on either side of the carriage and the vehicle's door opened. The occupant within exited one cautious step at a time like a fawn unsure of danger.

A swelling bubble of certainty finally popped in Ariesa's chest when the blue-clad woman raised her dark eyes from the earth and looked up at the Inn. A stinging sense of wrongs never righted propelled Ariesa forward, and she yelled as she went.

"Hey! You! Tsua Zuberi!"

Five steps away from the startled noblewoman and the pikes of the guards crossed to block Ariesa's path. She glared at them, dismissed them. "Zuberi! I recognize you. Still assaulting Tieflings in your spare time?"

The commanding officer lunged at Ariesa, grabbing her by the neckline of her robes. "How dare you accost Lady Zuberi in such a manner?" He shook her with enough force to rattle her teeth, almost picking her up by her collar. "I'll drag you to town and throw you in the stocks myself to teach you – "

"Captain, please! Oh, please, let her go."

One beat while the Captain and Ariesa both stared in round-eyed surprise at Tsua. Then an armored fist begrudgingly opened and Ariesa leaned on her staff to regain her balance. Tsua squeezed by the guards separating them and flung her silky, sepia arms around Ariesa's neck. Ariesa, frozen in confusion, barely caught the whisper that Tsua sighed against her ear. "Thank Tytos. I thought I'd never find you."
