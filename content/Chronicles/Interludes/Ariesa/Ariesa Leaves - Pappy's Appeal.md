# Ariesa Leaves — Backstory PoV: Pappy

*Written from the perspective of Volkar 'Pappy' Hecate — an appeal to the Patriarch on the night Ariesa leaves the Kula.*

---

The night the Radiant Beast was unleashed at Lake Sticaea was a mixed bag for the kula. We 
were far from the carnage, huddled outside a mountain village in western Tarwa’kem, blissfully 
unaware of any blood sacrifices, possible cataclysms, or mind-melting monstrosities. Though 
the coming days would be full of terror and uncertainty, that night itself, as I’ve noted, was a 
mixed bag. 
 
On the one hand, it was the night Garreth Teufelloaken came into his power. A crowd of us old-
timers were gathered around the dying embers of a bonfire. Like old-timers the world over, we 
didn’t need much sleep, and we habitually clung to the vestiges of fire and warm company. To 
this day I recall the awed stillness that fell over us all as Garreth, nothing more than a wisp of a 
child, stepped into the dim-bright shadows, conjured light between his palms, and smiling, 
released a glorious glowing sphere into the sky. The ease with which he commanded the spell, 
and without the aid of study, amazed even our most seasoned wizards. Out of nowhere, our 
Garreth had manifested sorcerous abilities. It was a moment of peace, of hope, of tranquil 
certainty that we, the “devilkin” had been blessed. 
 
On the other hand…the serenity that Garreth conjured was banished by a piercing shriek: “I 
CAN’T TURN IT OFF!” A tightly spinning ball of fire puttered around the koshakas, burning holes 
in tent canvas and catching wagon wheels aflame. Ariesa Hecate, barely ten years of age and in 
her night dress, chased the ball, batting out the fires with her bare hands, wailing in confusion 
and fear. If Garreth was blessed with power, Ariesa was cursed. The past ten years, he’s been 
refining his magical craft, astonishing all of us with his brilliance. He’s born to be a leader of our 
people, that’s for sure. And Ariesa? She struggles. We all know it. She can barely get her spells 
to work, and when she can, they’re usually out of her control. And where Garreth is a model 
Tiefling, adept at finding safe havens and navigating the world we live in, Ariesa is…not.  
 
Now, I know what folks say about Ariesa, and I’m not here to argue. I’ve heard her described as 
headstrong, overconfident, obnoxious, disrespectful, and reckless, and by Caphos, I’ve called 
her worse, myself. But I know her better than anyone. I practically raised her. Her Daddy and 
Mama don’t know what to do with her and never have – with her constant questions, arguing 
about everything from the proper way to mend a kettle to the structure of society. Then there 
was…the incident. When she beat up those kids who made fun of Garreth and we had to leave 
that city lickety-split? Her Daddy and Mama made a decision. “Stick her with her Pappy because 
he’s a grumpy bastard who can break the spirit of this little girl with a chip on her shoulder and 
a fast mouth.” So I got saddled with an apprentice Tinker and she got stuck with the kula’s 
misanthrope. But damned if we didn’t grow on each other. 
 
What was I saying? That’s right. Even though she’s bullheaded and won’t take wisdom from the 
Gods themselves, she’s got heart. No head, but lots of heart. I know her Daddy has his horns in 
a twist that she wants to leave, but maybe she should. Maybe she should go to Lake Sticaea. 
Maybe she will find answers about her sorcerous abilities and even get help controlling them. 
An old man can dream. And what about her dream? She’s been fighting tooth and nail for folks 
like us to be a real part of society. Is it stupid for her to think that, should she become some 
famous adventurer, she’ll convince everyone that our kind aren’t evil, aren’t bad luck, are just 
normal folks who fight with their husbands and worry about their kids and look forward to 
leaving work for the day? Oh, it’s stupid. It’s impressively stupid. But I’d rather she be stupid 
with a big heart than smart and cruel. 
 
I guess all I’m saying is…if Ariesa leaves, then she should be allowed to come back. We 
shouldn’t turn our backs on her because she’s not turning her back on us. I know it feels that 
way, especially for her parents and her little sister, but she’s already got a journey inside of her. 
I for one vote we let her go on it with our love and our blessing.  
 
Volkar “Pappy” Hecate 
An Appeal to the Patriarch
