# Ariesa Runs Away

*Ariesa kills a needle blight alone in the forest to prove the Kula's worth to a nearby town — and nearly dies doing it. The elders confine her to quarters. She starts packing.*

---

A stinging bead of sweat slid into Ariesa's eye. She tried to blink the sensation away, unwilling to pause her toilsome trudge to wipe the soot off her face. Victorious march, she mentally corrected. This is a victorious march, not a trudge. To an outside observer, she conceded, it might look like a trudge. A dirty tiefling woman dragging the smoldering remains of needle blight out of the forest would surely give off "trudge" vibes. And observers wouldn't even know how badly her feet hurt, how much her side burned, how many blisters had ripped open on her sap-covered hands. "But what am I supposed to do?" She muttered to her crispy cargo, "If I hack off your head it's just gonna look like I chopped up a shrub. I need all of you to get that 'gaze upon my vanquished foe; the forest is safe now' effect." She grunted as she heaved the blight's remains over a particularly clingy tree root. "Next time I'm bringing a cart."

By the time she hauled her trophy to the Kula's encampment, the sun had retreated below the tree line. Her vision swam, but somehow or other she managed to stumble to the Patriarch's tent. Muffled voices carried through the thick canvas. Good. She'd have an audience. She was surprised to note that her arm shook as she swept the tent cover to the side and stepped grandly into the warm, well-lit tent.

Each and every one of the Kula's leaders turned to stare at her in shock. She wanted to meet their gaze with a triumphant grin – she really did. She was quite proud of herself. But instead her legs gave out and she went down to her knees. The tent went blurry as figures rushed towards her.

"By Caphos' forge, Ariesa, you're bleeding! Someone get a healer!" Her mother's voice, maybe?

"Ariesa? Ariesa, can you hear me?" Oh, she could hear him alright. Garreth. What was he doing here? Wait, did her mother say she was bleeding? She glanced down to discover that she was indeed kneeling in a pool of blood. Must be hers, she concluded, since needle blights don't bleed, but she couldn't connect that glistening red puddle to her body, which had gone comfortably numb. She opened her mouth to assure everyone that she was fine and redirect their attention to the monster she had slain, but instead she fell forward, succumbing to darkness.

She woke up minutes later in her own tent. Someone must have carried her to her cot, where she was now wrapped in blanket and a buzz of voices.

"What was she doing out in the forest?"

"Nevermind what she was doing! Did anyone from town see her?"

"What are these sticks she was — Caphos! Is this a blight?"

"The blight that attacked the hunters from town, you think?"

"What does that matter? If she was seen, we must act, and quickly."

"We need to let her rest. We can decide how to handle the situation without disturbing her." Ugh. Garreth again. Ariesa shoved herself up on her elbows, bristling at being ignored like some sick child.

"I don't need rest. I was just…dehydrated, probably."

A handful of the Kula's most senior members (and Garreth) were standing in a semi-circle around the foot of her cot, watching her with a blend of trepidation and curiosity. Garreth wordlessly handed her a mug of water. She mumbled "thanks," and took a sip.

A stern-looking woman crossed her arms over her chest. "Ariesa, your ribs were practically laid bare. It's a miracle you made it back to us and, quite frankly, irritating that you're conscious. Garreth's right. You need rest."

Ariesa dimly remembered the needle blight lunging towards her, claws extended, and a sharp flash of pain along her side before her chromatic orb set the creature alight. Fresh bandages wrapped around her chest confirmed the truth of the wound. She shook her head, dismissing the concern.

"Listen. We need to take the blight's remains to town and tell them we took care of it. From what Nalisti heard at the tavern, this thing has been plaguing them for months. Surely they'll at least give us camping rights on the commons now, maybe even let us hunt."

A few of the elders exchanged thoughtful glances, but the man who spoke first was angry. "We can't tell them you killed a blight! Tieflings aren't allowed in their game woods, and you know that. We'll be run off!"

Ariesa shot back, "I've done them a service. They won't care that I was in the woods."

One Tiefling raised a large, gnarled hand, and the hubbub died down. "Ariesa, as ever, you've acted on impulse, without consideration." The Patriarch's voice was low and quiet. Like thunder in the distance, it bade you stop, listen, and be cautious. But Ariesa wasn't good at heeding warning signs.

"It's a good thing, too," she snapped. "That blight was hurting people, and now it's gone. This could change things for us! How can people think we're bad luck if we're solving their problems?"

Garreth broke the stunned silence that followed her outburst. "You haven't solved their problem, though." He held up the splintery hand of the needle blight. A sickly green symbol was carved into the palm. "This blight was a summons, I'd say from a hag. And it is still in those game woods, plotting who knows what." The Patriarch smiled proudly and placed a hand on Garreth's shoulder. Ariesa's jaw clenched.

"That doesn't change anything. It just adds an extra step. I'll go out tomorrow and track down the hag."

Garreth's eyes darted to meet hers. "That's delusional."

"I handled the blight, didn't I?"

"Hardly. You almost got yourself killed. If you keep chasing danger like this, one day you're only coming home in a pine box."

"It's my hide to risk."

"You're not risking it, Ariesa, you're throwing it away."

Ariesa, fuming, started to respond, but the Patriarch cut her off.

"Garreth is right, my dear. You're being far too reckless. We simply can't allow it." Where Garreth's tone was cold, the Patriarch's was warm, consoling. Ariesa wasn't sure which was more unbearable. "We'll see to it that you keep to your quarters tonight so that you can rest," — and so you don't sneak off to the forest or go into town with your prize, was the silent addition. "Come along, everyone. And Garreth, let's get you out of that bloody tunic."

The moment the tent flap stilled behind them, Ariesa pulled herself out of her cot. She winced as her wound protested but moved with a speed born of anger, defiance, and embarrassment. She grabbed a rucksack and began shoving her meager belongings inside.

"Going somewhere?"

Ariesa froze.
