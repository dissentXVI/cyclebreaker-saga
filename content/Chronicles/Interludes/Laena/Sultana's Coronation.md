# Sultana's Coronation

*Two years before the campaign begins — the Zahir family attends the coronation of Sultana Neema Karo-kem. The new Sultana's first decree shocks the nobility: all trade with the Vociran Empire is severed immediately.*

---

*Neema Karo-kem’s coronation and subsequent party, 2 years ago, in 4023 ApN. *

After waiting in line for longer than Oman thought was reasonable, given their status, the Zahir family finally entered the throne room to swear their allegiance and obedience to the new Sultana. 

Guards opened the double oak doors to reveal a gold and red carpet that stretched on for what seemed like eternity. And at the end, an opulent, huge, golden throne where the new Sultana sat. On either side of the carpets were scores of noble families that had already completed the oath. They were framed by huge tapestries on the walls behind them depicting the many battles Tarwa’kem has won, the Tefuti stream, and rain festivals, among other things. 

Servants hurried forward to offer the Zahir’s each a small serving of water. They all smelled it reverently before drinking. 

The Zahir family moved forward, down the carpet, with confidence. It was a lucky day, this coronation, blessed by an overcast sky, and more personally for the Zahir’s, a day Monifa could walk with some normalcy. 

As they reached the end of the carpet, all of the women curtsied, Oman bowed, and then, only he knelt in front of her majesty. A scribe on her majesty’s left stepped forward and declared:

 “Repeat these vows after me Oman Zahir, and you and your family will be under the rule and protection of her royal majesty from here until her last day”. Oman lifted his head and did as bid. 

“I, Oman Zahir, swear on my family’s honor to obey the Sultana, ruler of our glorious nation. I vow to uphold the laws and traditions of our land. I vow to protect and defend the Sultana’s sovereignty. May she live long, bear many heirs, and may Tytos guide her hand to the betterment of all of Tarwa’kem.” 

The Sultana nodded once, and the Zahir’s were ushered to the side to wait until the remaining nobles (the ones that ranked lower than them), completed their own vows. After the final swearings, both the highest ranking priest of Tytos’ temple and her majesty stepped forward in front of the throne, and faced each other. 

The High Priest declared loudly: “I come to charge and anoint you in Tytos’ name and speak on his behalf, Neema Karo-kem. Tytos charges you to dispense justice wisely and compassionately, to uphold the law and order that protects this nation, and to continue to seek Tytos’ guidance in matters large and small. Do you accept this charge?” 

“I do”, the Sultana’s voice rang out clearly. 

The priest moved to secure a silk sky blue cloak outlined in white fur around her shoulders. As he did so, he recited “Within the thunder’s roar lies the voice of justice. Let the skies guide your actions, for in order and law, harmony shall prevail.” 

The Sultana took a few steps forward, her sky blue cloak now trailing behind her, closed her eyes for just a moment, and then spoke: 

“I welcome all of you to my home and encourage you to take full advantage of the food, water, wine, and entertainment in the courtyards and halls beyond. It is my honor to begin my reign of prosperity, justice, peace, and equity surrounded by the lords, ladies, merchants, and servants that maintain and grow our beloved nation. And I will begin that reign as I intend to continue it, with justice in mind.” 

“My first decree as Sultana is…..any and all trade with the Vociran Empire is severed as of this moment, and any further trade is forbidden. Anyone found to have been trading with them in any capacity will face harsh and severe punishments.” 

A small rumble of whispers and murmurs moved through the crowd, but they were almost immediately silenced by a crash of thunder and lightning that echoed through the marble throne room. 

The Sultana gestured to the doors, “And now, please join me in celebration of a new era for Tarwa’kem”.
