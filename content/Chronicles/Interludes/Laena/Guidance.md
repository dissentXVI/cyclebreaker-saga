# Guidance

*In Tarwa'kem during the party's visit, Laena slips away to the Hall of Order for a long overdue session of meditation and prayer. Tytos shows her visions of what's still to come — and she cautiously reaches out toward Aspiria.*

---

Laena leaves her friends behind and to their own devices. She hopes they can stay out of trouble. Gothmog seems to be the only one of us whose past is not catching up to them here. Laena looks forward to visiting a liberated Ilsong one day so SHE can be the one relaxing and enjoying a new environment without a consequence around every corner. 

She makes her way through the historical quarter to the Hall of Order. Tytos’ familiar symbol is etched into both heavy wooden doors. She clasps her holy symbol and enters. This should have been her first visit after home, but she’s here now. The large windows are tinted blue, the marble floors and columns gleam as if they were just polished, and the beaming sun casts a warm light through the clear window above. The smell of incense perfumes the air, and it immediately brings Laena some peace before she’s even begun to pray. 

A novice attendant she’s never met brings her some priestess robes, which Laena dons. She sees a few familiar faces, but this is not the place for small talk. That’s what the antechambers are for. It is a relief to be back in this holy place. There is no comparing her meditations and prayers here vs elsewhere in the world. Tytos is here, and she can feel it. 

Laena makes her way to a corner of the chamber with plush carpeting, pillows, and incense ready to light. She sits, crosses her legs, and begins the process of focusing her mind to make room for Tytos’ guidance. 

It’s a familiar process, and similar to her morning prayers that get her mind centered for spell casting. It starts with breathing, in and out steadily, focus on the breathing in counts, keep counting until the rhythm is stable. Once your lungs can keep their own time, you breathe without counting but staying focused on the breathing. After some time, you can release the focus on breathing to allow thoughts to come to you. As they do, you acknowledge, but do not ruminate on them, and then let them pass. 

Many thoughts swirl through Laena’s mind, as is to be expected when her body is still. The thoughts that come are more….themed….than usual. Over and over, she thinks of the good that her group has accomplished. But more than the deeds, she sees the people. She sees Crusty and Jag Deck, Mireya and Jendrick, Zeyne and his fellow Dyadyans, Y’laena and Yenden, Jemma and Yara, Helen and Bella, the Said family, ever so briefly Reyne’s face appears, even Valeria and Alyndra float through.

After some time allowing her thoughts to wander, she begins the process of more firmly directing her thoughts to see how Tytos may guide them. She goes down paths of regrets from her time away from Tarwakem, thinks of leaving and missing her family, thinks of rashly killing the dwarven man in Azizi’s employ, of allowing so many to die in battle at Laconia when she could have acted, of Reyne’s death, of keeping the amulet’s power a secret from her group, of not seeing the goblins as people until Areisa forced her to.  

However at each of these threads, she is nudged away and sees events that have yet to be: an alliance against the Vociran, a thwarted cataclysm, Tieflings and Dyadyans moving freely and with legal protections, the Laconian Welcome Hall bustling with folks from all walks of life, a liberated Ilsong, a red headed teenager embracing her mother. 

Laena maintains her breathing, and almost doesn’t have the courage to do what’s next. Oh, so very tentatively, she thinks one word, “Aspiria”, and nothing else. She waits; waits to be cast down, waits to be cursed, waits for a castigation that would never come. 

Instead, in her chest, it feels like she just drank hot chocolate. She is warm and comforted. 

Laena opens her eyes to see that hours have passed. The sun hangs low in the sky, and the chamber is not as well lit. As she rises, she stretches up towards that sky. On her way out, she tosses a donation into the collection box. 

What she had told Valeria is true. It is impossible to imagine navigating this uncertain world without the guidance of her God, and she is grateful for it.
