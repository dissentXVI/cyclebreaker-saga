# A New Era Begins with a Party

*At the Sultana's coronation celebration, Laena learns disturbing news about her prospective fiancé Tamar from Lady Tsua, then hides under a desk while two members of the monetarist council plot against the crown.*

---

A New Era Begins with a Party

Despite the Sultana’s rather dramatic announcement, the coronation celebration went on in full force as planned. There was something new to see or hear around every corner. Music, dancing, hushed conversations, food, games. It was a feast for all your senses. Laena loves a good party, and this is a once in a generation celebration that would last well into the dawn hours.

Laena took a moment to check her dress and accessories before moving to the party itself. Her flowy, bright orange silk dress had slitted full length sleeves and layers and layers of skirts to give it movement. Her accessories were all golden: golden flats, a gold belt at her waist, and a small gold tiara with diamond drops sat upon her braids. It was all well in order.

After mingling for a bit, Laena found her family seated at a round table, listening to the orchestral band and watching many couples dance together on the wide open, polished dance floor. Laena asked, “How are the festivities treating you so far?”

“Laena, did you see the peacocks outside? They’re so bright and colorful, and when I went up to one, it snapped at me!” Oseye exclaimed in glee, laughing.

Monifa nodded along excitedly, “Oh, oh, and there’s a pastry tower that goes all the way up to the ceiling in the deserts room. Papa put me on his shoulders so I could get the highest one! It was rose flavored. How do they even get rose flavor in food?!” Monifa asked, bouncing in her seat as Oman smiled indulgently.

As Laena continued to learn about her family’s adventures, the bands changed out and began playing a different style of music. This music was upbeat and fast, still meant for coupled dancing, but with more flare. Monifa’s head snapped to the musicians, and her smile died as she began picking at her second desert instead of eating heartily.

Their mother Rhaena said “Why don’t we all take to the dance floor together? That’s your favorite song Moni!” with a smile that was just a bit too big.

Monifa shook her head, “I…wouldn’t look right, and besides it’s meant for two people to dance, not a group like us.”

Oseye pushed her chair back, got up with purpose, and reached her hand out to Monifa. Rhaena said “You don’t have to Moni, but if you want to, we shall. Who cares what anyone else thinks? All that matters is that we have fun, and the siren take anyone who thinks otherwise. What do you say?”

Monifa glanced around, but nodded, so the four ladies got up and took to the edge of the dance floor, where they danced with abandon. Laena twirled Monifa in the air; Rhaena dipped Oseye all the way to the ground; they all held hands and skipped in a circle. Their movements didn’t match anyone else’s, and Monifa’s movements were stilted, but the ladies Zahir did not notice any of that. They didn’t notice the looks of surprise and consternation on their fellow dancer’s faces; they didn’t notice the brief smile the Sultana sent their way as she passed through; and they didn’t notice Oman wipe a small tear away as he gazed at his girls adoringly.

Oman had ‘gently instructed’ Laena to find Tamar Taha at the party, and even though Rhaena had rolled her eyes at that, Laena did plan to try and speak with him at some point. Tamar’s family had a good, old name, but his father’s gambling put them in some degree of financial peril (something not many were aware of). And that’s all Laena really knew of him. They were not officially engaged as of yet, but talks were ongoing. So yes, she intended to find him, but was it her fault if she happened to run into her good friend Lady Tsua Zuberi first? Certainly not.

Laena entered the gaming room where she found Lady Zuberi finishing up a game of senet with an older woman. Tsua came up to Laena “Thank goodness. I’m glad you found me!” then lowered her voice to a whisper “I have something to tell you.”

Taking the hint Laena said “Yes, I’d love to take the air with you.” As they linked elbow in elbow, heading down towards the interior courtyard. On the way, Laena asked “Will you be playing your lute this evening? It is both your right and an honor to show your talents tonight.”

Tsua blushed and replied “I still have yet to play for an audience so publically. How can my first real performance be at the Sultana’s coronation?! It wouldn’t do. What if I….failed?”

Laena countered “What if you succeed? Everyone here is putting on an act or face in some way. Do the same, and embody a confident performer. I love your music. I know everyone else will too. Think on it, the hour grows late.”

Tsua nodded reluctantly as they finally strolled through the archway into the interior courtyard. Like everything in the palace, it was enormous. The moonlight and oil lamps cast jumping shadows as they walked, giving a new view of what normally one would only see during the day. The moonflower, which only blooms in moonlight, was on a vividly purple display right in the center of the gardens. The paths snaked around confusingly, but provided cover and privacy, if you knew where to look, and Laena and Tsua both did.

As they rounded a corner, they made their way behind a large willow bush. Tsua started “My apologies for the secrecy. I was sworn not to share this, but I had to give you the information.” She took a breath. “One of the maid servants in the Taha household has been blessed by Pegape.”

Laena raised one eyebrow, “And……”

Tsua continued “And this is the third unmarried maid servant to be so blessed in the household. No one knows what happened to the other two. They just….disappeared like grains of sand in the wind.”

Lanea sighed, “Are you sure it’s him and not his father? Or some other lecher?”

Tsua nodded “As sure as rumor and gossip can be. You must tell your father immediately. He would not want this future for you.”

Laena shook her head, “It would not matter. Our name is too new and his is old enough to see us to new heights of society. If this allows our family further footholds and influence among the council or royal family, it will be worth it. You know how this game is played.”

Tsua whispered angrily, “By the twelve Laena, your life is not a game. There are more important things than family ambition.”

Laena shook her head again. “Thank you for telling me. Shall we distract ourselves? I’ve heard there is a mirror maze set up on the third floor.”

Tsua sighed in resignation. “As you wish, but this conversation is not over.”

They left arm in arm again, in silence this time. As they both passed through the archway, Laena’s stomach dropped as she spotted Tamar across the foyer laughing and drinking with his friends, as if nothing was happening in his life at all. Everyone really WAS putting on an act tonight, it seemed.

Tsua noticed the same thing at the same time, did a double take between Tamar and Laena, and pointed in the other direction, “There, go past those red velvet curtains into the library. I shall distract them.”

Laena did as bid, slipping quickly behind the curtains to reveal a large library with several wooden desks, and bookcases from floor to ceiling. Before she even had a chance to peruse, she heard voices just beyond the curtain. In a panic, thinking it may be Tamar, she ducked under a desk. She immediately felt foolish, but it was too late. The voices drew nearer, and she certainly could not emerge now.

“Ramses, you were right. I can not believe she announced that decree right away, without any official input from us. It’s inconceivable.”

“Ah Ngor, in this case I hate to be right. Though, the butcher may call for her yet.”
