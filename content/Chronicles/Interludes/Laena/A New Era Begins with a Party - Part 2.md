A New Era Begins with a Party Part 2

Laena hugged her knees to her chest and attempted to even out her heart rate, sure that the two members of the monetarist council would sense her presence under the desk. They could not be allowed to find her here. She sent a prayer to Tytos and counted her breaths silently.

“Careful Ramses, that kind of talk is dangerous, though I tend to agree. This loss of revenue and trade will hurt all of us. Although one can hardly blame her for punishing those responsible for her father’s assassination.” Ngor stated.

“That is easy for you to say Ngor. Your assets are bound in commodities. I must rely on a veritable array of people and good fortune to ensure these trade routes’ profitability. She could have decreed that those found raiding trade caravans will be stripped and whipped through the public square. But no, she has decided to harm our economy under the guise of justice for her father.” Ramses seethed.

Ngor rebutted, “You do not mean to disobey the order though? Many will find the decree most reasonable. The battle of dagger canyon is not so far removed from the public consciousness.”

“And yet, the unwashed masses have no conception of how intertwined it all is. Pulling this thread will yield many unintended consequences, I have no doubt. But as to your question, I will not disregard her majesty’s commands. I have not made it this far by acting in haste. I will wait to see the consequences and act accordingly. Let her live with her decisions for some time.” Ramses answered, “But what of you Ngor, this impacts your interests as well?”

“Yes, it is all intertwined as you say. I am also inclined to observe, for now. The Sultana is young and eager to prove herself strong and independent of the council. A little time in the real Halinor, away from her tutors, will show her the error of her ways. And if not, may Eirune guide us.” Ngor said with barely concealed threat.

Ramses continued. “Then we are agreed. We might also consider a rendezvous with Oman.”

Laena had succeeded in regulating her physical responses, but that success was no more as her heart beat faster. What business could they have with her father related to the embargo?

It seemed Ngor agreed with Laena on that point, “That grasping, upjumped healer’s son? To what end would we agree to meet with him?”.

Ramses replied “I’ve heard rumors that he’s been in talks with the Vocirans using side channels. It would appear he is now looking for a non magical cure for his daughter’s illness and is inquiring about their people’s healing practices and techniques. I’m unsure as to where those inroads have led him, but he will likely be as troubled with this decree as we are.”

Ngor responded “Ah, I see. That is…interesting information and gives us several options indeed. We could use him to get into Vociran territory or as a convenient place for blame if things go wrong. And he should be a most willing mark. He is clearly desperate to gain our favor, and it could lead to healing for his daughter. Why would he refuse?”.

“Exactly,” Ramses said succinctly.

The two men continued to discuss various other matters of politics for enough time for Laena’s knees to ache. She desperately needed to stretch, and most importantly, leave this place with great haste.

It seemed Tytos would grant her succor in this at least, as the two men adjourned to watch the acrobatic performances in the interior amphitheater. Laena counted to 1,000 before allowing herself to rise. Her knees cracked, and her right foot was fully asleep. It mattered not. She knew where to go.

She walked through the castle in a daze. It would not be beyond her father to associate with the Vocirans, especially on behalf of Moni, and yet, they killed the previous Sultan. Too much interaction with them would bring unwanted enmity from the crown. He had kept this from her too, for her protection ostensibly, but was she or was she not training as heir? Their position was even more precarious than she realized, and her father must know of this conversation. That much was clear.

Her feet had taken her where her soul needed to be. She gazed up at Tytos’ statue, put a platinum piece in the lockbox, knelt, and prayed.
