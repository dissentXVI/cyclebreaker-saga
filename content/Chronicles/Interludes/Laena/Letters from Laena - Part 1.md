# Letters from Laena — Part 1

*Laena's farewell letters to her father and mother, her sister Oseye, and her sick sister Monifa — written the night she leaves Tarwa'kem to search for a cure.*

---

Dearest Papa and Maman, 

I’m sorry to have left you in this unruly manner, but my pleas fell on deafened ears. I can not bear to watch Monifa suffer in the dawn of her coming of age. I can not give up hope for her until every grain of sand has been sifted. I can not….do nothing. 

You have driven home the lesson of “In all things, act in service of the family, for it is the family that strengthens thee.” We can not be at our best and strongest until she is well, and she WILL be well again.

As for what you share with the public, or what you tell Tamar’s family, I will leave that to your capable political savvy. 

You must trust in my ability to retain the lessons you’ve taught me. I will stay safe; Tytos’ reach and protection are never ending. I do not plan to be gone longer than a few years at most, but I will track leads down wherever they may find me. 

I can only beg for forgiveness, since I have failed to ask for permission. 

Love, 

Laena

Oseye, 

I’m afraid your days of flitting from interest to interest and riding around aimlessly are over for now. But do not fret, I will be back to rescue you from my “life of tedium and sandal licking” as soon as I am able to find a cure for Monifa. 

In the meantime, it would be best if you followed Papa’s lead. He and Maman have enough to worry about between my journey and Monifa’s illness, and they don’t need any additional stress. You are old enough to take over my duties and responsibilities as the (temporary, acting) heir apparent. 

I’m leaving you two things. 1) My personal journal tracking the relationships among Tarwakem nobles, merchants, etc, notes on the city, and anything I thought might be useful. Knowledge can be more valuable than gold. and 2) A map to my secret hiding place on the edge of our domain. If it all becomes too much and you need a break, retreat there, it has a special restoration quality to it. 

You may borrow my garments and jewels for special occasions ONLY. 

I love you; don’t do anything crazy like run away. 

Laena 

My dearest Monifa, 

Leaving home is the hardest thing I’ve ever had to do. I’ll miss you, in particular, my baby sister, who bests me at cards, who finds the shade in every situation, who’s singing has brought grown men to tears. The thought of not seeing you every day is almost enough to make me reconsider my actions, almost. 

But seeing you face this painful, debilitating, and mysterious illness with bravery and determination has given me the courage to search for a cure in lands beyond our small horizon. And while it may be FOR you that I chose to leave home temporarily, it is not your fault I am gone. This you must understand. All any of us want is to see you dance and smile without a grimace of pain again. 

There is no need to worry for me, as I will be safe under Tytos’ guidance. Save your worry for Papa and Oseye having to work together. Surely, they won’t have sent each other to the Siren before I return. 

With all my affection and love, 

Laena

Addendum: Look under your bed. I commissioned the best seamstress in town, Madam Sharma, to begin working on some clothing that will be easier for you to get in and out of on days where your legs are not as mobile. This may be of even greater use if the wheeled chair the last physician proposed is something we can manufacture. She has been paid in advance, so work with her to make clothing that will ease some of your daily burden that I know you so despise. 

I hope I return with something to make these accommodations unnecessary, but in the meantime, you deserve to live fully and comfortably.
