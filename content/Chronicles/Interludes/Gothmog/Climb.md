# Climb

*A formative memory — young Gothmog scales 'The Scythe,' a glacial ice wall, to impress his mother Ochmaa. She cuts his safety rope and disappears into the forest with Batuhan. He finishes the climb alone.*

---

“Hey, mom! Are you looking?” The young orc shouts down from his precarious perch on a dizzyingly tall wall of solid ice. Freezing wind whips his blue hair in his face and stings his eyes. His breath puffs out in frozen clouds. His only tools are two ice hatchets, and the only safety measure is a slender hemp rope tied to a boulder 1,000 feet above him. This is the most difficult climb he has attempted since his mother brought him to the mountains north of Frostmarch Forest. Gothmog chose this cliff himself to show her that he wasn’t afraid and nicknamed it “The Scythe” because of its towering overhang of glacial ice that would force him to climb almost upside down before reaching the top. 

	“Yes, Gothmog. I see you,” Ochmaa Gansukh shouts back, her amber eyes tracking his progress. Her well-muscled arms are crossed in front of her chest and icicles have formed in her thick black and silver mane of hair. The ancient horse-sized direwolf pacing at the foot of the wall lets out a plaintive whine and paws at the ice. 

	“Be quiet, Batuhan,”  Ochmaa barks. 

	“Mom! Watch this!” Gothmog shouts and uses all of his might to launch himself upward, ripping both hatchets out of the ice as he leaps through the air. For a thrilling moment, he has no point of contact. Then he strikes out powerfully with both hatchets. One of them bounces wildly off the ice, leaving him dangling by one hand. He laughs crazily and waves at his mom before finding a more secure hold with his second hatchet. His progress has created slack in the rope, which he winds around his belt to keep the line taught in case he slips or the ice doesn’t hold. 

	“Did you see that, mom?” When she doesn’t answer, he cranes his neck to look down and freezes. She is aiming her great bow at the top of the cliff. Gothmog looks for the source of the danger, but it seems like she is shooting at nothing until he realizes – the rope. The hemp rope falls away where her arrow has pierced it and dangles uselessly from his belt. He stares down at her in shock. 

	“No one is coming to help you, child. No one will catch you if you fall. Finish the climb. Batuhan and I are going hunting.” She whistles for the direwolf and they disappear into the forest. 

	Many hours later in the pitch darkness, Gothmog stumbles through the door of their cabin. His hands are a bloody mess, and he’s shaking uncontrollably. His normally emerald green skin is tinged with blue. He looks mutely at Ochmaa, who rises and thrusts a warm mug in his hands. He recognizes his mother’s health potion by its acrid taste and chokes it down. She drapes a warm cloak over his shoulders and sits him down by the fire. A plate of charred meat and crudely chopped root vegetables is waiting for him. Batuhan curls up around him and gingerly licks his fingers. 

	Ochmaa regards him over the fire and says, “Your face may be the Shamed One’s face, but your heart is Gansukh. The blood of the noble house of Tatar’sar runs through your veins, son. Grond favors you. What are our words?” 

	Gothmog tries to speak and coughs violently. He takes a sip of the bitter herbal brew and rasps, “Better to die with honor than to live with shame.”
