# The King of the Wood

*At Shurevaire academy, M'ylaena convinces Gothmog and Theo to sneak through a fairy ring in Professor Neridove's office to summon the King of the Wood. The ritual goes badly wrong.*

---

The King of the Wood

M’ylaena walked ahead of Theo and Gothmog, illuminating the twisting corridor with a small ball of hovering light. Her dark velvet cloak brushed against the floor as she walked, making a soft whooshing sound. Gothmog tried his best to keep up without making too much noise in his chainmail, but he could tell from her occasional disapproving looks over her shoulder that he wasn’t as stealthy as she would have liked. Still, Theo’s note had said that whatever they were doing might be dangerous. 

“Thanks for coming,” whispered Theo. “We’ve done this with Neridove a few times, and it was perfectly safe. But I still feel better having you with us.” 

Gothmog puffed out his chest a little. “You don’t have to thank me, Theo. I’m happy to offer you my protection. Besides, I owe you for all of your tutoring. You would be so happy with my last healing spell – my left shoulder was completely out of joint this morning.” He flexed his left arm happily and missed Theo’s troubled expression. 

“Gothmog, you don’t owe me anything. I’m helping you because I like you. I’m just sorry I couldn’t give you more details – M’ylaena made me promise not to. She can be a little paranoid…” M’ylaena whipped around and gave them both a severe look, her dark brows furrowing. 

“Are you trying to get us caught? Be quiet. You can flirt when we get back.” Theo felt his face grow hot and shot a furtive glance towards Gothmog who seemed suddenly fascinated with the nearest tapestry. They continued down the hall, the metallic scraping of Gothmog’s armor the only sound until they stopped outside Professor Neridove’s office. M’ylaena said a few words in a harsh tongue that Gothmog didn’t recognize and the door swung open. She ushered them inside and quickly closed the door behind them. The sorceress’s office didn’t appear out of the ordinary. There was a desk covered in scrolls, tea cups, and a half-eaten honeycake. Across from the desk, were wall-to-wall bookshelves overflowing with a multitude of colorful magical tomes and a few armchairs with plump, inviting cushions. Gothmog couldn’t think of a reason why they would ask him to come along to break into a professor’s office. Surely they don’t expect me to fight Neridove. His insides churned at the thought of his mother’s reaction if he were expelled after all she had sacrified to bring him here. Am I losing focus? He brushed the thought away. He couldn’t say no to Theo. He owed him a debt, after all. 

Theo walked up to one of the bookshelves, squinted at it through his large silver spectacles, and waved his hands at it as if shooing it away in annoyance. To Gothmog’s surprise, the bookshelf disappeared in a cloud of purple smoke, and a small side chamber appeared. 

	The four-foot by two-foot chamber was empty except for a patch of soft green grass with cherry-red, spotted mushrooms sprouting in a circle. A gentle glow emanated from the mushrooms and Gothmog swore he felt a warm breeze across his face. 

	“More illusions?” he asked, looking at Theo. Theo shook his head. 

	“It’s real. It’s a fairy circle – a portal to the realm of fae. This one leads to a place deep within the Fauconwood. We’ve been there a few times with Neridove’s supervision.” 

	“Then why sneak in?” asked Gothmog. In answer, M’ylaena pulled a worn leather journal from one of the many pockets in her voluminous skirts. The light emanating from the fairy circle reflected in her violet eyes and gave her an otherworldly appearance. 

	“Theo trusts you so I’m going to trust you. And if you tell anyone about this, I will turn you into an insect and squash you before you have the chance to think about drawing that big sword of yours. Understand?” 

	“Believe me, I don’t want anyone finding out about this anymore than you do, especially my mother. I swear to Grond I won’t tell anyone. Now, willl you please tell me what we’re doing? I’m guessing that book isn’t just a hundred different ways to cook mushrooms?”

“This book,” said M’ylaena, caressing its cover, “gives instructions on summoning the King of the Wood, the fae lord who reigns over Fauconwood. That is our purpose. The King of the Wood is an immortal being of incredible power. Dealing with the fae is obviously risky, but surely I don’t need to tell you what our prize would be for successfully summoning him.” Gothmog shrugged his shoulders cluelessly. 

“It’s an Aqualondian legend,” filled in Theo. “If you summon him, he grants you three wishes.” 

M’ylaena shook her head. “Not wishes. He gives the gift of knowledge. He will answer any three questions you ask, and he must answer them truthfully.” 

	“Questions, wishes, boons, what have you. Listen, Aqualonde is on the edge of the Fauconwood. Everyone I know has tried to summon the King of the Wood to get their wishes. My brother and I tried a dozen times. One time, a friend convinced us we could do it by covering ourselves in vines and running around the forest at midnight. Well, the only thing we summoned was an oozing rash all over my …” 

	“This will work,” M’ylaena said confidently, waving the book at him. “This is one of hers.” 

	“M’ylaena was raised by a witch,” Theo explained. “When she was a baby, a witch stole her from whoever her real parents are and raised her to be a slave.” 

	“She only taught me what would be useful to her. She hid her most valuable spellbooks and hoarded her knowledge for herself. I escaped when I was thirteen, and this is one of the books I stole when I left.” 

Gothmog gave her a sympathetic pat on the shoulder. “I’m sorry M’ylaena. I will assist you in slaying this witch after I have finished with the Vocirans.” Gothmog couldn’t decipher her expression and worried he had offended her. “If you would like my help. I would save the killing blow for you, of course.” 

“I’ll think about it, Gothmog, thanks,” she said, smiling. “If we are successful tonight, we will each be able to ask a question. Do you need time to think about what you want to ask?” 

 “What is the quickest path to destroying the Vocirans and reclaiming my homeland,” he said without hesitation, looking at the other two with his brows raised. 

	“I’ll ask who my real parents are,” said M’ylaena. They both looked at Theo, who was picking at the embroidery thread on his sleeve. 

	“Me? Well, my favorite tunic has been missing for a few months so I might ask the King of the Wood where that ran off to.” 

	M’ylaena and Gothmog scowled at him. 

	“Okay, it’s just that I don’t have any heroic missions to go on or great mysteries from my past that I need answers to. I come from a loving, wealthy family in a prosperous nation. Aqualonda is very beautiful and peaceful – we hardly have any problems. My parents are artists. They sent me to school to study magic, not for any particular reason, just because it interests me. I don’t know what I would ask … but this is probably going to be an uneventful stroll through the woods in any case.” The silence stretched on as they all stared at the circle of mushrooms. Then M’ylaena took a deep breath and jumped into the circle, where she promptly winked out of existence.” Theo and Gothmog looked at each other. 

	“I don’t know how you and M’ylaena can stand being here with all of us spoiled rich children who don’t know anything about the real world. Maybe I shouldn’t have asked you to come,” said Theo awkwardly. 

Gothmog took Theo’s hand in his larger, calloused one. “I’m helping you because I like you.” Theo blushed and gestured at the mushrooms with his free hand. 

“Shall we?”

Part 2

Gothmog had been in many forests, often alone and in the dark. Nothing seemed special about this one, except perhaps the uncanny feeling that they were unwelcome here. Theo squeezed his hand a little tighter. If M’ylaena was uneasy, she didn’t show it. She cast her little ball of light up in the air, picked up a stick, and began scratching runes into the ground. When she was finished, she drew a circle around her work and consulted her book. Then she drew a small dagger from one of her pockets. 

“Woah, Ylaena. What are you doing with that?” asked Theo in alarm. 

“I just need a few drops of blood to seal the circle with,” she said, looking at her outstretched arm as if considering the best place to make a cut. 

“Well, why don’t you let me do that?” Theo offered, though he looked slightly sick. 

“Why would you do it, Theo? It was my idea in the first place, besides, you’re the biggest baby I’ve ever met. Don’t you remember when …” While they bickered, Gothmog unsheathed his sword and quickly dragged the pad of this thumb across its edge. He proffered his bleeding hand to M’ylaena, who took it quickly and squeezed a few drops of blood around the circle.

Then M’ylaena read from her journal in a sing-songy language and lightning danced along the edge of the circle. They all gathered closer and peered into it. Amidst ashes and fragments of broken egg shell sat a hare with solid black eyes. 

“Seek ye the Woodland King?” croaked the hare in a deep voice. “Seek ye His Majesty?” 

“So made we this pneuma ring,” answered M’ylaena. “To bind you for our answers three.” The hare’s long ears twitched toward her and it sniffed the air. 

“Thou shalt meet the king, witch’s daughter,” it said. It opened its paws to reveal three golden eggs. “Three eggs for three questions.” Its eyes snapped to Gothmog, and it held up an egg for him to take. “Dost thou want to know the path to victory?” 

Theo threw an arm across his chest before he could reach out to take it. “Don’t cross the circle,” M’ylaena barked. “What are the full names of my real parents? You have been summoned and bound. You must answer.” The hare bared its teeth at her. 

“Must I? Take an egg if you want your answer.” It turned its head to Theo. 

“Though he does not feel it yet, thy brother has not long. He sickens and shall die. Wouldst thou know the cure?” Theo paled but stood statue-still. The hare turned back to Gothmog. 

“Thy mother feeds you lies,” it hissed. “Wouldst thou know the truth?” Gothmog made a movement toward the circle, but Theo held on to him. “Don’t listen to it. Don’t break the circle.” The hare laughed. 

“Tell me their names. You must answer me!” cried M’ylaena. 

“Wouldst thou like to see thy mother? She has not forgotten you. It eats at her still.” Tears welled in M’ylaena’s eyes. 

“I ask you thrice, and you shall answer. Tell me her name.” 

“Sad child. Take it and you will know her name. She suffers so – she has lost everything trying to find you. I speak naught but the truth. Take it and know her name.” M’ylaena reached a shaky hand toward the circle. Ignoring Theo’s panicked cries, she snatched the egg, and it turned to smoke in her hand. The hare leapt at her, clawed onto her neck, and whispered furiously into her ear. M’ylaena screamed and ripped uselessly at it. 

Gothmog pulled his dagger from his belt and grabbed the hare by its neck. Before he could stab it, it vanished. M’ylaena sank to her hands and knees, sobbing. Theo screamed and pointed toward their summoning circle. A new figure was climbing from the ground – the torso of a powerfully built human man with the body of a deer. The man wore an antlered helm concealing his face and swinging from one of his hands was a vicious three-headed flail. Gothmog pushed Theo to the side, drew his sword, and took a defensive stance. 

“Here!” he growled. “To me!” The creature laughed hollowly and stalked toward him, swinging the fail in circles above his head. 

“The warrior wants to die first. Please, allow me to grant your wish.” The flail whipped toward his face. Gothmog caught it on his sword, but the force of the swing wrenched his blade from his hands, and he watched it go flying into the forest. He dodged behind a tree to avoid a second strike. The three spiked heads of the flail smashed into the tree trunk in an explosion of bark, and the tree came crashing down. Gothmog dove to the ground and rolled out of the way. He heard Theo shout a spell and the three of them went invisible. The creature let the flail fly right above where Gothmog lay on the ground. He held his breath. 

“Why are you hiding? Come out and play, children. I am the King of the Wood. I’ll answer all your silly questions and give you sweets and grant your wishes.” The flail obliterated another tree. Gothmog crouched on the ground and tried to think of what to do. Then he felt Theo’s shaking hand find his own. He reached out and felt M’ylaena on Theo’s other side. Silently, Gothmog followed Theo’s lead and saw that they were headed toward the circle of mushrooms. 

	Gothmog didn’t know if it was the sound of his armor or some eldritch sense that gave them away, but the King of the Wood turned and looked straight at them. A ball of green flames sprang to life and roared toward them. M’ylaena spat out a spell and a solid wall of ice appeared from nowhere. The fireball smashed into it and sent giant chunks of ice flying. A piece smashed into Theo and knocked him off his feet. Their invisibility was broken. The King of the Wood charged at them. M’ylaena shot a spell at him, but he brushed it aside. A bolt of green lightning shot from his fingertips and hit her full in the chest. She dropped to the ground. 

“Failing to protect the ones you love. Like father, like son.” The flail came smashing down at Gothmog. If he moved, it would crush Theo. He closed his eyes and braced for the impact that would surely break him into pieces … it never came. 

He opened his eyes to see Professor Neridove standing by the mushrooms. Her long curly hair was tied back into shining braids, and she had one hand on her hip as she surveyed the scene. The creature that called himself the King of the Wood was frozen in some sort of bubble. She gestured toward Theo and M’ylaena, and they floated toward her. Gothmog limped after them. 

With her ample curves and bright eyes, Gothmog might have described Professor Neridove as beautiful if she weren’t always looking at him with deep disappointment etched in her face. Without a word, he followed her back through the mushroom ring. 

Back in her office, she revived Theo and M’ylaena. They all watched her mutely as she conjured a tea service and poured them cups of steaming tea. Gothmog took the cup that came floating toward him. It smelled of lemon and raspberries, but he couldn’t drink – his throat felt tight and there was a stone in his stomach. 

“It was all my idea,” said M’ylaena tearfully. “Please just punish me. Theo and Gothmog were only trying to stop me.” Neridove conjured a plate of apricot tarts and took a dainty bite before responding. 

“I hope you realize how incredibly lucky you three are that the wards on my office alerted me. You should all be expelled.” 

“We should be expelled?” asked Theo. “Meaning we aren’t expelled?” 

“Drink your tea,” said Neridove. Gothmog took a small sip and felt the warmth of it seep into his sore muscles and ease some of the ache. 

“Are you familiar with the term, mutually assured destruction?” asked Neridove.

“I won’t tell anyone about what happened here, and you three won’t tell anyone that you were nearly killed because you went through the fairy ring I keep hidden in my office. Am I making myself clear?” They nodded. Neridove sipped her tea thoughtfully. 

“Additionally, all three of you are now my student assistants. Congratulations.”
