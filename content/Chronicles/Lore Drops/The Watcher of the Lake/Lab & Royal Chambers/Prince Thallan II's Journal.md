# Prince Thallan II's Journal

*Found underneath the pillow in [[Thallan Lael'Nathor II|Thallan II]]'s chambers. The journal is leather-bound and well-worn. The entries vary wildly in length and tone — some philosophical, some furious, some barely a sentence. A ruler writing to no one.*

---

History will no doubt term my life a tragedy — a mother lost so young, a queen so capable taken too soon by the whims of an indifferent universe. When even the great Prince should lose, what hope have the rest of us? It is compelling drama, is it not? First father, now mother, and all the great houses too busy propping themselves up to come to aid or plant their own standard on the mound of fresh corpses. So falls the See; so falls civilization!

They do not see my life for what it truly is — a comedy. For my whole life my mother has wrestled with one great foe, O Death! Our advisors, courtiers, sycophants have preached one great sermon, the triumphs of the Trueborn! *Fret not, young Thallan, bearer of your father's name, your family's burden, your people's hopes — you are the star lighting our bright future!* Irony is the root of humor, isn't it, and isn't it ironic that the structural refrains of my life's song be so unceremoniously broken?

I should be solidifying my house's political future, vying for marriage and allies and a crushing wave of power westward. I am Thallan the Second, the sole survivor of Lael'Nathor and Holy Sovereign of Navarre!

I am but an elf, an Emperor by hollow title only, and how can I aspire to save my people if I cannot even save my mother? She rests a healing sleep — I hear her breath even as I write this, scarcely audible above the scratching of my quill. I am a boy sleeping in his parents' room, scared of the dreams that chased me here.

Irony. Laughter. Comedy. The curtain's fall is all I see.

---

[[Thalaniel Shamashur|Thalaniel Shamashur]] wishes to move his labs into the audience chamber, and I'm of half a mind to let him. He can tap more directly into the Watcher's pneuma there and it's not as if we've had an abundance of courtiers. Yin'Balar's policies and the War Council's demands have not left us with a great plenty of young Trueborn over the generations, hm? One has to imagine the pains of the man if he could see the fifth century of a ten year war, at least if one wants to find some reason to smile instead of weep.

Shamashur leads a project from my mother's waking days, experiments on 'that last frontier that eludes us'. I asked the biomancer if he meant 'the western one', but nobody in the room found that funny.

---

I canceled the Council again today. 'The dwarves cower', they're 'on the verge of rout', their coffers 'flounder with too much gold and not enough steel'. It was a nice day. The [[Watcher of the Lake]] sits up and over rolling fields, greenery and woods for miles and miles. It's disorienting traveling within her halls, of course, unless you keep to the old trails from Asj'Norriah's day, but out here on the throne, it's quite nice to sit, watch Halinor, and pretend. On a clear day I can almost hear my mother's voice in a whisper on the wind.

---

I caught one of the Damned attempting to clean my mother's room. She has changed residences temporarily — Shamashur's work is showing some progress, after all, so it is best to be near where her physiker works. All the same, since she will be recovering soon, I did not want her return to be too jarring. I had the maid reassigned to the forges.

I was in good spirits in the afternoon so I held court in my mother's name. Attendance was sparse and short. He says he requires practice with the theories he and [[Allythoia]] have been working on, and since our people have given us a volunteer, I see no reason to deny him the space to practice. [[Allythoia]] suggests I spend more time at the throne — the air will do me good, she says.

But this cannot be the only cause. Here over these past weeks I have noticed the whispers and they are no imaginings. Over several nights I have been in contact with a creature holding no form nor name nor pneuma. Have you considered how curiously odd it is for something to possess no pneuma, when it permeates literally all of Creation? As it was called by my mother, preserved by her Will, fed by her allowance, my thoughts immediately went to dark places — alas but I had not found the source of her illness but rather a solution she'd been hopeful for.

Needless to say I did not grant its request for release. Too many unknowns, too much potential for ruination. It fed on pneuma — I did grant it a morsel — but my own people have the answer to the situation we've found ourselves in.

---

'Soon'. What is the point of having words if they do not communicate anything! 'Soon, my prince', 'we will be there soon', 'so hard at work, my prince, we toil day and night!'. Shamashur *plays* on his stage, and there are days he will not give me report at all. He sends [[Allythoia]] like a diplomat, and both know I will not cross the ballroom. How can I with so many simulacra peppering the floors! They lie in chambers of glass; when spent, they populate crates and vats. I cannot direct this despair at them — they are my mother, after all, or soon will be, one of the many.

The tower is lonelier of late. I have taken to calling the star-creature Estelle. She is always hungry, and any pittance instantly remands any insult she takes. When one comes from as dismal a place as apparently is the void between stars, a little hospitality goes a long way.

And it never hurts to have a horror from beyond the stars to feed one's biomancer too, should he fail.

---

Let his praises be sung and his story lifted ever to Taras'Nula — he has won, and she is **BACK**!

---

Something is terribly wrong with my mother. Shamashur has done the impossible and pulled her soul back from Death itself. She moves, she breathes, she speaks. She *lives*! So why then does she do so, all of the above, so listlessly? Her eyes lack all spark of the woman I knew, her tongue all the wit of our greatest queen.

The closest I saw to Life, to laughter, to her old self — the closest I have witnessed in this week was her choking one of the staff. He made some offense — I cannot quite recall — and her hand was about his throat in an instant. Watching his face go blue and his limbs limp while my mother kept squeezing, kept so very quiet.

The other servants have left this wing. My mother refuses to talk unless she is muttering something awful, content instead to stare dead-eyed at the distance. Shamashur, my supposedly brilliant wizard, has utterly failed and left in shame. My one advisor is not even an elf, and all she tells me besides is the awful state of our nation and our world!

It is up to me, all of it, she says, but what am I supposed to do?
