# Loose Page on Shamashur's Desk

*A single loose page found on [[Thalaniel Shamashur|Shamashur]]'s desk. Unaddressed, unsigned, apparently written for no audience but himself. The handwriting is faster here than in his formal reports — the writing of a man thinking aloud.*

---

Sometimes I wonder if I might not be the only flame of intellect lit within this tower. I've been pouring over the records of old — building a body out of naught but the raw elements of the universe is a field in which I, vaunted and accomplished as I am, do still stand on the shoulders of giants — the records of old biomancers, predecessors and contemporaries both of Vyrne, on the matter of living vessels. I am at the point of plucking my eyes from their sockets!

I spent so much of my youth, some two centuries and change, establishing the theoretical framework inside which even small creatures might be made, and now that I am at the point of true Creation, something so incredibly complex as one of my own blood, I find those same instructive — foundational! — tomes like the scribblings of a toddler! Take for example this excerpt from *Weavings of Life*:

> *The weavings of pneuma have at last settled into a harmonic stability. The captured elves of the Fauconwood have lent a fine basis upon which to stretch and arrange our existing avian capabilities. The birdsong Project Harpy has developed through this experiment have kept my fellow scholars delighted, the thrum of enchantment from their heritage part of even the smallest harpies we have bred. When the queen bends the collective knees of the elven tribes, we will have a bevy of harpy types to offer for each biome. We have geese from the eastern forests that will prove useful to splice for Heldenheim's frigid clime — and we hope to capture their ferocity as well for future development that direction.*

The limitations of small minds! I have kept counsel with [[Allythoia]] — who is wise enough to recognize mine own talents, credit to her, praise as she has showered on mine own deserving head — and even she recognizes that compared to even the Trueborn, I am as a god! I certainly do not need to waste time plucking qualities or elements from an extant species, hoping they might arise in my creations; clever, do not mistake, to mix and match, and certainly one could not blame trailblazers of centuries past BUT

But I am here, wringing from raw pneuma bodies worthy of a Trueborn elf's soul! Vyrne himself had a starting point, but there is a far cry between a tiefling and Subject 77. I can see — as [[Allythoia]] says she can see — the path between the raw materials of the cosmos and True Creation. When Death I have conquered, I shall turn my Art to Life, and mine own wonders shall make the harpies and chimaera of yesteryear pale in comparison!
