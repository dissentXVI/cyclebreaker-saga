# Dr. Shamashur's Personal Diary

*A personal diary recovered from [[Thalaniel Shamashur]]'s laboratory in the [[The Cosmic Pillar|Watcher of the Lake]]. The handwriting is fluid and self-assured throughout — the writing of a man who has never once doubted himself. The final entry is longer than the rest.*

---

**Entry #1**

Ah, mortality — an ill-conceived performance that begins with wails, staggers through misery, and ends with a whimper. Dull, predictable, and utterly uninspired. But I, the director of Life itself, will rewrite this tiresome script!

I have transformed and reshaped the physical forms of others with unmatched brilliance, molding flesh and pneuma together like clay and dye. Yet immortality remains elusive — a stage hidden behind the curtain of seeming impossibility. My work will rip that curtain down, never again to fall on my stage. I do not seek to delay death. No, I seek to shatter it completely, for Death demands a conqueror.

The lowborn sacrifices — ah, my actors in this grand drama — have provided their use. Their cries of protest, their fear, their anguish... exquisite, the preludes to applause at my masterpiece.

---

**Entry #2**

Necromancy, that crude dwarven parody of Life manipulation, is the antithesis of my Art. A corpse is no more Life than a shattered lute is music. The dwarves are a curse on this world, polluting the fields of innovation with gears and crude runework. Where they stumble in the dark, I illuminate the stage with genius.

Yet immortality evades me. Each experiment fails spectacularly. My latest attempt to preserve the pneuma in a body beyond its natural limits resulted in a grotesque collapse. The subject's form — unraveled. But their pain? Their screams? Encouraging! How dull that one must measure progress by inches, but how many inches it took to keep a body sensate while liquified!

The key to immortality is here. Somewhere. I feel it pulsing beneath my fingertips, just out of reach.

---

**Entry #3**

This trudgery of failure had spelled clouds on my mood until today, when I stumbled upon notes from Dr. Vyrne. That sniveling pillock had potential, but timidity was his undoing — imagine arresting progress for the sake of 'ethics'! The selfishness to withhold such genius!

Among his self-righteous half-measures and hand-wringing, however, sits a gem — a brilliant concept buried in the muck of his conscience. He toyed with the idea of transferring pneuma into new vessels and hybridity, though he lacked the spine to truly explore past the Damned. Such wasted potential!

Yet his failure inspires me. Cloning! The creation of a perfect vessel to house pneuma! Not the undead mockeries of the dwarves, but a vibrant, living form. I may have already found the ideal candidate — so like our sad emperor's mother in form, true, but in minor bonus a longtime thorn in my side!

Vyrne was a man of hesitation; I am a man of action.

---

**Entry #4**

The cloning experiments have begun. Subject #77 — a gift from fate herself — proves resilient enough for my alterations. The life pneuma surges through her veins, brighter and purer than I could have hoped.

Her screams as I work are a mournful aria to accompany my craft. The physical form bends beneath my will, yet the pneuma remains stubborn, difficult to extract and reattach without corruption. I will find the way to bind it securely. Every misstep only hones my genius further.

This is no longer an experiment — it is destiny manifest.

---

**Entry #5**

Subject #77 is ready. Her clone grows within the chamber, flawless in every way. Her original consciousness will serve as the prototype for the transfer process. If I succeed, the Empress herself will have the means to transcend mortality.

I write this with a steady hand and a burning resolve. I am not merely a biomancer. I am the harbinger of a new age. The gods themselves tremble at what I am about to achieve; my name shall be writ across Taras'Nula, no brighter star to shine in strange new epochs.

---

**Final Entry**

There is something to be said for keeping company with an equal every now and then. Of course Thallan, whiny boy as he is, has the rank — as much as he likes to deny it, he is Emperor. The boy lacks the Art, the mind, the gravitas to call himself my equal, truly, and with [[Amastacia Lael'Nathor|Amastacia]] dead and those capable fleeing for Taras'Nula, I just have the company of an audience, and adoration is sorry substitute for the stimulation of intelligent company.

[[Allythoia]] is in a sense heavensent in that case. Not literally hailing from Taras'Nula of course — she is no Trueborn after all — but I doubt even the brightest mind there could rival hers. She calls me equal, so that ought not come as surprise. She has toured my labs and workstations and I believe come away impressed. Through the talks we've been having, I understand not only have I been on the verge of breakthrough heretofore undreamt, but I am made of stuff far beyond my peers and predecessors.

Frankly it could come at no better time. The boy drifts every day deeper into his depression despite the promise of my research. He has been digging into the old wards, keeping to his own counsel. So be it. Once I deliver the Queen Revived, I'll be moving on to bigger and better things. Allythoia has friends she promises will explain everything — and if they should prove a bore, there's always Taras'Nula. The first of the Trueborn to ascend in a century at least — one more feather for a handsomely decorated cap, no?
