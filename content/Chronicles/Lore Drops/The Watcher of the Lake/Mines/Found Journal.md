# Found Journal

*A battered journal recovered from the mines beneath the [[The Cosmic Pillar|Watcher of the Lake]]. The handwriting shifts over time — beginning precise, growing uneven, then barely legible. The ink is smudged in places, as though the writer's hands were wet. Or shaking.*

---

**Date: 7th Moon, 15th Sun**

I'll never be used to these tunnels. The bright colors of the surface are lost to unending darkness, save for the cast of orange around the forge and lava pools. The others know I'm new. My skin is too red, toasted by my time in the sun. Theirs is so pale. And the work! It's different muscles. The Elves say the more grueling the labor, the more of this curse is excised. I hope so.

---

**Date: 9th Moon, 21st Sun** *(so they say)*

I cannot understand how they keep track of time down here! There is some way to tell the rising of the sun by the count of the hammers' drop, but I am too exhausted by even midday to keep count. When I set down my pick I can hardly see. The overseers are unacquainted with the concept of mercy. If not for the comfort of the others down here…

---

**Date: 11th Moon**

When I first arrived, I had to stoop to keep my horns from scraping the tunnel ceiling. Today I realized I'd missed the sensation of that friction. They've been blunted and my shoulders droop with weariness. I almost miss the chains from my assignment on the surface. They were heavy, but I had fresh air and sunshine. I had my family still. Wherever they are, I hope they're happy. I hope they never find themselves below.

---

**Date: 12/13th Moon**

Today I caught what I thought was a glimpse of the outside world through a crack in the cavern wall. The sight of the sunlit sky caused an ache in my chest I thought would break me, but alas, it was just a pocket of magma — a cruel mirage carried by the heat. The gases made me light headed, so Svati helped me to shelter and covered for me on the line. I never thought I'd miss the fields, but I know I'll dream of home.

---

**Date:** *(no date recorded)*

Who can tell when one day stretches to the next, one cycle of the moon to another? What's the point? We work until the pick cannot be lifted and practically sleep where we fall. I haven't tasted food in a few meals, and frankly, I'm nearly too beat to help the other Damned. That I think is the biggest shame. I know I'm no longer getting home. That anger fueled me for a few… months? Weeks? But these tiefling are my family now, and I am not so far gone that I cannot eke out a little more energy for them. Too shameful to not help lift your family up, not as one of us Damned.
