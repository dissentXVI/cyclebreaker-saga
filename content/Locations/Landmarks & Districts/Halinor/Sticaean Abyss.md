**Location:** Central [[Halinor]], between the [[Amrakebir Mountains]] to the west, the [[The Fauconwood|Fauconwood]] to the east, the Renault Hills to the south, and [[Laconia]] on its northern edge
**Formed:** 4009 ApN, during the [[The Trembling Foundations|Trembling Foundations]]

## Description

The Sticaean Abyss is a vast, fractured scar across the center of [[Halinor]] — the drained basin of what was once [[Sticaea Lake]], shattered and split by the catastrophic earthquakes of the [[The Trembling Foundations|Trembling Foundations]]. It is not a clean chasm. It is a shattered landscape: a wide central basin with branching rifts radiating outward like cracks in broken stone, the exposed lakebed dropping away into depths that have not yet been fully mapped. The terrain is fractured and unstable, riddled with new caves, sinkholes, and collapsed passages opened by the seismic violence of its formation.

Its scale is difficult to fully comprehend from ground level. From the elevated vantage of [[Laconia]] on its northern rim, on a clear day the far edge is barely visible. It has effectively split central Halinor into east and west, severing trade routes and geographic connections that existed for centuries. The rivers that once fed the lake now plunge into the Abyss or pool at its edges, with no outlet. The climate of the surrounding region has already begun to shift without the moderating influence of the lake's mass.

At its depths, bound by the sacrifice of [[Velethoui Arsenault|Archmage Velethoui]], the shade of [[Krisnarax]] still pulses. Echoes of his presence can be felt across the shattered landscape — pneuma imbalances, unsettled [[breathstone]] veins, and a faint wrongness that experienced mages describe as something between grief and rage pressed into the rock itself.

## History

Sticaea Lake was drained in 4009 ApN when [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Cassius gen Amata]]'s forbidden ritual beneath its shores reawakened the shade of [[Krisnarax]]. The resulting earthquakes and landslides carved a massive rift through the lakebed, draining it within days. [[Velethoui Arsenault|Archmage Velethoui]] arrived as Aqualondian and Tarwa'kin forces converged on the site and gave his life to bind Krisnarax's shade into the depths of the newly formed basin — preventing a catastrophe that would have been far worse than what occurred.

As the lake drained, the [[breathstone]] spire of [[the Cosmic Pillar]] rose from the exposed lakebed for the first time in two thousand years. [[Laconia]], already established on the northern shore, became an immediate hub for the scholars, adventurers, and relic hunters drawn to the site. Scholars from [[Shurevaire]], archaeologists, treasure seekers, and others began operating in the region shortly after.

[[Aspiria's Chosen]] ultimately entered the Tower, confronted [[the Empty]] within its systems, and drove it back. [[Reyn'lael Joran]] took the throne and vanished with the tower. The Abyss remains. The site where the Cosmic Pillar stood is now an empty point on the lakebed — still visited, still watched, its pneuma still unsettled.

## Current State

The Abyss is dangerous and largely unmapped below its upper reaches. The fractured terrain shifts unpredictably, new passages open and collapse without warning, and the ambient pneuma throughout the basin is unstable in ways that affect spellcasting and [[breathstone]] equipment. [[Sylviane Arsenault]] is conducting an active investigation into its depths, searching for traces of her grandfather's fate. [[Yenden Clearlade]] continues to research pneuma imbalance and residual echoes from [[Laconia]].

The shade of [[Krisnarax]] is bound somewhere in the dark below. Whether Velethoui's binding holds — and what it would take to break it — has not been tested.
