## Description

The Cosmic Pillar is a colossal tower constructed entirely from [[breathstone]] — the same crystalline pneuma-channeling material used throughout Navarresean architecture, but here in quantities and configurations that dwarf anything else surviving from the empire. Its spire is visible from an extraordinary distance, catching and refracting light in ways that shift with the time of day and the pneumatic state of its surroundings. Even dormant, it hums.

Its interior is organized across multiple wings and levels, each serving a distinct function within the empire's original design. Among the confirmed spaces: the **Garden of Asj'Norriah**, a breathstone teleportation hub created by Empress [[Eron Asj'Norriah]]; an arboretum housing flora not seen in centuries — some species with no known record outside its walls; a library housing primary source documents from across the Navarresean era; research laboratories in the upper levels; and at the tower's apex, the **imperial throne room** — the seat of Navarresean power, accessible only to those bearing the royal bloodline of the Lael'Nathor dynasty.

The throne itself is not merely ceremonial. It is the tower's control mechanism — the point from which the Cosmic Pillar's vast pneumatic systems can be directed. During the empire's height, this connection between sovereign and structure was the source of the Navarresean claim that their rulers were avatars of Life pneuma, beyond the reach of death.

### The Garden of Asj'Norriah

The Garden of Asj'Norriah is a breathstone teleportation hub located within the Cosmic Pillar, created by Empress [[Eron Asj'Norriah]] during the Arcane Enlightenment. It served as the tower's primary transit system — a method of teleportation that has never been replicated in the two thousand years since the empire's collapse.

The Garden takes its name from its appearance: a large platform made to resemble a flower, each leaf holding a portal to another place tied to the tower. Among the portals are pedestals designed to hold specific types of resplendent [[breathstone]], which power the teleportation magicks. When all pedestals are filled, teleportation to the upper sections of the tower becomes accessible.

## History

The Cosmic Pillar was constructed during the height of the [[Navarresean See]] as the empire's spiritual and pneumatic anchor — the physical expression of the empire's divine mandate. It served simultaneously as the seat of government and the most powerful arcane installation on the continent.

When [[Thallan Lael'Nathor II]] used the tower to bring down the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]] in 2012 ApN, the cataclysm that followed submerged the Cosmic Pillar beneath the waters of [[Sticaea Lake]]. It remained there for over two thousand years — its [[breathstone]] construction apparently intact, its systems dormant, its interior preserved in a state of arrested time.

In 4009 ApN, the events surrounding [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Cassius gen Amata]]'s ritual at [[Sticaea Lake]] triggered the [[The Trembling Foundations|Trembling Foundations]], a series of catastrophic earthquakes and landslides that carved a massive rift across central [[Halinor]] and drained the lake entirely. The drained basin became the [[Sticaean Abyss]]. The tower, revealed for the first time in two millennia, rose from the exposed lakebed — its breathstone spire intact, its doors sealed, and its purpose unknown to the scholars and adventurers who immediately converged on the site.

The town of [[Laconia]], already established nearby, became the primary hub for the resulting wave of expeditions, researchers, and treasure-seekers. None were able to enter the tower, and reports of unexplained disappearances, fractured terrain, and persistent goblin activity discouraged encampment near the base.

[[Aspiria's Chosen]] ultimately entered the tower and reached its apex. Within, they confronted the [[The Empty|Empty]] — the void phenomenon that had been drawn to the tower's pneumatic systems — and drove it back. [[Reyn'lael Joran]], the last surviving heir of the Lael'Nathor bloodline, took the throne. The tower recognized him. He ejected the party, assumed control of the Cosmic Pillar's systems, and vanished with the tower.

Its current location is unknown.

## Significance

The Cosmic Pillar is the single most significant surviving artifact of the [[Navarresean See]] — not merely a ruin but a functional pneumatic installation of empire-scale power, apparently intact after two thousand years of submersion. What it was used for, what it is still capable of, and where [[Reyn'lael Joran]] is are questions without current answers.

Its disappearance has not gone unnoticed. The [[Sticaean Abyss]] remains, and the site where the tower once stood is a focal point for scholars, intelligence operatives, and organizations with an interest in Navarresean history — including the [[Order of Ahura]], whose relic acquisition program has been active in the region.
