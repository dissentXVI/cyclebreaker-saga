**Location:** Unknown — Taras'Nula has not been relocated since the collapse of the [[Navarresean See]]. Its last confirmed position was somewhere within former Navarresean territory in central [[Halinor]], but two thousand years of overgrowth, seismic activity, and deliberate erasure from surviving records have obscured its exact coordinates.
**Affiliation:** The [[Navarresean See]] (historical); currently unaffiliated and largely unknown

## Description

Taras'Nula was the [[Navarresean See]]'s supreme scientific installation — a vast, self-contained research facility built to concentrate the empire's highest arcane and biomantic capability in a single location. Where [[the Cosmic Pillar]] was the empire's throne and spiritual anchor, Taras'Nula was its laboratory: the place where the empire's most ambitious and most dangerous work was conducted, away from the scrutiny even the Blessed might apply.

Its architecture reflected its purpose — monumental in scale, deliberately imposing, built to endure rather than to welcome. What is known of its interior suggests multiple research wings organized by discipline, deep subterranean levels, and chambers designed to contain things that should not be easily reached or released. The facility was constructed to be self-sustaining, its pneumatic systems capable of operating independently of any external supply.

At its heart, bound and dreaming, is [[Nottraxes]].

## History

Construction of Taras'Nula began under Emperor [[Phaendra Yin'Balar]], who envisioned it as the crown of the Navarresean military-scientific complex. It was completed and made operational during his reign, becoming the site of the empire's most significant — and most closely guarded — research programs.

[[Nottraxes]], overcome with guilt over the shade she had unleashed upon the world, surrendered herself to the empire and was brought to Taras'Nula. Bound within its deepest chambers, she was repurposed as a living source of energy and insight — her body tethered, her dreams mined for pneumatic and prophetic yield. She has remained there ever since.

In the empire's final years, Taras'Nula became the site of Dr. [[Thalaniel Shamashur]]'s most consequential and most troubling work. [[Amastacia Lael'Nathor]]'s decaying body was transferred there when the Cosmic Pillar could no longer sustain her, and Shamashur's cloning research continued within its walls. The facility also housed his Life pneuma suspension machinery — equipment designed to keep subjects alive indefinitely by continuously draining and re-injecting Life pneuma, at a cost the subjects bore entirely.

When the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]] struck in 2012 ApN, Taras'Nula was vacated in the chaos. Its technology reverted to a pre-Navarresean baseline, its personnel scattered or lost, and the facility fell silent. It was not destroyed. It was abandoned — sealed, overgrown, and eventually forgotten by a world too busy surviving the cataclysm to go back for it.

It has not been found since.

## Current Status

Taras'Nula is intact and inaccessible. [[Nottraxes]] remains bound within it, her consciousness still threading through the facility's arcane grid — warping pneuma, disrupting time within its walls, and dreaming in the dark. Whether she is aware of how much time has passed is unknown. Whether she would wish to be woken is a more complicated question.

[[Amastacia Lael'Nathor]]'s body is also believed to still be within the facility, in whatever state two thousand years without Shamashur's maintenance has left it.

The facility's location is one of the most consequential unsolved questions in Aspirian scholarship — though few scholars know enough about what it contains to understand why.

## Significance

Taras'Nula represents the empire's willingness to pursue knowledge and power without limit or oversight — the logical endpoint of a civilization that believed its divine mandate excused whatever methods it required. Its research programs produced breakthroughs that have never been replicated and horrors that were never fully accounted for.

It is also, practically speaking, one of the most dangerous locations on the continent. A sealed facility containing a bound ancient dragon whose consciousness actively warps the surrounding pneuma, the body of the empire's last empress, and research notes from one of history's most unethical biomancers — all sitting undisturbed for two thousand years, waiting for someone to find the door.
