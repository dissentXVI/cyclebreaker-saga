## Description

Towering above the forest canopy and piercing the clouds, the Elder Tree is the heart of Aqualonde—both in myth and in form. It is said to be the first tree planted in [[The Fauconwood]], sown by the goddess [[Demeria]] herself at the dawn of time. Its bark is silvered with age, its leaves shimmer with gold at dusk, and its roots span leagues beneath the forest, feeding the breathflow of the land. [[Aqualonde]] was built in harmony around it, with homes grown into its lower boughs and sacred rites held within the hollow spaces between its roots. Though no one has ever reached its upper canopy, druids claim its highest branches cradle stars, and that it listens to those who speak with reverence. The Elder Tree is not worshipped—but it is honored, and its health is watched as closely as the seasons themselves.
## Significance

Axis and omen of [[Aqualonde]], the Elder Tree anchors the city’s lifeways—its roots encircle [[The Rootwell]], its lower boughs host sanctuaries and dwellings, and its seasonal shifts are read as civic auguries. [[The Grovebound Circle|Grovebound]] rites and [[The Earthspeakers Circle|Earthspeaker]] counsel often begin at its base, where breathflow is strongest, and many city wards are tethered to its living bark rather than carved stone. Golden leaf-falls out of season are treated as warnings; sapflow and bloom cycles inform planting, pilgrimage, and even [[The Council of Guilds|the Council]]’s docket. In times of crisis, the Elder Tree serves as both refuge and barometer: if its light dims, Aqualonde knows the forest itself is in peril.
