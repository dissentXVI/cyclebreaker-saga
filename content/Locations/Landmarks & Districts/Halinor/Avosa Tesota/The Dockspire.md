**Affiliation:** [[The Congress of Captains]] (operated by the Bannerhall; maintained by dock guild engineers)

## Description

The Dockspire rises from the bay like a spear of sunlit stone, visible from nearly every deck in Gilded Bay.

Its base is broad and fortified, anchored into the oldest pier foundations where timber gives way to coral-faced masonry. From there the tower climbs in narrowing tiers, wrapped in exterior stairways and balcony rings that jut outward like ship decks stacked vertically. Bronze struts brace the structure against coastal winds, and every surface bears the stains of salt and rain—washed clean, then weathered again, as though the sea itself insists on leaving its signature.

At the tower’s midpoint, a ring of signal platforms encircles the spire, packed with pulley rigs, pennant lines, and lantern mounts. Above that, the spire becomes slimmer, capped by a glass-and-brass beacon chamber that catches the sun in the day and throws a steady guiding light across the water at night. When storms roll in, the beacon shifts to a dimmer, shielded glow, and the tower’s pennants are hauled down in practiced silence.

Inside, the Dockspire is all function: chart tables, message tubes, bell-pulls, and narrow rooms full of logs and coded ledgers. Couriers climb its stairwells until their legs tremble. Signal hands watch the horizon until their eyes ache. The tower never truly sleeps.

## Significance

The Dockspire is Avosa’s nervous system.

From its platforms, ship arrivals are recorded, patrol routes coordinated, and warnings broadcast across Gilded Bay and beyond. Its signal codes are understood by every Avosan captain worth the title—colored pennants by day, lantern patterns by night, bell tones during fog, and emergency drum calls when storms drown everything else.

In calmer seasons, the Dockspire is simply a marvel of coordination. In crisis, it becomes sacred. It is the first to spot foreign sails, the first to relay storm omens, and the first to announce when the sea has turned hostile.

In the current political climate, control of the Dockspire’s information flow is quietly consequential. Whoever knows which ships are docking at [[Serpiosa]], which captains are arriving to vote, and which cargo manifests don’t match their hull marks holds leverage in the election—leverage the Bannerhall guards closely and [[the Red Net]] would dearly love to steal.
