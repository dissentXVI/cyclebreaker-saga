**Location:** Terraced upper district overlooking [[Gilded Bay]], [[Avosa Tesota]]  
**Affiliation:** [[Avosa Tesota]] (merchant houses, retired captains, and diplomatic enclaves)

## Description
The Pearlsteps climb above the harbor in pale tiers, a sweep of terraces and stairways that catch the sea breeze before it reaches the lower streets. From below, the district looks like a cascade of sun-bleached stone and terracotta roofs spilling down toward the bay. From within, it feels like a different Avosa—quieter, cleaner, and curated.

Wide stairways of white coral-stone connect plazas lined with fruit trees and tiled fountains. Balconies curve outward in Moorish arches, draped in flowering vines and shaded by silk canopies. The air is scented with citrus peel, polished wood, and expensive incense that tries—unsuccessfully—to mask the salt.

Here the noise of Crescent Port becomes distant music, the chaos of [[the Cradle]] muffled by elevation and wealth. Sailors still pass through, but they do so in pressed coats rather than torn sashes. Guards at courtyard gates wear bright uniforms and brighter smiles.

At the highest terrace, mansions and embassy villas overlook the water with commanding views. Private piers extend below, accessible by guarded stairwells carved into the cliffside, where sleek yachts and diplomatic cutters dock far from the common crowd.

## Significance
Diplomats from [[Shurevaire]] and [[Tarwa’kem]] stay here. Merchant princes host banquets here. Retired captains build legacies here, trading in influence rather than cannon fire. The Bannerhall keeps several administrative offices nearby, close enough to [[Admiralty Hall]] to matter, far enough from the docks to remain orderly.

Politically, the Pearlsteps are pivotal because they bankroll Avosa’s stability. Their merchant houses favor the Charter of Dignity, trade alliances, and legitimacy—yet many of its oldest captains still romanticize piracy, especially those who grew rich in the “glory years” and now speak of freedom as entitlement.

[[Solana Varros]] moves through the Pearlsteps like she owns it, hosting lavish dinners beneath lantern-lit courtyards and turning nostalgia into recruitment. [[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]] is respected here, but not universally loved—some see their reforms as necessary, others as restrictive, and many simply fear the economic chaos that open piracy could bring.

In the coming election, the Pearlsteps will not decide the vote through volume.

It will decide it through leverage.
