**Location:** Eastern upper piers of [[Gilded Bay]], beneath the terraces of [[The Pearlsteps]] in [[Avosa Tesota]] 
**Affiliation:** [[Corella Marante|Admiral Corella Marante]] and the Admiralty (authorized by [[The Congress of Captains]])

## Description
Set slightly apart from the crowded public docks, the wharf extends into deeper water on broad stone pilings faced with dark jungle hardwood. Heavy iron rings are set into the pier at regular intervals, each etched with tide-marks and ship sigils. The boards are kept immaculate—salt-scrubbed, oil-treated, and replaced the moment they splinter. Even the gulls seem to mind their distance.

A pair of tall signal masts rise at the wharf’s edge, their colored pennants visible across the bay. Winches and cranes line the inner stretch, built for speed rather than spectacle. Here, supply crates are moved with quiet efficiency. Sailcloth is folded tight, not draped. Ropes are coiled in perfect spirals. The discipline is deliberate—an unspoken message that Avosa can be free without being careless.

Above the pier, a low complex of white stone and timber sits beneath tiled roofs: offices, armories, chart rooms, and a sparring court open to sea wind. From certain angles the structure resembles a ship’s stern embedded into the land, as if the wharf itself is an anchored flagship.

At night, lanterns burn with steady, shielded light, and patrols move in measured intervals. There is no music here. No shouting. Only the sea and the creak of rigging.

## Significance
Marante’s Wharf serves as the Admiral’s private harbor and operational nerve center—part headquarters, part training ground, part statement of authority. While [[Admiralty Hall]] is where Avosa argues, Marante’s Wharf is where Avosa acts.

This is where the Admiral’s Banner gathers in times of crisis, where patrol routes are assigned, and where sensitive cargo is inspected beyond the noise of Crescent Port. It is also where recruits are tested—not only for skill, but for temperament. Those who cannot follow orders do not remain long.

In the current election season, the wharf has become a symbol as much as a facility. To Marante’s supporters, it represents the discipline needed to keep Avosa alive in a world threatened by cataclysms. To Solana’s traditionalists, it is proof that Avosa is becoming “too clean,” too controlled, too close to the very institutions it once defied.
