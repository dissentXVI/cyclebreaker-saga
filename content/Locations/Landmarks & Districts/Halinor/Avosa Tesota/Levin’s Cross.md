**Location:** Weatherworn stone promontory on the western edge of [[Gilded Bay]], [[Avosa Tesota]]  
**Affiliation:** [[Avosa Tesota]] (maintained by the Bannerhall; respected by sailors and Rangers)

## Description
Levin’s Cross sits apart from Avosa’s noise, where the city’s bright terraces thin into wind-scoured rock and salt grass. A narrow footpath leads to the promontory, bordered by low cairns and offerings left by passing sailors—fruit rinds, coins, knotted cord, and small carved tokens meant to be carried out to sea.

The memorial itself is simple and unsettling: a **trident-shaped monument** of dark driftwood and sea-worn bone set into coral-stone, bound in salt-bleached rope and hung with predator teeth and shell charms. At its base, a ring of weather-smoothed stones forms a boundary no one steps across without purpose. The sea below is always restless here, waves breaking against jagged rock with a sound like distant drumbeats.

The air tastes sharper at Levin’s Cross, as though the wind remembers lightning.

On certain evenings, when clouds bruise purple over the bay, the water beneath the promontory glows faintly—an eerie shimmer locals swear is not moonlight.

## Significance
Levin’s Cross marks the place where Captain Levin the Blue made his fatal choice.

Long ago, he brought a mythic sea-beast—remembered now as the Lightning Eel—too close to shore, believing he could bargain with it, bind it, or boast of it. Whatever his intent, the result was catastrophe. The creature’s landfall devastated ships and docklines, leaving the bay scarred and Avosa humbled. The tragedy became known as the [[Folly of Levin the Blue]], a lesson etched into Avosan memory: there are forces the sea does not permit mortals to master.

Sailors come here before voyages to ask [[Tyrachon]] for mercy and to swear they will not let pride steer their hand. Rangers and old captains treat the site as a warning marker as much as a memorial. In election season, candidates rarely speak its name lightly—because Avosa still fears being remembered as reckless.
