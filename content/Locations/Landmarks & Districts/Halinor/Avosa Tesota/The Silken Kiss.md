**Location:** [[Crescent Port]], [[Avosa Tesota]] 
**Affiliation:** [[Pegape]]

## Description

The Silken Kiss is simultaneously Avosa's most celebrated brothel and one of its most sincere places of worship — a distinction locals see as no contradiction at all. The building sprawls across three interconnected structures along [[Crescent Port]]'s upper lane, its facade a cascade of painted terracotta, hanging lanterns, and silk banners in deep crimson and gold that catch the sea breeze and never quite stop moving. Inside, each room is themed after a different facet of desire: longing, surrender, triumph, grief, hunger, tenderness, and several that have no single word. The décor shifts accordingly — some rooms are draped in soft candlelight and woven fabrics, others open to sea air through latticed screens, others still enclosed and hushed as confession chambers. Shrine dishes of rose petals sit on every threshold; by morning, the vanished ones are tallied by the priests as prayers heard.

Dreamwine — a lightly narcotic vintage brewed from [[Gragato Jungle]] fruit and aged in salted casks — flows freely and is considered part of the rite rather than mere indulgence. Live performances run throughout the evening: singers, instrument players, and spoken-word artists moving between rooms rather than holding a fixed stage, their pieces chosen to match the emotional register of whoever is present.

## Significance

The priests of the Silken Kiss are ordained clergy of [[Pegape]], trained in emotional as well as physical healing. They serve as courtesans, mediators, and celebrants, understanding desire in all its forms — including longing, grief, and the need to simply be witnessed — as sacred territory. For Pegape's faithful, pleasure is not escape but arrival: the truest expression of a self no longer in chains.

Sailors, merchants, and captains arriving from long crossings routinely make a stop here before unloading cargo. The Kiss functions as a threshold rite: you shed the sea's weight, the violence of the crossing, the loneliness of open water, before re-entering the city's obligations. It is considered poor form in some circles to conduct serious business with someone who has not yet had the chance to visit.

The [[The Congress of Captains|Congress of Captains]] has never formally chartered the Silken Kiss, but neither has it seriously moved against it. It is one of the few institutions in [[Avosa Tesota]] that has never needed political protection — because too many of the politically powerful have already knelt at its shrines.
