**Location:** Upper terraces overlooking [[Gilded Bay]], [[Avosa Tesota]]  
**Affiliation:** [[The Congress of Captains]]

## Description
Admiralty Hall rises above the harbor like the prow of a ship turned permanently toward the sea. Built of sun-bleached stone and dark jungle hardwood, its façade is lined with arched colonnades that open directly to ocean wind. Terracotta tiles flash red beneath the tropical sun, and long pennants bearing captain crests stream from iron poles fixed along the roofline.

The interior chamber is circular, tiered in descending steps toward a central speaking floor shaped like a compass rose. Twenty carved seats ring the chamber, each marked with the sigil of a Fleet Captain, Guild Delegate, or Harbor Representative. The Admiral’s chair stands not elevated above them, but set slightly apart at the eastern arc, facing both the chamber and the open balcony beyond.

Behind the hall, vast doors remain open whenever weather permits, framing the shimmer of Gilded Bay. Salt air moves constantly through the chamber. The sound of distant gulls and creaking rigging seeps into debate, reminding all present that Avosa’s authority is born of tide and sail — not marble and crown.

## Significance
Admiralty Hall is the deliberative heart of [[Avosa Tesota]]. Here the twenty members of [[the Congress of Captains]] cast their votes openly, their voices carrying across stone and wood without secrecy or veil. A simple majority of eleven decides law, treaty, and election. In the event of a ten-to-ten deadlock, the sitting Admiral casts the deciding vote.

It was within these walls that the Charter of Dignity was ratified, reshaping Avosa from pirate confederacy into maritime republic. It is here that [[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]] and Congresswoman [[Solana Varros]] now vie for the future of the city.

Public galleries line the upper ring, allowing citizens to witness debate. Applause is permitted. Jeering is common. On critical days, the hall becomes less chamber and more arena — rhetoric as blade, reputation as shield.
