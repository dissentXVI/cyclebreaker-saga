**Location:** Flooded sea cave outside [[Avosa Tesota]] 
**Affiliation:** [[Tyrachon]]

## Description

The Shrine of the Breaking Wave occupies a flooded cave carved by centuries of tidal force into the rock face of the coastline outside [[Avosa Tesota]]. There are no ornate doors, no worked stone, no chandeliers — Tyrachon is not worshipped in comfort. The approach is a slick path of salt-eaten rock passable only at low tide, and the cave itself smells of brine, wet bone, and old offerings pressed into the crevices of the walls. At its heart sits the basin: a natural depression in the cave floor that fills only when storm tide rolls in, fed through a submerged channel that gurgles and hisses as the sea breathes. The walls around it are hung with the bones of great beasts — leviathan ribs, the jaws of deep predators, fragments of sea serpent hide — left by sailors who survived the uncanny and wished to prove it. Coral grows undisturbed along the cave's lower reaches; no priest of Tyrachon would permit it to be touched.

When the tide rises high enough to fill the basin, the priests read the foam. The patterns that form during offerings of blood and bone are interpreted as the Tempest Lord's will — whether to sail or wait, whether a voyage will meet fury or favor, whether the sea has judged a particular captain worthy. The readings are not gentle, and they are rarely reassuring.

## Significance

The Shrine of the Breaking Wave is Avosa's most austere and most respected holy site. Where [[The Silken Kiss]] offers restoration, the Shrine offers reckoning. Sailors come before dangerous crossings not to ask Tyrachon for mercy — His faithful do not pray for mercy — but to be measured. The offering of blood into the basin is understood as a direct address to the god: _I am here. I am willing. Judge me._ Those who receive a clear foam-sign leave with resolve. Those who receive nothing often do not sail.

The priests who serve here are few, weathered, and uninterested in ceremony for its own sake. They keep no schedule beyond the tide.
