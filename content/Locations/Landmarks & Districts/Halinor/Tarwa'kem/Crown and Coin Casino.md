**Location:** [[The Promenade]], [[Tarwa'kem]]  
**Affiliation:** [[Sharifa Raha]]

## Description
The Crown and Coin Casino occupies one of the most commanding addresses in [[The Promenade]], its exterior a study in restrained opulence — sandstone and polished obsidian beneath strings of hanging lanterns that transform the building's face into something resembling a constellation after dark. The name is etched above the entrance in gilded script alongside two symbols: a crown for the house, a coin for [[Eirune]], the goddess of games and chance who lends the establishment a veneer of divine sanction. The chips used at every table throughout the casino are minted in pinchbeck — a nod to her favor that no one takes entirely seriously and everyone observes regardless.

Inside, the casino unfolds across several levels, each calibrated to a different kind of indulgence. The lower floors are open to anyone who can afford the entry price: gaming tables, card rooms, and performance halls where entertainers from across [[Halinor]] cycle through themed engagements — one room a desert court, another a maritime celebration, another draped entirely in silk and candlelight with no apparent theme at all beyond excess. The illusion work is exceptional throughout, with conjured scenery shifting to suit the evening's programming. It is not the most refined venue in [[Tarwa'kem]], but it is the most alive, and those who know the difference between gilded and gold find themselves attending anyway.

The upper levels are another matter entirely. Access is by invitation or private arrangement, and what takes place there is described in terms that grow more elaborate with each retelling — private card games with stakes that could fund a small expedition, gatherings attended by foreign dignitaries and council members who would deny it publicly, entertainments that do not appear in any advertised programme. [[Sharifa Raha]] presides over all of it from a private suite at the building's apex, and it is said she never leaves an evening without knowing something she did not know when it began.

## Significance
The Crown and Coin is [[Sharifa Raha]]'s instrument as much as her establishment. She built it into the social and political nexus of Tarwa'kem — a place where merchants close deals over cards, where nobles float rumors into rooms carefully selected to carry them, and where the line between entertainment and leverage is maintained just loosely enough to be useful. Her private archive of debts, favors, and scandals is rumored to be kept somewhere in the upper levels, and the casino's guest list over any given month reads like a map of the city's true power structure.

For younger nobles, the Crown and Coin serves as an early education in how Tarwa'kem actually works — less the formal institution of the [[Hall of Order]] or the dynasty of the [[Royal Palace]], and more the informal currency of reputation, access, and obligation that underlies all of it. [[Oseye Zahir]] has begun attending without her family's knowledge, drawn to both the parties and the contacts. She is not the first of her generation to treat the casino as a finishing school, and [[Sharifa Raha]] notices every new face that walks through her doors.
