## Description
The Ahura Antiquarium announces itself before you reach its doors. Its facade of pale sandstone and platinum-veined stone rises above the surrounding estates of the [[Historical Quarter]], its grand columns carved with imagery of ancient gods, forgotten empires, and the silhouettes of explorers mid-discovery. The friezes do not limit themselves to Tarwa'kin history — they reach outward across all of Aspiria, a declaration of ambition as much as scholarship. The building is one of the largest in the quarter, its footprint sprawling back into the mountain face, its deeper galleries carved directly into the rock.

Inside, polished corridors lead through a vast collection of artifacts — ceremonial weaponry, funerary pottery, towering statues of long-dead kings, and relics of civilizations whose names survive only here. Each piece is meticulously cataloged and lit to best advantage. The air is cool and dry, the silence enforced by the weight of the space and the quiet authority of the staff. Docents move through the halls with the unhurried confidence of people who know exactly what surrounds them and what it is worth.

At the heart of the building lies the Grand Ballroom — a chamber of platinum chandeliers, marble floors, and panoramic murals depicting historical battles and divine myths in extraordinary scale. When not in use for private events, it stands open to ticketed visitors, its sheer scale lending every occasion that takes place within it a sense of consequence. The ballroom is considered by much of Tarwa'kem's elite to be the most appropriate venue for occasions too grave for a casino and too lively for a temple.

## Significance
The Ahura Antiquarium is inseparable from [[The Order of Ahura]] — the team of archaeologists, explorers, and field scholars who fill its halls. Known colloquially as the Oa, they scour ruins, deserts, and jungles across [[Aspiria]], returning with treasures that [[Tarwa'kem]] receives as national pride made physical. Their exploits have generated books, plays, and no shortage of public admiration. The scholars of [[Shurevaire]] maintain that the Oa prioritize plunder over knowledge; the Tarwa'kin maintain that the distinction hardly matters when the result is this magnificent.

The Antiquarium is presided over by [[Tali Kouri]], its Director and a seated member of [[The Monetarist Council]]. Under her tenure, the institution transformed from a noble curiosity into a cultural citadel — a museum, a vault, and a political instrument in equal measure. It is widely suspected, and rarely spoken of as a concern, that a number of acquisitions arrived through channels that would not survive scrutiny. Tarwa'kem's appetite for the art and history of other nations has never required the permission of those nations to flourish.

Sultana [[Neema Karo'kem]] has restricted the Order's official activities in Laconia, a move designed to curry favor with [[Shurevaire]] and its arbitration mandate. The Antiquarium has responded quietly — a private assessment branch now operates at arm's length from official Oa channels, and the flow of artifacts has not meaningfully slowed.
