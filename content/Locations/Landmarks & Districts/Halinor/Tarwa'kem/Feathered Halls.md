## Description
The Feathered Halls do not announce themselves as a temple. From the street they read as a market — an enormous, colonnaded hall of sandstone and painted cedar, its soaring interior open to a central courtyard where vendors crowd around an octagonal fountain. Hawk feathers hang from every archway in long trailing arrangements, the sole consistent indicator that something other than commerce is being practiced here. The columns are richly painted in deep teal, burnt ochre, and gold, carved with imagery of roads, coins, winged figures, and open hands. At the courtyard's center, rising above the fountain on a tall plinth, a great statue of [[Haelaxa]] spreads painted wings over the whole of it — masked, open-handed, watching.

The market that fills the hall operates across every hour of the day and well into the night. Stalls sell everything from imported spices and foreign textiles to curios of uncertain provenance and goods that bear no labels and invite no questions. The noise is constant. Devotion here is expressed through the act of transaction — followers of [[Haelaxa]] argue that a fair bargain is a prayer, an audacious swindle a hymn. The priests who move through the space, known as the Mask, are indistinguishable from market staff at a glance: robed in layered cloth and partial face coverings, blessing stalls, arbitrating disputes, and collecting offerings into the fountain basin.

Each night at midnight, a column of white smoke rises from the roof of the Feathered Halls — visible from most of [[Tarwa'kem]], a quiet signal that another day's offerings have been received. No announcement accompanies it. Most Tarwa'kin use it to mark the hour without thinking much about what it means.

## Significance
The Feathered Halls operate on two levels simultaneously, and everyone in [[Tarwa'kem]] knows it to varying degrees of specificity. The surface — the market, the Mask, the devotional commerce — is real and functions exactly as presented. Beneath it operates the Face: the guild infrastructure that runs the Halls as a shadow court, handling illicit goods, contract brokerage, and services that do not appear in any stall's inventory.

The guild is structured after the suits of the mamluk playing card, which is why the lower classes call the place the Jester's Court — a reference to its unseen head, the Joker, whose identity is unknown even to most guild members. The Face operates under a genuine theological framework: [[Haelaxa]]'s favor is understood to require a balance between personal enrichment and the welfare of the lower classes. The guild is well-paid to remain uninvolved in the affairs of [[Tarwa'kem]]'s most powerful merchants — no theft, no extortion, no assassinations against names on a protected list. In exchange, it operates without official interference and directs a portion of its income toward the [[Dustway]] and [[Misrata]].

The guild's theological sincerity is not universally believed, but it is not dismissed either. [[Sharifa Raha]] maintains a careful non-interference understanding with the Halls. [[Ngor Azizi]] has tested the boundaries of that understanding more than once. Among the lower classes, the Feathered Halls are something between a community institution and a myth — a place where the city's rules bend just enough to matter.
