## Description
Siren's Vault is the primary temple to [[Siren]] in [[Tarwa'kem]], and it carries the atmosphere of a place that understands itself to be at the intersection of two permanent things. The entrance is unassuming — a wide stone archway set into a quieter street, the god's image carved above the door in his Tarwa'kin aspect: a bearded dwarven figure in heavy robes, his skull visible beneath the hood, one hand resting on a sealed chest. Inside, the air is cool and still regardless of the season outside, carrying the faint smell of incense and old stone.

The hall opens into a vaulted chamber dominated by the great statue of [[Siren]] — depicting him as the Tarwa'kin have always rendered him, a dwarf of evident age and evident authority, his face half in shadow beneath the heavy cowl, his expression neither welcoming nor forbidding but simply present. The statue's scale is deliberate: large enough that visitors must look up, but not so large as to overwhelm. [[Siren]] is not a god of spectacle. At the statue's base sits Siren's Wheel — a large circular mechanism of engraved bronze, its rim marked with symbols denoting stages of life, death, inheritance, and memory. Worshippers leave coin here, placed at the point on the Wheel that corresponds to their petition: remembrance of the dead, protection of legacy, preparation for one's own passing.

The deeper chambers of the Vault function as a necromantic archive — records of lineage, death rites, and ancestral contracts maintained by the temple's priests. Necromancy in [[Tarwa'kem]] has diminished significantly since the [[War of Enchanted Anvils]], but Siren's Vault preserves its sacred practices in traditional form. The priests who serve here are called Watchers. They conduct funerary rites, hold ancestral consultations, and maintain the records of noble house succession that some families trust here rather than to the [[Hall of Order]].

[[Siren]]'s image appears on one face of [[Tarwa'kem]]'s currency, with [[Haelaxa]]'s on the other — the two gods who together are said to govern everything a person does with what they have while alive.

## Significance
Siren's Vault occupies a specific cultural position in [[Tarwa'kem]]: it is the place where the city's relationship with mortality is given formal shape. Death is not hidden here, nor is it mourned in the way other traditions mourn. The Vault's theology holds that the flesh returns to the earth and the soul passes to the [[Sea of Pneuma]], but what a person built — wealth, name, legacy — passes downward through the living. The coin left at Siren's Wheel is not tribute. It is acknowledgment of the covenant: that what you earned will outlast you, and that [[Siren]] is its custodian.

[[Omar Zahir]] has been a quiet patron of the Vault for decades, visiting under various pretenses and leaving coin anonymously. He brought his granddaughter [[Laena Zahir|Laena]] here once when she was young — not to pray, but to speak to her about the two inevitabilities, as he put it: death, and inheritance. It is the kind of lesson the Vault seems designed to teach.

[[Omar Zahir|Omar]] is not alone in his quiet devotion. Several of [[Providence]]'s older families maintain discreet relationships with the Vault's Watchers that they do not advertise to the [[Hall of Order]]'s Assessors of [[Tytos]]. The two temples represent different answers to the same question of what outlasts a life, and not everyone arrives at the same answer.
