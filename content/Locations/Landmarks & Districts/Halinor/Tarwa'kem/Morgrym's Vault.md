**Location:** [[Historical Quarter]], [[Tarwa'kem]]  
**Affiliation:** [[Amasis Ramses]]

## Description
Morgrym's Vault sits below street level in the [[Historical Quarter]], reached by a short descent of worn stone steps and a door of iron-banded cedar that has darkened with age to something close to black. The tavern takes its name from the dwarf who originally carved the space out of the rock face centuries ago — a brewer and sometime moneylender whose actual history has long since been smoothed into myth. What remains is the room he left behind: low-arched ceilings of exposed stone, columns carved in the old dwarven style, and walls decorated with hieroglyphic borders that predate the building above it. Teal-glass lanterns hang at intervals from the ceiling on brass chains, casting the interior in the kind of light that makes everyone look like they are keeping a secret.

The bar runs the length of one wall, its counter a single slab of dark-veined stone behind which an impressive array of bottles is kept — imported spirits, rare infusions, and several unlabeled options that regulars know to order by description rather than name. Tables are round, low, and spaced with deliberate generosity. Sound does not carry well here. The floor is laid in old stone tile, uneven in places but swept clean. Toward the back, the space widens into a second room lit by candlelight alone, where the tables are fewer and the conversations quieter.

[[Amasis Ramses]] acquired the Vault some years ago through means that satisfied all legal requirements and left no obvious trace of his involvement.

## Significance
Morgrym's Vault functions as a tavern in the way that [[Amasis Ramses]] functions as a merchant — completely, and also as cover for something else. The Historical Quarter location is deliberate: old enough to be unremarkable, far enough from [[Providence]] to attract a different kind of clientele, close enough to the [[Sekhet Markets]] to justify anyone's presence. Couriers, minor guild representatives, foreign traders, and low-profile political contacts all pass through without drawing attention to themselves or to each other.

The back room is where Ramses' network does its quieter work — not dramatically, not with obvious ceremony, but in the way that information and obligation change hands over a drink between people who will each describe the evening differently if asked. Ramses keeps no visible ledger here. He does not need to. The Vault is one of several instruments through which he maintains knowledge of [[Tarwa'kem]]'s moving parts, and its greatest asset is that it looks like nothing worth watching.
