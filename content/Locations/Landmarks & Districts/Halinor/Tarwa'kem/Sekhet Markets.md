## Description
The Sekhet Markets are the commercial heartbeat of [[Tarwa'kem]], sprawling across the lower-mid city in a dense network of covered bazaars, open plazas, and narrow trading lanes that seem to expand and contract depending on the hour. Named for the ancient Tarwa'kin word for abundance, the district runs from early morning to well past midnight without meaningful pause — the character of what is sold simply shifts as the day progresses. At dawn, farmers from the [[Shai Fields]] and suppliers from the [[Ash Ward]] move raw goods through the wider lanes. By midday the district is at full roar, every surface occupied by merchants hawking textiles, spices, metalwork, imported curiosities, food, and livestock. By evening the food stalls give way to entertainment, the lanes narrow with crowds, and the more discreet transactions of the night begin.

The architecture of the Sekhet Markets is layered and ungoverned, built over centuries without central planning. Permanent stone buildings lean against wooden additions lean against canvas awnings strung between them. Carved sandstone columns mark the entrances to older covered halls where guild merchants maintain fixed stalls. Solar and ancestor iconography appears throughout — painted above doorways, inlaid into the older pavement — though much of it has been partially obscured by generations of signage and commerce. The air carries the mingled smell of spice, charcoal, and heated stone.

Lost in the markets' labyrinth is the [[Feathered Halls]], the temple-market of [[Haelaxa]] that functions as the spiritual and practical center of the Sekhet Markets. Its midnight smoke is the one moment each night when the entire district briefly orients itself toward something other than profit — and then returns to business.

## Significance
The Sekhet Markets are where Tarwa'kem negotiates with itself. Nobles send agents here rather than coming in person; the [[Dustway]] and [[Ipet District]] come in person and cannot afford agents. The same lane can see a transaction between a [[Providence]] merchant house and a [[Ash Ward]] guild supplier in the morning and a D'yadyan refugee selling salvaged goods by the afternoon. The district has no formal governance of its own — disputes are arbitrated either through the [[Hall of Order]] for serious matters, or through the informal authority of the [[Feathered Halls]] for everything else.

[[The Monetarist Council]]'s influence is felt here through tariffs, guild licensing, and the quiet leverage of its wealthiest members over which suppliers get favorable positions. [[Ngor Azizi]]'s Kustara Consortium maintains several prominent depot stalls along the district's main thoroughfare. [[Amasis Ramses]] has no visible presence in the Sekhet Markets and is accordingly present in nearly all of it.

For visitors to [[Tarwa'kem]], the Sekhet Markets are often the first and most lasting impression of the city — louder, more colorful, and more densely human than the sandstone grandeur of the [[Kemtabi Shelf]] suggests is possible.
