## Description
The Historical Quarter is the oldest surviving section of [[Tarwa'kem]], and it shows. Where the rest of the city has grown upward and outward over centuries of ambition and expansion, the Historical Quarter has remained largely as it was — dense, low, and carved from the mountain's face with the particular logic of a people who measured a good room by its walls, not its ceiling. The buildings here were built by dwarves and built for dwarves: doorways that require taller visitors to mind their heads, ceilings that sit closer to the floor than anywhere else in the city, corridors wide enough for a laden cart but not generous beyond that. The stone is older and darker than the sandstone of the upper districts, worn smooth at corners and thresholds by generations of passing hands and boots.

The streets of the Quarter run in the irregular pattern of a settlement that predates urban planning — narrow lanes opening unexpectedly into small plazas, stairways cut directly into the rock descending to subterranean workshops and storage vaults, buildings sharing walls so completely that the district reads from above as a single continuous structure interrupted by gaps of light. Ancestor reliefs and clan sigils are carved above doorways and into cornerstones throughout, marking lineages that in some cases stretch back to the founding of the city itself. Many have been worn to near-illegibility; the families who know what they say have not forgotten.

The Quarter is also home to two of [[Tarwa'kem]]'s most notable institutions. The [[Ahura Antiquarium]] occupies one of its largest buildings, its grand facade an incongruity against the low rooflines surrounding it. [[Morgrym's Vault]], the tavern carved from the rock by its dwarven namesake centuries ago, remains one of the oldest continuously operating establishments in the city. Both draw visitors from outside the district regularly, which gives the Quarter a foot traffic it might not otherwise see.

## Significance
The Historical Quarter takes its formal name from the period when Tarwa'kem was administratively divided into Quarters — a system of organization from the city's early centuries when the dwarven clans governed their own sections with relative autonomy before unification consolidated power upward. The Quarters are long dissolved as political units, but the name persisted here because no other district wears its age as openly. Locals and outsiders alike still call it Dwarf Town, a nickname that carries no particular malice in most mouths — and which dwarven residents have long since reclaimed as a point of identity rather than diminishment.

The Dwarven Clans who remain in the Quarter represent some of the oldest lineages in [[Tarwa'kem]], families whose names predate the sultanate itself. Their political influence has diminished over generations, displaced by [[the Monetarist Council]]'s merchant class, but their cultural and historical weight is not easily dismissed. [[Moses Avari'kem]], whose [[Sha'khaw Smithing Company]] is headquartered in the [[Ash Ward]], keeps quarters here by choice — a statement about where he considers himself to belong regardless of where his business operates.

For visitors to [[Tarwa'kem]], the Historical Quarter offers the clearest window into what the city was before it became what it is. The grandeur of [[Providence]] and the noise of the [[Sekhet Markets]] are more legible as spectacle. The Quarter is simply old, and in a city that trades heavily on its wealth and ambition, age is its own kind of authority.
