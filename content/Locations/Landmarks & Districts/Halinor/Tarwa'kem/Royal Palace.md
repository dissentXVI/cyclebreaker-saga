## Description
The Royal Palace sits at the highest point of the [[Kemtabi Shelf]], pressed against the face of the [[Amrakebir Mountains]] themselves, its back towers built directly into the rock. From below, in any district of [[Tarwa'kem]], the Palace is visible — a crown of sandstone and gold-capped spires above the Shelf's edge, catching the morning light before anywhere else in the city does. Reaching it requires the ascent of [[Seteek's Lift]], the great dwarven platform that hauls passengers and cargo from the city floor to the Shelf above. There is no other practical way up for those without the time or the knees for the secondary rock stairway carved into the cliff face.

The Palace's public face is one of calibrated grandeur — wide receiving halls floored in polished obsidian and gold-veined stone, columns carved with the solar reliefs and dynastic records of the [[Karo'kem Dynasty]], audience chambers large enough to hold delegations from multiple city-states simultaneously. Everything visible in these spaces is meant to be seen and to communicate the same thing: that this house has ruled here for a very long time, and intends to continue.

Beyond the public spaces, the Palace is a city unto itself. Noble and merchant families engaged in extended business with the crown maintain apartments here — personal suites and household rooms kept at palace standard, used for the duration of whatever dealings require proximity to the Sultana's court. The late Sultan [[Shakir Karo'kem]] was known to extend these arrangements with particular generosity to favored allies; [[Omar Zahir]] has jested more than once that Shakir gave him his own room, so often was he in residence.

The Palace's interior geography is not fully known to anyone who has not lived there for years. Passages connect wings without apparent logic. Alcoves open into antechambers that open into further rooms. Stairways descend to levels not on any official plan. The palace staff navigates by familiarity rather than map, and new residents spend their first weeks simply learning which corridors lead where. It is said there are more secret passages here than in any other structure in the world — a claim impossible to verify and entirely plausible.

Weapons are not permitted inside the Palace walls. The prohibition is enforced at the Shelf gate and again at every public entrance. It is one of the few rules applied equally to everyone regardless of rank.

## Significance
The Royal Palace is the seat of the [[Karo'kem Dynasty]] and the formal center of Tarwa'kem's governmental life, but it functions as something more layered than that in practice. Sultana [[Neema Karo'kem]] governs from here, navigating a court whose architecture of hidden rooms and private apartments means that proximity to power — who has chambers here, who has access to which corridors — is itself a form of politics.

The Palace has always been porous in the way that complex institutions are: too large, too old, and too full of people with competing interests for any single authority to fully control. [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]]'s predecessors understood this and managed it with varying degrees of success. She is learning its geography in more than one sense.
