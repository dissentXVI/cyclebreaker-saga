**Location:** Deep within the [[Amrakabir Mountains]], north of [[Tarwa'kem]]
**Affiliation:** None (abandoned); sacred to Dwarven pilgrims

## Description

Setkha Citadel was built during the era of war between the [[Navarresean Empire|Navarresean Elves]] and the ancient Dwarven clans — conceived as both a military stronghold and a weapons production center from which Dwarven arms and armor could be forged in quantities sufficient to sustain a prolonged defensive campaign. The **Great Forge** at its center was the largest ever constructed in Halinor and roared continuously for centuries. The citadel held through the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]], its walls and defenders enduring one of the most devastating conflicts in Halinor's recorded history.

The citadel is carved directly into the [[Amrakabir Mountains]] rather than built against them. Its interior descends through the rock in interlocked halls and chambers connected by ramps and lifts built for laden workers and heavy equipment. Geometric carvings mark every load-bearing archway; clan sigils are inlaid above workshop thresholds. The Great Forge chamber is imposing even cold and empty — a space of such scale it commands silence. The citadel's stone ledgers, still intact in their recesses, record the name of every weapon produced and the clan that commissioned it.

When the conflict ended and mass production was no longer needed, the Great Forge cooled and the workers dispersed. Setkha has stood uninhabited since, accumulating reverence. Dwarven pilgrims — particularly from [[Tarwa'kem]]'s [[Historical Quarter]] — make the journey with some regularity, placing themselves within the long line of Dwarven history by touching the same stone as the smiths and soldiers who came before them.

## Significance

Setkha Citadel is among the most significant intact Dwarven sites in Halinor. Its historical records alone would be of enormous value to scholars of the Navarresean period — including those researching the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]] and the Dwarven clans' role in it. The Great Forge is structurally functional; if fuel and expertise were brought to it, it could theoretically be relit — a prospect the Dwarven clans have not formally settled. The citadel's depth and remoteness also mean that whatever has accumulated in its lower reaches during centuries of abandonment has not been fully inventoried.
