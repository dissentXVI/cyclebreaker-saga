## Description
Seteek's Lift — called the Teek by nearly everyone who uses it — is the primary means of ascent between the lower city and the [[Kemtabi Shelf]] above. It is a feat of dwarven engineering on a scale that still draws comment from visitors who have seen it a hundred times: a series of broad brass-railed platforms, each large enough to carry passengers, livestock, cargo crates, and the occasional carriage, hauled vertically up the sheer face of the cliff by a system of counterweighted cables, geared mechanisms, and geothermal-assisted winches housed in chambers cut directly into the rock. The platforms rise and descend in alternating rhythm — one ascending as another descends — operating continuously from before dawn until well past nightfall.

The base station sits at the cliff's foot, a wide stone forecourt where queues form by time of day and social station. Merchant cargo moves in the early morning hours; midday belongs to the general flow of visitors, staff, and residents making the crossing; the evening platforms carry nobles, their guests, and the evening's deliveries to [[Providence]] and the [[Royal Palace]]. The upper station opens onto the Shelf's receiving terraces — a broad, guarded plaza where arriving passengers are noted and, depending on their business, escorted or waved through.

The mechanics of the Lift were designed and first installed by the engineer [[Seteek]], a dwarven architect of the city's middle centuries whose name has outlasted nearly everything else about him. The system has been repaired, expanded, and partially rebuilt several times over since, but the core logic of the original design — counterweights, cables, geothermal assist — remains unchanged. The current mechanism is maintained by a guild of specialist engineers who train within the Lift's own infrastructure and rarely work elsewhere.

A secondary stairway exists for those unwilling or unable to wait — steps cut directly into the cliff face, exposed to wind and weather, ascending the full height of the Shelf in a climb that takes the better part of an hour. It is used by the stubborn, the impatient, and those who have reason to avoid being noted at the base station.

## Significance
The Teek is the single point of controlled access between [[Tarwa'kem]]'s lower city and the seat of its power. Everything and everyone that reaches the [[Kemtabi Shelf]] — goods, guests, information, influence — does so through this mechanism or not at all. The base station is accordingly watched, its passenger logs maintained with care, and its operation treated as a matter of civic security rather than mere infrastructure.

For most Tarwa'kin, a ride on the Teek is an occasion. The view from the ascending platform — the city spreading below in its full layered complexity, the [[Shai Fields]] visible beyond the northeastern walls, [[Misrata]] a low irregular outline across the [[Tefuti Stream]] — is one of the clearest ways to understand [[Tarwa'kem]]'s geography all at once. Those who live on the Shelf ride it without looking. Those who don't, tend to.
