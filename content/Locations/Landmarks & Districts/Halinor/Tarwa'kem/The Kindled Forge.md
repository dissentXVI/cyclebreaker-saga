## Description
The Kindled Forge lies beneath the [[Ash Ward]], carved into the rock at the base of the [[Amrakebir Mountains]] where the earth runs hottest. It is not a temple in the way that the [[Hall of Order]] or [[Siren's Vault]] are temples — no marble columns, no grand facade, no processional entry visible from the street. Access is through the Ward itself, down a staircase cut between two forge buildings, marked only by a brass relief of an open flame above the lintel. Those who do not know to look for it do not find it.

The chambers below are powered entirely by geothermal heat drawn from the vent the temple is built upon — the same vent, according to Tarwa'kin religious tradition, where [[Caphos]] pressed his hand into the earth and stilled a volcano rather than let it consume what the dwarven clans had built. The heat here is constant and profound. The walls radiate warmth even in the deepest winter above. Forge chambers ring the central sanctum, each one maintained by working smiths who are also, in this context, worshippers — the act of shaping metal understood as devotion, the ringing of hammers as prayer. The inner sanctum is never silent. A hundred hammers sing [[Caphos]]' name each day in overlapping rhythm, a sound that can be felt in the chest before it is heard.

The sanctum at the Forge's center houses a perpetual flame fed by the geothermal vent directly — unbanked, untended by human hand, burning since the temple's founding. Offerings left here are understood to pass directly to [[Caphos]]: tools worn past use, metal poured into purpose, work done and dedicated. The priests of the Forge, called Kindled, wear no ornamental vestments — their mark of office is their own scarred and calloused hands, evidence of the labor that qualifies them.

## Significance
The Kindled Forge represents a strain of [[Caphos]] worship that is native to [[Tarwa'kem]] and distinct from how the god is honored elsewhere. Where other traditions may honor him as a deity of fire and destruction, the Forge's theology centers on creation through heat — the transformation of raw material into something lasting. The volcano-stilling myth is foundational: [[Caphos]] did not destroy, he redirected. This reading of his nature aligns with dwarven craft tradition, and the Kindled Forge has historically been the spiritual anchor of the [[Ash Ward]]'s guild culture.

[[Moses Avari'kem]] is among the Forge's most consistent patrons, though he visits privately and without ceremony. His presence there is one of the few things he does not treat as a public statement.

The Forge sits in quiet tension with the [[Ash Ward]]'s current political climate. Its priests have said nothing publicly about [[Ngor Azizi]]'s labor practices or the treatment of D'yadyan workers — but the Kindled have a long institutional memory, and the Ward's craftsmen know it.
