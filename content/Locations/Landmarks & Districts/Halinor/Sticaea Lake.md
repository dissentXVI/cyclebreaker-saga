**Location:** Formerly central [[Halinor]], between the [[Amrakebir Mountains]] to the west, the [[Fauconwood]] to the east, and the Renault Hills to the south. The basin it occupied is now the [[Sticaean Abyss]].
**Status:** Destroyed — drained entirely in 4009 ApN during the [[The Trembling Foundations|Trembling Foundations]]

## Description

Sticaea Lake was one of the largest bodies of fresh water in [[Halinor]] — a vast, central lake that served as a geographic anchor for the continent's interior. Fed by snowmelt from the [[Amrakebir Mountains]] and drained by rivers flowing east toward the [[Fauconwood]], it stretched across a basin wide enough to shape the climate, agriculture, and trade routes of everything within a day's travel of its shores.

It was not a place of myth. It was a place of commerce. Fishing fleets worked its waters year-round, river trade moved goods between the settlements on its banks, and the towns that grew along its shoreline — [[Laconia]], [[Crowtown]], and others — built their economies directly on its resources. The lake was simply there, as it had always been: deep, cold, and enormous.

What was not commonly known was what lay submerged in its depths. The [[the Cosmic Pillar|Cosmic Pillar]] — the great [[breathstone]] tower of the [[Navarresean See]] — had rested on the lakebed since 2012 ApN, sunk by the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]] that ended the empire. For two thousand years, the lake sat above it, unknowing fishermen casting nets over the ruins of a civilization. When the lakebed gave way and the tower rose, the people of [[Laconia]] had no knowledge of its true name — and so they called it what it appeared to be: the Watcher of the Lake.

## History

Sticaea Lake was formed in the aftermath of the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]] of 2012 ApN, when the great floods of the Third Cataclysm reshaped the geography of central Halinor and filled the basin left by the earthquake damage of the era. What had previously been lower terrain became one of the continent's defining geographic features over the following centuries.

By the Third Radiant Age, the lake was fully established and the settlements on its banks were thriving. [[Laconia]] grew on its western shore, [[Crowtown]] to the east, with river trade connecting them to [[Tarwa'kem]] to the west and [[the Fauconwood]] region to the east. The lake sustained all of it.

In 4009 ApN, [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Cassius gen Amata]]'s forbidden ritual in the caves beneath its shores triggered the [[The Trembling Foundations|Trembling Foundations]]. The earthquakes and landslides carved a massive rift through the lakebed and drained the lake entirely in a matter of days. The basin that remained became the [[Sticaean Abyss]]. The economy of every settlement on its shores collapsed overnight.

Sticaea Lake no longer exists.
