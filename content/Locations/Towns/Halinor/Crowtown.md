**Location:** Eastern shore of the [[Sticaean Abyss]], [[Halinor]]
**Affiliation:** None (destroyed)

## Origin and Brief History

Crowtown was a lakeside fishing village on the eastern shores of Sticaean Lake, built and sustained across generations by families who worked its waters. It took its name from the crows that colonized the surrounding trees in vast numbers — a constant presence in the skies and in the cultural life of the town. The annual Crow's Festival marked the height of the fishing season and was the heart of village life. The community was known for its distinctive black-sailed fishing boats, passed down through families alongside the knowledge of how to sail them.

The **Trembling Foundations** ended it. The cataclysm's earthquakes altered the lake basin dramatically, causing the waters to surge and flood the settlement. Crowtown was not swallowed by the newly-formed Sticaean Abyss as some places were, but the devastation was enough to scatter its population and leave the town uninhabitable.

## Description

What remains today is a ghostly silhouette along the Abyss's eastern edge. Crumbled stone buildings, collapsed docks, and the weathered hulls of black-sailed boats list in the dried remnants of the lakebed. The crows have never fully left — they still nest in the broken rafters and wheel above the ruins in unsettling numbers.

Travelers on the Abyss road give Crowtown a wide berth after dark. Locals speak of mournful cawing on windless nights, and of shadowy figures glimpsed among the ruins. Whether these are stories born of grief, genuine hauntings, or something stranger that has moved into the abandoned settlement is debated in nearby towns.

## Notable People

**Captain Thalina Blackfeather** *(deceased/rumored)* — Former leader of the Crowtown fishing fleet. Rumor holds that her spirit still guards the ruins, and that a ghostly fleet led by her spectral figure sails the dried lakebed on full moons.
