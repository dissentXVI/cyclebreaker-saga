**Location:** Northern [[Halinor]], base of the Crystal Bridge
**Affiliation:** [[Carinthia]] (Secular Arm)

## Origin and Brief History

Snowfinch Rest grew from a waystation into a permanent settlement out of necessity. Perched at the southern base of the Crystal Bridge — the great glacial crossing that connects [[Halinor]] to Carinthian territory — it became the last stop for travelers heading north and the first warm ground underfoot for those descending from the cold. Over generations the outpost accumulated a garrison, a civilian population, and enough infrastructure to sustain both.

## Description

The town is modest but sturdy: hewn stone and dense timber, sloped roofs built to shed heavy snowfall, lantern-lit streets that stay lit through the night for the sake of travelers as much as residents. Most establishments along the main thoroughfare keep their shutters open late. The atmosphere is watchful but not unwelcoming — Snowfinch has always drawn soldiers, guides, and merchants in equal measure, and the locals have learned to read strangers quickly.

At the edge of town stands the **Frostguard Bastion**, a keep of hewn stone and reinforced ice built for endurance rather than siege. It holds the approach to the Crystal Bridge against incursion from the south and projects Carinthian authority into a region that sits just outside [[Heldenheim]]'s direct sight. [[Bernard Snowtree]] commands from here, managing the town's security and the growing friction between its civilian population and the [[The Inquisition|Inquisition]] patrols that have become a more frequent presence on the frontier.

To the west, the **Frostbough Woodlands** — a dense forest of ancient cold-weather pines — provides timber and natural cover for the town's flanks. Logging parties and hunters venture into it regularly. The deeper reaches have a reputation for claiming the unwary.

## Notable People

[[Bernard Snowtree]] — Commander of Frostguard Bastion. The town's protector and de facto heart; beloved by common folk and soldiers alike.
