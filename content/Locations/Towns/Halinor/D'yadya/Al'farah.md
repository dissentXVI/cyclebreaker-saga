**Location:** [[Khadra Woods]], eastern [[D'yadya]], at the foot of the [[Amrakabir Mountains]]
**Affiliation:** Independent

## Origin and Brief History

Al'farah was founded by nomadic tribes who settled the fertile edge of the Khadra Woods and organized their society around the worship of Pegape, goddess of passion, fertility, beauty, and desire. What began as a permanent camp became a town, and the town built its entire civic identity around Pegape's devotion — not as occasional observance but as the organizing framework of daily life. Al'farah has remained largely self-contained throughout its history, which has insulated it from the wider politics of Halinor.

## Description

Al'farah is vivid: buildings bright with color and intricate tilework, lush garden enclosures on every block, narrow streets opening into market squares fragrant with spice and perfume. A grand central fountain anchors the town center, where musicians are a near-constant presence. The labyrinthine layout rewards slow exploration and resists quick navigation.

The **perfume and fragrance trade** is Al'farah's primary export. The botanicals native to the Khadra Woods produce scents that cannot be replicated elsewhere, and the town's master perfumers are considered among the finest in Halinor. This trade provides Al'farah its one reliable external connection while its culture remains deeply inward-facing.

Despite sitting within D'yadyan territory, Al'farah has not been occupied by the Vociran Empire. Its position deep in the Khadra Woods, hemmed in by dense forest and the slopes of the Amrakabir Mountains, makes it difficult to garrison and strategically low-value. The Empire has shown no interest in stretching its supply lines to control a town whose primary export is perfume.

## Notable People

**Nasira Al'Amal** — High Priestess of Pegape and de facto civic leader. Her word carries both spiritual and political weight.

**Zayn al-Sarraf** — Master perfumer. His fragrances are sought across Halinor; he holds the secrets to countless formulations found nowhere else.
