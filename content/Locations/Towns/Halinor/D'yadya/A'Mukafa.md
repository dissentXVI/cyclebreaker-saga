**Location:** [[Sha'qal Flats]], northern edge of the [[H'abyadie Desert]], [[D'yadya]]
**Affiliation:** [[Vociterra|Vociran Empire]] (occupied)

## Origin and Brief History

A'Mukafa was built on salt. The Sha'qal Flats that surround the town are among the most extensive salt deposits in Halinor, and for nearly a century the community's entire existence organized around extracting, processing, and trading that resource. Salt caravans departed regularly, moving product across D'yadya and beyond. The town was prosperous in the way that resource towns can be — stable, purposeful, and proud of what it produced.

The Vociran invasion ended that. A'Mukafa's salt output was valuable enough that the Empire moved quickly to control it rather than destroy it. The town was occupied without a battle, its production redirected to Imperial military logistics, its profits leaving with the garrison. The workers remain; ownership does not.

## Description

A'Mukafa's architecture still reflects its better era — buildings adorned with geometric tilework in faded but still-vivid colors, towers and minarets rising above the flat landscape, a central marketplace designed for a volume of trade it no longer sees. The Sha'qal Flats stretch away on three sides, vast and white under the desert sun.

The salt operation continues under Imperial oversight. Residents who once took pride in the flats as a source of communal identity now work them under conscription. An undercurrent of resistance — loose, cautious, not yet coordinated — has begun to form under the name the **Salt Rebellion**.

## Notable People

**Nadia Al-Qahtani** — Once A'Mukafa's most prominent salt merchant. Now oversees the limited operations that remain in local hands, quietly hoping for recovery.

**Rashid El-Mansoor** — The town's historian and storyteller. Preserves A'Mukafa's cultural history with particular urgency, aware that memory may be all that survives if the occupation persists.
