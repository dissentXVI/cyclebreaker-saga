**Location:** Southern [[D'yadya]], shadow of the [[H'abyadie Desert]]
**Affiliation:** [[Vociterra|Vociran Empire]] (occupied)

## Origin and Brief History

Buchara began as a small saltwork settlement on the southwestern edge of D'yadya. Its deposits were never extensive, and the local salt industry exhausted itself within a few generations. Rather than abandon the site, the people who remained found new purpose in the land's flora — the unique plants along the H'abyadie's northern edge produced rare pigments found nowhere else in Halinor, and Buchara rebuilt its identity entirely around the dye trade. Its colors became its pride long before the occupation arrived.

The Vociran invasion ended that era. Buchara sat directly in the path of the advancing forces and had no means of resistance. It was occupied swiftly, its dye trade redirected to Imperial supply needs, its profits leaving with the garrison.

## Description

The buildings that were once painted in the vivid colors the town produced have faded. The central square, once a gathering point for festivals and markets, sits quiet. The dye trade still operates — the Empire has use for the product — but under Imperial oversight and for Imperial benefit.

Beneath the surface, small acts of preservation continue. The master horticulturists **Eldra and Kael** quietly maintain the cultivation techniques that produce Buchara's dyes, passing their knowledge to younger residents in private.

## Notable People

**Eldra and Kael** — Elderly couple and master horticulturists. Secretly preserving Buchara's dye-cultivation traditions and cultural knowledge for future generations.

**Captain Sareya** — Local resistance figure. Works carefully within the town's margins, gathering information and maintaining contacts while waiting for conditions that make larger action viable.
