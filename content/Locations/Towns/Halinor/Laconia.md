**Location:** Western rim of the [[Sticaean Abyss]], central [[Halinor]]
**Affiliation:** Independent; nominally recognized by [[Tarwa'kem]]

## Origin and Brief History

Laconia predates the cataclysm as a modest settlement on the western shore of [[Sticaea Lake]], sustained by lake trade and its position along interior Halinor routes. The [[The Trembling Foundations|Trembling Foundations]] destroyed the lake and left Laconia perched on the edge of the newly formed [[Sticaean Abyss]] — a dramatic change in circumstance that could have ended the town but instead transformed it.

When the [[the Cosmic Pillar|Watcher of the Lake]] rose from the exposed lakebed, Laconia became the closest established settlement to one of the most significant sites in Halinor. Scholars from [[Shurevaire]], relic hunters, adventurers, archaeologists, and opportunists converged on the region, and Laconia absorbed them all. It ballooned from a quiet lakeside community into a chaotic frontier hub in a matter of years — too fast for any city-state to govern, and with no formal authority of its own.

[[Zeyn Choukri]] was assigned as Sheriff by [[Tarik Tazi]] of Tarwa'kem — a pragmatic appointment that most city-states treated skeptically. When the goblin horde attacked Laconia, Zeyn stood with [[Aspiria's Chosen]] in the town's defense, though he was ultimately forced to retreat rather than risk becoming [[Fraying|frayed]] like so many others. [[Mireya Lunacrest]]'s ability to resist the warping of her mind proved critical to the town's survival. The attack left Laconia shaken but standing, and in its aftermath Mireya — proprietor of the Sailor's Whimsy and the town's emotional anchor through the crisis — was informally recognized as Mayor by the people themselves.

## Description

Laconia is a town that grew faster than it could plan. Its streets are a layered accumulation of original lakeside buildings, hasty construction from the post-cataclysm influx, and structures put up by the wave of scholars and expedition groups who followed. The result is dense, mismatched, and surprisingly functional.

The **Sailor's Whimsy** — the inn run by Mireya Lunacrest — is the town's de facto social center: the place where information changes hands, alliances form, and arrivals to Laconia inevitably end up on their first night. The **Registrar's Offices**, where [[Yenden Clearlade]] and [[Theomaire Dalinestri]] operate, serve as the administrative hub for expedition permits and archaeological access to the Abyss. The **Gatehouse** houses [[Ylaena Sharie]]'s ongoing fieldwork on behalf of [[Shurevaire]].

The Abyss is visible from most of the town — a vast, fractured darkness at the edge of everything, impossible to ignore and impossible to fully map. It defines Laconia's purpose and its danger in equal measure.

## Notable People

**[[Mireya Lunacrest]]** — Unofficial Mayor of Laconia. Proprietor of the Sailor's Whimsy. The town's emotional center and its most trusted voice; her authority is informal, unelected, and entirely real.

**[[Zeyn Choukri]]** — Sheriff of Laconia. Maintains order through will, reputation, and a trained militia composed of the displaced and the determined. His title is not recognized by every city-state; his results are harder to argue with.

**[[Jendrick Ironbrook]]** — Based at the Sailor's Whimsy. Quietly beloved by the neighborhood.

**[[Yenden Clearlade]]** — Registrar for Archaeological Oversight. Manages expedition logistics and academic access to the Abyss and surrounding ruins.

**[[Theomaire Dalinestri]]** — Resident scholar at the Registrar's Office; liaison between local researchers and broader academic institutions.

**[[Ylaena Sharie]]** — Shurevaire field researcher operating from the Gatehouse; studies magical instability, anthropology, and relics in the region.
