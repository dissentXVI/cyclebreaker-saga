**Location:** Colwater Marsh, [[Frostmarch Forest]], eastern [[Halinor]]
**Affiliation:** Independent

## Origin and Brief History

Quagton was founded by a reclusive group of halflings who found the isolation of the Colwater Marsh appealing rather than forbidding. Seeking a life removed from the wider world's noise, they built their homes on stilts above the waterline and developed their own way of living across generations of near-total insularity. The community remains small, tight-knit, and deeply eccentric by outside standards.

## Description

Every structure in Quagton rises on tall wooden legs above the marsh, connected by a labyrinthine network of walkways that wind, fork, and double back in ways that confound newcomers. The whole settlement shifts slightly when water levels rise, which the Quaggers treat as entirely unremarkable.

Several practices define the community: **silt-collecting** — marsh silt is gathered and used in daily rituals and ornamentation; **frog choirs** — communal evening gatherings where villagers mimic the calls of the marsh's amphibians, believed to maintain harmony with the Colwater; **cryptic speech** — a cultural habit of answering direct questions obliquely, considered a form of politeness; and **floating gardens** — food cultivation on reed-and-debris rafts anchored in the shallower channels. None of these are affectations. To the Quaggers, they are simply how things are done.

Visitors who engage on the village's terms tend to find its residents remarkably knowledgeable about the marsh and forest beyond it. Those who arrive expecting straightforward answers leave frustrated.

## Notable People

**Thistledown Greenleaf** — Village elder and spiritual leader. Known for intricate silt paintings and enigmatic prophecies.

**Bramble Toadstool** — The village's resident gardener and inventor of the floating garden system.
