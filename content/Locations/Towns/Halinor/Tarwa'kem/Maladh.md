**Location:** Near [[Dagger Canyon]], [[Tarwa'kem]] territory, [[Halinor]]
**Affiliation:** Independent (isolationist)

## Origin and Brief History

Maladh was founded centuries ago by settlers seeking refuge from the harsh elements of Dagger Canyon. They established a wool and sheep farming community and developed a deliberate isolationism that has deepened across generations. The village trades with [[Tarwa'kem]] during the annual Wool Festival but otherwise prefers to rely on itself. Its traditions, craftsmanship, and way of life have been carefully maintained precisely because outside contact has been carefully limited.

## Description

The village is built from locally quarried stone and adobe — thick-walled, low, and designed to hold cool air in the midday heat and warmth at night. Buildings cluster tightly, sharing walls where possible. Surrounding the village are the terraced fields and open flats where the sheep herds are kept.

The **Wool Festival** — held at the culmination of the farming season — is the one time Maladh opens to outside traders. Villagers display their finest textiles and celebrate with traditional music and dance; buyers from Tarwa'kem prize the quality of Maladh's craft.

The **Canyon Pilgrimage**, undertaken once per generation by the village's adults, is a spiritual journey into Dagger Canyon to reconnect with ancestral roots. It is not discussed with outsiders.

## Notable People

**Ahman Bat** — Village elder. Deep knowledge of sheep farming and wool production; de facto agricultural authority.

**Aya Antor** — Master weaver and textile artist. Her designs, inspired by desert skies, are highly sought after by outside traders.
