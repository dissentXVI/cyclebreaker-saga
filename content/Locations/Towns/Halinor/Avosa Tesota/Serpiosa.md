**Location:** Western fringe of Avosan territory, [[Gragato Jungle|The Gragato Jungle]]
**Affiliation:** Nominally [[Avosa Tesota]], but semi-autonomous banner town operating in legal gray space

## Origin and Brief History

Serpiosa was never meant to be rebellion, but relief.

In the early years after [[Avosa Tesota]]’s founding, not every captain welcomed the tightening structure of [[the Congress of Captains]]. The Charter of Dignity promised order and legitimacy, but to some it felt like domestication. Rather than drive those captains into open revolt, the Congress allowed settlement along the western mangrove coast — distant enough to limit influence, close enough to maintain oversight.

Serpiosa took root there.

What began as a loose anchorage of stubborn corsairs and independent traders slowly grew into a permanent town built from scavenged hulls and reclaimed timber. It became a haven for those unwilling to abandon piracy entirely, yet not foolish enough to declare war on Avosa itself. The Congress tolerated it as a pressure valve — a place where restless captains could operate without destabilizing the republic.

For years, that uneasy balance held.

Under [[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]], privateering restrictions tightened and slave trafficking was outlawed without exception. Serpiosa adapted, as it always had, slipping further into shadow rather than open defiance. Smuggling routes shifted. Cargo manifests became more creative. Certain [[The Red Net|Red Net]] cells found the town useful as a conduit for trade too delicate for official ledgers.

Now something darker stirs beneath that pragmatism. Reports of missing gnoll tribes have begun to converge near Serpiosa’s jungle routes. Ships leave harbor with no record of cargo. Wealth flows quietly through hands that do not answer to the Congress.

Serpiosa is no longer just a compromise, but a liability
## Description

Serpiosa rises from swamp and sea like a crooked grin against the horizon.

Its docks are uneven, layered upon the bones of older piers that rotted or sank. Buildings lean into one another as though sharing secrets, their walls fashioned from plundered ship timbers and mangrove wood. Sails once meant to catch wind now hang as awnings over balconies and gambling tables. Lanternlight spills across rope bridges and weathered walkways, casting gold and crimson reflections across tidal pools.

The air is thick with salt and spice, rum and wet earth. Music drifts constantly through the streets — drums, fiddles, laughter. It is a town that performs itself, as though defiance were a festival.

At its heart lies the Viper Pit, an arena carved into natural low ground near the mangroves. Once little more than a dueling ring for settling grudges, it has grown into a spectacle venue where fighters clash before roaring crowds. Blood has soaked its sand often enough that even those who cheer speak of it with reverence.

Beneath the celebration, however, Serpiosa hums with quiet machinery. Warehouses sit too close to the waterline. Certain ships dock only at night. Narrow limestone tunnels thread from storage cellars into the jungle’s edge, known to smugglers and [[The Red Net|Red Net]] operatives alike. Official patrols rarely linger long enough to notice.
## Notable People

**Captain Roderigo “Ironhand” Valera**
Commands _The Crimson Serpent_ and maintains a careful public neutrality in the Admiral’s election. He speaks fondly of tradition and old freedoms, and his influence stretches through dockworkers and certain [[The Red Net|Red Net]] cells. Few doubt where his sympathies truly lie.

**Ravenna Blackthorn**
Runs the Black Thorn Tavern, a place where music is loud but conversations are quieter. She trades in rumor as easily as rum, and many suspect she serves as a Weaver within the Red Net. Whether her loyalty lies with Solana Varros, Admiral Marante, or only with Serpiosa itself remains an open question.

**Finn “Two-Tooth” McCready**
Reigns over the Viper Pit as its champion. He claims indifference to politics, yet political banners increasingly appear among his sponsors. In Serpiosa, spectacle and power are rarely separate things.

And beyond the edges of town, gnoll scouts have been seen less frequently. Their absence is noticed most by those who understand what silence in the jungle truly means.
