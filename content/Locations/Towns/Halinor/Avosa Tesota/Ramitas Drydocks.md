**Location:** Northern coast of the Serveaus Sea, just beyond the outer patrol lines of [[Avosa Tesota]]
**Affiliation:** Allied to [[Avosa Tesota]], semi-independent maritime guild settlement

## Origin and Brief History

Ramitas Drydocks began as a practical necessity disguised as opportunism.

In the early decades of Avosa’s rise, pirate captains needed somewhere to mend hulls between raids without answering too many questions. A natural inlet north of the city offered deep water, protective cliffs, and access to jungle timber. What began as a hidden repair cove gradually expanded into permanent slips, workshops, and housing for craftsmen who preferred sawdust to swordplay.

When [[the Congress of Captains]] formalized the Charter of Dignity and curtailed privateering, Ramitas did not collapse, but adapted

The pirates who once refitted raiders became master shipwrights. Smugglers became procurement specialists. Dockyard engineers refined their craft until vessels bearing the mark of Ramitas were considered among the finest in [[Halinor]].

Today, Ramitas Drydocks operates legally under Avosan oversight, paying tithe to the Congress while maintaining its own internal guild authority. Officially, it is a cornerstone of Avosa’s naval strength. Unofficially, it still knows how to modify a hull for speed, conceal compartments beneath ballast, and ask no questions when coin changes hands.

It's legitimacy didn't erase it's pirate-origins, but polished them.
## Description

Ramitas curves along the sea like an open palm.

The shoreline is dominated by vast stepped drydocks carved into stone and reinforced with heavy timber braces. Massive winch systems and counterweight cranes swing above the slips, lifting hulls from the water with mechanical precision. The air smells of salt, tar, resin, and fresh-cut wood.

Color remains everywhere despite the labor: bright shutters, clay-tiled roofs, and woven banners snap in the wind. Workshops spill into narrow streets where sparks from forges mingle with drifting sawdust. Shipwrights argue loudly over hull curvature while apprentices haul planks thicker than their torsos.

At the center of it all is the Pitchworks — a fortified complex where the famed [[Tyrachon's Pitch]] is refined. The resin, drawn from deep within the Gragato Jungle and treated through guarded processes, produces a sealant of remarkable resilience. Ships treated with it ride higher in storm surf, resist rot longer than common tar, and are said to “sing” differently under heavy wind.

Whether that last claim is superstition or subtle pneuma resonance is debated.

The drydocks can accommodate anything from merchant schooners to Avosa’s war galleons. When a new flagship is commissioned, its keel is often laid in Ramitas beneath ritual offerings to [[Tyrachon]], asking the Tempest Lord for favor.

Even in daylight, there is a sense that Ramitas works on two levels — the visible craftsmanship and the quiet modifications made after dusk.
## Notable People

**Captain Isabella “Red Tide” Marquez**  
Once a feared corsair, Isabella now oversees the largest slip in Ramitas. She transitioned from piracy to shipbuilding when the Charter of Dignity rendered her trade untenable. Her temper remains volcanic, her bargaining ruthless, but her loyalty to Avosa’s survival is genuine. She publicly supports Admiral Marante’s naval modernization efforts, though she refuses to speak openly about the election.

**Diego “The Lighthouse” Velasco**  
Master shipwright and navigator, Diego is the mind behind many of Ramitas’ most innovative hull designs. He can read tides, reef shifts, and wind patterns with uncanny accuracy. Sailors swear that ships he designs feel “alive” under storm conditions. He is one of the few craftsmen trusted with the most sensitive applications of [[Tyrachon's Pitch]]

**Guildmaster Arceo Ramitas** 
Descendant of the settlement’s original founder, Arceo presides over the Drydock Guild. Politically cautious, he maintains formal neutrality in the Admiral’s race, though both Solana Varros and Admiral Marante have courted his endorsement. Whoever controls Ramitas controls naval production — and that leverage is understood by everyone.
