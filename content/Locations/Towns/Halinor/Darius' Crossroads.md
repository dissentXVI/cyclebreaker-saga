**Location:** South of the [[Sticaean Abyss]], central [[Halinor]]
**Affiliation:** None (independent trading outpost)

## Origin and Brief History

Darius' Crossroads traces its origins to a makeshift trading camp established centuries ago by a merchant named Darius, who recognized the value of a convergence point where multiple regional trade routes met. The camp grew into a settlement, the settlement grew a reputation, and the reputation attracted the kind of people who prefer to operate where oversight is thin. It has been an essential and unsavory fixture of Halinor's interior ever since.

## Description

The Crossroads is sprawling and improvised: makeshift stalls, tents, and rudimentary buildings assembled from whatever the trade routes have deposited here over the years. The central marketplace — called the **Dust Bowl** by everyone who uses it — operates continuously, with goods ranging from legitimate cargo to items whose provenance is better left unasked. Haggling is the native language. No city-state governs it; no charter binds it. Order, such as it is, emerges from the collective self-interest of people who need the place to keep functioning.

It sits at the intersection of several key interior routes — including those connecting [[Laconia]] and the [[Sticaean Abyss]] to the north, [[Tarwa'kem]] to the south, the [[Kustara Logging Company]], and the [[Kustara Mining Company]] to the east. Anyone moving goods through central Halinor passes through eventually.

## Notable People

**Raven-Eyed Tarra** — Information broker. Has operated from the Crossroads for years and knows who has passed through, what they carried, and who was looking for them. Charges exactly what the information is worth.
