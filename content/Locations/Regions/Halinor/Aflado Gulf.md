###### Type and Location
Body of water — coastal gulf along the southern edge of [[D'yadya]] and southwestern [[Tarwa'kem]], opening into the broader southern sea

## Overview
The Aflado Gulf is a broad coastal inlet that curves along the southern shoreline of [[D'yadya]] and the southwestern reaches of [[Tarwa'kem]]'s outer territory. It has historically served as one of D'yadya's primary maritime access points — a natural harbor where coastal trade moved goods that the inland caravan routes couldn't carry efficiently, particularly salt from the [[Sha'qal Flats]] and textiles bound for southern markets.

Under Vociran occupation, the Gulf's ports and fishing communities have been absorbed into the imperial administrative structure. Vociran naval vessels now patrol its waters, and what was once a relatively open maritime region has become another monitored threshold. The fishing communities along its coast — smaller and more scattered than D'yadya's inland cities — have been among the last to feel the full weight of occupation, given their distance from the major Vociran garrisons, but they have not escaped it.

The Gulf's southern mouth opens toward the broader sea routes that eventually connect to [[Avosa Tesota]] and, further east, to the [[Serveaus Sea]]. This makes it strategically relevant to [[Vociterra]] beyond mere resource extraction — controlling the Aflado Gulf means controlling one of the western continent's maritime flanks.

## Climate & Ecology
The Gulf benefits from warm southern currents that moderate the arid coastal climate of [[D'yadya]]'s south. Fishing is productive year-round, with seasonal variation in species. Coastal vegetation is sparse but more present than in the interior desert. The Gulf's calm waters make it navigable by smaller vessels that would struggle in the open sea, which historically made it attractive to both honest traders and less scrupulous ones.
