###### Type and Location
Geographic region — expansive white salt plains along the western coastal margins of [[D'yadya]], western [[Halinor]]

## Overview
The Sha'qal Flats are a vast expanse of white salt plains running along [[D'yadya]]'s western coast where the land meets the sea. They were the economic foundation of D'yadyan civilization — the source of the salt trade that made the nation wealthy and gave its caravans their purpose. Under the sun, the plain blazes white and flat in all directions, disorienting to outsiders and striking in a way that D'yadyan poets spent entire careers trying to describe.

Before the [[World Encyclopedia/Historical Events/Conflicts/The Siege of the White Sands|Siege of the White Sands]], the Flats were worked by tribal salt guilds whose harvesting rights and caravan territories had been negotiated across generations. The coastal settlement of **A'Mukafa** on the northern edge of the Flats was one of the primary trade hubs in the region, connected by road to the inland market town of **Al'Farah** near the [[Khadra Woods]].

Under Vociran occupation, those patterns have been broken. Imperial extraction contracts have consolidated the harvesting operations, redirecting profit north to [[Patria Noster]]. Patrol routes now cut across the salt in straight imperial lines indifferent to the old caravan paths. The Flats remain productive — they simply no longer belong to the people who built their lives around them.

## Climate & Ecology
The Sha'qal is arid, swept by sea winds from the west that moderate the coastal temperatures. The flat terrain offers no windbreak, and salt crystals carried on the breeze sting exposed skin and clog mechanisms. Very little grows beyond salt-tolerant scrub at the margins. The fauna — seabirds, shore lizards, burrowing creatures — is adapted to salt and wind. Occasional ground collapses occur where burrowed tunnels undermine the salt crust without warning.
