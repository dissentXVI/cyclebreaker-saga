###### Type and Location
Geographic region — arid desert forming the eastern border between [[D'yadya]] and [[Tarwa'kem]], west of the [[Amrakebir Mountains]]

## Overview
The H'abyadie Desert is the harsh natural boundary separating [[D'yadya]] from [[Tarwa'kem]], stretching across the eastern edge of D'yadyan territory in a wide band of shifting sands, rocky outcroppings, and sun-bleached stone. It is not impassable — caravans have crossed it for centuries, and established routes wind between its most dangerous stretches — but it is unforgiving to the unprepared.

For most of [[Halinor]]'s history, the H'abyadie functioned as a natural buffer zone: too harsh for large armies to cross efficiently and too sparse in resources to make permanent occupation worth attempting. [[Vociran Empire|Vociran]] forces patrol it now, but selectively — the desert's terrain defeats supply lines and overheats machinery. Resistance fighters and smugglers have long known which passages the patrols skip.

The desert narrows toward the north where it meets the foothills running down from the [[Amrakebir Mountains]]. [[Duea's Pass]] cuts through this margin — the primary overland route connecting D'yadya to Tarwa'kem, and the threshold that leads into [[Dagger Canyon]] to the south. The name H'abyadie comes from an old D'yadyan word meaning roughly "the long refusal" — a reference not to any battle or treaty, but to land that has always refused to be anything other than what it is.

## Climate & Ecology
Classic desert terrain — searing heat by day, cold nights, and irregular but occasionally violent sandstorms. Sparse vegetation clings to wadis and lower elevations where moisture briefly collects. Hardy reptiles, insects, and desert birds populate the scrubland margins, while the deep interior supports very little visible life. D'yadyan lore speaks of deep aquifers and pneuma-rich stone buried beneath the sand — resources that Vociran geological surveys have been quietly investigating.
