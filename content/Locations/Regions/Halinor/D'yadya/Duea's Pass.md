###### Type and Location
Desert steppe crossing — arid mesa and rock spire terrain on the border between [[D'yadya]] and [[Tarwa'kem]], following the eastern bank of the Al'azraq River

## Overview
Duea's Pass is the primary overland crossing point between [[D'yadya]] and [[Tarwa'kem]], threading through a stretch of open, sun-baked steppe studded with dramatic mesa formations and shattered rock spires. It is not a mountain pass in any conventional sense — there are no peaks to summit or foothills to climb. Instead, the terrain is defined by the flat-topped mesas and eroded stone columns that rise from the desert floor, forcing travelers to navigate between them along routes worn into the dust by generations of caravans. The Al'azraq River runs along the crossing's western edge, one of the few reliable water sources in the region and a natural landmark that guides travelers north toward [[Tarwa'kem]].

It has served as a trade corridor, a refugee route, and a military threshold for as long as the two nations have had a relationship. The terrain's openness makes it easier to traverse than [[Dagger Canyon]] to the south, but also harder to hide in — a fact that has defined its character under Vociran occupation. Patrols monitor it regularly, and movement through it is logged. The exile networks smuggling people and goods between [[D'yadya]] and [[Tarwa'kem]] tend to use the desert's less-watched margins rather than the pass itself, precisely because the flat, exposed steppe offers nowhere to disappear.

## Climate & Ecology
Hot, dry, and wind-scoured. The mesa terrain breaks the wind into unpredictable eddies that carry fine grit across the open ground. Temperatures drop sharply at night, and the rock formations hold the cold long into the morning. Scrub and drought-tolerant grasses cling to the base of the mesas and along the Al'azraq's banks, where the soil is marginally less punishing. Wildlife is sparse but present — lizards, birds of prey using the mesa updrafts, and the occasional desert predator working the riverbank.
