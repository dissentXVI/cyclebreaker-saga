###### Type and Location
Mountain range — large central range running roughly north-south through western [[Halinor]], forming the geographic spine between [[D'yadya]], [[Tarwa'kem]], and the interior of the continent

## Overview
The Amrakebir Mountains are the dominant geographic feature of western [[Halinor]] — a massive, jagged range that stretches from the northern interior down through the heart of the continent, forming a natural wall between the arid western territories and the greener lands to the east. [[Tarwa'kem]] is built into and against their western face, its subterranean halls carved directly into the stone and its upper districts perched on the [[Kemtabi Shelf]] at the mountains' base.

The range has defined the political and military geography of the region for thousands of years. It sheltered the dwarven tribes from the floods of the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]], providing refuge from which they eventually emerged to found [[Tarwa'kem]]. It served as the natural eastern boundary of [[Navarresean See|Navarresean]] expansion during the [[The Second Celestial Campaign|Second Celestial Campaign]], where dwarven defenders used the terrain to brutal effect against elven legions. More recently, the caves beneath the mountains' northern reaches provided the approach route for [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Cassius gen Amata]]'s disastrous expedition to [[Sticaea Lake]], with consequences that reshaped the entire continent.

The mountains are rich in ore, breathstone veins, and geothermal activity — all of which [[Tarwa'kem]] has exploited with considerable engineering ingenuity. [[Duea's Pass]] and [[Dagger Canyon]] cut through the range's southern reaches, providing the primary overland connections between the western and central regions.

## Climate & Ecology
The Amrakebir range creates a sharp climate divide: the western slopes are arid, catching little moisture from the [[The H'abyadie Desert|H'abyadie Desert]] below, while the eastern faces receive more rainfall and support denser vegetation at lower elevations. The high peaks are permanently snowcapped. The mountains are home to significant underground ecosystems in addition to the dwarven engineering of [[Tarwa'kem]] — cave fauna, subterranean rivers, and pneuma-rich stone formations that make the deep tunnels as much a resource as a danger.
