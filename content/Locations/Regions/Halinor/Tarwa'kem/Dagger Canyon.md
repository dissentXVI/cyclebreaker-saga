###### Type and Location
Geographic region — deep canyon cutting through the southern [[Amrakebir Mountains]], on the border between [[D'yadya]] and [[Tarwa'kem]], northern [[Halinor]]

## Overview
Dagger Canyon is a deep, narrow gorge that carves through the southern reaches of the [[Amrakebir Mountains]], forming one of the two primary overland crossings between [[D'yadya]] and [[Tarwa'kem]]. Where [[Duea's Pass]] to the north is relatively navigable, Dagger Canyon is something else entirely — a harsh, high-walled passage where the terrain does the fighting before any soldier has to. Its sheer rock faces channel wind into a near-constant roar, limit visibility to what lies directly ahead, and compress any column of troops into a formation that has no room to maneuver. It is the kind of geography that rewards defenders and punishes ambition.

[[Tarwa'kem]] has long understood this. The canyon sits within their territorial sphere, and [[The Gilded Legion]] maintains a practised awareness of its approaches. When [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Cassius gen Amata]] led an unauthorized incursion through the canyon in 4008 ApN, the Legion was waiting. The narrow terrain stripped the Vociran force of every tactical advantage it might have had on open ground, and the rout was total. The battle became known as the [[World Encyclopedia/Historical Events/Conflicts/The Skirmish of Dagger Canyon|Skirmish of Dagger Canyon]] — a name that understates the consequences it set in motion.

Before and beyond its military significance, the canyon has always been part of the landscape people live around. The village of [[Maladh]], a wool-farming community near the canyon's eastern mouth, has existed for centuries. Its residents undertake a Canyon Pilgrimage once per generation — a spiritual journey into the gorge to reconnect with ancestral roots — though they do not discuss it with outsiders. [[Vociterra|Vociran]] forces now patrol the canyon's western approaches as part of their broader occupation of [[D'yadya]], and Vociran control is described by D'yadyans as strongest here — the canyon being one of the few places in the region with a clear defensive value the Empire has actively invested in holding.

## Climate & Ecology
The canyon's depth creates a microclimate distinct from the surrounding desert and mountain terrain. Wind funnels through it consistently, keeping temperatures lower than the open desert to the west. The rock faces trap shadow for most of the day, and moisture clings to the lower sections in ways that support sparse vegetation — scrub, root plants, and the occasional hardy tree that has found purchase in a crack. The upper walls are bare stone. Wildlife tends toward the adaptable: birds of prey that use the updrafts, small mammals in the lower reaches, and the predators that follow them. The canyon is not hospitable, but it is not lifeless.
