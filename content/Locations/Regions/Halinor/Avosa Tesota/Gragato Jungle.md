###### Type and Location

Tropical jungle and mangrove basin • Southeastern Halinor

## Overview

The Gragato Jungle rises dense and breath-heavy behind the coast of [[Avosa Tesota]], its canopy layered in emerald shadow and stormlight. Vines hang thick as rigging, roots twist like coiled serpents, and rainfall comes in sudden gold-washed sheets. The jungle does not move like the Fauconwood — it presses, expands, and endures.

Where the sea winds falter, the Gragato begins.

It feeds Avosa’s docks with hardwood and resin, shelters ancient gnoll tribes beneath fruit-laden groves, and conceals trade paths known only to Rangers and smugglers. Storms gather faster here. Sound carries strangely. Fires burn reluctantly.
## Settlements & Notable Sites

[[Avosa Tesota]] — Coastal maritime republic at the jungle’s eastern edge, dependent on Gragato timber, resin, and inland trade routes.

[[Serpiosa]] — Semi-autonomous mangrove settlement on the western fringe; hub of black-market trade and suspected nexus of recent disappearances.

[[Ramitas Drydocks]] — Northern coastal shipyard complex renowned for refining Tyrachon’s Pitch from Gragato resin and hardwood.

Ranger’s Hold — Hidden command post of [[The Gragato Rangers]], built into elevated stone and root systems deep within protected jungle territory.

Mangrove Labyrinth — A tidal network of shifting waterways west of Serpiosa; safe passage requires local knowledge. Frequently used for covert transport.

## Climate & Ecology

Tropical coastal climate with violent afternoon storms and humid dawn fog. Rainfall is heavy and sudden, followed by intense sunlight that draws mist from the forest floor. The jungle canopy traps heat, creating a layered ecosystem from root-shadow to treetop bloom.

Flora includes:

- Resin-bearing pitchwood trees used in [[Tyrachon's Pitch]] refinement
- Fruit groves sacred to gnoll tribes
- Venomous flowering vines
- Medicinal herbs known only to Rangers and tribal shamans

Fauna range from brightly plumed birds and river serpents to jaguars and territorial boars. Larger predators tend to avoid Ranger patrol zones, though behavior has grown erratic in recent months.

Pneuma density is moderate but fluctuates unpredictably during storms.

## Native Inhabitants

The Gragato is home to multiple gnoll tribes, each organized around clan groves and hunting territories. Their culture centers on oath-keeping, ancestor memory, and fruit-sharing rites tied to the land’s cycles.

[[The Gragato Rangers]] serve as diplomatic bridge between jungle and city, patrolling trade routes and enforcing the Fruitbound Oath sworn generations ago.

Smugglers, prospectors, and [[The Red Net|Red Net]] operatives occasionally establish temporary camps along the outer jungle margins. Few last long without Ranger tolerance.

The jungle itself feels inhabited — not by fae courts as in the east, but by older instincts. Storm patterns sometimes gather unnaturally over specific groves. Animals fall silent without clear cause.

## Narrative Relevance

The Gragato Jungle stands at the center of Avosa’s current crisis.

Gnoll tribes have reported missing hunters near the western mangroves. Trade routes near [[Serpiosa]] show signs of forced movement. Ranger patrols have uncovered abandoned camps containing shackles not of gnoll make.

If confirmed, slave trafficking within the jungle would violate the Charter of Dignity and the original Fruitbound Oath — an act that could fracture Avosa internally.

[[Corella Marante]] cannot send naval forces into the jungle without congressional support. [[Solana Varros]]’s rhetoric of “freedom without chains” resonates differently when chains are discovered inland.
