###### Type and Location
Body of water — southern sea of [[Halinor]], lying between the southeastern coastline and the island of [[Shurevaire]]

## Overview
The Serveaus Sea is the warm, relatively calm body of water separating [[Shurevaire]] from the [[Halinor]] mainland. It is bounded to the northwest by the coasts of [[Avosa Tesota]] and to the north by the southern shores of central Halinor, with [[Shurevaire]]'s island sitting at its southeastern reach. Its mild currents and predictable wind patterns have made it one of the most heavily trafficked stretches of water in the known world — a corridor of trade, scholarship, and the occasional covert passage.

Unlike the storm-prone northern seas or the treacherous straits near [[Eisfeld]], the Serveaus is forgiving to sailors. The crossing between [[Avosa Tesota]] and [[Shurevaire]] is a routine voyage for experienced captains, and the sea lanes have been charted with precision by both nations' cartographers. [[Ramitas Drydocks]], a semi-independent shipbuilding settlement allied to Avosa, sits on the northern coast of the sea and serves as one of the region's primary maritime facilities.

[[Shurevaire]]'s recent self-imposed isolation has disrupted the sea's traditional patterns. Ships that once docked freely at the island's ports are now turned away, and lanes that once carried scholars now carry rumors.

## Climate & Ecology
The Serveaus enjoys a temperate to warm climate year-round, moderated by southern currents. Seasonal storms occur but rarely close the crossing entirely. The sea supports abundant marine life, and fishing fleets from both Avosa and the coastal settlements of southern Halinor work these waters regularly. Shurevairian scholars have catalogued scattered pneuma anomalies at depth — unusual breathstone concentrations on the seafloor and patches where navigational instruments behave erratically — without reaching consensus on their source.
