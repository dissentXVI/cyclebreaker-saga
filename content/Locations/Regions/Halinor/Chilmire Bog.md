###### Type and Location
Wetland region — western margin of [[The Fauconwood]], central-northeastern [[Halinor]]

## Overview
The Chilmire Bog is a broad, fog-prone wetland that runs along the western edge of [[The Fauconwood]], sitting between the forest proper and the more open central terrain of [[Halinor]]. It is not the most hospitable stretch of land on the continent, but it is far from empty. Lizardfolk communities have made the bog their home for as long as anyone has kept records, moving through its waterways and reed-choked passages with the ease of people who have never needed to apologize for the terrain they live in. They range occasionally into the Fauconwood's western margins as seasonal traders, maintaining a quiet but longstanding relationship with the Aqualondian communities to the east.

The Chilmire is ecologically dense — layers of standing water, peat, rare fungi, and plant life that grows nowhere else on the continent. Herbalists and alchemists from [[Aqualonde]] have long valued it as a source of ingredients, though navigating it without a guide is inadvisable. The bog's interior is genuinely difficult to cross, and the light behaves strangely in ways that experienced travelers attribute to pneuma accumulation in the standing water and peat.

In recent seasons, [[The Earthspeakers Circle|Earthspeakers]] from Aqualonde have reported disturbances near the Chilmire — something in the bog's pneuma signature has shifted in ways that correlate with the broader fae silence affecting the Fauconwood. Whether the Chilmire is a cause, a symptom, or simply the place where the disturbance is most legible remains under investigation.

This phenomenon has since subsided since the arrival of [[Aspiria's Chosen]].
## Climate & Ecology
Perpetually damp, fog-prone, and rich with standing water and peat. Temperatures are moderate but rarely warm, and the ground underfoot ranges from soft to impassable depending on season and rainfall. The bog supports an extraordinary density of plant and fungal life, much of it found nowhere else — some pneuma-reactive, some mundanely useful, some neither. The lizardfolk who live here have an intimate working knowledge of which is which. Seasonal flooding extends the bog's reach into the Fauconwood margins during heavy rains.
