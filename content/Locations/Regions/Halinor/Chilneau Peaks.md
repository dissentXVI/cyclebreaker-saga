##### Type and Location
Mountain range — eastern edge of [[The Fauconwood]], bordering [[Aqualonde]] territory in eastern [[Halinor]]

## Overview
The Chilneau Peaks are the mountain range that forms the eastern boundary of [[The Fauconwood]] and the broader [[Aqualonde]] region. They are not as massive or historically prominent as the [[Amrakebir Mountains]] to the west, but they are significant to the ecology, culture, and daily life of eastern Halinor in quieter ways. The peaks feed the [[Riviera Scintillante]] — the glittering trade river that runs from their slopes south to the [[Halinor]] coast and serves as a key corridor for Aqualondian commerce and the Windriders who navigate it. [[Serinvale]], the prosperous retreat-town founded by a splinter group of Carinthian elves, sits at the base of the range, its crystal-clear streams fed directly by mountain springs.

The Chilneau Peaks are not known for military significance or dramatic history. They are known for being beautiful, for producing clean cold water, and for the way their upper ridgelines catch the morning light in a manner that Aqualondian poets have been describing — never quite satisfactorily, by their own admission — for centuries. The range forms a natural windbreak that contributes to the Fauconwood's temperate climate, and its lower slopes blend into the forest with the kind of gradual transition that makes it difficult to say exactly where the trees end and the mountains begin.

## Climate & Ecology
The peaks are temperate at their lower elevations, giving way to colder, rockier terrain as altitude increases. Snow caps the highest points through winter and into spring, feeding the streams that run west into the Fauconwood. The slopes support dense coniferous growth on their western faces and more sparse, windswept terrain to the east. Wildlife is varied and largely undisturbed — the Chilneau Peaks have never been mined or settled in any significant way, and their relative remoteness has kept them intact.
