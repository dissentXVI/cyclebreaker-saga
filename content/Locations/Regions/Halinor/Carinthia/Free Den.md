###### Type and Location
Forest region — south of [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]], north of central [[Halinor]], bordering the approaches to the [[The Crystal Bridge|Crystal Bridge]]

## Overview
The Free Den is a dense, largely ungoverned forest that lies between [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]'s southern border and the open terrain of central [[Halinor]]. It has never been claimed by any city-state and has no formal governance — which is precisely why it has always attracted people who need neither. Deserters from Carinthian military service, heretics fleeing the [[the Light of Alophia|Church's]] inquisitors, pilgrims who ran out of money or faith before reaching the [[The Crystal Bridge|Crystal Bridge]], and travelers who simply needed to disappear for a season have all found shelter here at one point or another. The Free Den earns its name honestly.

Old pilgrim roads once cut through the forest from the south, connecting the broader [[Halinor]] road network to [[Snowfinch Rest]] and the Crystal Bridge beyond. These roads still exist in rough form, but they are no longer well-maintained, and in recent seasons they have become considerably less safe. Iceheart cultist activity has been confirmed in the Free Den, and the [[Knights Templar]] have doubled their patrols at the Crystal Bridge's western gate in response. The roads that once brought pilgrims toward [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]] now bring as much uncertainty as passage.

For those who know how to move through it carefully, the Free Den remains what it has always been — a place where the rules of the surrounding world apply loosely, and where a person who needs to not be found probably won't be.

## Climate & Ecology
Dense temperate forest with thick canopy and limited sight lines. The undergrowth is heavy, and the forest floor stays damp through most of the year. The Free Den is not as pneuma-rich or fae-touched as [[The Fauconwood]] to the southeast, but it is old enough to have its own particular quality of quiet — the kind that makes sounds carry strangely and distances feel unreliable. Wildlife is abundant and largely undisturbed by the sparse human presence.
