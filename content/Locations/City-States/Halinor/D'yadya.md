## Summary
West of the sun-beaten borders of [[Tarwa'kem]], across the H'abyadie Desert, lies the broken homeland of D'yadya — once a beacon of hospitality, trade, and cultural harmony, now a fragmented memory beneath the iron heel of the [[Vociran Empire]]. Founded in 2382 ApN during [[the Fourth Radiant Age]], D'yadya was born of nomadic tribes who chose peace and cooperation over conquest. Their salt and textile trades made them wealthy; their traditions of ritual combat and reasoned tribal councils made them proud. But that pride has been weaponized. Since 3995 ApN, D'yadya has lived under occupation — its youth twisted into enforcers of imperial rule, and its culture smothered under foreign banners. Even so, its exiled children whisper of return.

## Cultural & Aesthetic Profile
- ***Influences***:  
  Moroccan and Marrakesh-inspired with nomadic and tribal traditions
- ***People/Demonyms***:  
  D'yadyans
- ***Naming Conventions***:  
  Arabic-inspired names (e.g., Zeyn, Tazi, Choukri); family and tribal names carry significant cultural weight, often signaling lineage, region, and standing
- ***Architecture & Aesthetics***:  
  Sun-bleached domes, columned courtyards, fabric-draped marketplaces, and salt-stained monuments carved from timeless stone. Though many now lie ruined or repurposed by Vociran forces, their beauty endures as testimony to D'yadya's former grandeur. Vociran installations — steel watchtowers, fortified garrisons — cut through the landscape like scars.

## Political and Religious Landscape
##### Government Structure
Once governed by rotating councils of elected tribal leaders, D'yadya now exists under military occupation by the [[Vociran Empire]]. [[Caedus seq Magnus]], the Vociran crown prince, serves as imperial governor — enforcing compliance through conscription, cultural erasure, and economic extraction. His most effective instrument is the **Vis Populus**, a youth militia made up of re-educated D'yadyan children tasked with policing their own elders, promising citizenship and safety in exchange for loyalty.

##### Key Factions
- **[[Vis Populus]]** – Vociran-aligned D'yadyan youth militia enforcing imperial law from within; made up of children raised in Vociran ideology
- **Tribal Remnants** – Elders and traditionalists who resist cultural erasure through storytelling, food, and hidden ceremony; increasingly fragmented
- **The Exiles** – D'yadyan diaspora scattered across [[Tarwa'kem]] and [[Halinor]]; organized into tight-knit communities preserving language, music, and tradition
- **The Saltbinders** – Secret historians and linguists preserving D'yadyan language and stories in hiding; operate out of the refugee communities in [[Tarwa'kem]]
- **The Grain Pact** – An ancient religious-political tradition tied to [[Demeria]], the Harvest Lady; once governed community resource-sharing; now practiced in secret

##### Spiritual Beliefs
D'yadyans traditionally worship [[Demeria]], goddess of harvest and family. Temples once doubled as meeting halls and food banks, reinforcing the spiritual link between governance and hospitality. Vociran rule has outlawed all divine worship within D'yadya's occupied territories. Many still pray in secret, hiding icons in food stores and decorating meals with subtle symbols of reverence — a tradition that has become as much a political act as a spiritual one.

## Geographic and Urban Details
##### Notable Districts/Regions
- **[[Sha'qal Flats]]** – Expansive white salt plains once central to D'yadya's salt trade; now scarred by Vociran patrol routes and refugee footprints
- **[[Dagger Canyon]]** – Strategic highland pass where Vociran control is strongest; site of the [[The Skirmish of Dagger Canyon|Skirmish of Dagger Canyon]]
- **The Woven Market** – Former capital center and cultural heart of D'yadya; now rebranded and administered under imperial rule
- **The Whispering Clays** – Rural and semi-nomadic regions where resistance clings to life; difficult terrain the Vocirans have never fully pacified
- **[[Misrata]] ("The Sift")** – Refugee shantytown in [[Tarwa'kem]], home to thousands of displaced D'yadyans; the largest concentration of the exile community

##### Key Locations
- **Pillar of Trade** – A ruined salt monument once marking D'yadya's founding trade pact; now overtaken as a Vociran garrison
- **Temple of the Grain** – Hidden sanctuary dedicated to [[Demeria]], now protected by the Saltbinders; its exact location is one of the most closely guarded secrets among the exiles
- **The Spire of Hands** – A once-proud monument of tribal unity, depicting the joining of the founding clans; now defaced with Vociran sigils
- **The Sandpost** – Vociran watchtower and conscription center; central to recruitment for the Vis Populus
##### Climate/Biome
Arid deserts, rocky coastlines, salt-crusted rivers, and shifting sands define D'yadya's harsh beauty. Droughts and dust storms are common, but so too are flower-bloom oases and moonlit caravan gatherings. [[The H'abyadie Desert]] to the east forms a natural border with [[Tarwa'kem]] that the Vocirans patrol but never fully control.

## Narrative Role
##### Historical Significance
Founded in the Fourth Stygian Age, D'yadya evolved from disparate nomadic tribes into a peaceful, resource-rich city-state. Its governance emphasized debate, diplomacy, and ritual martial arts. The nation's decline began with the [[World Encyclopedia/Historical Events/Conflicts/The Siege of the White Sands|Siege of the White Sands]] in 3995 ApN — a devastating Vociran blitz under [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Gallus gen Quietus]] that conquered most of D'yadya within a year. Though many fled before the Dagger Pass closed, most who remained were subjected to systematic cultural erasure. The occupation accelerated under [[Caedus seq Magnus]] in 4010 ApN, who replaced Gallus's military pragmatism with something more ideological and deliberate.

##### Current Events
The **Vis Populus** now enforces imperial rule from within, its members D'yadyan children raised to see their own culture as backwards and their compliance as progress. Traditional customs — language, music, clothing, worship — are criminalized or replaced. Elders whisper resistance through storytelling, food, and secret gatherings.

Exiled D'yadyans in [[Tarwa'kem]] form tight-knit communities, passing on their culture and training in secret, hoping one day to return. Some have begun coordinating aid networks and smuggling books, icons, and even people back across the border — at great risk. [[Zeyn Choukri]], now sheriff of [[Laconia]], represents a harder edge of D'yadyan exile pride: the belief that if the world won't give D'yadyans a home, they'll carve one out themselves.

Meanwhile, [[Tarik Tazi]] — a D'yadyan refugee who rose to become General of the [[Gilded Legion]] — occupies an uneasy position: beloved by his people as proof that they can survive, and quietly resented by some for surviving in a foreign uniform.

##### Notable Characters
- **[[Zeyn Choukri]]** – D'yadyan-born; now Sheriff of [[Laconia]]; trains a militia of the displaced and the determined
- **[[Tarik Tazi]]** – D'yadyan refugee turned General of the Gilded Legion; a symbol of exile survival and complex compromise
- **[[Caedus seq Magnus]]** – Crown prince of the [[Vociran Empire]] and imperial governor of occupied D'yadya; architect of the Vis Populus
