## Summary
A theocratic alpine stronghold founded in **2168 ApN**, **Carinthia** was established by elves fleeing the collapse of the [[Navarresean See|Navarresean Empire]]. Built atop a mountain range in northern [[Halinor]], Carinthia is both a sanctuary of divine order and a fortress against the dragon broods that once ruled the skies. Governed by the [[Church of Alophia]], Carinthia blends religious dogma with military discipline. Once forged in alliance with dragons, a tragic betrayal led to generations of bloodshed now known as the [[Wyrmsfall War]]. Today, only [[Heldenheim]] remains standing—its last bastion of faith and fire beneath the endless snows.

## Cultural & Aesthetic Profile
- ***Influences***:  
  French Gothic + German theocratic states  
- ***People/Demonyms***:  
  Carinthians (predominantly elven)  
- ***Naming Conventions***:  
  French or Germanic names. Illegitimate children often bear metaphorical surnames (e.g., *Frost*, *Snowtree*).  
- ***Architecture & Aesthetics***:  
  Snow-laden stone spires, tiered fortifications, and stained-glass cathedrals rise amid frozen cliffs. Brass ornamentation, candlelight, and austere iconography embody divine discipline. Each structure reflects the Carinthian creed: solemn beauty, clarity of purpose, and unyielding devotion.

## Political and Religious Landscape
##### Government Structure
Carinthia is ruled by the [[Light of Alophia]], both church and state in one divine hierarchy. The current Archbishop, **[[Aedelweg von Achaard]]**, holds ultimate spiritual authority, his word binding in both law and faith. Secular enforcement is commanded by **[[Adrien Frost]]**, Lord Commander of Carinthia, chosen by [[The High Houses]]. Governance intertwines sacred scripture with military order, with the [[Dawnbound]] and [[The Inquisition|Inquisition]] ensuring purity of doctrine.

##### Key Factions
- **[[The Light of Alophia]]** – The central theocratic body that defines law, faith, and education.  
- **[[The Dawnbound]]** – Elite inquisitorial agents devoted to preserving Alophia’s truth through blade and sacrifice.  
- **[[The High Houses]]** – The four noble bloodlines descended from the surviving Dawnbound:  
  - *House Vaillant* (military)  
  - *House Courtemanche* (arcane)  
  - *House Achaard* (ecclesiastical)  
  - *House Bellefeuille* (craftsmanship)

##### Spiritual Beliefs
Carinthians worship **[[Alophia]]**, goddess of knowledge and war strategy, as the sole divine authority. Her doctrine emphasizes logic, clarity, and lawful sacrifice. Followers pursue a life of discipline—whether through sacred study, martial service, or clerical devotion. Faith in Alophia is not merely worship; it is law, life, and language.

## Geographic and Urban Details
##### Notable Districts/Regions
- **[[Heldenheim]]** – The capital and last surviving city of Carinthia; a sanctuary of stone and sanctity.  
- **[[Snowfinch Rest]]** – Border town guarding the Crystal Bridge, gateway to the northern frontier.  
- **[[The Maiden’s Covert]]** – A field of jagged ice spires said to sing in the wind; site of countless fallen knights.  

##### Key Locations
- **[[Cathedral of the Sacred Light]]** – Grand seat of the [[Church of Alophia]] and the Archbishop’s throne.  
- **[[Skyward Keep]]** – Command citadel of the Lord Commander and headquarters of the [[Knights Templar]].  
- **[[Vaillant Hall]]** – Fortress-estate of House Vaillant; training ground for the next generation of Carinthian officers.

##### Climate/Biome
Perpetually cold and mountainous. Long winters and piercing winds dominate the alpine highlands. Frost blooms on every surface, and aurorae often shimmer across the night sky—regarded as Alophia’s blessing or warning.

## Narrative Role
##### Historical Significance
Founded by **[[Saint Carine Reivalur]]** and his elven followers after fleeing the tyranny of the [[Navarresean Empire]], Carinthia was born under divine guidance. Saint Iseult later forged peace with the dragon **[[Durnirax]]**, securing an age of enlightenment. But in 2854 ApN, the dragon **[[Skadraxes]]** was betrayed and slain under false pretenses, igniting the **Wyrmsfall War**. The [[Dawnbound]] rose in defense of Archbishop Louis, defeating Skadraxes at ruinous cost. Only four knights survived—their lines ennobled to form the High Houses, forever binding faith and steel.

**The Hidden Truth:**  
Archbishop Louis orchestrated the betrayal. He ordered the Dawnbound to ambush Skadraxes, tearing her eyes from her skull to fuel his apotheosis as **[[Arch de Soleil]]**, a self-proclaimed god-king. The slaughter destroyed nearly all Dawnbound. Enraged, **[[Wodr]]** swore vengeance, offering one of his eyes to **[[Durnirax]]** in a pact of mourning—a covenant that would doom Carinthia to centuries of dragonfire.

##### Current Events
Though the god-king fell in **3004 ApN**, his sins endure. **[[Wodr]]** and his brood wage unending war upon Carinthia, reducing its domain to the fortified city of **[[Heldenheim]]**. The **Wyrmsfall War** grinds on in ice and blood. Within the cathedral walls, faith falters as whispers spread of a heretic—**[[Renai Aymere]]**, who claims to be **[[Saint Iseult]]** reborn and seeks to reconcile dragon and elf.

##### Notable Characters
- **[[Aedelweg von Achaard]]** – Archbishop of Carinthia; cold and calculating, the spiritual and political center of the realm.  
- **[[Adrien Frost]]** – Lord Commander of the Knights Templar; stoic protector of Heldenheim’s gates. 
- **[[Sebastien Cellier]]** – Lightwarden; a reclusive dragon-slayer whose faith burns as fiercely as his rage.  
- **[[Renai Aymere]]** – Charismatic heretic who believes herself Saint Iseult reborn.  
- **[[Dorian Vaudrin]]** – Inquisitor and loyal enforcer of Aedelweg’s will.  
- **[[Alsten Duvaine]]** – Chronicler of Heldenheim and secret sympathizer of Renai’s ideals.  
- **[[Bernard Snowtree]]** – Captain of Snowfinch Rest; honorable, steadfast, and beloved by the common folk.  
- **[[The Dawnbound]]** – Elite holy warriors bound by oath to the Archbishop, their numbers ever dwindling.
