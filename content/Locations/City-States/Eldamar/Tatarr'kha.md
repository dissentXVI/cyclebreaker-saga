## Summary
Tatarr'kha is the vast open steppe at the heart of [[Eldamar]] — a wind-scoured expanse of grassland, dry river valleys, and stone-ridged plains that stretches from the southern foothills of [[Vociterra]]'s highlands to the northern borders of [[Gholmeshi]]'s forests. It has no capital, no walls, and no throne. What it has is space, and the people who have always known how to live in it. The orcish clans of Tatarr'kha are among the oldest peoples of Eldamar — riders, hunters, and warriors whose society is organized not around cities or crowns but around strength, kinship, and the yearly contest that determines who speaks for the whole of the steppe. To outsiders, Tatarr'kha appears ungoverned. To those who live there, it is governed by something older and more honest than any throne: the outcome of a fair fight.

## Cultural & Aesthetic Profile
- ***Influences***:
  Mongolian nomadic culture — horsemanship, clan loyalty, oral tradition, portable dwellings, a deep spiritual relationship with the land and sky; a people defined by movement, endurance, and the belief that strength is the only credential that cannot be forged
- ***People/Demonyms***:
  Tatarr'khans; orc
- ***Naming Conventions***:
  Single given name followed by clan name (e.g., Gothmog Gansukh — Gothmog of Clan Gansukh). Clan names are earned by lineage and sometimes by deed; a clanless orc is a serious social stigma. Naming tends toward short, hard consonants and open vowels
- ***Architecture & Aesthetics***:
  No permanent cities. The clans travel in seasonal circuits across the steppes, dwelling in large felt-and-hide gers — round, portable structures that can be raised or struck in hours. A clan's wealth is measured in oxbeasts, horses, weapons, and the quality of its gers. The great gathering of the **Tugdaal** — the yearly contest — is the closest thing Tatarr'kha has to a shared public space, and even it is assembled fresh each year from the gathered camps of every clan. Color and bone ornamentation identify clan allegiance. Totem carvings mark every entrance post.

## Political and Religious Landscape
##### Government Structure
Tatarr'kha has no permanent government. Each clan is led by a **Khan** — a chieftain whose authority rests on demonstrated martial supremacy, the loyalty of their riders, and the respect of their elders. Khans are not hereditary by right; a son may inherit, but only if no one challenges the succession successfully.

Once per year, at the height of summer, every clan sends its greatest champion to the **Tugdaal** — the Grand Trial of the Steppes. The Tugdaal is a multi-day contest of mounted combat, wrestling, archery, and feats of endurance. The champion who wins the Tugdaal's final trial is named **Khagan** — paramount khan, first among chieftains — and holds that title until the next Tugdaal. The Khagan does not rule the clans directly; they cannot command a Khan to act against their clan's interest. But in matters affecting all of Tatarr'kha — war, border disputes, the treatment of outsiders — the Khagan's word carries the weight of the entire steppe behind it.

There is no appeal above the Tugdaal. There is no court, no council, no written law. Disputes between clans are settled by negotiation, by trial combat, or occasionally by blood — and the Khagan is expected to prevent the last wherever possible.

##### Key Factions
- **The Rider Clans** – The many independent orcish clans of the steppe; each maintains its own territory, herds, and internal customs; united only by shared tradition, the Totem faith, and the authority of the Tugdaal
- **The Tugdaal Council of Elders** – A body of senior clan representatives who organize and witness the yearly contest, certify its results, and serve as the institutional memory of the steppes; they hold no executive power but command enormous moral authority
- **The Clanless** – Orcs who have been exiled from or lost their clan through disgrace, defeat, or circumstance; they wander the steppe margins, sometimes hired as sellswords or scouts; viewed with a mixture of pity and wariness by settled clans
- **The Exile Communities** – Refugees from [[Ilsong]] and other occupied territories who have settled on the steppe's edges; welcomed by some clans as useful outsiders, resented by others as a disruption to tradition; their presence is an ongoing point of debate among the Khans

##### Spiritual Beliefs
The Tatarr'khans revere a tradition of **Totems** — primal divine spirits tied to the forces of the natural world: the sky, the storm, the hunt, the herd, the blood. Totems are not organized into a hierarchy or a pantheon; each clan typically venerates one or two primary Totems alongside broader reverence for the Sky Father and the Earth Mother, who are understood as the source from which all Totems flow. Worship is immediate and physical — offerings of food and blood, ritual combat performed in a Totem's honor, the carving of a Totem's image into weapons and dwelling posts. There are no temples. The steppe is the temple. [[Grond]] the Blood Wolf is among the most widely venerated Totems across the clans, patron of warriors and the hunt.

## Geographic and Urban Details
##### Notable Districts/Regions
- **The High Steppe** – The central plateau of Tatarr'kha; the oldest clan territories; open, wind-battered grassland that stretches to the horizon in every direction; where the Tugdaal is held each summer
- **The Dry River Valleys** – Eastern Tatarr'kha; seasonal rivers that run full in spring and crack dry by midsummer; home to the most mobile of the clans, who follow the water
- **The Stone Ridges** – Western Tatarr'kha where the steppe buckles into low rocky escarpments; difficult terrain that has historically kept [[Vociterra]]'s expansion from pressing too far south; several clans treat these ridges as sacred ground
- **The Southern Margins** – Where the steppe blends into the northern forests of [[Gholmeshi]]; warmer, more sheltered; home to the Exile Communities and to clans with closer trade relationships with southern neighbors
- **The Border Reaches** – The northern and western edges of Tatarr'kha where proximity to [[Vociterra]] and formerly [[D'yadya]] creates ongoing tension; Vociran patrols have been sighted here with increasing frequency

##### Key Locations
- **The Tugdaal Grounds** – No fixed location; the site of the Grand Trial rotates across the High Steppe by tradition, chosen each year by the outgoing Khagan and announced to all clans a full moon's cycle before the contest begins; when the clans gather, the camp that forms is one of the largest concentrations of people anywhere on [[Eldamar]] for the days it lasts
- **The Stone of First Khan** – A massive natural rock formation on the High Steppe, carved over generations with the names and marks of every Khagan in Tatarr'khan memory; the oldest carvings are worn nearly smooth; it is the only fixed monument the steppe possesses, and it is considered neutral ground by all clans
- **The Edge Markets** – Informal trading posts at the steppe's margins where Tatarr'khans exchange hides, oxbeast products, and horses with Gholmeshi merchants and Ilsongese exile traders

##### Climate/Biome
Open temperate steppe — hot, dry summers with high winds, bitterly cold winters with driving snow. The grass grows tall in spring, burns gold by midsummer, and lies flat and frost-white by the long winter. Oxbeasts are the dominant large fauna: enormous, thick-hided herd animals that the clans herd, ride, and hunt in roughly equal measure. The sky over Tatarr'kha is enormous — flat land and no trees mean the horizon is always visible, and the stars at night are a navigation system as much as a spectacle.

## Narrative Role
##### Historical Significance
The orcish clans of Tatarr'kha predate most recorded history on [[Eldamar]]. They were present before [[Vociterra]] was founded, before [[Ilsong]] developed its warrior culture, and before [[Gholmeshi]] built its first pagoda towers. They have outlasted every attempt to absorb, conquer, or permanently settle them — not through walls or armies, but through movement. You cannot hold what will not stay.

The Tugdaal tradition is believed to be as old as the clans themselves, though its current form was codified in living memory after a particularly brutal inter-clan war threatened to fracture the steppe permanently. The Council of Elders emerged from the peace negotiations that followed, and the Tugdaal became the mechanism by which contest replaced war as the primary means of determining precedence.

##### Current Events
Tatarr'kha watches the wider world with careful eyes. [[Vociterra]]'s expansion into [[D'yadya]] brought imperial forces to the steppe's western border — close enough that several clans have begun organizing joint patrols of the Stone Ridges, a level of inter-clan cooperation that would have been unthinkable a generation ago. The Khagan's position on Vociran encroachment is the central political question of the current era, with some Khans arguing for pre-emptive alliance and others insisting the steppe has never needed a foreign policy and does not need one now.

The growing Exile Communities on the southern margins — primarily Ilsongese survivors of the fall of [[Zhu'ruhai]] — are reshaping the steppe's social edges in ways the clans are still reckoning with. Some have been absorbed into clan life. Others remain apart. A handful of the more martial exiles have begun participating in smaller Tugdaal trials, which has produced no small amount of controversy.
