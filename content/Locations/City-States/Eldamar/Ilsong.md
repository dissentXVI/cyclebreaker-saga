## Summary
Ilsong is an island nation off the western coast of [[Eldamar]] — a temperate, cliff-studded land of bamboo forests, tidal rivers, and castle complexes built into the rock above crashing seas. For centuries it was home to one of the most refined warrior cultures in the known world: a society governed by the Shanyu, upheld by an ancient code of martial discipline and ancestor reverence, and protected by the elite royal guard known as the Shield of the Rising Moon. That world ended in 4006 ApN, when the [[Vociterra|Vociran Empire]] invaded under [[Caedus seq Magnus]] and the mechanized 6th Fleet. Deceived by a false treaty, Ilsong fell in weeks. The Shanyu was killed. The capital was occupied. And a nation that had survived every storm the sea could offer found itself unable to survive the tide that came by land.

## Cultural & Aesthetic Profile
- ***Influences***:
  East Asian warrior culture — lamellar armor, sword-saint traditions, river trade, castle architecture built into cliffsides and coastlines; a society where martial excellence and aesthetic refinement are considered inseparable
- ***People/Demonyms***:
  Ilsongese; predominantly orc
- ***Naming Conventions***:
  Family name precedes given name (e.g., Kioshi Yurei, Mirai Tsuga, Runa Araki). Titles such as Shanyu (ruler) and Tengushin (supreme commander of the royal guard) are spoken before the name in formal address
- ***Architecture & Aesthetics***:
  Multi-tiered pagoda castle complexes built into coastal cliffs and highland plateaus, overlooking tidal rivers and open sea. Stone fortification walls descend to rocky shores. Wooden bridge infrastructure connects cliff-side districts. Lacquered black and deep crimson are the dominant colors of ceremony and military attire. In peacetime, markets and temple grounds occupy lower terraces, while the Shanyu's halls crown the uppermost tiers. Much of this beauty now flies Vociran banners.

## Political and Religious Landscape
##### Government Structure
Ilsong was governed by the **Shanyu** — a sovereign ruler whose legitimacy was rooted in warrior lineage, martial mastery, and the covenant of protection between ruler and people. The Shanyu held both political and symbolic authority; to be Shanyu was not merely to govern but to embody the nation's highest ideal of disciplined, principled strength. The royal guard — the **Shield of the Rising Moon** — served as both the Shanyu's personal protectors and the martial heart of the state, commanded by the **Tengushin**.

Since the fall of Shoran in 4006 ApN, this structure has been dismantled. The Vociran Empire governs through the **Ilsong Provisional Authority**, with [[Kaihana Uritami]] installed as Imperial Viceroy since 4010 ApN. The Shanyu line is officially declared extinct as no legitimate heir exists.

##### Key Factions
- **The Ilsong Provisional Authority** – Vociran administrative body governing the occupied island; enforces compliance, cultural suppression, and economic extraction; headed by Imperial Viceroy [[Kaihana Uritami]]
- **The Shield of the Rising Moon** – The former royal guard; disbanded and largely destroyed during the fall; surviving members have scattered, gone underground, or been conscripted; [[Tsuga Mirai]] was its last Tengushin
- **The Ilsong Liberation Front** – Armed resistance movement that rose in the years following the occupation; rallied exiles, scholars, and warrior-priests under a shadowed leader known only as Shinsei the Gray; crushed at the Siege of Shuvalan Temple when the Front was betrayed and Vociran forces besieged the complex; remnants operate in scattered cells or individually, with no confirmed central leadership

##### Spiritual Beliefs
Ilsong's spiritual life is woven into its martial and ancestral traditions rather than organized around a single deity. The divine beings of Ilsong are known as **Reishen** — sacred spirits whose presence is felt through natural forces, bloodlines, and the conduct of the living. The sword-saint traditions — including the Kioshi-lineage form **Tenkū-no-Tachi** and the guard style **Raiun-no-Kata** — treat the mastery of combat as a form of spiritual discipline: a covenant between the warrior's will and the Reishen who watch over them. Ancestor reverence is central; the honored dead are believed to stand among the Reishen, observing through the actions of those who carry their name. Temple complexes serve as both places of worship and martial training grounds. The Vociran occupation has suppressed public practice, but private observance continues in homes and hidden sanctuaries.

## Geographic and Urban Details
##### Notable Districts/Regions
- **Shilu'an Province** – A coastal region of Ilsong; birthplace of [[Araki Runa]]; known for its warden clan traditions
- **The River Lowlands** – Tidal river valleys connecting inland settlements to coastal ports; historically the economic backbone of the island's trade network; now subject to Vociran customs enforcement
- **The Clifftop Reaches** – Highland plateau regions where the major castle complexes were built; home to the Shanyu's court and the Shield of the Rising Moon's training grounds
- **The Interior Forests** – Dense bamboo and mixed woodland covering much of Ilsong's interior; used historically for lumber, spiritual retreat, and — since the occupation — guerrilla resistance

##### Key Locations
- **Shoran** *(Capital — occupied)* – The imperial capital of Ilsong; built into cliffsides above the sea, its tiered districts rising from rocky shoreline markets to the Shanyu's palace at the summit. Taken by the Vociran 6th Fleet in 4006 ApN after three days of outer defense and a final stand in the throne room by Shanyu [[Yurei Kioshi]]. Now rebranded as a Vociran protectorate city; Vociran banners and installations have replaced most royal iconography. The palace itself has been repurposed as the seat of the Provisional Authority.
- **Shuvalan Temple** – Sacred martial temple complex; site of the final battle of the Ilsong Liberation Front; hundreds died here when the rebellion was betrayed and Vociran forces besieged the complex. Now a ruin under Vociran control, its significance suppressed in official records.
- **The Kioshi Palace** *(ruined/repurposed)* – Former seat of the Shanyu's court at the summit of Shoran; scene of [[Yurei Kioshi]]'s last stand; now the administrative center of the Provisional Authority

##### Climate/Biome
Temperate island climate — warm summers, mild winters, frequent sea mist and seasonal storms rolling in from the western ocean. The interior is lush with bamboo groves, broadleaf forest, and river networks fed by highland rainfall. The coastline alternates between dramatic cliffs, tidal flats, and sheltered coves. It is a beautiful island. The Vocirans have not changed that. They have simply changed who it belongs to.

## Narrative Role
##### Historical Significance
Ilsong's fall was not inevitable — it was engineered. In 4006 ApN, [[Caedus seq Magnus]] led the Vociran 6th Fleet in a naval invasion disguised behind a false treaty negotiation. Caught in a period of political transition and unprepared for the scale of the mechanized assault, Ilsong's coastal defenses were overwhelmed within weeks. Shanyu [[Yurei Kioshi]] initially surrendered Shoran to prevent its destruction, accepting a nominal role within the occupation in order to buy time for an organized resistance.

That resistance — launched openly when Yurei judged the moment right — briefly reclaimed the palace before Vociran reinforcements arrived and crushed it. Yurei died in the throne room. In the years that followed, a second resistance emerged: the **Ilsong Liberation Front**, rallying exiles, scholars, and warrior-priests under a leader known only as Shinsei the Gray. It was bold, and for a time dangerous to the occupation — until it was betrayed. The Siege of **Shuvalan Temple** ended in slaughter. Hundreds died. The Front collapsed. Shinsei the Gray was never identified, and their fate remains unknown.

##### Current Events
Ilsong is occupied but not reconciled. The Vociran **Provisional Authority** governs through compliance decrees, cultural suppression, but rebellion still strikes though with seemingly less and less conviction.

##### Notable Characters

- **[[Tsuga Mirai]]** – Former Tengushin of the Shield of the Rising Moon; survivor of the fall; now wandering in grief and drink
- **[[Araki Runa]]** – Former royal blade of the Shield; still operating in the shadows beyond Ilsong's borders
- **[[Kaihana Uritami]]** – Imperial Viceroy of the Provisional Authority; a Gholmeshi national installed as the Empire's face on the occupation
