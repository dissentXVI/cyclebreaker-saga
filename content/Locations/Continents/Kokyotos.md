## Summary
Kokyotos is a storm-wracked chain of rocky islands in the far south of [[Aspiria]]. Humid, rugged, and difficult to navigate, it is home to various beast tribes and dragonkin who have made their lives among its cliffs and coves. The continent is more an archipelago than a landmass — no single power has ever claimed dominion over its scattered shores. Its peoples are tribal and deeply rooted in oral tradition, with a mythology centered around storms, sea, and ancient powers that once walked among them.

## Geography & Climate
- **General Terrain**: Rocky island chains, sea cliffs, storm-battered coastlines, and dense humid lowlands
- **Climate Range**: Humid and storm-prone throughout; persistent squalls make long-range sailing treacherous
- **Notable Features**: No confirmed capital or unified settlement; the islands are numerous and irregularly charted; strong pneumatic storm currents are believed to concentrate naturally around several of the larger islands

## Regions & City-States
Kokyotos has no established city-states. Its populations are tribal and scattered across the island chains. Beast tribes and dragonkin communities are the dominant peoples, and their territories shift over generations.

## Lore & Narrative Role
Kokyotos sits at the edge of the known world — rarely visited, poorly mapped, and remembered mostly through the stories sailors bring back. Its tribes carry oral traditions of great winged patrons who once protected their shores, though the specifics of those legends vary wildly by island and generation. Like every continent, Kokyotos has been touched by the Cataclysms that have periodically reshaped the world — though how deeply each age of disaster altered its island chains is largely unrecorded, as no scholarly tradition has taken root there to preserve the memory. In the current age, Kokyotos remains largely outside the politics of [[Halinor]], though sailors who venture south bring back accounts of unusual pneuma concentrations, shifting coastlines, and tribes that appear to be mobilizing in ways not seen in living memory.

> [!dm] DM Only — Not Revealed to Players
> The tribal mythologies of Kokyotos are not legend — they are memory. [[Krisnarax]] and [[Nottraxes]], two of [[Ymirethor]]'s First Brood, were the continent's ancient patrons. Krisnarax was a bronze storm-caller beloved by seafarers; Nottraxes, an iridescent shape-shifter revered as a goddess of truth and change. Both are now dead — Krisnarax slain during the [[The First Celestial Campaign|First Celestial Campaign]] and bound as a shade in the [[Sticaean Abyss]]; Nottraxes bound deep within [[Taras'Nula]]. The unusual pneuma concentrations and tribal mobilizations sailors are reporting may be connected to the disturbance of Krisnarax's shade in the events at the [[Watcher of the Lake]].
