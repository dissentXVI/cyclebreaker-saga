**Title**: Knight-Commander of the [[Knights Templar]]

**Allegiance**: [[Carinthia]] (Templars, Secular Arm)

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Female

**Age**: Early 30s

**Eyes**: Ice blue

**Hair**: Ash-blonde, tied back in a warrior’s knot

**Attire**: Blue and silver Templar plate with golden inlays

**Home**: [[Heldenheim]], [[Carinthia]]




## Appearance

Claudia stands with an air of absolute control — poised, unreadable, and unyielding. Her armor is worn, but not without care - mithril chased with gold, bearing the Templar insignia without embellishment. A long scar traces her left cheekbone, a remnant of her earliest campaigns. Her posture is rigid but never strained; she carries herself like someone who expects to be obeyed — and usually is.

## Personality

Claudia is pragmatic, disciplined, and unsentimental. She speaks little, but her words are clear and precise, and her tone brooks no misinterpretation. To her soldiers, she is a commander to be respected — not loved. To her enemies, she is terrifyingly efficient.

That said, Claudia is not without a moral compass. Her loyalty to [[Adrien Frost]] is absolute, and she shares his commitment to protecting the people of Carinthia above institutional ego. Where [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]] softens with diplomacy, Claudia sharpens into resolve — their contrast is what makes them effective.

She does not suffer foolishness or sentimentality, but she holds a quiet respect for those who endure, persevere, and choose to serve.

## Character History

Trained from an early age for military action, Claudia has served as Knight-Commander of the Templars, second only to [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]] himself. Her loyalty has never wavered, and she has played a critical role in shaping the discipline, mobility, and modern tactics of Carinthia’s armies — especially as the [[Wyrmsfall War]] intensifies.

## Current Status

Claudia currently serves as Knight-Commander of the [[Knights Templar]], overseeing operations across Carinthia’s eastern and northern fronts. She is often deployed on missions requiring speed, precision, and discretion — commanding elite strike units, coordinating defenses at key border outposts, and inspecting newly trained officers for signs of weakness or fanaticism.

While [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]] commands the war as a general, Claudia ensures the army functions at all levels — chain of command, supply cohesion, battlefield doctrine, and personal conduct.

## Combat Style

Claudia fights like a surgeon with a blade — swift, deadly, efficient. She favors a longsword and dagger pairing, eschewing shields for speed and control. She often fights in lighter mithril plate reinforced at critical joints, allowing her to move freely across the battlefield.

Her signature technique involves precision strikes at command targets — officers, standard bearers, spellcasters — designed to break enemy cohesion from within. She rarely gives second chances.

## Notable Relationships

[[Adrien Frost]] – Her commander, savior, and closest (perhaps only) confidant. She would die before betraying his trust. Their relationship is built on mutual respect, battlefield partnership, and unspoken understanding.

[[Bernard Snowtree]] – Claudia admires Bernard’s passion and conviction, though she finds his idealism naive. Still, she respects his loyalty to [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]] and defers to him when local diplomacy is required.

Archbishop [[Aedelweg von Achaard]] – Claudia respects his authority but mistrusts his motives. She carefully navigates Church oversight but does not disguise her preference for secular command.

The [[Dawnbound]] and [[The Inquisition]] – Claudia views both as valuable but dangerous. She allows no interference with Templar operations and has had Inquisitors quietly reassigned when they overstepped.

## Secrets

Claudia Metellus was not born in Carinthia. She was born in [[Vociterra]] and trained from a young age as an infiltrator within their officer corps. She was sent to Carinthia on a covert mission to gather intelligence on the High Houses and their relic-based warfare.

But somewhere along the way, she defected.

The details remain sealed — even within the upper echelons of the Templar command — but what is known is this: [[Adrien Frost]] discovered her true identity, offered her a choice, and she chose to stay.
