## Overview  
  
**Founders:** Early intelligence agents appointed under the First Fleet Accord  
**Primary Location:** Decentralized; safehouses throughout [[Gilded Bay]] and [[Crescent Port  ]]
**Current Leader(s):** The Netmaster (identity concealed); multiple cell leaders known as Weavers  
  
The Red Net is [[Avosa Tesota]]’s unofficial intelligence and smuggling network — a web of informants, couriers, dockworkers, merchants, and sea-captains who trade not only goods, but secrets.  
  
Originally formed to protect Avosa from imperial retaliation and pirate warlords, the Red Net evolved into a shadow apparatus operating parallel to the Congress of Captains. It gathers intelligence, disrupts foreign threats, and ensures Avosa’s interests are protected beyond the reach of official law.  
  
Today, the Red Net stands divided.  
  
Some remain fiercely loyal to [[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]] and Avosa’s republican ideals.  
  
Others have begun accepting coin from [[Solana Varros]] and her allies, redirecting smuggling profits and political leverage toward a return to traditional piracy.  
  
## Structure and Ranks  
  
The Red Net operates in decentralized cells to avoid exposure.  
  
### The Netcaster  
The unseen coordinator of the network. Identity unknown to most members. Communicates through coded ledgers and symbolic markings in dockyards.  
  
### Weavers  
Regional cell leaders who manage intelligence gathering and black market operations within specific districts.  
  
### Threads  
Field agents, smugglers, dockworkers, and informants embedded in:  
- Harbor guilds  
- Ship crews  
- Serpiosa trading circles  
- Foreign ports
  
## History  
  
The Red Net was born during Avosa’s early years, when rival fleets and foreign powers sought to destabilize the young republic. The First Fleet Captains recognized that open freedom required hidden vigilance.  
  
The Net was sanctioned quietly — not by law, but by necessity.  
  
Under [[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]], the Red Net shifted from piracy coordination to intelligence defense and smuggling containment. Certain operations were curtailed, particularly those involving slave markets and predatory trade. This created internal fractures.  
  
Traditionalist elements believed the Net had grown timid under reform.  
  
When [[Solana Varros]] began courting the old guard captains, Red Net cells sympathetic to her ideology found new funding streams and renewed purpose and now the Net no longer pulls in one direction.

## Narrative Relevance  
  
The Red Net is the silent battlefield beneath Avosa’s election.  
  
- Certain Weavers are secretly funneling campaign funds to [[Solana Varros]].  
- Others continue reporting directly to [[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]].  
- Some cells enable black-market trade through [[Serpiosa]], including weapons and illegal cargo.  
- Whispers suggest a faction within the Net is aware of — and possibly complicit in — the rumored gnoll slave trade.
