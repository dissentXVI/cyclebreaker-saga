## Overview

**Founders:** The First Fleet Captains of Avosa Tesota  
**Primary Location:** Ranger’s Hold, Gragato Jungle  
**Current Leader(s):** [[Reyes Lobo]], Warden of the Gragato  

The Gragato Rangers are [[Avosa Tesota]]’s inland wardens — guardians of the [[Gragato Jungle]] and mediators between the city and its oldest neighbors, the gnoll tribes.

Founded in the early years of Avosa to defend settlements from gnoll raids, the Rangers were once a militant frontier force. Over time, conflict gave way to uneasy diplomacy. Through bloodshed, survival, and eventually the binding of the **[[Fruitbound Oath]]**, the Rangers transformed from hunters into stewards.

Today, they serve as protectors of the jungle trade routes, enforcers of sacred oaths, diplomatic intermediaries between Avosa and the gnoll tribes, and guardians against exploitation of the jungle's resources.

They answer to [[the Congress of Captains]] — but their loyalty lies first with balance.

## Structure and Ranks

The Rangers are smaller than Avosa’s navy but operate with autonomy and discipline.
### Warden of the Gragato
The supreme leader of the Rangers, responsible for both military defense and diplomatic relations with the gnolls.

Current Warden: [[Reyes Lobo]].
### Pathfinders
Senior officers who oversee patrol regions within the jungle. Often tasked with negotiation and treaty enforcement.
### Rangers
Veteran members skilled in both combat and survival. They protect trade caravans and respond to threats.
### Greencloaks
Initiates and scouts trained in tracking, herbalism, and jungle warfare.
### Oathbound
A special designation given to Rangers who have personally sworn Fruitbound Oaths with gnoll chieftains. These individuals serve as formal envoys during tribal disputes.

## History

When Avosa was founded in 3567 ApN, expansion into the [[Gragato Jungle]] sparked violent conflict with its indigenous gnoll tribes. Early Rangers were tasked with clearing territory and protecting settlers, earning a reputation as relentless hunters.

The turning point came during a prolonged frontier war that neither side could decisively win. Facing mutual devastation — and mounting cataclysmic threats from beyond Halinor — both sides agreed to parley.

There, the first **Fruitbound Oath** was sworn.

From that moment, the Rangers shifted from conquest to coexistence.

Under subsequent Wardens, they became joint patrol partners with gnoll trackers, enforcers against illegal logging and poaching, guardians of the jungle shrines, and mediators, during territorial disputes.

Their transformation is often cited by [[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]] as proof that Avosa can evolve beyond piracy.

Traditionalists see them as softened.

## Narrative Relevance

The Gragato Rangers now stand at the center of growing instability. They're reporting missing tribal gnolls near [[Serpiosa]] and have raise alarm. Evidence suggest black-market trade routes. Warden [[Reyes Lobo]] suspects [[The Red Net|Red Net]] involvement. [[Solana Varros]] question whether the Rangers are loyal to Avosa or the gnolls at this time.

If the rumored slave trade is confirmed, the Rangers may be forced into open confrontation — not just with criminals, but with political interests tied to the Congress itself.
