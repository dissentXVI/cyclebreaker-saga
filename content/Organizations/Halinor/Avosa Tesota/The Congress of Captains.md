## Overview

**Founders:** The First Fleet of Captains of [[Avosa Tesota]]  
**Primary Location:** [[Admiralty Hall]], [[Gilded Bay]], [[Avosa Tesota]]  
**Current Leader(s):** [[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]] (incumbent), [[Solana Varros]] (Congresswoman and declared challenger)  

The Congress of Captains consists of 20 voting members, representing fleets, guilds, and harbor districts. In times of division, the Admiral will cast the deciding vote.

## Structure and Ranks

### Total Seats: 20

The Congress is composed of:

#### Fleet Captain Seats (10)

Active ship captains registered under Avosan charter.
Each commanding vessel grants its captain one vote.
If a captain loses their ship, they lose their seat.

Fleet captains represent the raw maritime strength and cultural heritage of Avosa.

#### Guild Delegate Seats (5)

Representatives elected from major maritime trades:
- Shipwrights
- Merchants
- Navigators
- Dockworkers
- Quartermasters

Guild delegates reflect Avosa's economic backbone and often favor stability over spectacle.

#### Harbor Representative Seats (5)

Elected representatives from major districts:
- [[Crescent Port]]
- [[The Cradle]]
- [[The Pearlsteps]]
- [[Gilded Bay]]
- Outer Docks

Harbor representatives embody the civilian population and often serve as swing votes during contested legislation.

### Executive Authority

The Congress elects an **Admiral** from among its members.

The Admiral:
- Commands the navy
- Enforces maritime law
- Negotiates treaties
- Oversees foreign diplomacy
- May veto legislation (subject to supermajority override)

In the event of a **10–10 tie**, the Admiral casts the deciding vote.

This rule was established to prevent legislative paralysis during naval crises — though in election seasons, it can become politically explosive.

## History

The Congress was formed after Avosa's early fleets nearly destroyed one another over disputed spoils. Rather than crown a Pirate King, the captains agreed to create a structured council where authority was distributed and no single fleet ruled unchecked.

Early sessions were turbulent — duels were not uncommon, and votes were occasionally settled with blades.

Under Admiral Corella Marante, the Congress passed the **Charter of Dignity**, formalizing Avosa's transformation from loose pirate confederacy into recognized maritime republic. This shift curtailed unregulated plunder and emphasized trade alliances and naval legitimacy.

The current election represents the greatest ideological divide since the Congress's founding.

## Narrative Relevance

Avosa now stands at a political crossroads.

[[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]] campaigns on:
- Structured freedom
- Trade legitimacy
- "A rising tide lifts all ships"
- Building something lasting in a world threatened by cataclysms

[[Solana Varros]] campaigns on:
- Restoration of traditional piracy
- Rejection of diplomatic restraint
- "Freedom without chains"
- The belief that Avosa's strength lies in bold plunder, not bureaucracy

With 20 seats divided between captains, guilds, and harbor delegates, the Congress is nearly evenly split. A narrow majority could decide the election outright.

If the vote ends 10–10, the Admiral will cast the deciding vote — a constitutional mechanism that could secure their re-election, but at the risk of public controversy.

[[Santigo del Sol]]'s endorsement may sway critical swing members and tip the balance before the final tally.

If the Congress fractures:
- Fleet rivalries may reignite.
- The Red Net may gain influence.
- [[Serpiosa]]'s instability may spill into open conflict.
- Avosa's democratic experiment could collapse back into factional piracy.

[[The Congress of Captains]] is not merely a government.

It is Avosa's attempt to prove pirates can govern themselves.
