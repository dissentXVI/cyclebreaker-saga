## Overview

**Founders:** [[Serkaf Tarwa]], founder of [[Tarwa'kem]]; the Karo'kem line established as the ruling dynasty in the city's early centuries
**Primary Location:** [[Royal Palace]], [[Kemtabi Shelf]], [[Tarwa'kem]]
**Current Leader(s):** Sultana [[Neema Karo'kem]]

## History

The Karo'kem Dynasty traces its legitimacy to [[Serkaf Tarwa]], the dwarven leader who united the mountain clans through the [[Ibshah Oaths]] and founded [[Tarwa'kem]] in 2115 ApN. The Karo'kem name — appended to the royal line by convention, marking only those of direct dynastic blood — has presided over the city through every Radiant and Stygian Age since, surviving cataclysm, invasion, and internal fracture by virtue of popular legitimacy and, where necessary, political ruthlessness.

The Dynasty's relationship with [[the Monetarist Council]] has defined much of its modern history. As merchant wealth accumulated and trade influence expanded, the council's advisory role quietly became something closer to co-governance, and successive sultans navigated this balance with varying degrees of success. Sultan [[Shakir Karo'kem]] was among the more capable of these, maintaining genuine authority over both the military and the council until his assassination in 4008 ApN. His death left [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]], then five years old, as the sole surviving heir — and left [[Tarwa'kem]] under effective council rule for thirteen years.

[[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]]'s coronation in 4021 ApN at eighteen marked the Dynasty's formal restoration of direct authority. Whether that authority will hold against the council's entrenched interests is the defining question of the current moment.

## Narrative Relevance

The Karo'kem Dynasty is the nominal sovereign power of [[Tarwa'kem]], but sovereignty in the city has always been contested terrain. [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] governs with the loyalty of [[Tarik Tazi]] and [[the Gilded Legion]], the cautious support of the lower classes, and the wary opposition of most of [[the Monetarist Council]]. She is the last of her line — there is no other heir, no sibling, no cousin with a credible claim. If she falls, the Dynasty ends, and with it the sultanate's claim to stand above the council's interests.

The circumstances of Sultan [[Shakir Karo'kem|Shakir]]'s death remain officially unsolved. The common belief is Vociran involvement. The truth is considerably closer to home.
