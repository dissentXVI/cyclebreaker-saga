> "The past does not belong to those who buried it. It belongs to those who find it."

## Overview

**Founders:** Scholars and archaeologists of early [[Tarwa'kem]], formalized under the patronage of the [[The Karo'kem Dynasty|Karo'kem Dynasty]]
**Primary Location:** [[Ahura Antiquarium]], [[Historical Quarter]], [[Tarwa'kem]]
**Current Leader(s):** Director [[Tali Kouri]]

## Structure and Ranks

- **Director** – Head of the Order and the [[Ahura Antiquarium]]; sets acquisition priorities and controls access to restricted collections. Currently [[Tali Kouri]].
- **Expedition Masters** – Senior archaeologists who lead major field operations; each specializes in a region or period.
- **Field Scholars** – Active excavators and researchers deployed on expeditions across Aspiria.
- **Archivists** – Specialists in classification, preservation, and documentation; maintain the Antiquarium's records and restricted vault catalogues.
- **Initiates** – New members undergoing training in excavation methods, relic handling, and Order protocols.
- **Independent Contractors** – Unlisted operatives funded by the Order under assumed names; used for acquisitions the Order cannot officially authorize.

## History

The Order of Ahura was established in [[Tarwa'kem]]'s early centuries as a scholarly body dedicated to the excavation and preservation of historical artifacts, with particular focus on the pre-sultanate dwarven period and the ruins of the [[Navarresean See|Navarresean Empire]]. It operated for generations as a respected academic institution, its expeditions funded by the sultanate and its findings displayed in the [[Ahura Antiquarium]] for public and scholarly access.

Under [[Tali Kouri]]'s directorship, the Order's mandate expanded in practice if not in formal description. Acquisition methods grew more aggressive, with field teams operating in regions and under circumstances that could not always withstand scrutiny. The Order's celebrity status in [[Tarwa'kem]] — its scholars are genuinely famous, their expeditions followed with public interest — provided cover for operations that quieter institutions could not have conducted.

The Order's activities in certain regions drew sufficient concern that [[Neema Karo'kem|Sultana Neema Karo'kem]] formally restricted the Oa's presence in one such area, citing sovereignty and relic protection concerns. [[Tali Kouri]] absorbed the restriction without public protest and responded by funding an independent assessment branch — which operates covertly and is not subject to the restriction's terms.

## Narrative Relevance

The Order of Ahura is [[Tarwa'kem]]'s most publicly celebrated institution and one of its most quietly dangerous. [[Tali Kouri]]'s control of the Antiquarium's restricted vault — housing relics too sensitive or politically volatile to display — gives her a form of leverage that operates entirely outside [[the Monetarist Council]]'s commercial frameworks. She knows things about the city's past, and about the [[Navarresean See|Navarresean]] era, that she has chosen not to share.

The Order's unofficial acquisition of [[Navarresean See|Navarresean]] relics through independent contractors is the most active of its current operations. What [[Tali Kouri|Tali]] intends to do with those relics — or what they may reveal — has not yet become clear.
