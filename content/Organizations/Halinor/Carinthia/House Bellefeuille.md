> "Beauty and order are divine reflections."

## Overview

**Founders:** Guildmasters elevated during Carinthia's founding exodus  
**Primary Location:** [[Heldenheim]]  
**Current Head:** [[Thibault von Bellefeuille]]  
**Coat of Arms:** A gold-bordered roundel on a white field, with a trio of gold bezants arranged along a blue stripe  
**Domain:** Craft, commerce, sacred architecture, and economic stewardship

House Bellefeuille is distinguished not by the sword or sermon, but by mastery of craft, trade, and stone. While Carinthia is defined by austerity and martial theocracy, Bellefeuille has quietly carved a space of enduring influence within its cold walls — not through conquest, but through artistry, coin, and legacy.

Where others build faith into their banners, House Bellefeuille builds the walls that protect the faithful, the bridges they cross, and the reliquaries that hold their saints.

## Structure and Ranks

The house operates through guild contracts, architectural commissions, and patronage of artisans, scholars, and templars alike. While Bellefeuille members rarely take the field, their coin equips many who do. They are also formally responsible for the maintenance of Carinthia's sacred sites, including the great statues of [[Saint Carine]] and the shrines of the Twelve Saints.

## History

Founded in the early days of Carinthia by guildmasters elevated for their service during the founding exodus, House Bellefeuille earned its noble standing through the construction of [[Heldenheim]]'s earliest fortifications and cathedrals. The family name became synonymous with stonecraft and sacred architecture.

Over time, Bellefeuille expanded beyond masonry into jewelcraft, fine weaving, metallurgy, and economic stewardship — serving as patrons to guilds and stabilizers of commerce in an otherwise isolated and warlike realm.

## Leadership and Lineage

**[[Thibault von Bellefeuille]]** — Current head. A former architect of cathedrals turned noble steward, known for deliberate speech, a steady hand, and unwavering belief that beauty and order are divine reflections. Not a warrior, but commands deep respect from guildmasters, architects, and the more pragmatic arms of the Church.

**[[Lady Nathalie von Bellefeuille]]** — Thibault's wife. A renowned weaver whose intricate tapestries adorn several Carinthian sanctuaries. Said to broker half her husband's alliances before he has even spoken to the other party.

**[[Eloane von Bellefeuille]]** — Eldest daughter. Meticulous and discreet; manages the family's ledgers, oversees guild contracts, and acts as a quiet liaison with the Church's logistics offices. Said to speak more with coin than with words.

**[[Offrec von Bellefeuille]]** — Second-born, known as the Thunder of Heaven. A [[The Dawnbound|Dawnbound]] paladin who traded the chisel for the sword. His martial zeal and battlefield presence often surprise those who expect the house's reserved refinement; the Bellefeuilles regard him as a necessary symbol of artistic force turned divine fury.

**[[Bertrand von Bellefeuille]]** — Youngest son. A celebrated poet and bronze sculptor who moves between noble courts and artisan salons. Charismatic but politically unwieldy — a social risk and a cultural darling, dangerous in conversation but too beloved to silence.

**[[Emelie von Bellefeuille]]** — Middle daughter and master sculptor, famed throughout Carinthia for her work *Wisdom's Spear: An Homage to Alophia's Piercing Clarity*, housed in the [[The Light of Alophia|Light of Alophia]] and considered one of the finest modern expressions of the Goddess. Lives in quiet devotion, shaping doctrine through marble.

## Narrative Relevance

House Bellefeuille is Carinthia's economic and cultural engine — the force that gives the theocracy its material beauty and financial stability. Underestimated by more militant houses, their influence runs through every building, guild, and sacred artwork in the realm.
