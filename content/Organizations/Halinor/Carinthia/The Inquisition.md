## Overview

**Founders:** Established under the authority of the [[The Light of Alophia|Church of Alophia]]; formalized during the era of [[Louis'roi Arch de Soleil]]  
**Primary Location:** [[Cathedral of the Sacred Light]], [[Heldenheim]]  
**Current Leader(s):** Operates under the Archbishop [[Aedelweg von Achaard]]; field operations led by figures such as [[Dorian Vaudrin]]

The Inquisition is the Church of Alophia's enforcement arm — the body charged with identifying, interrogating, and eliminating heresy, apostasy, and doctrinal corruption wherever it takes root within Carinthia. Where the [[Knights Templar]] guard Carinthia's borders and the [[The Dawnbound|Dawnbound]] confront external threats of singular magnitude, the Inquisition turns inward, operating within the kingdom's own walls.

Inquisitors hold considerable ecclesiastical authority. They may detain, try, and sentence citizens — including clergy — in matters of faith. Their investigations are final, their jurisdiction broad, and their methods not subject to civil appeal.

## Structure and Ranks

The Inquisition is organized around independent agents who report directly to senior Church hierarchy:

- **Grand Inquisitor** — The presiding authority over Inquisitorial operations; typically a senior cleric appointed directly by the Archbishop
- **Inquisitors** — Fully empowered agents; each carries both investigative and judicial authority in matters of heresy, also known as "Greycloaks"
- **Inquisitorial Templars** — Armed escorts and enforcers who accompany Inquisitors on field operations
- **Informants and Scrutineers** — A network of civilian and clerical informants who report suspected deviance

Several members of the [[The Dawnbound|Dawnbound]] have Inquisition backgrounds — notably [[Erika the Red]], whose crimson sash is a remnant of her time in the order.

## History

The Inquisition grew out of the Church's early need to maintain doctrinal consistency across a population living in close quarters under siege conditions. In a city-state perpetually at war, the definition of heresy expanded over generations to include not just theological deviance but any ideology that questioned the Church's authority, expressed sympathy for the dragonkin, or challenged the legitimacy of the [[High Houses of Carinthia|High Houses]]' founding myth.

The growing influence of [[Renai Aymere]] and the Icehearts has placed the Inquisition under considerable pressure. Aymere's claims — that Saint Iseult has been reborn, that mankind and dragonkind were once allies, and that the Wyrmsfall War is built on a lie — represent the most structurally dangerous heresy the Inquisition has faced in living memory, because parts of it are true.

## Narrative Relevance

The Inquisition is both antagonist and institution — the face of the Church's less merciful qualities. Its presence creates pressure on characters who question the official narrative, harbor sympathies for the heretics, or simply know too much.
