## Overview

**Founders:** Founding Dawnbound knight (ancestor of current line)  
**Primary Location:** [[Vaillant Hall]], [[Heldenheim]]  
**Current Head:** [[Gerard von Vaillant]]  
**Coat of Arms:** A red unicorn head on a black field, bordered in steel  
**Domain:** Military and civic defense

One of Carinthia's oldest noble families, House Vaillant is defined by martial excellence and selfless public service. It has produced some of the kingdom's finest knights, fortress commanders, and border protectors across generations. Though its political clout at the capital has waned as the [[The Light of Alophia|Church of Alophia]] and [[House Achaard]] consolidated power, Vaillant remains the cornerstone of Carinthian defense — a house that values duty over doctrine and service over ambition.

Many Vaillant soldiers still refuse the title "holy knight," considering it vainglorious.

## Structure and Ranks

House Vaillant operates through a martial hierarchy. Its knights lead town watches, guard remote roads, and man the walls of Carinthia and its outlying fortresses. Its members earn their standing through service, not sermon.

## History

House Vaillant's rise began during the early foundation of Carinthia under [[Saint Carine]], when its ancestor was among the first to swear loyalty to the fledgling faith and crown. The house became instrumental during the early [[Wyrmsfall War]], holding key fortresses and rallying scattered defenses during the continent's darkest hours.

Unlike more theologically aligned houses, Vaillant has always maintained a secular martial code, which has led to dignified distance from the Church's growing dominance — and at times, quiet friction.

## Leadership and Lineage

**[[Gerard von Vaillant]]** — Current head. A battle-hardened veteran and scholar of fortification doctrine who leads with quiet dignity. Holds no ecclesiastical rank, but commands respect from commoners and nobles alike.

**[[Matthias von Vaillant]]** — Eldest legitimate son. Stoic and fiercely disciplined; commands respect within the military elite.

**[[Beau von Vaillant]]** — Younger son. Spirited and less formal, known for a flamboyant demeanor and a fondness for leveraging his status socially.

**[[Bernard Snowtree]]** — Gerard's illegitimate son by a former steward's daughter. Not formally acknowledged, but trained as a knight under Gerard's quiet arrangement. Serves with honor in [[Snowfinch Rest]], embodying the house's ideals more purely than almost anyone who bears the name.

## Narrative Relevance

House Vaillant and its members — particularly Bernard — serve as grounded, humanistic counterweights to the Church's doctrinal rigidity. Their quiet friction with [[House Achaard]] and the ecclesiastical hierarchy runs as an undercurrent through Carinthian politics.
