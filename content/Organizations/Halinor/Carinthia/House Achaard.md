## Overview

**Founders:** [[Louis'roi Arch de Soleil]] (Archbishop Louis-roi Achaard)  
**Primary Location:** [[Heldenheim]], Carinthia  
**Current Head:** [[Aedelweg von Achaard]]  
**Coat of Arms:** A gold-bordered white shield bearing a crowned purple gryphon clutching a scepter  
**Domain:** Ecclesiastical authority and holy military power

House Achaard is Carinthia's most powerful and controversial noble family — a relatively young house that rose swiftly through divine alignment with the [[The Light of Alophia|Church of Alophia]]. Where other noble lines draw legitimacy from age or martial conquest, Achaard claims its supremacy through righteous destiny and unyielding doctrine.

Founded by [[Louis'roi Arch de Soleil]], a charismatic noble whose legacy transformed him into a near-mythic figure, House Achaard has since become synonymous with the fusion of crown and altar. No house more thoroughly binds itself to the Church — and none is more shielded by it.

## Structure and Ranks

House Achaard fills the ranks of the [[Knights Templar]], the [[The Inquisition|Inquisition]], and the [[The Dawnbound|Dawnbound]]. Its members are known for unquestioning loyalty to divine law. The current head, [[Aedelweg von Achaard]], serves simultaneously as Archbishop of Carinthia and head of the house — a rare convergence of spiritual and noble authority few dare to challenge.

## History

House Achaard's rise is inseparable from the legend of Archbishop Louis. Elevated during a time of great unrest, he led with divine fervor and strategic vision, transforming his family from noble upstarts into the sacred standard-bearers of Alophia's will.

The defining moment came when the great dragon [[Skadraxes]], once thought neutral, struck without warning — attacking Archbishop Louis and his Dawnbound retinue. Though the betrayal shattered hope for peace with the wyrms, it forged House Achaard's myth in fire. Louis led a holy retaliation, guided — so the Church claims — by visions from Alophia herself. He personally struck down Skadraxes, tearing out her eyes in a moment of apocalyptic fury. The relics of that victory were consecrated as divine artifacts, and the surviving knights were ennobled, forming the foundation of Carinthia's four High Houses.

Louis later vanished under miraculous and controversial circumstances. The Church declared his actions righteous vengeance and sanctified House Achaard as the living extension of divine law.

## Leadership and Lineage

**[[Aedelweg von Achaard]]** — Current head and Archbishop of Carinthia. A man of immense gravitas and chilling clarity, known for his sermons on unflinching obedience and intolerance for heresy in any form. He does not publicly acknowledge his children, and they do not name him as father — duty supplants blood in all things.

**[[Martin von Achaard]]** — Known as the Everbright Sword. A Dawnbound paladin and commander of holy precision. Embodies the martial rigor of House Achaard; executes orders without hesitation. Defined entirely by his faith and role, whatever his private bloodline may be.

**[[Sabine von Achaard]]** — Titled Wisdom's Shield. A defensive bastion of the Dawnbound and a paragon of sacred restraint. Wields her faith like a fortress. Bears the family's severity and pride in every word and action, though she has never publicly spoken of her lineage.

## Narrative Relevance

House Achaard is the institutional face of Carinthia's theocratic order. Its members are both its enforcers and its ideological champions. The house's mythology — built on a betrayal the Church has sanctified — sits at the heart of Carinthia's unresolved relationship with the dragons and with its own history.
