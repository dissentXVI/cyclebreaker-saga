## Overview

**Founders:** The surviving knights of [[Louis'roi Arch de Soleil]]'s retinue; reconstituted as an elite holy order in the aftermath of the [[Wyrmsfall War]]  
**Primary Location:** [[Cathedral of the Sacred Light]], [[Heldenheim]]  
**Current Leader(s):** [[Martin von Achaard]], the Everbright Sword (Captain)

The Dawnbound are Carinthia's most elite holy warriors — a selective order of champions bound by sacred oath to the Archbishop and to [[Alophia]] herself. They are the Church's sharpest instrument: deployed where standard templars are insufficient, where heresy has taken root in dangerous form, or where draconic threats exceed ordinary military response.

Each member of the Dawnbound is both a warrior and a figure of symbolic weight within Carinthian faith. Their names, titles, and deeds enter into the Church's living canon of holy exemplars.

## Member Profiles

#### [[Martin von Achaard]] — The Everbright Sword
The image of the perfect knight: tall, broad-shouldered, immaculate in bearing. Armor etched with divine light and the twelve-pointed star of Alophia; a white cloak with golden trim. Bears a shield emblazoned with a lily blooming a sword — purity in purpose. Golden-blonde hair, sky-blue eyes that carry expectation but rarely cruelty. Beneath his composure is a constant tension — as if striving to prove something he dares not name. Disciplined and unfailingly faithful to the Church. Serves as the captain and anchor of formation-based tactics, channeling divine judgement through sword and ward.

#### [[Sabine von Achaard]] — Wisdom's Shield
A vision of solemn nobility in gilded white. Armor polished to mirror-brightness; long blonde hair bound with a clasp engraved with the twelve-pointed star in battle. Carries a tower shield etched with a phoenix rising through vines, and a short sword forged for precision over power. Composed and serene, never soft. Believes in order above emotion. Fiercely protective of [[Diana von Everett]], her battlefield partner. Serves as the Dawnbound's arbiter — not because she is fair, but because she is final. Specializes in protection spells, positioning control, and retributive counterattacks.

#### [[Diana von Everett]] — Wisdom's Spear
Radiates precision and poise in white and gold-trimmed armor built for speed and flexibility. Wields twin longswords, mirror-forged and engraved with Alophia's balance — which she can lock together into a double-bladed staff. Her golden eyes reflect clarity rather than passion. Carries herself like a dancer on the edge of war. Her bond with [[Sabine von Achaard]] is nearly unbreakable; they fight as one. Emphasizes fluid transitions between dual-blade precision and sweeping staff form — each fight a sacred performance.

#### [[Viktor von Doome]] — Hammer of Faith
Towering and fortress-like in resplendent white and gold armor marked by the honest scars of war. Wields a massive warhammer with one hand; his shield bears a golden hammer and downward-pointing flame. Long black hair, typically tied back before battle. Silent and stern — when he speaks, his voice rumbles like distant thunder. A moral absolutist: there is right and there is wrong; gray is the color of compromise. Devout and protective by nature. Wields radiant auras and defensive blessings alongside devastating area-of-effect hammer strikes.

#### [[Offrec von Bellefeuille]] — The Thunder of Heaven
Heavy, radiant armor etched with gold like a storm carved into steel. A great two-handed sword across his back, its blade engraved with celestial runes that flicker during spoken prayers. Long dark hair worn loose, wild as the weather he channels. Charismatic, loud, and unapologetic — faith should be lived out loud. Close to [[Gisele von Courtemanche]] and [[Diana von Everett]]; considered kindred spirits. His laughter and singing fill cathedral hallways. Considered too brash by some but undeniably effective. Known for shock-charge tactics, sundering lines, and booming concussive strikes from channeled radiant energy.

#### [[Gisele von Courtemanche]] — Seeker of Heavenly Mysteries
Appears less like a knight and more like a saint made flesh. Flowing white and gold ceremonial robes etched with arcane geometries drawn from Alophian scripture. Hood often pulled up, half-shrouding his face. Long dark hair kept behind his shoulders; eyes glowing with quiet intensity. Always carries a massive golden-bound tome chained at the waist. Contemplative and soft-spoken; believes everything — from dreams to the shape of a crack in stone — carries meaning. Keeps secrets not maliciously but sacredly. Serves as the Dawnbound's spiritual compass: translating visions, deciphering divine signs, and keeping records. Channels divine energies to bolster allies and counter enemy spells through sacred geometry.

#### [[Nikolaus von Straxia]] — Caller of Falling Stars
Tall and commanding in layered white and gold mage robes embroidered with celestial patterns that shift like constellations. Carries a gilded staff topped with a sun-and-star sigil inlaid with a pulsing gemstone. Sharp features; eyes shine with a faint inner, astral light. His presence is still, like the silence between stars — when he speaks, the room tilts toward his voice. Detached but not cold; perceives the world through the lens of order and prediction. Deploys starfall magic, radiant beams, gravitational effects, and predictive spells. Signature technique: *Falling Judgement*, a formation of radiant meteors called down in geometric array.

#### [[Erika the Red]] — The Igniter
White and gold-trimmed plate scorched at the edges; radiant metal that glows faintly from within. A deep crimson sash — made from her old Inquisition cloak — belts her waist. Fiery red hair spills from beneath her hood. Golden-amber eyes that see where weakness will crack before it forms. Wields a curved dagger in each hand, conjured from radiant magic through sheer conviction. Fiercely passionate; lives on the edge between righteous fury and destruction. Believes hesitation is the sin before all others. Clashes with more measured members, but her loyalty to her comrades is absolute. Serves as the shock troop — first into the breach, last to retreat. Specializes in close-range, high-speed magical assaults.

#### [[Ralf von Oskar]] — Censor of Alophia's Grace
Dressed in ivory and gold robes; ceremonial plate polished but unembellished. At his hip swings a burning brass censer on a reinforced chain, emitting not just flame but sacred incense laced with divine magic. Handsome in a quietly understated way — dark tousled hair, warm brown eyes, a steady expression. Hands wrapped in white cloth, both to protect from burns and to honor his oath: to heal before he harms. Beneath his gentleness is a steel that comes from watching too many comrades fall and choosing to keep fighting anyway. Empathetic; acts as a trusted confidant to [[Martin von Achaard]] and [[Gisele von Courtemanche]]. Balances battlefield triage with area denial through radiant fire and sanctified smoke.

#### [[Emilia von Veshrec]] — Alophia's Reaper
Elegant plate over priestly vestments, white and gold tailored for mobility. Long black hair falling like a banner over her shoulders. A golden cloak pinned with a twelve-pointed star at the collar. Carries a ceremonial spear reinforced for battle — its haft reinforced with radiant steel, used and repaired many times over. Carries herself like a battlefield saint sculpted from iron: serene on the surface, burning beneath. Sees sin not as moral failure but as defiance of Alophia herself — and defiance must be answered. Executes divine orders without hesitation, though not without compassion; she believes excessive leniency is what allowed heresy to take root. Channels radiant energy through her spear in rapid, precise strikes. Signature technique: *Veshrec's Mirror*, a radiant ward that stores received damage and reflects it on her next strike.

#### [[Hubert von Corbenne]] — of the Flight of Knives
Lean and honed, with a shaved head and sharp elven features. Wears close-fitting white and gold combat robes built for mobility over ceremony. Carries a quiver of black-fletched arrows, throwing knives strapped across chest, wrists, and boots, and a darkwood, gold-laced longbow custom-carved to his frame. Never looks unprepared — or unready to vanish. Witty, irreverent, and razor-sharp; a stark contrast to most Dawnbound. Treats his faith with casual intimacy — not distant, not blasphemous, but familiar. Uses humor as a shield; beneath it is a calculating, intelligent mind. Specializes in rapid engagement and disengagement, flanking, marking targets, and hitting weak spots with divine accuracy.

#### [[Amelie von Corbenne]] — The Unerring
Cropped to a snowy buzz cut. Fitted white leathers traced with gold — functional, unadorned, flawlessly maintained. A sleek black-and-gold bow, reinforced for precision. Her quiver is arranged with obsessive care. Gray eyes steady and expressionless. Where her brother [[Hubert von Corbenne|Hubert]] is charm and speed, she is focus and finality. Deadly serious and just — deadly. Trains more than she rests; speaks less than she knows. Deep love and loyalty to her brother, rarely expressed. Holds intense respect for [[Sabine von Achaard]] and [[Diana von Everett]], often following their tactical lead without hesitation. Known for splitting arrows mid-flight and piercing enchanted defenses.

## History

The Dawnbound as an order trace their origin to the knights who served [[Louis'roi Arch de Soleil]] during the confrontation with [[Skadraxes]]. Only four survived that battle; their bloodlines were ennobled into the [[High Houses of Carinthia]]. The order was later reconstituted by the Church as a standing elite unit, drawing from the High Houses and the most exceptional consecrated warriors of each generation.

Their numbers have always been small and their selection deliberate. The Wyrmsfall War has taken a toll — their ranks are ever-dwindling, each fallen member a loss the Church mourns publicly and guards its details about privately.

## Narrative Relevance

The Dawnbound are the player characters' primary institutional allies within Carinthia. Their internal dynamics — faith, doubt, loyalty, and the weight of an order built on a myth — run as a through-line across the Carinthian arc. Several members carry secrets, conflicting loyalties, or private doubts that surface over the course of the campaign.
