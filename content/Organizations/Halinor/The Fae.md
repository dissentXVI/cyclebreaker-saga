>“All that grows must listen to the forest’s breath — even those who think themselves above its song.”

## Overview

**Founders:**  Unknown; the Fae Concord formed in the earliest ages of [[the Fauconwood]], said to have arisen when beasts first learned the words of the trees.
**Primary Location:**  [[The Fauconwood]], centered around the hidden Concord Glade, where the Courts gather in secret.
**Current Leader(s):**  [[Baron Lotor]], Lord of Gloom and Glitter, Arbiter of the Concord

## Structure and Ranks

[[The fae]] are the sentient beasts of the natural world, bound by instinct, cunning, and ancient pacts rather than law or lineage. Their society is organized into Courts, each representing a great branch of life within the forest. Together, they form the Concord of the Courts, a council through which nature itself speaks.

When disputes arise or decisions must be made, each Court sends two representatives to the Concord. Should votes reach stalemate, [[Baron Lotor]] casts the deciding judgment.

### **The Court of Gloom and Glitter**

_“We thrive where light forgets to look.”_

- **Members:** Raccoons, squirrels, rabbits, mice, rats, opossums, porcupines, beavers, spiders, insects
- **Role:** Scavengers, builders, artisans, spies
- **Represented by**: [[Baron Lotor]] (raccoon, tie-breaker and Concord arbiter) and The Prophet of Earth (ancient groundhog seer)
### **The Skyflock Court**

_“We are the sky’s reflection upon the leaves.”_

- **Members:** Songbirds, owls, raptors, waterfowl
- **Role:** Diplomats, messengers, strategists, sentinels    
- **Represented by:** **Minister Whiteplume** (dove diplomat) and **Chancellor Tyton** (owl strategist and tactician)
### **The Court of Croak and Scale**

_“The water remembers what the land forgets.”_

- **Members:** Frogs, lizards, salamanders, turtles
- **Role:** Mystics, healers, keepers of rivers and the Reflecion Lagunes
- **Represented by:** **The Mud-Speaker** (frog sage who speaks for rivers and ponds) and **Elder Mossback** (ancient turtle and lorekeeper)
### **The Greatbeast Court**

_“Law is the fang that guards the forest’s heart.”_

- **Members:** Bears, wolves, boars, elk, lynx, foxes
- **Role:** Guardians, judges, and enforcers of the Concord’s law    
- **Represented by:** **High Fang Greymane** (wolf patriarch, enforcer of fae law) and **Matron Orsa** (bear-mother, keeper of winter rites and hibernation lore)
### **The Wild Army (The Briarhost)**

_“When balance breaks, the forest marches.”_

- **Members:** Scouts (opossums), heavy cavalry (bears), vanguard (badgers, boars), infantry (wolves)
- **Role:** Defensive host of all fae Courts, summoned only when the forest itself is threatened
- **Represented by:** **Commander Shrike** (ruthless bird-of-prey general, commander of the Briarhost)

## History

The origins of the fae reach back to the dawn of [[the Fauconwood]]. Some say they were once mere beasts granted thought by the goddess [[Eirune]], to ensure the forest would always have a voice. Others claim they are older still — fragments of [[Aspiria]]’s own [[pneuma]], given shape and instinct.

The Concord of Courts formed to maintain harmony among predator and prey, claw and feather, river and root. Their earliest recorded act was the establishment of the Green Accords, laws that forbade senseless bloodshed and declared the forest itself sacred ground.

Over the centuries, the Concord grew divided in philosophy. The Skyflock sought diplomacy with elves, the Greatbeasts enforced ancient order, the Croak and Scale whispered of spiritual balance, and the Gloom and Glitter pursued survival through cunning and adaptation.

Recently, the Courts have grown fearful of Sharl, whose draining of pneuma threatens the lifeblood of the Fauconwood. In desperation, a sect within the Concord summoned [[the King of the Wood]], an ancient Paragon in the form of a colossal elk with antlers like living trees. Whether his arrival will save the forest or doom it remains unknown.

## Narrative Relevance

The Fae serve as [[Aqualonde]]’s unseen counterpart — a reflection of elven culture in wild form. Where the elves govern through salons and guilds, the fae rule through instinct and balance. Their Concord influences the health of [[the Fauconwood]] itself: when divided, the forest sickens.

The summoning of [[the King of the Wood]] marks their first united act in centuries, but it may also be their last. [[Baron Lotor]]’s precarious leadership, the Prophet’s visions, and Commander Shrike’s hunger for war place the fae — and the forest — on the brink of catastrophe.
