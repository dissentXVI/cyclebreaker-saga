>"The ground is more than dirt - it remembers what we forget."

## Overview

**Founders:** The first Circle was formed in the early Fourth Radiant Age by druids and rootcallers who refused to bind [[Aqualonde]]'s growth to mortal timelines.  
**Primary Location:** [[The Rootwell]], an underground grove beneath Aqualonde's [[The Elder Tree|Elder Tree]]  
**Current Leader(s):** [[Brynna Hollowroot]]  

## Structure and Ranks

- **Hollow Seer** – Visionary and conduit to the deepest spirits of the earth; a role that is recognized rather than appointed.
- **Rootcallers** – Senior members who shape and interpret the Circle's will, often serving as advisors to Aqualonde's Council of Guilds.
- **Tenders** – Full members entrusted with tending sacred groves, restoring blighted land, and mediating disputes between mortals and nature spirits.
- **Saplings** – Apprentices and initiates learning to attune themselves to the breathflow of the land.
- **Soilbound** – Honorary members or allied hermits who share the Circle's values but live apart from its hierarchy.

## History

The Earthspeakers began as a loose fellowship of druids, herbalists, and earth-channelers who sought to temper Aqualonde's expansion with the needs of the forest itself. Their earliest challenge came during the vineblight plague, when the Circle's intervention and warnings kept the Elder Tree from collapse—cementing their role as protectors of the city's living foundation. While they have always been wary of over-formalizing, the Circle's influence has grown quietly, rooted in whispered visions and ancient rituals that often baffle outsiders.

## Narrative Relevance

The Earthspeakers stand at the spiritual heart of Aqualonde's environmental balance. [[Brynna Hollowroot]]'s increasingly urgent warnings about the [[Chilmire Bog]] suggest the Circle will soon be drawn into direct conflict with forces that threaten not just the city, but the forest's memory itself.
