>"Deliberation is our shield; concord, our crown."

## Overview

**Founders:**  Formalized by [[Aqualonde]]’s core guilds in the late Fourth Radiant Age, shaped by Carlotte Cordray’s salon tradition and the early Loom and Forge circles.
**Primary Location:**  [[Eirune’s Gap]], the open-canopy council ring in [[Aqualonde]]
**Current Leader(s):**  No permanent head; sessions are convened by a rotating **Convener of the Gap**. (Currently: [[Maylin Lurel]], by consent of the seats.)

## Structure and Ranks

- **Seven Seats (voting):** [[The Sunweavers Guild|The Sunweavers]], [[The Starcaller Salon|The Starcallers]], [[The Windriders Guild|The Windriders]], [[The Earthspeakers Circle|The Earthspeakers]], [[The Moonblade Forge|The Moonblades]], [[The Grovebound Circle|The Grovebound]] , [[The Tree Shapers Guild|The Tree Shapers]].
- **Convener of the Gap (rotating):** Chairs debate, sets the docket, and mediates procedure; has no extra vote.
- **Scribes of Record:** Appointed by [[The Sunweavers Guild|the Sunweavers]] to maintain impartial minutes, seals, and textile tallies of decisions.
- **Advisors-at-Need:** Petitioners invited for single issues (e.g., Brambleblade captains, scholars of [[Shurevaire]], [[Riverwalk]] merchants).
- **Decision Rule:** Consensus is sought first. If unresolved, a binding measure requires **five of seven** votes with quorum of five seats present.
## History

What began as open-air debates among scholars and artisans in [[Riverwalk]] hardened into necessity during Carinthian pressure in [[the Age of Arch de Soleil]]. The salons’ ad hoc compacts evolved into a standing council that could coordinate defense, diplomacy, and rites without a crown. Seats were later codified as guild authority matured—first the [[The Sunweavers Guild|Sunweavers]], [[The Starcaller Salon|Starcallers]], [[The Windriders Guild|Windriders]], [[The Moonblade Forge|Moonblades]], and [[The Earthspeakers Circle|Earthspeakers]]; then [[The Grovebound Circle|the Grovebound]] and [[The Tree Shapers Guild|The Tree Shapers]] as guardians of balance and living architecture. The Council’s authority was reaffirmed after [[the vineblight]] crisis and again following [[The Trembling Foundations]], when a unified voice proved the difference between panic and policy.
## Narrative Relevance

With the fae silent, routes threatened, and rumors of decay from [[Chilmire]] to [[Serinvale]], the Council meets in increasingly frequent closed sessions. The balance between outreach and caution is razor-thin. Whether the Council can hold consensus—and act swiftly when needed—will shape [[Aqualonde]]’s stance in the conflicts to come.
