>"We right the world by writing its stories."

## Overview

**Founders:** Carlotte Cordray and a circle of poet-orators at the end of the Fourth Radiant Age
**Primary Location:** [[Salon Stellaire]] ([[Riverwalk]], [[Aqualonde]])
**Current Leader(s):** [[Renzo Altire]] – Director of the Starcallers

## Structure and Ranks

- **Director** – Artistic and strategic head of the salon.
- **Constellars** – Senior fellows who set season themes, civic campaigns, and narrative strategy.
- **Dramaturges** – Lead playwrights, poets, and debaters who craft policy-shaping works.
- **Selenographers** – Astral-lore archivists and researchers who ground performances in myth and cosmology.
- **Maskwrights & Stagewrights** – Illusionists, designers, and builders of sets, masks, and symbolic props.
- **Chorus** – Principal performers and orators who carry the salon's voice to the public.
- **Novae** – Apprentices rotated through research, staging, and performance training.

## History

Born as a countercurrent to dogma and conquest, the Starcallers coalesced around Carlotte Cordray's salons, where art, satire, and sky-lore were wielded as instruments of reform. Their clandestine productions during [[The Age of Arch De Soleil, the Panoptic Will of God]] helped galvanize resistance, and Cordray's legacy endures in civic tradition and memorial. Over centuries the salon formalized into a guild-like institution, blending theater, scholarship, and public discourse to steer [[Aqualonde]]'s conscience without holding office.

## Narrative Relevance

Under [[Renzo Altire]], the Starcallers are staging The Twelvefold Reprise, a masked critique of divine apathy meant to provoke debate across guild lines. The salon's narratives increasingly intersect with current tensions—the fae's silence, external pressures on [[Aqualonde]], and the emergence of [[Aspiria's Chosen]] as living myths—positioning the Starcallers to sway public will before formal policy takes shape.
