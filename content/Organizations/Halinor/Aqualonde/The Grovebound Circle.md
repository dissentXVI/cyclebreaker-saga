>"Between bloom and wither, we keep the accord."

## Overview

**Founders:** Elder druids and treaty-keepers who formalized Aqualonde's fae compacts in the early Fifth Radiant Age
**Primary Location:** [[The Petal Hollow]] (near [[Eirune’s Gap]], [[Aqualonde]])
**Current Leader(s):** [[Maylin Lurel]] – Seedspeaker of [[Aqualonde]]

## Structure and Ranks

- **Seedspeaker** – Spiritual head and final arbiter of rites and accords.
- **Veilwalkers** – Envoys who negotiate with fae courts and maintain planar boundaries.
- **Accord-Keepers** – Lorewardens who preserve pact law, sigils, and seasonal obligations.
- **Bloomwardens** – Ritual druids who conduct blessings, funerary passages, and restorative workings.
- **Greenhands** – Full members charged with tending sacred sites and assisting Veilwalkers.
- **Saplings** – Acolytes undergoing years of observation, silence practice, and seasonal study.

## History

The Circle coalesced when early Aqualondians recognized that survival required more than walls—it required harmony with the Fauconwood and respect for the fae who dwell within it. From those first oaths emerged the Olive Branch Accord, a set of seasonal compacts renewed in song and silence. During the vineblight crisis, Grovebound rites stabilized wild pneuma around [[The Elder Tree]], and their mediation helped prevent reprisals after missteps with curious scholars and overbold shapers. Over centuries, the Circle has remained deliberately apolitical, stepping into civic matters only when balance itself is threatened.

## Narrative Relevance

With the fae now ominously silent and signs of decay spreading from [[Chilmire]] to [[Serinvale]], the Grovebound stand at a crossroads: either discover why the accords have gone unanswered, or prepare [[Aqualonde]] for a world without them. Under [[Maylin Lurel]], Veilwalkers probe [[The Petal Hollow]]'s springs for fractures in breathflow, while Accord-Keepers scour pact-songs for omissions that might explain the silence.
