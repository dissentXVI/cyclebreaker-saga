## Overview

**Founders:** [[Sharl van der Cleev]]
**Primary Location:** Formerly operating across Halinor; final base established in [[the Fauconwood]]
**Current Leader(s):** None — [[Sharl van der Cleev]] is dead. The organization is dissolved.

## Structure and Ranks

The Crimson Cleavers operated as a disciplined mercenary regiment under the absolute authority of [[Sharl van der Cleev]]. There was no formal rank structure beyond Sharl himself — but in practice, the organization functioned through a clearly understood inner hierarchy of lieutenants, each with defined operational roles.

**Commander**
[[Sharl van der Cleev]] held total authority. He set contracts, assigned roles, managed loyalty through carefully cultivated dependency, and personally handled any matter too delicate or dangerous for his lieutenants. He was the organization's singular gravitational center. Without him, there is no organization.

**Personal Retinue**
[[Ariel]] and [[Uriah]], the Twin Fangs, served as Sharl's de facto bodyguards, interrogators, and ambassadors. Ariel is dead. Uriah escaped and is unaccounted for.

**Lieutenants**
- **[[Pierre Poinsard]]** – Bannerman. Commanded armored infantry. Deceased.
- **[[Megrind the Monsoon]]** – Arcane Stormwright. Deceased.
- **[[Dyna Dee]]** – Chief Engineer and Demolitions. Escaped. Whereabouts unknown.
- **[[Chauni Stag-Eye]]** – Intelligence and Omens. Deceased.
- **[[Molko Beambearer]]** – The Silent Hammer. Released by [[Aspiria's Chosen]]. Whereabouts unknown.

## History

The Crimson Cleavers began as a ragged band of sell-swords gathered around Sharl van der Cleev — outcasts, exiles, and the discarded, each collected with the same cold precision Sharl applied to everything. In his early years, he operated small contracts, building a reputation for results without sentiment. The transformation from gang to regiment came after his three-day disappearance into the Fauconwood, from which he returned changed — colder, more certain, and with [[Chauni Stag-Eye]] at his side.

Under his direction, the Cleavers grew rapidly into one of the most feared private armies in [[Halinor]], taking contracts across the continent: strike-breaking, political enforcement, targeted wet work, and more. They did not take ideological work. They took paying work.

Quiss de Barghest — known now as [[Frog Gulgalla]] — was once among the Cleavers' most trusted operatives: lead scout, assassin, and Sharl's favored agent. After she left, Sharl learned she had borne his daughter. In his final years, he scaled back the Cleavers, established a home in the Fauconwood, and devoted himself to finding Quiss and the child. That search ended with his death.

## Dissolution

The Crimson Cleavers ceased to exist as a unified force following a direct confrontation with Aspiria's Chosen at Sharl's Fauconwood stronghold. [[Sharl van der Cleev]], [[Ariel]], [[Pierre Poinsard]], [[Megrind the Monsoon]], and [[Chauni Stag-Eye]] were killed in the battle. [[Uriah]] and [[Dyna Dee]] escaped. [[Molko Beambearer]] was released.

The organization had no succession plan because it was never conceived as something that could exist without him. It didn't.

## Narrative Relevance

The Crimson Cleavers are gone. Their surviving members — [[Uriah]], [[Dyna Dee]], and [[Molko Beambearer]] — are unaccounted for, each now unmoored from the only structure that defined them. Whether their paths cross again, and what they become without the Cleavers to give them context, remains to be seen.

The capture of [[Ashera]] — one of their final operations — remains consequential. She was saved by Aspiria's Chosen, and what she knows about [[Mireya Lunacrest|Mireya]]'s origins did not die with the people who took her.
