## Overview

The Archorans are a secretive collective of individuals whose origins, true numbers, and ultimate purpose remain unknown.

Their existence is not widely known. Most people who have encountered one have not lived to recognize what they saw, or did not understand it at the time. What has surfaced in recent history is the involvement of at least one Archoran — [[Kyrichos]] — in events that helped precipitate the [[The Trembling Foundations|Trembling Foundations]] of 4009 ApN. Whether the others act in concert with him, in opposition, or according to some shared design that simply looks different from different angles, is unknown.
## Structure and Ranks

Unknown.

Two distinct modes of dress have been observed among those believed to be Archorans: white robes and black robes. Whether this reflects rank, role, allegiance, or something else entirely has not been found out. [[Kyrichos]] has been seen in white. [[Allythoia]] and [[Haxidemos]] have been seen in black. The significance of this difference, if any, is not yet understood.

## History

Unknown in full. Archoran involvement has been identified — in retrospect, and only partially — in several major historical events, though the extent of that involvement and what drove it remains a matter of inference rather than record.

Their oldest confirmed act known to current scholarship is [[Allythoia]]'s approach to the Navarresean scholar [[Thalaniel Shamashur]] in the final years of the [[Navarresean See|Navarresean Empire]] — an intervention that ultimately contributed to the catastrophe that ended it. More recently, [[Kyrichos]] has emerged as a recurring hand behind Paragon summonings across [[Halinor]]: he guided the Vociran [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Cassius gen Amata]] toward the ritual beneath [[Sticaea Lake]] that triggered the [[The Trembling Foundations|Trembling Foundations]]; instructed goblin tribes in the conditions necessary to summon [[Dagozog, the Galvanic Fury|Dagozog]]; and taught the lizardfolk of the [[Chilmire Bog]] the rites that called forth [[Thessakor, the Rot that Hungers|Thessakor]].

What connects these acts, whether they serve a common goal, and how long the Archorans have been moving through history, is not known.

## Narrative Relevance

[[Aspiria's Chosen]] are aware that an Archoran — [[Kyrichos]] — was instrumental in the events at Sticaea Lake. They have also encountered or have knowledge of [[Allythoia]] and [[Haxidemos]], though the full scope of any of their activities remains opaque.

The Archorans have taken notice of the Chosen. Whether that attention is predatory, curious, or something else entirely has not been made known.

> [!gm] GM Only
> The Archorans are surviving [[Predecessors]] — beings from the age before the world was sundered. Their collective purpose is the restoration of the unsundered world, though they disagree profoundly on method. [[Kyrichos]] believes the current world must be ended to force a true restoration and acts as the group's most dangerous operative, seeding Paragon summonings and manipulating broken individuals toward catastrophic ends — including teaching goblin tribes how to summon [[Dagozog, the Galvanic Fury|Dagozog]] and instructing the lizardfolk of the [[Chilmire Bog]] in the rites for [[Thessakor, the Rot that Hungers|Thessakor]]. [[Allythoia]] engineers collapse through subtler means — identifying desires and offering them in their most corrosive form. [[Psyronos]] is also among their number, though his identity as [[Meonus sep Magnus]] and founding architect of [[Vociterra]] is not yet known to the players.
>
> [[Diatitos]] is the most enigmatic — his title, "Emissary of Divine Purpose," suggests a role distinct from the others, possibly one of observation or arbitration rather than direct action.
