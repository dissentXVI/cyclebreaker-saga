The Battle at Sticaea Lake was a desperate, multi-faction clash fought in 4009 ApN on the shores of [[Sticaea Lake]] in central [[Halinor]], precipitated by the Vociran [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Cassius gen Amata]]'s forbidden attempt to resurrect the long-dead dragon [[Krisnarax]]. It is not a battle remembered for tactical elegance or political ambition. It is remembered as the day a man consumed by grief nearly ended the world — and the day an old scholar chose not to let him.

In the months following his ruinous defeat at [[World Encyclopedia/Historical Events/Conflicts/The Skirmish of Dagger Canyon|Dagger Canyon]] and the death of his sister [[Megaera cen Magellus]], Cassius had been approached by the Archoran [[Kyrichos]], who fed his rage and his despair in equal measure. Kyrichos offered him something Vociran engineering could not: not conquest, not victory — but devastation. He told Cassius of [[Krisnarax]], the great dragon slain in the final century of the [[Navarresean See]], and of the heretical rites by which the beast might be restored. What Kyrichos wanted from the chaos that would follow, Cassius did not ask. He had stopped caring about the answer.

Cassius led a clandestine column through the [[Amrakebir Mountains]] and into the cave systems beneath Sticaea Lake's western shores — moving without the knowledge or authorization of [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Gallus gen Quietus]] or [[Lucius sep Magnus|the Emperor]]. His men did not return from those caves as soldiers. They were sacrificed, one by one, in the ritual Kyrichos had given him. The rite tore open something beneath the lakebed that had not been opened in a thousand years.

[[Gallus gen Quietus]], who had been tracking Cassius's unauthorized movement since the Amrakebir approach, arrived at the lake with a recovery force. What he found was not a disgraced officer to be retrieved but a catastrophe already in motion. Landslides and earthquakes radiated outward from the ritual site as Krisnarax's shade began to reform, and skirmishing broke out along the lake's northern shore as Gallus's men pushed toward the cave entrance. Aqualondian and Tarwa'kin forces, drawn by the tremors and by intelligence that something was badly wrong in the central highlands, arrived to the east and south — too late to stop what had been started, but in time to witness what came next.

[[Velethoui Arsenault|Archmage Velethoui Arsenault]] of [[Shurevaire]] had traveled ahead of any national force. He had known something was coming — he and his students had been watching the escalating instability across [[Halinor]] for months, and he had not waited for institutional permission to act. He reached the lake as the ritual neared completion and Krisnarax's shade began to claw free of whatever cage two thousand years of death had made for it. The earthquakes had already cracked the lakebed. The water was retreating. The breathstone tower of the old [[Navarresean See]] — [[the Cosmic Pillar]], sunk since the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]] and unknown to living memory — was beginning to rise from the exposed basin.

Velethoui did not hesitate. He drove his staff into the earth and cast the binding that would cost him everything — a pneumeric working of extraordinary scale and precision, built not to destroy Krisnarax's shade but to contain it, to drive it down into the new chasm the earthquakes had carved and seal it there. The working succeeded. The shade did not fully manifest. The Trembling Foundations did not consume [[Halinor]].

All that remained of Archmage Velethoui was the shattered head of his staff, Duisaratte, later recovered from the crater where he had stood.

The lake did not survive. The rift carved by the ritual and the earthquakes drained it entirely over the following days, leaving behind what is now called the [[Sticaean Abyss]]. The economies of [[Laconia]], [[Crowtown]], and every settlement on the former lakeshore collapsed overnight. And rising from the drained basin, visible for the first time in two thousand years, was the breathstone tower the people of Laconia would come to call the [[the Cosmic Pillar|Watcher of the Lake]] — ignorant of its true name or what it once housed.

What became of [[Cassius gen Amata]] in the immediate aftermath is a matter of conflicting report. Gallus recovered what remained of his disgraced colleague. The official Vociran account does not detail the specifics, and [[Vociterra]] has never publicly acknowledged the role its officer played in the destruction of one of [[Halinor]]'s great geographical landmarks.
