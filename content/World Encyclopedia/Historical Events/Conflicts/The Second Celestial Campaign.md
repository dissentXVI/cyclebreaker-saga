The Second Celestial Campaign was the [[Navarresean See]]'s prolonged military campaign against the dwarven tribes of eastern [[Halinor]], launched by Emperor [[Isandor Vael'Dorath]] following his victories in the [[The First Celestial Campaign|First Celestial Campaign]] and spanning approximately 1237 to 1572 ApN. Where the First Campaign had been a war against dragons, the Second was a war of territorial expansion — a bid to bring the entirety of Halinor under Navarresean dominion and eliminate the last significant opposition to empire-wide control. The dwarves called it the War of Enchanted Anvils.

The dwarves proved a far more costly enemy than anticipated. Entrenched in mountain strongholds, organized into tribal confederacies that resisted both diplomacy and direct assault, and fighting with the particular ferocity of people defending their ancestral territory, the dwarven tribes held out for over three centuries against the most powerful military force in Halinor's history. Their most devastating weapon was the [[Dreadnought]] — colossal necromantic war suits powered by the souls of fallen warriors, capable of shrugging off Navarresean spellwork and breaking elven legions beneath their weight.

The campaign's extended and punishing nature forced decisions the empire would not otherwise have made. When conventional labor could no longer sustain the war effort, Vael'Dorath commissioned the [[Infernal Hybridization Initiative]] — a research program whose full scope and consequences have never been publicly acknowledged.

The Second Celestial Campaign never achieved a decisive conclusion. Under Emperor [[Phaendra Yin'Balar]], who inherited it and drove it to its most aggressive phase, the war ground forward until Yin'Balar himself died on the battlefield — undone by a dwarven feint he mistook for a retreat. His successor [[Thallan Lael'Nathor]] effectively ended offensive operations by pivoting the empire's focus inward. The dwarven tribes were never fully subdued and eventually, in the aftermath of the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]], retreated to the mountains and rebuilt as [[Tarwa'kem]].

The campaign's legacy is one of the most consequential and least examined chapters of Navarresean history — not for its military outcome, which was ultimately inconclusive, but for what the empire chose to do in order to sustain it.
