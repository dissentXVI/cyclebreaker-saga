The Fall of Ilsong was the [[the Vociran Empire|Vociran Empire]]'s conquest of the island nation of [[Ilsong]] in 4006 ApN, executed by [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Caedus seq Magnus]] and the mechanized 6th Fleet under the cover of diplomatic fiction. It stands as one of the Empire's most coldly effective campaigns — not because the resistance was weak, but because it was never given the chance to form.

[[Vociterra]] had long regarded [[Ilsong]] as a strategic prize: an island nation off the western coast of [[Eldamar]] with disciplined military traditions, refined trade networks, and a coastal position that could anchor imperial projection eastward. The avenue they chose was not force, not initially. Caedus arrived bearing terms — a treaty negotiation, framed as a mutual recognition agreement, conducted with the ceremonial courtesies that Ilsong's warrior culture demanded be honored. Shanyu [[Yurei Kioshi]] was a careful man and had spent years watching the horizon, but the deception was thorough. The 6th Fleet did not look like an invasion force until it was already inside Ilsong's waters.

The outer coastal defenses held for three days. The river fell on the fourth. Ilsong's military excellence was real, but its doctrine had not been built for mechanized naval assault, and the sheer scale of vultek armament negated advantages that generations of training had made second nature. The Shield of the Rising Moon — Ilsong's elite royal guard, commanded by Tengushin [[Tsuga Mirai]] — fought for every district of [[Ilsong|Shoran]], but the math was never in their favor. Yurei made the decision his legacy would never resolve: he surrendered the capital rather than see it burned district by district around his people.

The Vocirans accepted the surrender with the practiced grace of an empire that had done this before. They installed Yurei as a nominal deputy administrator — a local face behind which the occupation could present itself as orderly transition rather than conquest. Caedus held the title of [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Imperius]] over the occupied territory. The royal court was formally dismantled. High-ranking officials who had commanded the defense were either executed publicly or subjected to disgrace proceedings designed to strip them of any standing they might have retained among the people. Yurei wore the Vociran courtesies and attended their ceremonies. He planned in the hours between.

He waited for what he believed was the right moment — intelligence reaching him that Vociterra was overextended, its resources strained across too many fronts. He moved openly. The Ilsong Liberation Front rose. For three days, the Kioshi banner flew again over Shoran and the palace was briefly reclaimed. The fracture in Vociterra closed faster than Yurei had predicted. The reinforcements that arrived did not negotiate. In the final hours, as the palace fell room by room, Yurei sent his son [[Amano Kioshi|Amano]] out through the eastern passage alongside Tengushin [[Tsuga Mirai]] and a trusted retainer, with orders to flee and survive. The retainer's body was later recovered in the streets below the cliffsides. Amano and Tsuga are recorded as killed during the flight — lost to the chaos of the city's fall, their remains never confirmed.

Yurie stayed behind and faced Caedus's vanguard alone in the throne room of Shoran, in full regalia, with Ilsong's ancestral blade drawn. He was not defeated quickly. That, too, is known.

What followed in the years after was a second attempt. The Ilsong Liberation Front re-formed under a leader known only as Shinsei the Gray, rallying exiles, scholars, and warrior-priests from beyond Ilsong's borders. It was bold and, for a time, genuinely dangerous to the occupation. It was betrayed. The Siege of Shuvalan Temple ended in slaughter — hundreds died, the Front collapsed, and Shinsei the Gray was never identified. By 4010 ApN, [[Kaihana Uritami]] had been installed as Imperial Viceroy, formalizing the permanent character of the occupation.

Within Vociterra, the Fall of Ilsong is presented as a bloodless stabilization — a wayward nation brought into order. Outside its borders, those who knew what Ilsong was understand that what ended in 4006 ApN was not merely a government. It was a way of life that had survived every storm the sea could make, and fell to one that came wearing a treaty.

> The Shanyu line is officially declared extinct by the Vociran Provisional Authority. It is not. [[Amano Kioshi]] — Yurei's son, believed killed during the fall — survived. He was Shinsei the Gray. He is now somewhere in the west, in the tribal steppes of [[Tatarr'kha]], living under a name that means nothing. He is one of the Chosen's closest points of contact with Ilsong's unfinished story, though he does not yet know it.
>
> [[Tsuga Mirai]] also survived. He now wanders in grief and drink — a former Tengushin who outlived his purpose and cannot find a new one.
