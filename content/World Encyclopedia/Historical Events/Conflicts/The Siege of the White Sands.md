The Siege of the White Sands was the [[the Vociran Empire|Vociran Empire]]'s invasion and conquest of [[D'yadya]], launched in 3995 ApN under [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Gallus gen Quietus]] with [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Tribunis]] [[Cassius gen Amata]] as his second. It remains the most decisive Vociran military victory of the modern era, and the event that formally ended D'yadya's existence as a sovereign state.

The campaign was swift and surgical. Exploiting long-standing internal divisions within D'yadyan politics and a prolonged drought that had weakened both the population and its defenses, Gallus moved with characteristic precision. Rather than a grinding siege, the Vocirans struck at multiple strongholds simultaneously, severing supply lines and communications before D'yadyan forces could consolidate. The capital fell in eleven days. Its gates, according to survivors, were opened from within — the identity of the betrayer has never been confirmed.

Gallus returned to [[Patria Noster]] in triumph, leaving the newly promoted [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Legatus]] [[Cassius gen Amata|Cassius]] behind as [[Vociran Military Ranks & Terms|Imperius]] of the occupied territory. The conquest marked the beginning of a brutal occupation that drove thousands of D'yadyans from their homeland, many of whom eventually took refuge in [[Tarwa'kem]].

The Siege of the White Sands is celebrated within [[Vociterra]] as a masterwork of imperial strategy. Outside its borders, it is remembered as the day a city that had survived centuries of drought, civil war, and hardship fell in under two weeks to an empire that had never once been invited.
