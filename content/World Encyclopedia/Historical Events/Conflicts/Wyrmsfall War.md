The Wyrmsfall War is the centuries-long conflict between [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]] and the dragon brood of [[Wodirethrax]], ongoing since 2854 ApN. It is the defining struggle of Carinthian history — the crucible in which its institutions were forged, its [[High Houses]] were born, and its faith was tested to the edge of breaking. It has not ended.

According to [[The Light of Alophia]] and the histories taught in every Carinthian school and cathedral, the war began when [[Wodirethrax|Wodr]] attacked without provocation. The dragons, despite the peace secured by [[Saint Iseult]] centuries prior, turned on their Carinthian allies without warning or just cause. [[Archbishop Renard Louis]] and the [[The Dawnbound|Dawnbound]] met the assault with desperate valor, fighting to defend the city and the faithful at catastrophic cost. The battle claimed all but four of the Dawnbound's number. Those four survivors — [[Ser Thibault Achaard]] and three others — were ennobled for their sacrifice, their bloodlines becoming the [[High Houses]] of [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]: Vaillant, Achaard, Courtemanche, and Bellefeuille.

Wodr, driven off but not destroyed, retreated north. Wodr rallied the remaining dragon brood and has waged unending war against Carinthia ever since, his hatred a force as enduring as winter.

In the generations since, the Wyrmsfall War has ground slowly but relentlessly against Carinthian territory. What was once a nation of many cities has been reduced to [[Heldenheim]] alone — its last city, perched in the alpine peaks, battered but unbroken. The war has shaped every aspect of Carinthian life: its militarized faith, its inward suspicion, its reverence for the Dawnbound, and its willingness to sacrifice individual liberty for collective survival.

The war shows no sign of ending. Wodr still lives. And within Heldenheim's walls, a heretic named [[Renai Aymere]] whispers that the Church's account of how it began is not the whole truth.

> [!gm] GM Only
> The Church's account is a lie. [[Archbishop Renard Louis]] ordered the betrayal — [[Skadraxes]], Wodr's sister, was lured into a trap, killed, and her eyes torn from her skull to fuel Louis's apotheosis as the [[Paragons|Paragon]] [[Arch De Soleil]]. Wodr did not attack without cause. He attacked in grief and fury after witnessing his sister's murder. The four [[High Houses]] descend from men who participated in that betrayal and were rewarded with nobility for their silence. [[Renai Aymere]] is closer to the truth than the Church will ever admit.
