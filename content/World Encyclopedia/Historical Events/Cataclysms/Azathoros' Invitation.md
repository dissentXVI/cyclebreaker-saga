# The Fourth Cataclysm
## Origin
Azathoros' Invitation swept across [[Halinor]] in 2203 ApN, during the fragile recovery following the Tempest of Madness. A surge of chaos pneuma destabilized the bonds of community and governance that survivors had painstakingly rebuilt, turning neighbor against neighbor and fracturing families and institutions from within. Many scholars draw a direct line from the lingering psychological damage of the Tempest of Madness to the ease with which this cataclysm took hold — arguing that the madness of the third cataclysm had already loosened the world's resistance. [[The Light of Alophia]], however, holds a different view entirely: that the demon Azathoros stirred in his slumber, and that the resonance of his dreaming bled through the world's veil and into mortal minds.
## Overview
Unlike the floods or the famine, Azathoros' Invitation left no visible scar on the land. Its devastation was social. Civil war erupted across most of [[Halinor]], and even nations that avoided open conflict found themselves consumed by lawlessness, banditry, and a breakdown of civic trust that would persist long after the chaos pneuma dispersed. Theological institutions faltered, with many churches experiencing crises of faith and internal schism.

[[Heldenheim\|Carinthia]] proved a notable exception — its rigid theocratic structure and military discipline insulated it from the worst of the internal strife, a fact the Church has never been shy about invoking. It was ultimately the dragons who dispersed the chaos energies and prevented total civilizational collapse, though the effort left a permanent mark on [[Wodirethrax|Wodr]], scarring his mind in ways that would shape his future wrath.

Amid the chaos, [[Saint Iseult]] undertook a remarkable diplomatic mission, forging peace with the dragons — and specifically with [[Durnirax]] — at a moment when such an alliance seemed impossible. Their bond, and its tragic end, became one of the most enduring stories of the age.

## Legacy
Azathoros' Invitation never fully ended — its shadow lingered in the banditry and lawlessness that persisted across [[Halinor]] for generations. It also marked a turning point for [[Heldenheim\|Carinthia]], whose relative stability during the crisis became a cornerstone of Church doctrine and national identity. For [[Wodirethrax|Wodr]], the damage ran deeper still, planting seeds of madness that would eventually bloom into the [[Wyrmsfall War]].
