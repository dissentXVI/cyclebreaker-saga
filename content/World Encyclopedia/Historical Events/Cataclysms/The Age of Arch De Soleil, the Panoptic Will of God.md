# The Fifth Cataclysm
## Origin
The Fifth Cataclysm is unique among the Stygian Ages in that its destruction was not wrought by flood, fire, or storm, but by the treachery of dragonkind. In 2854 ApN, the dragon [[Skadraxes]] launched an unprovoked assault against [[The Light of Alophia]], targeting Archbishop Renard Louis himself. In answer to his prayers, [[Alophia]] granted him Her holy power, transforming him into the [[Paragons|Paragon]] [[Arch De Soleil]]. The surge of law pneuma that followed did not raze cities or drown harvests — it imposed order, and in doing so, reshaped the world.
## Overview
The battle against [[Skadraxes]] and the draconic assault that followed was devastating. Only four of the twelve survived. Their bravery was rewarded with nobility and a new sacred order of knights known as the [[Lightwardens]], founding the four High Houses of [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]: Vaillant, Achaard, Courtemanche, and Bellefeuille. Louis-roi, now crowned the first and only [[Arch De Soleil]], led [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]] to a position of dominant influence across [[Halinor]]. His paladins marched south to quell bandit camps and lawless territories. Charitable histories remember this as a period of enforced peace, though records of the time also speak of inquisitions, occupations, and a pervasive fear that settled over any who did not conform to the strict laws of the Church. Louis-roi's reign ended with his assassination. The titles of Arch De Soleil and -roi died with him, and [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]] withdrew into itself, its ambitions turned inward.
## Legacy
The Fifth Cataclysm reshaped [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]] into the insular theocracy it remains today and established the Four High Houses whose influence still defines Carinthian politics. The [[Wyrmsfall War]] that followed hardened [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]'s stance toward dragonkind for generations. The four knights who gave their lives are remembered as martyrs of the faith, their names etched into the sanctuary floor of [[The Light of Alophia]] in [[Heldenheim]]. The memory of [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]'s reach across [[Halinor]] during this period left a lasting unease among the other city-states. In 3004 ApN, that unease found its answer in the founding of [[Shurevaire]] — a city built not on conquest or faith, but on neutrality. Its founders sought to ensure that no single city-state could ever again extend its dominion over the others unchecked, dispatching trained arbiters across [[Halinor]] to mediate disputes and preserve the balance of power. Where [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]] had imposed order through force and doctrine, [[Shurevaire]] would offer it through reason and negotiation.
