## Origin
The Salamandron's Famine struck during the First Stygian Age, roughly 400 years before the Navarresean calendar. A surge of fire pneuma swept across [[Aspiria]], scorching the atmosphere and baking the land beneath a relentless, unnatural heat. The precise cause was unknown to those who lived through it — myth and early scholarship attributed it to divine wrath or celestial misalignment — though later historians would connect it to deeper imbalances in the world's [[pneuma]] currents.
## Overview
The heatwave was global in scope. Farmland cracked and turned to dust, crops withered before harvest, and rainfall became a rarity across most of [[Halinor]]. Rivers ran shallow or dried entirely, and famine spread through early settlements with little resistance. Populations that had no established infrastructure for food storage or long-distance trade were hit hardest.

The famine was ultimately broken by the intervention of a proto-Navarresean scholar who invoked Higher Magic to calm the sun — the first recorded use of Higher Magic in Aspirian history. While the act ended the immediate crisis and ushered in the First Radiant Age, it released an uncontrolled discharge of magical energy into the world's [[pneuma]] currents, an imbalance that scholars would later blame as a contributing cause of the Second Cataclysm.
## Legacy
The Salamandron's Famine is remembered as the first great test of mortal endurance on [[Aspiria]]. It also marks the earliest known use of Higher Magic, an event that would cast a long shadow — celebrated as salvation in its moment, but scrutinized for centuries as the seed of further catastrophe. The tension between necessity and consequence that defines this event echoes through Aspirian theological and arcane debate to this day.
