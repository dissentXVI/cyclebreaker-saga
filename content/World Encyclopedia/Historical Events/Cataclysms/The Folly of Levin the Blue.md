## Origin
The Folly of Levin the Blue began not with prayer or ambition, but with greed. Levin the Blue, a boastful Freeman and infamous plunderer of the sea, ransacked merfolk sanctuaries and looted sacred relics from the cliff-temples of [[Avosa Tesota|Avosa]], never questioning the volatile [[pneuma]] sealed within. By accident — or perhaps by destiny — he completed a long-forgotten rite, summoning [[Tupachachum, the Coiled Lightning|Tupachachum]]: a vast sky-serpent of storm and wrath, crackling with lightning pneuma and cloaked in swirling thunderclouds. She struck him down instantly, reducing Levin to ash and ozone. Above his smoking remains, the sky turned silver-blue. The [[Paragons|Paragon]] had arrived.
## Overview
Tupachachum's coils thundered across the skies of [[Aspiria]], dragging superstorms in her wake that flattened coastal cities, severed trade routes, and disrupted pneuma currents across the globe. Lightning pneuma surged unnaturally, wreaking havoc on arcane artifacts and destabilizing climate patterns far inland. Entire forest canopies were incinerated. [[The Branded Shore]] — once a golden shoreline — was fused into a stretch of black obsidian by divine lightning. Three adventuring parties were dispatched to stop her. The first never reached the battle, ensnared by the Paragon's aura and turned against one another under divine mental influence — the first confirmed instance of Paragon-induced madness, later termed [[Fraying]]. The second and third groups suffered the same fate. Only a final team, for reasons still unexplained, proved immune to [[Tupachachum, the Coiled Lightning|Tupachachum]]'s enthrallment. While they engaged the beast across storm-wracked skies, a circle of Shurevairian wizards anchored the battlefield with arcane grounding fields, neutralizing the Paragon's ambient pneuma long enough for a killing blow. [[Tupachachum, the Coiled Lightning|Tupachachum]] crashed into the sea, where her form crackled and steamed for days before the skies above [[Aspiria]] finally began to clear.
## Legacy
The Folly of Levin the Blue reshaped both the coastline and the study of Paragons. The Branded Shore of [[Eldamar]] endures as a permanent scar carved by the Eel's passage, while [[Levin's Cross]] in [[Avosa Tesota]] stands as a quiet memorial and cautionary tale at the site where [[Tupachachum, the Coiled Lightning|Tupachachum]] first made landfall. More significantly, the cataclysm gave rise to modern research into Paragon influence on pneuma and cognition — the term [[Fraying]] entered arcane lexicons as a lasting condition, and the collaborative effort between the final adventuring team and [[Shurevaire|Shurevairian]] wizards established a precedent for arcane support in future Paragon encounters. Sailors to this day invoke [[Tupachachum, the Coiled Lightning|Tupachachum]]'s name not in prayer, but in curses: *"Coils take me," "By Her blue eye,"* and *"Struck by Tupachachum."*
