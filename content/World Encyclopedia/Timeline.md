## [[The First Stygian Age]]

###### **~400 AvN** 

*The Fifth Shard falls to fire pneuma causing…*

***[[The Salamandron's Famine]]*** - A heatwave encompassing the world, drying farmland to cracking ground and dust, withering crops as they stood, and severely limiting rainfall and water flow across the planet. Myth holds that the proto-Navarreasean scholar invoked Higher Magic for the first time and calmed the sun, though this would later be blamed for the [[Wodr's Revenge |second cataclysm]].

***[[Telothians]] attack [[Thyraxia]]*** - On the crystalline world of [[Thyraxia]], where dragonkind soared among volcanic skies and lattice-mountains of sentient crystal, a silence fell. The [[Telothians]] — synthetic intelligences forged for eradication — descended from beyond the firmament, intent on harvesting pneuma-rich life. Their engines of nullification swept across continents.

[[Ymirethor]], eldest and strongest of the celestial brood, resisted. But even he could not halt the Unmakers. Gathering his unhatched children — seven eggs of breath and storm — he soared through space and fled. As [[Thyraxia]] crumbled into voidmetal ruin, [[Ymirethor]] searched the celestial sea until he collided with a new world: [[Aspiria]].

***[[Ymirethor]] Arrives on [[Neumeris]]*** - Rather than reject him, the world breathed. The will of [[Aspiria]], ancient and slumbering, stirred — and offered shelter. [[Ymirethor]] wept upon the mountains of [[Halinor]]. The eggs were nestled in hidden groves, storm-chambers, and sacred cradles beneath the stars.

In gratitude, [[Ymirethor]] taught the earliest mortals of [[Aspiria]] how to shape pneuma, sparking the beginnings of magic. These early teachings would ripple across ages, eventually warping into divine rites, arcane empires and ending the Famine.

***[[Omnistrix]] crash lands on [[Neumeris]]*** - As [[Ymirethor]] fled [[Thyraxia]], he was pursued by [[Omnistrix]], a [[Telothian]] Prime Hunter built to extinguish pneuma-bearing life. The construct tracked him across realms and eventually crash-landed on [[Neumeris]] (now called [[Aspiria]]), its descent carving a fiery scar into [[Halinor]]'s crust. The impact, repelled by [[Aspiria]]'s divine pneuma, ruptured the land and embedded [[Omnistrix]] deep beneath the surface—dormant but not dead. Its core still hums in silence, buried beneath leyline-rich stone, while [[Ymirethor]] sealed the site with breathwarding glyphs and warned his brood never to return.

***[[The First Brood]] Hatches*** - [[Skadraxes]], [[Wodirethrax]], and [[Durnirax]] settle in Northern [[Halinor]] and [[Eisfeld]]. [[Krisnarax]] and [[Nottraxes]] settle in [[Kokyotos]]. [[Alvaderax]] settles in [[Saptha Sindhu]].

## [[The Third Radiant Age]]

##### 2115 - 2203 ApN

***2115 ApN Founding of [[Tarwa'kem]]*** - Following decades of fractured clan rivalries and ancestral decline, the dwarven leader Serkaf Tarwa united the mountain clans through a pact known as the Ibshah Oaths. Guided by prophetic visions of sunlit vaults carved into sacred stone, he led his people westward to the [[Kemtabi Shelf]] beneath the [[Amrakebir Mountains]]. There, they founded the city of [[Tarwa'kem]], blending necromantic ancestry rites, solar devotion, and stonecraft into a proud new civilization. Temples were carved to gods of death, trade, and law, while the Royal Throne of [[Tarwa'kem]] was established — shaping a city that would become a spiritual haven and trade beacon across [[Halinor]].

***2168 ApN Founding of [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]*** - Led by the sorcerer [[Carine Reivalur]], elves who were disillusioned by the endless wars and authoritarian rule ventured into the wintry north. Carine claims to have received a vision from [[Alophia]] commanding him to forge a sacred order and establish a church in her name. Thus, [[The Light of Alophia]] was formed, merging faith and military. The first order of [[The Dawnbound]] was formed under the now titled [[Archbishop Carine von Reivalur]]. Against the dragons, they managed to carve out a new home, establishing the holy city of [[Heldenheim]] in the mountains. 

## [[The Fourth Stygian Age]]

##### 2203 - 2311 ApN

*The Fourth Shard falls to chaos pneuma causing…*

***[[Azathoros' Invitation]]***- Civil war erupts in most countries, and those that avoid it find brother turning against brother, families falling apart, and an increase in lawlessness and banditry that has not subsumed even to this day. Scholarly research has loosely tied the previous, temporally-close cataclysm to this one, calling the influence of the maddening waters to task for spreading long term instability and in-fighting across the world. Equally strong, however, is the doubt cast upon this theory, particularly in [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]] in the modern day. [[The Light of Alophia|The Carinthian Church]] holds that the demon [[Azathoros]] was stirred in his slumber and the snores of the Fiend resonated through the world's veil, driving mad the mortals that heard it. It is interesting to note that [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]] in this time saw less of the civil feuds plaguing other nations and none of the theological acedia that other churches did. The dragons were able to dispel the energies eventually and prevent total madness, but the mark was left in [[Wodirethrax|Wodr]], scarring his mind.

## [[The Fourth Radiant Age]]

##### 2311 - 2854 ApN

***2382 ApN Founding of [[D'yadya]]*** - In the arid reaches of western [[Halinor]], where canyon winds once scattered kin across salt flats and trade roads, a rare unity took root. Guided by shared dreams of permanence and protection for future generations, the nomadic tribes of the desert gathered under one banner to build a lasting home along the glittering eastern coast. They named it [[D'yadya]], meaning "a gift, our gift" — a promise to their children that rootless hardship would end. With stone drawn from sun-bleached cliffs and textiles dyed from native herbs and sands, they built a city proud of its survivalist traditions and fierce independence. [[D'yadya]] grew into a thriving trade nexus, a place where water, salt, and kinship held greater value than gold.

***2698 ApN Founding of [[Aqualonde]]*** - Disillusioned by the rigid doctrine and endless wars of [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]], a sect of elven scholars, artists, and druids departed the Holy North in search of balance. Their journey led them deep into [[the Fauconwood]], a forest untouched by conquest and rich with living magic. There, beside rivers that sang and roots that whispered, they founded [[Aqualonde]] — a city woven into nature rather than built upon it. Rejecting hierarchy in favor of guilds and salons, they dedicated themselves to light, art, and harmony with the world around them. In time, they would come to revere the goddess [[Eirune]], and their forest refuge would blossom into a bastion of elegant engineering, arcane gamesmanship, and woodland beauty.

## [[The Fifth Stygian Age]]

##### 2854 - 3004 ApN

*The Ninth Shard falls to law pneuma causing…*

***[[The Age of Arch De Soleil, the Panoptic Will of God]]*** - In the chaos that followed after the previous cataclysm (or what some call an unfortunate dark age, denying the time period the title of 'cataclysm' due to its more insidious nature), the nation least exposed to the perils of the world grew in prominence and promise. It is in this, the dawning of the best known histories of [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]], that the first and final [[Arch de Soleil]] was crowned. Leading the valiant defense of the southlands against dragonkind, Louis-roi (or King Louis) also established the dominance of [[The Light of Alophia]] inside her borders and abroad. Charitable histories called this a time of peace as paladins of the Church marched south to quell bandit camps and bring order to otherwise lawless lands, but records of the time share the other side of history–the inquisitions, the occupations, and the general insularity and fear that permeated the lands. These were indeed dark times for those that did not abide the strict laws of the realm, and following the assassination of Louis-roi the title of [[Arch de Soleil]] and -roi were never continued. The Church continued to borrow from their ancestral titles, of course, as the Arch-Bishop title was taken by Louis, but as their focus withdrew into the now insular nation familiar to modern day, this became less of an imminent concern.

***2854 ApN Skadraxes is betrayed by Archbishop Renard Louis*** - [[Archbishop Renard Louis]] ordered his [[The Dawnbound|Dawnbound]] to betray the dragons, trapping and killing [[Skadraxes]] before ripping her eyes out. They consumed her eyes allowing the Archbishop to transform into the [[Paragons|Paragon]], [[Arch De Soleil]] and empower his [[The Dawnbound|Dawnbound]]. [[Wodirethrax|Wodr]], learning of his sister's death, leapt into battle with the Carinthians. During the battle, one of [[The Dawnbound]] - [[Ser Thibault Achaard]] wrested [[Wodr]]'s own eyes out of their sockets. Forced to retreat and facing certain death without his eyes, Wodr met with [[Durnirax]]. Refusing to give up on his vengeance, [[Wodirethrax|Wodr]] convinced his reluctant brother to give him his left eye, so that [[Wodirethrax|Wodr]] might live and rally the draconic horde to continue his newfound war with [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]], which would become known as the [[Wyrmsfall War]]. 

Wodr's own Eyes, however, would persist as powerful relics. [[The Light of Alophia]] would cover up [[Skadraxes]]'s death and claim that [[Wodirethrax|Wodr]] had attacked them unprovoked as justification for taking his eyes. Rather than consuming them outright as they did [[Skadraxes]]'s, the Carinthians would keep the Eyes and continually draw on their power. This battle which kicked off the [[Wyrmsfall War]] cost the lives of all but four of [[The Dawnbound]], whose secrecy was rewarded with nobility and started a special order of knights known as the Lightwardens. This is the beginning of the Four [[High Houses of Carinthia]]: Vaillant, Achaard, Courtemanche, and Belleveuille.

## [[The Fifth Radiant Age]]

##### 3004 - 3038 ApN

***3004 ApN Founding of [[Shurevaire]]*** - In the aftermath of [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]'s growing militarism and the devastation wrought by past cataclysms, a coalition of philosophers, archivists, and mages withdrew southward to the mist-veiled island beyond the reach of war. There, they founded [[Shurevaire]] — a grande, marble-clad city of elevated thought, open-air libraries, and cloistered colleges. Governed not by kings or clergy, but by a council of learned stewards, [[Shurevaire]] swore itself to neutrality and preservation, vowing to safeguard knowledge from dogma and destruction. From their academies emerged arbiters — trained negotiators and peacekeepers dispatched to resolve disputes across [[Halinor]], ensuring no city-state would rise to dominate the others unchecked.

## [[The Sixth Stygian Age]]

##### 3038 - 3556 ApN

*The Third Shard falls to wind pneuma causing…*

***3038 ApN [[The Howling Winds of Y'Set]]*** - The appearance of [[Y'Set]] loosed hurricane-force sandstorms across [[Tarwa'kem]], disrupting trade, agriculture, and city life. These storms had no natural pattern—they pulsed outward from the [[Paragons|Paragon]] itself, forming a living vortex of dust and death. As its cultists drove it ever forward, whole villages were swallowed in the storm's maw. The weather across [[Aspiria]] shifted, its pneuma thrown into disarray by the unnatural winds.

The creature's march threatened to carry it toward the heartlands of [[Halinor]], prompting an elite band of seven to set out from [[Tarwa'kem]] to stop it. They succeeded, but at great cost—only two of the original seven survived the battle. Before they could return home, they were ambushed and slain by remnants of the cult, leaving no champions to claim the tale. The cult, never fully destroyed, splintered into shadows, its members slipping across [[Aspiria]] like grains of sand.

[[Tupachachum, the Coiled Lightning|Tupachachum]] was finally slain, her serpentine form crashing into the sea, where it crackled and steamed for days as the skies above [[Aspiria]] slowly began to clear.

Damage had already been wrought with landslides and earthquakes which formed a giant rift in the middle of Halinor draining the lake leaving what is now referred to as [[Sticaean Abyss|the Sticaean Abyss]]. The drained lake revealed a tower made of breathstone which was called [[The Cosmic Pillar|The Watcher of the Lake]]. 

While this was not formally recognized as a cataclysm, the devastation caused was severe enough to earn the title…

***4009 ApN [[The Trembling Foundations]]*** - Earthquakes took the land in many places, even those not usually in danger of fault activity. Landslides, sinkholes, new caves and more opened around the world, but the cataclysm was cut short by [[Velethoui Arsenault|Velethoui]]'s sacrifice and binding of the Radiant Beast [[Krisnarax]], stabilizing energies there and abroad.

They discovered a way to speak with [[Aspiria]] and discovered the plan to evacuate [[Neumeris]]. They begin hoarding all knowledge and specimens for [[the Ark]].
