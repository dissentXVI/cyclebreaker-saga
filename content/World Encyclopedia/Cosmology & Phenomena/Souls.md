In [[Aspiria]], a soul is the pneuma that gives form and identity to living beings. At the moment of death, this pneuma begins to dissipate and return to the [[Sea of Pneuma]], where it is gathered into the eternal cycle of life. Under natural circumstances, the transition is seamless—life ends, pneuma flows home, and eventually reemerges in new form.

Yet this passage can be disrupted. A curse, a binding ritual, or even an overwhelming sense of unfinished purpose may halt a soul’s return. When this occurs, pneuma lingers and warps, manifesting as a **ghost**. Such spirits may haunt the living or cling to objects, unwilling or unable to release themselves into [[Siren]]’s keeping.

Souls, like all pneuma, can be trapped or manipulated. [[Breathstone]] sometimes captures fragments of the departed, intentionally or by accident, and these can be exorcised by skilled priests or mages. For such displaced [[pneuma]] to persist, however, it must find anchorage in a physical vessel—a body, construct, or enchanted medium. Without it, even the strongest soul will eventually fade into silence.
