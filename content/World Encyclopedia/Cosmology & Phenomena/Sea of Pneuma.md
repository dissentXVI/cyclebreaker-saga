Beneath the surface of the world flows the Sea of Pneuma, an endless current of living essence that sustains [[Aspiria]]. Every being draws upon it in life, and at death their pneuma returns to its depths, mingling with the greater tide before emerging anew. Scholars describe it as the planet’s breath made visible: a vast, unseen river of light that nourishes the soil, heals the land, and binds all creatures together.

The god [[Siren]] is said to rule the Sea of Pneuma, guiding souls into its depths and ensuring their return to the world when the cycle begins anew. Priests depict him as both stern warden and gentle shepherd, guarding the threshold between life and death. For many cultures, to commit the dead to the Sea is to entrust them to [[Siren]]’s keeping.

While inaccessible to most, the Sea of Pneuma occasionally wells up in places of great trauma or wonder—shimmering pools in ancient ruins, rivers of light beneath sacred groves, or radiant geysers after cataclysms. To touch it is to feel eternity: many report visions of relatives, ancestors, gods, or even [[Aspiria]] herself. Carinthian priests claim their saints walk its currents; Aqualondian druids believe it is the song of the forest made infinite.

Legends warn that disrupting the Sea of Pneuma can unravel the balance of the world. It is both cradle and grave, memory and promise—the eternal cycle from which life rises and to which it returns.
