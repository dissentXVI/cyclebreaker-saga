Fraying is the loss of self that occurs when a mortal is overwhelmed by the presence of a Paragon. Drawn in by the being’s [[pneuma]]-saturated aura, the victim’s own pneuma begins to unravel, their thoughts and will fraying into the pattern of the summoned god. To those afflicted, the Paragon’s voice becomes truth, their purpose becomes law, and their former identity dissolves into devotion.

The process is not instant. Early signs include dreams of the Paragon, compulsive prayer, or sudden surges of strength and clarity when acting in its service. Over time, memories and personality are eroded until nothing remains but fanatic loyalty. Once fully frayed, a person cannot be reasoned with—they are bound to the Paragon’s will until death.

Scholars note that Fraying can spread quickly in groups, as shared belief and fear strengthen the Paragon’s hold. Carinthian doctrine describes it as a corruption of the soul; Aqualondian poets liken it to a thread pulled loose until the whole garment comes undone. While rare individuals possess the strength—or blessing—to resist, for most, exposure to a Paragon is the beginning of the end.
