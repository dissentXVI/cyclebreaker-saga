Paragons are godlike beings born when vast amounts of [[pneuma]] are shaped by mortal will, faith, or desperation into living divinity. Unlike the eternal gods of the Highest Heavens, Paragons are summoned—called into being by rituals that bind [[breathstone]], blood, and belief into a single, overwhelming form. They embody the hopes and fears of their summoners, granting blessings to the faithful while unraveling those who fall under their influence.

Though awe-inspiring, Paragons are inherently unstable. Their forms demand constant sustenance of pneuma, often drawn from both the land and their worshippers. Prolonged exposure leads to [[Fraying]], a condition where mortals lose their sense of self, becoming fanatically loyal to the Paragon’s will. Entire tribes and cities have fallen under their thrall, sparking cataclysms that reshaped the world.

In [[Halinor]], Paragons are feared as calamities—divine echoes that consume more than they give. [[The Vociran Empire]], rejecting all gods, refers to them as **Exemplars**, viewing them not as divinities but as dangerous weapons born of mortal weakness. Still, whether worshipped or reviled, their presence is undeniable: wherever mortals pray too hard, despair too deeply, or cling too tightly to hope, a Paragon may rise.
