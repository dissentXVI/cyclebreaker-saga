## Alias(es)

#### Element  
Death  
#### Cleric Domain  
Grave Domain  
#### Associated Domains  
The Underworld, the Dead, Passage, Memory, Burial  
#### Materials of Reverence  
Wood is sacred to Siren, symbolizing the vessel that carries souls across the Sea of Pneuma. Traditional burials include wooden coffins or urns, and even the poorest are buried with two small wooden coins—one for each eye—as passage toll for the Ferryman.  
  
## Summary  
Siren stands on the far shore of life, waiting. He is the one constant, the final presence, the inevitable ferryman who carries every soul across the Sea of Pneuma to whatever lies beyond. While each culture paints His demeanor differently—dreadful, sorrowful, stoic, or greedy—none deny His role. Siren is not cruel nor kind. He is necessity. Mystery clings to His myths like mist on a black sea, and while none agree on what comes after the crossing, all agree: the journey cannot be denied. To honor Siren is to delay death, to dishonor Him is to risk the silence of a soul never ferried home.  
  
## Spread  
Siren is one of the most universally acknowledged Divinities in [[Aspiria]]. His temples, shrines, and rites vary wildly, but His name is known in every funeral, remembered in every tolling bell, and invoked in every final farewell. Whether He is loved, feared, or paid in cautious respect, no god is more intimately tied to mortal fate.  
  
## Cultural Reception  
  
#### [[Aqualonde]]  
To the elves of [[Aqualonde]], Siren is not an end, but a shepherd. They teach that all souls rise and fall with the seasons. Death is but winter—Siren's gentle hand returns souls to the Font so they may bloom again. His shrines are circular, open to the elements, and filled with wind chimes carved from driftwood or bone. His priests speak softly and sing the dead home.  
  
#### [[Avosa Tesota]]  
Avosans hold a darker view. Siren lives beneath the [[Sea of Pneuma]], in a sunken barge that drifts as much through nightmare as water. He is not cruel, but He is hungry, and tithes are paid to delay the toll. Sailors whisper His name when they see skeleton-fingered waves or find barnacle-covered coins floating on the tide. His Wheel is carved into storm-battered docks and tattooed on the backs of undertakers.  
  
#### [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]  
The elves of [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]] have long embraced Siren as the final gatekeeper. He is honored with reverence, especially among the knights and kin who fight [[Wodr]]’s brood. Carinthian theology holds that those who die in righteous battle are escorted across the [[Sea of Pneuma]] to a place of luminous honor, while cowards and traitors are denied passage, left to wander the frozen shoreline. His image here is stoic and just—cloaked in black, bearing a lantern and the Wheel.  
  
#### [[D'yadya]]  
The desert people regard Siren as a watchful presence in the canyon winds. Funerary songs in the Sift call Him the “One Who Waits Between Stones.” When burial is not possible, bones are adorned with wood-carved symbols and buried beneath cairns shaped like rudders to guide the soul westward across the Sea.  
  
#### [[Shurevaire]]  
In scholarly circles, Siren is studied as the symbolic finality of all cycles. While personal worship is rare, His myths are debated heavily in death rites, soulcraft theory, and philosophical treatises. His Wheel is the dominant funerary symbol across noble and commoner graves alike.  
  
#### [[Tarwa'kem]]  
Siren is deeply embedded in Tarwa’kem’s cultural memory—some claim He was once a dwarven god, a grim accountant of both wealth and soul. In the Hall of Siren’s Vault, He is portrayed as a hooded dwarf draped in relics and finery, His skeletal hand resting on a chest of unclaimed debt. [[Tarwa’kem]]’s coins bear His face on one side, [[Haelaxa]]’s on the other, symbolizing life’s final transaction. While His role as death’s guardian is acknowledged, most shrines focus on His nature as a collector—and a god not easily cheated.  
  
## In Art  
Siren’s depiction varies more than any other god.  
  
In [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]], He is a hooded man or elf, draped in black, lantern in one hand, Wheel in the other.  
  
In [[Aqualonde]], He is a serene figure of mist and memory, walking barefoot across silver waves.  
  
In [[Avosa Tesota]], He is skeletal, with rings on one hand and seaweed tangled in His robes, His face always obscured.  
  
In [[Tarwa’kem]], He is a dwarven crypt-lord surrounded by gold and silence, seated atop a vast vault.  
  
Despite His varied forms, three elements endure: the cloak, the Wheel, and His maleness. While His demeanor and species shift by culture, Siren is always a He, and always the one who waits at the end.

## Notable Holy Sites  
**[[Siren’s Vault]]**, [[Tarwa'kem]]  
A temple of carved obsidian and sunless wealth, this shrine beneath the sands houses both the dead and their fortunes. Each noble who dies in Tarwa’kem leaves behind a sealed offering in the Vault—a symbolic payment to the Ferryman. The Vault is guarded by silent monks, and its halls are lit with braziers of bone ash and oil.  
  
**[[The Driftwood Chapel]]**, [[Avosa Tesota]]  
Floating on the harbor outside the city of [[Avosa Tesota]], this chapel is built from the wreckage of doomed ships. It is weighted to drift but never sink. Avosans send their dead here in small boats or upon tides of wood and ash, letting the [[Sea of Pneuma]] carry them to Siren’s door.
