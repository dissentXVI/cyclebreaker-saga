## Alias(es)  
*The Tempest Lord*  
#### Element  
Water  
#### Cleric Domain  
Tempest Domain  
#### Associated Domains  
Seas, Storms, Beasts  
#### Materials of Reverence  
Bone from great beasts—leviathans, sea monsters, or apex predators—is sacred to Tyrachon. Coral is honored but must never be removed from His waters; doing so drains it of color and invites the Tempest Lord’s curse upon the thief.  
  
## Summary  
Tyrachon commands the most feral and awe-striking forces in [[Aspiria]]: the oceans, the raging storm, and the untamable wilds. Revered with equal parts fear and veneration, He is the Patron of sailors, beastmasters, and storm-chasers alike. Though rarely called benevolent, His presence is seen as necessary; without Him, the natural world would spiral into even greater chaos. Where the people of Aspiria face the fury of nature, His name is spoken—sometimes in reverence, sometimes in dread.  
  
## Spread  
Tyrachon’s worship follows the coastline, the wilderness, and the sky. He is strongest in [[Avosa Tesota]], respected in [[Aqualonde]] and [[Tarwa’kem]], and quietly acknowledged even in the frigid, storm-beaten north. Wherever beasts roam and storms descend, Tyrachon’s name is known and offered to.  
  
## Cultural Reception  
  
#### [[Aqualonde]]  
Reverence for Tyrachon exists alongside devotion to [[Demeria]]. The wood elven druids of the outer forests honor Him as the wild god of balance and fury. These Tyrachonic circles embrace communion with beastkind and the raw tumult of nature, forming a respected if peripheral tradition within Aqualondian culture.  
  
#### [[Avosa Tesota]]  
Nowhere is the Tempest Lord worshipped more fiercely. To the cliffside cities and seafarers of the Free Peninsula, Tyrachon is master of their lives and deaths. They do not pray for Him to spare them—but to see them strong enough to survive. His temples are wild and wind-battered, built into rocky shores or raised on ships. He is freedom, fury, and the untamed soul of [[Avosa Tesota|Avosa]] itself.  
  
#### [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]  
Though not openly worshipped, Tyrachon is not vilified. Carinthians see themselves as resilient, shaped by hardship. Their climate’s cruelty is not blamed on the god of storms but on the rebellious dragons who defy His dominion. While [[Alophia]] reigns in doctrine, some see in Tyrachon a reflection of their own cold endurance — a force that can be met, if not mastered.  
  
#### [[D'yadya]]  
Before their diaspora, D’yadyans prayed to Tyrachon for guidance through sandstorms and protection from canyon beasts. Among exiled elders, this faith endures in wind-whispered chants and carved bone totems. Storm-sign reading is still practiced in some refugee circles.  
  
#### [[Shurevaire]]  
Scholars interpret Tyrachon as a metaphor for unchecked instinct, wild genius, and elemental entropy. Some theologians study His myths as commentary on nature’s place in reasoned society, but His worship remains largely symbolic, not devotional.  
  
#### [[Tarwa'kem]]  
Though landlocked, [[Tarwa’kem]] knows storms and predators well. In the cutthroat capital, Tyrachon’s dogma of survival and dominance resonates. His worship is less about weather and more about hierarchy—He who is strong enough to take, shall rule. His likeness appears in blood rites, in the arena, and in whispered prayers before violent ambition.  
  
## In Art  
Tyrachon is the most primal of the Divinities, and His depictions reflect that. He appears as a muscular man with dark, wind-tangled hair and a braided beard filled with shells and predator teeth. He is never seen without his iconic Trident, a symbol of divine judgment and command.  
  
In many portrayals, His lower body shifts into wild sea-beast forms: the tentacles of an octopus, the tail of a shark, or limbs scaled like a deep-sea serpent. When not personified, He is rendered as a storm cloud, crashing wave, or the dreaded Eye of the Storm—considered a literal manifestation of His attention.

## Notable Holy Sites  
**[[Shrine of the Breaking Wave]]**, [[Avosa Tesota]]  
Found in a flooded cave near outside [[Avosa Tesota]], this wave-blasted sanctuary features a basin that fills only during high storm tide. Local priests claim Tyrachon’s will can be read in the foam patterns that emerge during offerings of blood and bone.
