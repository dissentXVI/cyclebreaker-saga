## Alias(es)  
*The Butcher Lord, the Blooded One, the Culler, the Beast of Iron*  
#### Element  
Evil  
#### Cleric Domain  
War Domain  
#### Associated Domains  
War, Violence, Bloodshed  
#### Materials of Reverence  
Blood is the one true offering to Meninos. The ideal sacrifice is the blood of a bull or ox, spilled in a ritual space. Rams are acceptable in lean times. Implements of war—broken blades, worn gauntlets, cracked helmets—may be added as tokens, but they are meaningless without the blood. Where His name is spoken, something must bleed.  
  
## Summary  
Meninos is the Divinity of violence made holy—of bloodshed as renewal, of slaughter as necessity. While some call Him a god of war, He is not a tactician or a strategist. That is Alophia's mantle. Meninos represents war for its own sake—conflict as correction, battle as purification. His followers argue that He does not delight in senseless death, but rather in the ruthless efficiency of trimming the fat from the world. His is the philosophy of strength through elimination. Those too weak to endure are not punished, merely removed.  
  
He is invoked by gladiators, marauders, cullers, hunters, and generals who believe survival is a crucible, and that mercy too often breeds rot.  
  
## Spread  
Meninos has no centralized church. His worship spreads like wildfire through bloodstained soil: present where war festers, where arenas roar, and where the knife is swifter than the pen. His rites are visceral, his songs more often screams than hymns. In cities, He is given temples. In the wilds, His name is carved into bones and bark and shouted into the sky.  
  
## Cultural Reception  
  
#### [[Aqualonde]]  
A small but persistent druidic order in the Fauconwood sees Meninos as a primal reality: predator and prey, the cycle of the wild. They neither venerate nor vilify Him. Rather, they emulate the necessary violence He embodies. These druids do not glorify slaughter, but they do accept it as part of nature's balance. Their rituals are quiet, blood-soaked affairs held under the cover of forest storms.  
  
#### [[Avosa Tesota]]  
Meninos splits opinion in [[Avosa Tesota|Avosa]]. On the coast and ships, He is cautiously respected—pirates and sailors offer Him blooded coins and fish offal, hoping to divert His wrath. Inland, however, in the dense rainforests of the peninsula, He is openly worshipped by the Pa'wak'te raiders. These warriors conduct heart-sacrifices before raids, donning jaguar pelts and invoking the Butcher Lord through animal hunts and war chants. Their vision of Meninos is primal, beautiful, and terrifying.  
  
#### [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]  
[[The Church of Alophia]] teaches that Meninos is the shadow cast by undisciplined force—a warning, not a guide. His worship is banned in [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]. Sermons cite Him as what awaits those who forsake wisdom and clarity in favor of blind conquest. The Wyrm is said to have been born of such violence. His name is uttered only in cautionary tale or whispered accusation.  
  
#### [[Shurevaire]]  
[[Shurevaire]]'s devotion to scholarship, diplomacy, and cultural refinement leaves little room for Meninos's ideology. Officially, no shrines are permitted within the college-city's walls, and those few who whisper the Butcher God's name do so in reference to historical cautionary tales. However, some philosophers study Meninos's cults in comparative theology, and a few controversial artists have painted martial allegories that evoke His principles of purging weakness—more as satire than tribute. Even so, some fear student dueling societies may quietly invoke His name for strength.  
  
#### [[Tarwa'kem]]  
In [[Tarwa'kem]], violence is packaged, sold, and cheered. A gladiatorial arena doubles as a temple to Meninos, where blood spilled on sand is His hymn and coin is His tithe. The priests of the arena are wealthy, charismatic, and thoroughly merciless. Though some whisper that true reverence is lost in spectacle, the faithful argue: What better praise for a god of war than a battle fought well and watched by thousands?  
  
## In Art  
![[Meninos_The_Butchers_Visage.png]]  
*The Butcher's Visage: Swear Fealty to Violent Authority by Jannalor Rokas*

Meninos wears the mask of His worshippers.  
  
Among humans, He is a broad-shouldered man painted in blood, jaw clenched, fists wrapped, horns mounted on his helm.  
  
Among dwarves, He is shown as a bull with burning eyes and a warhammer, his beard braided with teeth.  
  
Among elves, He is rarely shown as humanoid at all—more often an animal-headed figure, typically a bull or jaguar, wreathed in crimson.  
  
In [[the Fauconwood]], He appears as a shadowed beast, half-man and half-wolf, caught in eternal pursuit.  
  
In [[Avosa Tesota|Avosa]]'s rainforests, He is a sleek, divine jaguar, golden-eyed and silent until He strikes.  
  
In the capital, He is a brutal man in jaguar skins, drenched in blood and crowned with bones.  
  
Where Meninos appears, so too does a trail of ruin.

## Notable Holy Sites  
**[[Meninos' Theatre]]**, [[Tarwa'kem]]  
A grand sandstone coliseum where combatants fight for honor, glory, and favor. Blood spilled on this sand is considered sacred, and its central pit is rumored to be built atop a buried altar to Meninos Himself. His priests officiate matches, bless champions, and anoint the sand with oxblood each dawn.
