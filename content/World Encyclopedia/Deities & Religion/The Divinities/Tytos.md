## Summary  
Tytos is a human god often depicted with the majesty and dignity heaped upon His domain of the Rule of Law. As master of the sky, there is none higher than His rule—but despite the power and splendor, Tytos is often seen as a fair and caring judge, dispensing justice with both wisdom and compassion. Heavy may lay the crown on the head of royalty, but Tytos remains perfectly postured. As the patron of judges, rulers, and other men and women of law, the Divine King ensures the stability of Aspiria.  
  
## Spread  
Worship of Tytos is strongest in [[Tarwa'kem]], where legal ritual and civic tradition still hold divine weight. In other city-states, His dominion over law has been reinterpreted or replaced, but His symbolism of balance and judgment persists, especially among scholars and judges.  
  
## Cultural Reception  
  
#### [[Aqualonde]]  
Though [[Aqualonde]] is not without law, the forest state emphasizes personal liberty and guild-based governance. Tytos is respected, but not actively worshipped. Pilgrims may thank Him for long peace or fair weather, but most shrines are disused. His presence is primarily as an ideal of high justice — rarely practiced, but quietly revered.  
  
#### [[Avosa Tesota]]  
In the rebellious peninsula, worship of Tytos is fragmented. Sky-captains and sailors toast His name when hoping for clear skies, but few honor His law. He is invoked as a distant, majestic god — powerful, but not particularly beloved. As a land of self-rule and populism, [[Avosa Tesota|Avosa]] finds little comfort in divine monarchy.  
  
#### [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]  
Once honored as a primeval source of law, Tytos's worship has been eroded by the theological supremacy of [[Alophia]]. His role survives only symbolically in older court rituals and architecture, but no clergy actively serve Him within [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]'s church. Many Carinthians would call Him ancient, noble — and obsolete.  
  
#### [[D'yadya]]  
Among older generations, Tytos was honored in ancestral legal traditions, particularly among salt-judges and market tribunals. Since the Vociran annexation, His shrines have been defaced or hidden. Secret worship continues in some canyon enclaves, passed down as whispered law among refugee elders.  
  
#### [[Shurevaire]]  
Seen as a moral allegory rather than a living deity. Shurvairian philosophers debate His principles but rarely pray to Him. In theology courses, Tytos is studied alongside foreign legal constructs and Enlightenment ideals.  
  
#### [[Tarwa'kem]]  
Tytos is officially the Patron of Law in [[Tarwa'kem]], and the ruling families use His name to legitimize their authority. The Hall of Order stands as a central civic temple. Yet in practice, law bows to coin. Public reverence masks private critique, and since Sultana [[Neema Karo'kem]]'s rise, satirical portrayals of the Divine King as aloof or ineffective have increased in both street art and masked theater.  
  
## In Art  
![[Tytos_The_Trial_Beyond_the_Firmament.png]]  
*The Trial Beyond the Firmament by Tannatar Valna*

Tytos is often depicted as a strong elder man, white of hair and beard, with dark skin and regal bearing. His white robes and golden crown—said to be a gift from [[Eirune]]—mark Him unmistakably, as does the blue cloak symbolizing the sky. Elven depictions often omit the beard, while [[Shurevaire]] favors abstract forms like balanced scales or sun-discs above a mountain.  
  
Across the ages, He has faded from the center of artistic depictions of the pantheon. While never mocked directly outside of Tarwa'kem, He is increasingly presented as a passive background figure — watching, but rarely acting.  
  
## Orders & Cults  
Order of the Platinum Crown – Legal scholars and magistrates in Tarwa'kem who strive to interpret law as divine order. The Order hosts debates, codifies precedent, and sends Commuter-Judges to settle disputes in [[Halinor]].  
  
Skybound Tribunal – Rural judges or clergy who refuse to pass verdicts unless the sky is clear, believing storms reflect Tytos's disfavor.

## Notable Holy Sites  
**[[Hall of Order|The Hall of Order]]**, [[Tarwa'kem]]    
A grand civic temple located at the heart of [[Tarwa'kem]]'s administrative quarter. It houses the Assessors, neutral arbiters of high law. Nobles partake in an annual ritual to "weigh their soul," offering blood in golden vessels — sometimes their own, sometimes purchased from virtuous donors. The temple gleams with platinum accents and is built to evoke the shape of a rising sun piercing storm clouds. Despite its majesty, the Hall is often seen as a place where law and wealth entangle.
