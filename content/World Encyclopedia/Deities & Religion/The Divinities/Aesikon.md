## Alias(es)
*The Prince of Song, The Avowed, The Healer-Prince*
#### Element
Life
#### Cleric Domain
Life Domain
#### Associated Domains
Healing and Disease, Oath and Truth, Dance and Music
#### Materials of Reverence
Though diamond is said to delight Aesikon most, high-quality quartz is widely accepted as a symbol of His clarity and brilliance. Quartz instruments, blade inlays, and gem-encrusted holy symbols are common offerings.

## Summary
Aesikon is a Divinity of contrasts: joy and discipline, art and duty, healing and judgment. As the patron of music, oaths, and recovery, He guides both the swordhand of the paladin and the voice of the bard. His presence is radiant—clad in fine silks, crowned with curls of gold, and surrounded by song and light. Though called the Prince of Song, His domain encompasses truth itself, made evident through divine harmony, sacred vows, and unshakable devotion. He is the balm to the world’s wounds and the melody that binds community together.

## Spread
Aesikon’s worship flows across the continent in rivers of sound and silence—celebrated in the concert halls of [[Avosa Tesota|Avosa]], the forest clearings of [[Aqualonde]], and the oathbound temples of [[Tarwa’kem]]. Though [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]] downplays His influence, even they cannot fully ignore His role in healing and sacred vows.

## Cultural Reception

#### [[Aqualonde]]
[[The Fauconwood]] resounds with His music. Bards, dancers, and troubadours hold Aesikon in highest esteem, and even druids offer blessings through harmonies and rhythm. His temples double as theaters, and His festivals often blur into competitions of artistry and oration. The elves of [[Aqualonde]] see Him not just as a god of healing, but as the heartbeat of their culture.
#### [[Avosa Tesota]]
Here, devotion to Aesikon is as deliberate as it is passionate. The bardic college of [[Avosa Tesota|Avosa]], widely considered the finest in [[Aspiria]], is dedicated to Him. His worship is practical and celebratory: sailors sing His praises to keep plague and misfortune at bay; warriors take oaths before His likeness; lovers whisper truths in His name. Where laughter resounds or music uplifts, there too is Aesikon.
#### [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]
While He is acknowledged, Aesikon’s colorful grandeur finds little favor in [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]’s rigid and austere theology. Official Church doctrine emphasizes Alophia, His sister, whose discipline and clarity are deemed more fitting for the northern temperament. Some Carinthians mockingly say that Aesikon’s absence explains the lack of joy in their sunless cities. Others whisper of a divine rivalry between the siblings, one of warmth and wit against cold and logic.
#### [[D'yadya]]
Among surviving D’yadyan enclaves, Aesikon is remembered as the healing fire of their people. Songs in His name once carried across salt flats to guide the sick and broken. Now, those same melodies are sung in refugee camps and burial rites—soft but unbroken.
#### [[Shurevaire]]
Academics in [[Shurevaire]] appreciate Aesikon’s ties to rhetoric and musical structure. His hymns are studied as foundational poetic forms, and students of divine law often swear their honor by His name. Though less spiritually expressive, His worship exists here as refinement: the divine as art, and art as divine.
#### [[Tarwa'kem]]
Aesikon's worship here has D’yadyan roots. The Temple of the Promised Morn, founded by refugee priest-healers, once emphasized pacifism and guidance. In the years since the fall of the west, however, its paladins have risen in power, turning it into a bastion of oath-bound warriors and righteous protectors. The Avowed Prince is honored as the source of all sacred vows, and initiates undergo ritual confession before taking up arms. His music lives less in song and more in discipline, camaraderie, and solemn purpose.
## In Art
Aesikon is nearly always shown in radiant attire—a golden-haired elf or human, robed in flowing white or gold silk, with luminous blue eyes that seem to shine. He is smiling, sometimes laughing, wine or lyre in hand. His depictions vary:

In [[Tarwa’kem]], he wears armor marked by oaths, bearing a crystal-edged sword and a gleaming shield.

In [[Aqualonde]], he dances barefoot through sun-dappled groves, lyre on his back and ribbons in the wind.

In [[Avosa Tesota]], he is mid-song, a silver flute at his lips, commanding both joy and reverence.

In [[Shurevaire]], he is shown composing scrolls of radiant music, etched into air.

Notably, His artwork often includes playable notation, with some temples transcribing songs that are believed to invoke actual divine energy.
## Orders & Cults

## Notable Holy Sites
**The Temple of the Promised Morn**, [[Tarwa’kem]]
Built atop the reclaimed remains of an old salt fortress, this temple blends martial discipline with devotional artistry. Paladins train here, and oaths are witnessed beneath stained-glass depictions of Aesikon guiding broken warriors through the fields of dawn.
