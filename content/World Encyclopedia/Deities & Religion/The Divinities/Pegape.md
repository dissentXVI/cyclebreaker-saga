#### Materials of Reverence  
Offerings to Pegape take many forms, but all share one quality: indulgence.  
  
Silks, especially vibrantly dyed or sensuously embroidered  
  
Perfumes, oils, incense, or scented water  
  
Sweets and decadent food or wine  
  
Intimate or beautiful handmade crafts, including poetry, sculpture, or embroidery  
  
Most sacred of all: an authentic expression of desire, whether romantic, artistic, or bodily  
  
Some temples ask devotees to whisper their desires to a rose petal and place it upon a shrine dish—if the petal vanishes by morning, their prayer was heard.  
  
## Summary  
Pegape is the god of passion in all its forms—the thrill of pursuit, the intimacy of shared affection, the ache of longing, the joy of indulgence. Neither male nor female, and often both, They are mutable, taking on the form that best evokes the desire or beauty of the moment.  
  
Their presence is felt in the hush of a stolen kiss, the heat of dance, the yearning of a ballad, the languid hours spent in fragrant smoke and silken touch. They are pleasure sanctified, desire unchained, and beauty expressed without shame.  
  
But Pegape's worship is not without tension. Though most see Them as a liberator of expression, others fear Their influence as distraction, excess, or temptation.  
To Pegape's faithful, those fears are merely chains not yet broken.  
  
## Spread  
Worship of Pegape is ubiquitous and varied. Temples may be brothels, bathhouses, theaters, or even quiet studios. Some shrines are hidden behind curtains, others wear their ecstasy openly with floral garlands, laughter, and music. The Divine is found wherever pleasure, beauty, and desire are honored.  
  
## Cultural Reception  
  
#### [[Aqualonde]]  
Clerics of Pegape walk freely but discreetly. While open indulgence is not as widespread as in the south, the elves of the Fauconwood respect the role of desire in artistic and personal growth. Pegape's temples here are often performance halls, featuring music, dance, and the aesthetics of beauty over carnal pleasure. Their form is most often shown as elven and androgynous.  
  
#### [[Avosa Tesota]]  
In the pirate haven of [[Avosa Tesota|Avosa]], Pegape is everywhere. Every brothel, smokehouse, pleasure barge, and opulent feast bears Their sign. Their priests serve as courtesans, mediators, poets, and celebrants, bridging gaps between communities through shared indulgence. Sex work is legal and respected, and it is not uncommon to hear Pegape's name praised before and after a moment of bliss. They are beloved, shameless, and honored.  
  
#### [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]  
Outlawed entirely. [[The Light of Alophia]] views Pegape's worship as dangerous distraction, a heretical seduction that draws the soul away from righteous purpose. Those found serving Them are publicly punished—and rarely seen again. Still, Pegape's worship persists in secret. Hidden chambers, coded prayers, and false sanctums keep the flame alive in the shadows.  
  
#### [[Shurevaire]]  
Pegape's worship in Shurevaire is quiet but enduring. While devotion to carnal or aesthetic pleasures is not a civic virtue, scholars of art, literature, and psychology frequently study Their cults. The Conservatory of Aesthetic Logic, a controversial philosophical guild, argues that the pursuit of pleasure is itself a form of spiritual exploration. Pegape's name is also invoked in private salons and masked revels, where ideas of identity, beauty, and gender are examined and expressed. In public, Their shrines are rare, but Their influence dances through every masquerade.  
  
#### [[Tarwa'kem]]  
Though no stranger to indulgence, [[Tarwa'kem]] approaches Pegape's worship with a transactional lens. Taverns, brothels, and pleasure parlors abound, but they serve a clear social purpose—relief for workers and rewards for the wealthy. The priests of Pegape are often viewed as servants, not spiritual equals. While no formal disrespect is allowed, true influence is rare. Still, even the richest merchant may whisper a prayer to the Carnal Celebrant after a long night.  
  
## In Art  
![[Pegape_Beyond_Innocence.webp]]  
*Beyond Innocence by Genesala Holabanise*

Pegape is the most fluidly depicted of the gods, Their form shaped by desire, identity, and beauty. They may appear:  
  
As a lithe elven dancer with skin like moonlight and a body adorned in silk  
  
A curvaceous human woman laughing in mid-embrace  
  
A muscular man bathed in candlelight and covered in rose petals  
  
A winged and feathered being, glittering and impossible to pin down  
  
A faceless lover, wearing the mask of whoever you want most  
  
Common across all depictions:  
  
Floral motifs—roses, peonies, orchids  
  
A soft, cascading veil or draped silks  
  
A sensual posture, mid-step, mid-glance, or mid-kiss  
  
Some artists even claim Pegape appeared to them in a dream and posed for their work.

## Notable Holy Sites  
**[[The Silken Kiss]]**, [[Avosa Tesota]]  
An opulent brothel and temple in one, each room themed after a different facet of desire. Known for its live performances, dreamwine, and priests trained in emotional as well as physical healing. Ships arriving in Avosa often make a stop here before unloading cargo.
