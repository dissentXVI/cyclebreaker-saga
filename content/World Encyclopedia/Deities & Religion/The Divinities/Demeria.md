## Alias(es)  
*The Harvest Lady, She of the Crook and Seed, Mother of the Living Earth*  
#### Element  
Earth  
#### Cleric Domain  
Nature Domain  
#### Associated Domains  
The Land and Wilds, Harvest, Families  
#### Materials of Reverence  
Since the extinction of the nostos flower in 1800 ApN during the Storms of Y’Set, Demeria’s preferred offering has become earth fertile enough to sustain life. In agrarian regions, a third of every field—“Demeria’s Share”—is rotated and ritually dedicated to Her, ensuring blessing through seasonal cycles. In harsher regions, red clay or shaped earth in the form of bowls and pitchers is used as symbolic tribute.  
  
## Summary  
Demeria governs the growing earth and the hearths that draw life from it. She teaches mortals the rhythm of sowing and reaping, of scarcity and abundance, and of enduring hardship through community and care. Families of all kinds—traditional, chosen, or estranged—fall under Her domain. She brings grain and shelter, flocks and firewood. Her staff doubles as a crook for animals and a walking stick for matriarchs. Demeria is not indulgent, but she is faithful. Her seasons are stern but instructive: all things must grow, must wither, and may return.  
  
## Spread  
Demeria’s worship is most rooted in Aqualonde, but echoes of Her cult persist across all Halinor, especially in farming communities and among druids. Her rites are performed at planting, harvest, birth, and even funerals—whenever life transitions through nature’s rhythm. In frontier settlements, especially among refugees and displaced peoples, She is often the first and last deity invoked.  
  
## Cultural Reception  
  
#### [[Aqualonde]]  
She is revered as the land’s living soul. Aqualonde’s architectural and cultural styles reflect Her influence—homes built into the forest, stone paths following natural contours, and offerings laid beneath ancient trees. Druids and sages devoted to Her maintain the delicate balance between wilderness and society. Her greatest temple, carved into the side of the Chilneauan range, is a marvel of integrated stonework and arboreal reverence.  
  
#### [[Avosa Tesota]]  
The wild peninsula has forged a complicated bond with Demeria. She is seen here as a fierce, primal mother—both nurturing and unforgiving. Druids in the outer provinces are storm-kissed and survival-minded, their rites more visceral than contemplative. Within Avosan cities, especially along the coast, Demeria is honored through family traditions. Offerings take the form of fired clay vessels, representing food and home.  
  
#### [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]  
Demeria is not openly worshipped. Carinthia’s frozen soil resists Her gifts, and the Church of Alophia deems the veneration of wild, unaudited growth a form of spiritual indiscipline. Yet among outlanders and secret gardeners, prayers are whispered to Her beneath the snows. Hunters invoke Her when feeding their households, though Her shrines remain rare.  
  
#### [[D'yadya]]  
She is still deeply revered among the displaced, especially in the Sift and its kin-clans. In refugee homes, tokens of brown fabric or clay serve as symbolic hearths. Demeria is remembered as a founder-queen, protector of ancestral lands lost to time and empire. Her name is invoked when bread is broken, children are fed, or past lives are mourned.  
  
#### [[Shurevaire]]  
Demeria is treated more as an anthropological or ecological figure than a religious one. However, agrarian colleges study Her teachings as metaphors for cyclical governance and ethical stewardship. Her likeness appears in civic art within farming communes or cooperative estates.  
  
#### [[Tarwa'kem]]  
Despite its desert climate, Tarwa’kem finds ways to honor the Harvest Lady. In regions rich with red clay, Her altars are adorned with compact mounds of sunbaked earth. Urban shrines resemble kitchens or storage vaults, evoking the home’s heart rather than the soil itself. She is often honored during childbirth, marriage, or the founding of a new household.  
  
## In Art  
Demeria’s depictions vary sharply by region.  
  
In D’yadya and Tarwa’kem, she is honored in her original form: a bronzed matriarch with steel-grey hair, clad in a brightly patterned thawb and margruna. She is tall, broad-shouldered, commanding in presence, with a staff-crook at her side and baskets slung over her arms.  
  
Among the elven cultures of Aqualonde, she appears as a more meditative figure: slender but strong, cloaked in layered earth-toned robes, her green hair wild as springtime vines. Her eyes are closed in half-smile serenity, and flowers bloom from her footprints.  
  
In Avosa, she is often shown waist-deep in vines, lifting a child or animal above her head, smiling through dirt-smudged skin and storm-frayed cloth.  
  
Her iconography blends strength with patience—always surrounded by food, family, or flocks. Whether nurturing or solemn, Demeria is depicted as the one who stays through every storm.

## Notable Holy Sites  
**The Roots of Mercy**, [[D'yadya]] *(now scattered)*  
Once a vast orchard and sanctuary in western Halinor, now only fragments remain in exile. Refugees recreate it with potted saplings, traveling from camp to camp to teach its stories and preserve the dying fruitline that once fed their nation.
