## Alias(es)  
*The Moon Maiden, The Silver Mother, She Who Watches, Luna's Bloom*  
#### Element  
Ice  
#### Cleric Domain  
Twilight Domain  
#### Associated Domains  
The Moon, Love, Women, Children  
#### Materials of Reverence  
Petra's sacred offerings are made in silver, whether solid or in its liquid form known as quicksilver. These may take the form of:  
  
Moon-shaped charms or rings  
  
Etched silver plates inscribed with prayers  
  
Liquid silver dripped into cool water under moonlight  
  
Silver-inlaid tokens passed between lovers or children and their parents  
  
Moonstone and pale blue or white flowers are also common at her shrines, especially during full moons.  
  
## Summary  
Petra is the most beloved of the Divinities—goddess of love in all its forms, from the innocent affection of childhood to the enduring strength of parental care and the fierce heat of romantic longing. She watches over women, children, lovers, and the vulnerable. She is said to bless the faithful through moonlight, guiding the lost, soothing the grieving, and protecting those in need.  
  
Though not always seen as a god of "importance" in the grand schemes of war, statecraft, or invention, her influence is gentle but profound. To be loved is the first lesson of life; to give love, the final.  
  
Her worship is often quiet, intimate, and heartfelt—a whispered prayer, a lullaby, a silver charm left on a pillow. Yet during the full moon, her name is sung in wild ecstasy by her followers who dance barefoot under the stars.  
  
## Spread  
Petra's faith is nearly universal, though rarely institutional. Her influence spreads through childhood tales, songs, lullabies, and small acts of affection. Shrines are common in bedrooms, gardens, and beside cradles, often shaped like crescent moons or decorated with feathers, silver dust, or ice-colored flowers.  
  
The traveling Moonlit Revel, a nomadic cult of women and children, keeps her worship alive in the wild places. Wherever the moon shines, Petra is close.  
  
## Cultural Reception  
  
#### [[Aqualonde]]  
Petra's worship is woven into seasonal and emotional cycles. Children are taught of her as a gentle guardian; young lovers ask her blessing beneath full moons; parents pray to her when illness strikes. Her Moonlit Revel often wanders [[the Fauconwood]], welcomed by elves who respect the goddess's gentle philosophy. Artists depict her in different life stages tied to the lunar phases.  
  
#### [[Avosa Tesota]]  
Petra is deeply beloved among Avosan bards, sailors, and street folk. Her shrines—often small and well-tended—dot back alleys, brothels, and balconies. Though not dominant in public worship, no Divinity inspires more songs. Her priestesses serve as midwives, matchmakers, and storytellers.  
  
#### [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]]  
Though [[The Light of Alophia]] discourages open veneration of other Divinities, Petra is tolerated more than most. Her worship is "acceptable" so long as it remains confined to parental affection and childhood stories. Yet Carinthian children still whisper prayers to the moon, and many soldiers wear her silver charms near their hearts beneath cold plate.  
  
#### [[Shurevaire]]  
Petra is gently venerated but rarely formalized in [[Shurevaire]]. Many scholars recall Her teachings with fondness from childhood, and the Moon Maiden is frequently cited in literature, lullabies, and romantic poetry. While She has no major temples, smaller shrines exist in student quarters, maternity wards, and orphanages. The Moonlit Revel is known to visit the outer gardens of the city during their circuit, where they are welcomed more as harmless mystics than true prophets. Some professors debate whether Petra's influence is emotional resonance or divine archetype—a debate that only deepens their quiet reverence.  
  
#### [[Tarwa'kem]]  
Love is often blurred with duty in this merchant kingdom. Petra's worship persists, but is rarely prioritized. Parents often couch their love in terms of expectation or responsibility, and Petra's message of unconditional affection is seen as childish or idealistic. Still, many nurses, wetnurses, and midwives quietly keep her shrines, particularly in the Dustway.  
  
## In Art  
![[Petra_Under_Her_Pale_Light.webp]]  
*Under her Pale Light by Jui Kan*

Petra is depicted in three distinct forms corresponding to the lunar cycle:  
  
Crescent Moon – A hopeful youth with pale blue eyes and windswept hair. She represents innocent love and first affections.  
  
Half Moon – A fierce and passionate adult, strong and alluring. This is romantic love, powerful, inspiring, sometimes dangerous.  
  
Full Moon – A serene and wise mother, robed in silver and carrying a pale blue wolf pup. This is parental love, enduring and selfless.  
  
Artists sometimes render her as the moon itself, casting silver light across a dark canvas. Her wolf companion, said to be a gift from [[Tyrachon]], appears often—watchful, loyal, and luminous.  
  
## Orders & Cults  
The Moonlit Revel – A nomadic group of women and children who perform ecstatic rituals beneath full moons. Their worship is seen as eccentric but harmless by some, liberating and beautiful by others. They serve as herbalists, bards, and caretakers as they roam.

## Notable Holy Sites  
**[[The Wolfstone]]**, [[Aqualonde]]    
A pale blue rock shaped like a reclining wolf, high on a wooded hill. The Moonlit Revel holds revels here during lunar holidays. Moonlight hits it only during the full moon's zenith.
