## Origin

Thessakor was summoned from the depths of death and decay by the lizardfolk of the Chilmire Bog, who sought to expand their territory through divine power. Guided — and likely manipulated — by [[Kyrichos]], they performed the rites that called the death god forth.

Thessakor manifested as a grotesquely regal, frog-like entity of immense stillness and rot: bloated and vast, crowned in a tangle of antler-like bark slick with mold and sap, enthroned not on carved stone but on growth itself — a seat of living decay at the heart of the bog. Around his throne, dozens of breathstone crystals lay spent and cracked in the soft earth, their once-green radiance entirely consumed. He was not yet at his full strength when Aspiria's Chosen reached him.

## Legacy

[[Aspiria's Chosen]] defeated Thessakor before he could grow powerful enough to threaten the wider world — the third time in history that a Paragon was contained before its destruction could reach a global scale, following the sacrifice of [[Velethoui Arsenault|Archmage Velethoui]] against [[Krisnarax, the Radiant Fury|Krisnarax]] and the Chosen's own defeat of [[Dagozog, the Galvanic Fury|Dagozog]]. The lizardfolk who summoned him were defeated alongside him.

Whether [[Kyrichos]] intended for the Paragon to be stopped so early, or whether this outcome was simply an inconvenience, remains an open question.
