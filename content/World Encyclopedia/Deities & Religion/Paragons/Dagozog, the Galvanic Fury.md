## Origin

Dagozog was summoned in the shadow of desperation and hubris. In the years following the draining of Sticaean Lake, goblin clans from the northern caves — driven by famine and the collapse of their home in the Kustaran Forest — sought new power through pneuma ritual. Inspired — perhaps manipulated — by [[Kyrichos]], they scoured breathstone from surrounding locations and beneath the lakebed, ultimately awakening something far more dangerous.

Dagozog was a god made for vengeance. He was engineered: a storm-forged entity of judgment and galvanic wrath, a construct given pneuma-fed consciousness, wrathful and cold. His aspect was that of a towering thunder-brute, crowned in stormlight and wreathed in lightning. He emerged as a divine judge meant to punish those who had brought imbalance to the land — namely, everyone.

## Legacy

Aspiria's Chosen's defeat of Dagozog was the second time in history a Paragon was contained before its destruction could reach a global scale, following the sacrifice of [[Archmage Velethoui]] against [[Krisnarax, the Radiant Fury|Krisnarax]]. To some, the Chosen are hailed as Scions of Good — champions touched by the Divine. To others, they are viewed with suspicion, even fear — mortals who dared to slay a god, and in doing so, may have upset a balance they do not fully understand.

Goblin survivors remain unraveled; some now view Aspiria's Chosen as thieves who stole their god's justice.
