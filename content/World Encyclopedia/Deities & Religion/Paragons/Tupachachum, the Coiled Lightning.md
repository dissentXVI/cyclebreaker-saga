## Origin

Tupachachum was not summoned through faith, but through folly.

It was the arrogance of Levin the Blue, a boastful Freeman and infamous plunderer of the sea, that stirred the storm. In his greed, he ransacked merfolk sanctuaries and looted sacred relics from the cliff-temples of Ramitas, never questioning the volatile pneuma sealed within. By accident — or perhaps by destiny — he completed a long-forgotten rite.

The result was Tupachachum, the Coiled Lightning: a vast sky-serpent of storm and wrath, crackling with lightning pneuma, cloaked in swirling thunderclouds. Once venerated by coastal and inland tribes as a bringer of divine judgment, Her followers had all but vanished — until Levin's desecration gave them what their prayers could not: a god reborn in fury.

She struck him down instantly. Levin was reduced to ash and ozone, and above his smoking remains, the sky turned silver-blue. The Paragon had arrived.

## Legacy

The Beach of Glass in [[Avosa Tesota]] remains a stark reminder of the Eel's wrath: a stretch of obsidian shoreline fused by divine lightning.

Modern research into Paragon influence on pneuma and cognition began here. The term "storm-madness" entered arcane lexicons as a lasting condition.

Sailors now invoke Her name not in prayer, but in curses: *"Coils take me," "By Her blue eye,"* and *"Struck by Tupachachum."*
