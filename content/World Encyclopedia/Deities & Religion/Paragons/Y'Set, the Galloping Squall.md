## Origin
Once a minor god of wind worshipped by the now-extinct sand minotaurs in the deep deserts of [[Tarwa'kem]], Y'Set might have passed into total obscurity were it not for a fringe cult of fanatics who rediscovered its rites. With no formal knowledge of Paragons prior to this incident, few recognized the significance of what was being summoned — until it was too late.

Y'Set was brought into full manifestation through forbidden rites performed in a remote canyon stronghold. Its form — a towering camel-headed monstrosity with serpentine tongues and feathered wings — emerged dragging with it unnatural winds, roaring storms, and the promise of erasure.

This event marked the first known Paragon manifestation in recorded Aspirian history: a being fueled by prayer and belief twisted into divine wrath.

## Legacy
The cult of Y'Set, though fractured, still persists. Their rituals are secret, their movements like wind through dunes — seen, but never grasped.

Some believe Y'Set's death was incomplete, that its pneuma lingers in the shifting storms of the desert, awaiting another breath to call it back.
