Dreadnoughts were colossal war machines created by the dwarves during their struggle against [[the Navarresean Empire]]. Forged of stone, steel, and ancestral craft, each dreadnought was powered not by gears alone but by the pneuma of fallen warriors, bound through necromancy to march once more. Within these towering suits, the dead fought a final battle, their souls fused to the machine until victory—or destruction—released them.

Terrifying on the battlefield, dreadnoughts were said to turn the tide of entire campaigns, shrugging off volleys of arrows and breaking elven legions beneath their iron steps. Yet their cost was immense. Each dreadnought consumed dozens of souls, binding them away from [[Siren]]’s [[Sea of Pneuma]] and denying them rest. Even the dwarves came to fear what they had created, seeing the suits as both miracle and blasphemy.

With the unification of the dwarven tribes and the founding of [[Tarwa’kem]], necromancy was outlawed and the dreadnoughts dismantled or buried. Only fragments remain—twisted armor-plates in forgotten vaults, half-buried frames beneath the sands. To this day, rumors persist of hidden dreadnoughts slumbering in sealed chambers, waiting for the day someone dares to wake them.
