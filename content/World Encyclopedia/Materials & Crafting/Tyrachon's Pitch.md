A resin harvested from the storm-oaks of the [[Gragato Jungle]], Tyrachon’s Pitch is famed among Avosan shipwrights for its ability to harden wood to stone-like resilience. Once refined and applied to hulls or masts, the pitch cures into a near-impervious seal, protecting vessels against both cannon fire and the endless salt of the sea. Its preparation is dangerous—raw pitch is caustic and volatile—and the precise methods of refinement are guarded secrets of Avosa’s naval guilds.

Named in honor of [[Tyrachon]], god of storms and seas, many sailors believe ships coated with the pitch carry his blessing and are less likely to founder in tempestuous waters. Beyond [[Avosa Tesota]], the resin is coveted as a naval trade good, though rarely sold in large quantities. More than a material, Tyrachon’s Pitch has become a symbol of Avosan independence: a reminder that their fleets are bound as much by craft and divine favor as by courage.
