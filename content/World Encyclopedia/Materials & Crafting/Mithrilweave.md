Developed in [[Aqualonde]] by [[the Sunweavers Guild]], Mithrilweave is a fabric spun from enchanted silk interlaced with powdered mithril. It drapes and flows like cloth but bears the strength of light mail, prized for garments that must balance elegance, protection, and enchantment. Mithrilweave naturally channels pneuma, making it especially effective for storing wards or enhancing spellwork.

The material is most often used in ceremonial robes, scholar’s mantles, and the discreet armor of diplomats or duelists. Its threads shimmer faintly under moonlight, a property attributed to the moon’s affinity with mithril itself. Due to the difficulty of producing it, Mithrilweave remains a symbol of prestige in [[Aqualonde]], and owning even a single robe is considered proof of high station. Outside [[The Fauconwood]], it is vanishingly rare, more often seen in reliquaries or as heirlooms than in trade.
