Atma is the unseen current of will and emotion that shapes reality in ways pneuma cannot. Unlike [[pneuma]], which flows tangibly through all living things and can be harnessed for spells or enchantments, Atma is born from the inner spirit—hope, despair, rage, devotion—and bends the world subtly but profoundly. Most scholars describe it as the breath of the soul, an echo of thought and feeling given weight.

Ordinary people rarely perceive Atma, though its influence is everywhere: a soldier standing unbroken against impossible odds, a grieving parent who somehow stirs life back into a dying child, a people united in faith who spark miracles without spellcraft. Such acts, small or grand, reveal Atma’s truth—that willpower itself can alter the weave of the world.

In Aspiria’s legends, Atma is often described as the key to resisting unraveling and even defying gods. Though difficult to study, many believe [[Aspiria]] herself responds not to ritual or command, but to the Atma carried within mortal hearts.
