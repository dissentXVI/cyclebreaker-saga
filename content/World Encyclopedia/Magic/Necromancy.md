Necromancy is the rare and taboo art of binding or commanding souls. In the ancient wars between [[Navarresean See|the Navarresean Empire]] and the dwarves, necromancy was refined into a terrible craft. Dwarven necromancers devised the [[Dreadnought|dreadnoughts]]—giant mechanized suits powered by the souls of fallen warriors, allowing the dead to march once more in defense of their people. These creations became symbols of defiance against the elves, but also of the terrible price paid to wage such wars.

With the unification of the dwarven tribes and the founding of [[Tarwa’kem]], necromancy was outlawed. The art was declared a violation of both divine law and ancestral honor, and its practice faded into secrecy. Today, necromancy survives only in whispers and hidden rites, practiced by outcasts and cults in the darkest corners of [[Halinor]]. Most cultures revile it, viewing the disturbance of souls as a crime against [[Siren ]]and an affront to the natural cycle of the [[Sea of Pneuma]].

Though nearly lost, the memory of the [[Dreadnought|dreadnoughts]] endures, a chilling reminder of the age when dwarves dared to bind their dead for one last battle.
