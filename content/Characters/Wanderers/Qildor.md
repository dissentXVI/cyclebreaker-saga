**Title**: Wandering Merchant

**Allegiance**: Himself

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Mid-40s

**Eyes**: Brown

**Hair**: Balding under his favorite hat

**Attire**: Sand-toned robe, weathered sash, and a coin-clinking vest of many secret pockets

**Home**: "Wherever the deal is," as he likes to say.

## Appearance

Quildor is built like a merchant wagon: round, sturdy, and rumbling with hidden surprises. His clothing is comfortably layered, functional for desert travel yet colorful enough to catch the eye. A maroon headwrap shades his face, and his ever-present satchels and lockboxes jingle with suspicious weight.

## Personality

Quildor is a fast-talking, opportunistic con man with an infectious laugh and a suspicious price tag. He'll swear he's your friend, call you by a nickname he made up seconds ago, and sell you a potion that "probably doesn't explode anymore." He never outright lies — he just neglects to clarify.

He loves gossip, golden trinkets, rare curiosities, and playing both sides of any local power struggle. He's never where he shouldn't be... until someone catches him there.

## Character History

Where Quildor comes from is anyone's guess — including Quildor's. His story changes depending on the region, audience, and weather. Sometimes he claims to be the son of a pirate king. Sometimes he insists he was trained by dwarves in [[Tarwa'kem]]. Sometimes he mumbles something about a girl in [[Aqualonde]] and changes the subject.

What is known is that he's been kicked out of at least two city-states, once disguised himself as a cleric to sell miracle balms, and has never actually completed a proper trade license application.

## Current Status

Seen recently in [[Tarwa'kem]], claiming to have an incredible deal for Frog. It has yet to prove itself to be.

Who knows where the coin will guide him next?

## Combat Style

Quildor avoids combat the way a cat avoids a bath — desperately and loudly. If cornered, he uses smoke bombs, flammable potions, blinding powders, and a surprising turn of speed for a man of his girth. He's not incapable, just highly motivated to run away.

Rumors suggest he once disarmed a bounty hunter with a collapsible umbrella and a bag of flour. He insists it was "mostly planned."

## Notable Relationships

**Everyone he's ever sold to** – Most owe him a punch, a debt, or a grudging thank-you.
