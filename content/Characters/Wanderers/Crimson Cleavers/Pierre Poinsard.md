## Appearance

Pierre is the embodiment of martial order: every edge of his armor polished, every hair in place. His chiseled jaw is matched by a meticulously trimmed mustache and a brow that rarely lifts in humor. Even amidst blizzards, his armor gleams — steel etched with the faded crest of House Vaillant, now defaced just enough to avoid treason. His cloak is notably absent, a sign of dishonorable discharge in the Knights Templar.

He carries himself like a man who expects the world to fall in line. Most of it does — or bleeds trying.

## Personality

Pierre is the kind of man who sharpens rules until they cut. Stern, honorable in his own fashion, and unyielding in the face of disorder, he considers most of the Cleavers beneath him — but useful. He disdains improvisation, loathes flamboyance, and tolerates chaos only when it serves strategy.

His pride is enormous, but not loud. He speaks like a general addressing a field of statues. He is not cruel — he is efficient, and expects the same from all who serve under him.

## Character History

Once a decorated captain of House Vaillant's 3rd Banner Company, Pierre believed Carinthia's stance against the dragons was too reactive. His solution: to lead a charge outside the city walls against a prowling wyrm. The maneuver failed spectacularly — the dragon flew over his troops, entered the city unopposed, and razed a civilian district.

His punishment was swift: stripped of title and exiled for dereliction of duty.

Disgraced but unbroken, Pierre wandered Halinor until [[Sharl van der Cleev|Sharl]] found him. Unlike the nobles of [[Heldenheim]], Sharl saw purpose in Pierre's discipline and gave him a new command — not bound by politics or sanctity, but by strength.

## Legacy

Pierre Poinsard died as he lived — standing his ground. He was killed by Aspiria's Chosen in the final confrontation at [[Sharl van der Cleev|Sharl]]'s Fauconwood stronghold. He died in formation, doing his duty for the sovereign he had chosen. There is a grim dignity in that, even if he would not have recognized it as such.

## Combat Style

Pierre is a tactician's warrior, a master of disciplined formations and adaptable weaponry. He enters battle with a halberd, perfect for reach and cavalry-breaking, and switches to a knight's longsword and shield when commanding the front line directly.

He prefers measured engagements — shield walls, pincer strikes, field positioning. No flair, no flourish. Just clean victory.

If forced into a duel, he fights like a fortress — immovable, methodical, and terrifyingly hard to break.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Sharl van der Cleev]]** – Mutual respect. Sharl saw him as a weapon. Pierre saw Sharl as a sovereign. He served him to the end.

**[[Megrind the Monsoon]]** – Strategic alliance. Pierre trusted his spells, but not his motives. They shared contempt for the undisciplined.
