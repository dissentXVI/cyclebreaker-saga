**Title**: Spy of the Crimson Cleavers

**Allegiance**: [[Crimson Cleavers]]

**Race**: Half-Elf

**Gender**: Female

**Age**: Mid-40s

**Eyes**: Deep brown-black, wide and reflective like a stag's

**Hair**: Matted brown, shoulder-length, tangled in knots and braids

**Attire**: Cloaks of bark-hued wool, soaked furs, and woven plant fibers

**Home**: Formerly wherever the Cleavers encamped; the Fauconwood

**Status**: Deceased — killed by Aspiria's Chosen

## Appearance

Chauni is unsettling to behold — not for scars or deformity, but for how wrong she seems in stillness. Her wide, glossy eyes resemble those of a deer or horse, reflecting firelight and moonlight alike in unnatural ways. They rarely blink, and never look away. Her stare is soft, but never warm — always watching, absorbing, calculating.

Her robes are a tangle of soaked fibers, clinging furs, and stitched hide. Her hair grows wild and feral, often tied back with thorn-vine or scraps of linen. A carved staff of rootwood never leaves her side, wrapped in old string, feather bundles, and knotted charms. She does not smell of blood or steel, but of wet soil, rot, and crushed leaves.

## Personality

Quiet, cold, and preternaturally aware. Chauni rarely speaks, and when she does, it's to state a truth no one else dares name. She does not bluff. She does not flatter. She simply observes.

While others among the Cleavers revel in performance or power, Chauni remains apart — part oracle, part predator. She reveres old powers, the primal chaos of nature, and the justice of tooth and root. She served [[Sharl van der Cleev|Sharl]] because he understood that fear is the truest law — not because he inspired her.

There is no cruelty in Chauni. Only inevitability.

## Character History

Born of obscurity, Chauni lived in the moss-drowned wilds of the Fauconwood, where old druidic cults whispered to soil and stone. Her mother was a trap-setter. Her father, unknown. She grew up speaking to trees before she ever spoke to people.

[[Sharl van der Cleev|Sharl]] found her during a solo contract to kill a man-eater in the woods. He returned days later — changed, bloody, and with Chauni at his side. She had saved him, healed him, and marked him. Since then, she had never left his shadow, serving as his watcher and his warden of omens.

## Legacy

Chauni died at [[Sharl van der Cleev|Sharl]]'s side in the Fauconwood — the same forest that birthed her and shaped them both. Whatever she communed with in those woods, whatever nameless force she carried her bundle of his blood-soaked hair to pray over, it did not save her. She died where she belonged.

## Combat Style

Chauni is a battlefield ghost. As a druid, she controls the terrain with creeping vines, sudden mists, and choking spores. Her magic is old — not flashy, but relentless. Fogbanks roll at her feet. Roots claw from underfoot. Beasts emerge from nothing to gnaw and vanish again.

She avoids direct conflict, instead overwhelming enemies with control and misdirection. If she is forced to fight, she uses her staff to keep enemies at bay, or transforms into an owl, fox, or serpent — forms meant to escape, infiltrate, or strike unseen.

Every spell she casts smells like damp moss. Every strike feels like nature reclaiming what was borrowed.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Sharl van der Cleev]]** – The only one she ever followed willingly. She saved him once and served him until the end.

**[[Frog Gulgalla|Quiss]]** – Distrusted. Chauni always saw her as weak-willed and emotionally compromised. She was not wrong — and not entirely right.

## Secrets

Chauni communed regularly with something in the Fauconwood — a force or presence even she could not name.

She carried a bundle of [[Sharl van der Cleev|Sharl]]'s blood-soaked hair from the day she saved him. She prayed over it each new moon. It is buried with her now, in the forest where they both died.
