**Title**: Master Tinker of the Southern Kula

**Allegiance**: The Southern Kula

**Race**: Tiefling

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Elderly

**Eyes**: Grey

**Hair**: Grey/white, perpetually mussed

**Attire**: Well-worn craftsman's clothes, perpetually smudged with grease and tool dust; thick spectacles; belt hung with small tools

**Home**: The Southern Kula

## Appearance

Bony and small even at his physical peak, age has further wizened Volkar Hecate into something that looks less like a person and more like a complaint given form. His skin is a deep red and his white hair sticks up in whatever direction it pleases. His left horn was severed flush at the skull long ago; the right juts prominently from his temple, unbroken and slightly crooked. He cannot see a hand in front of his face without his thick spectacles, which he wears at all times and peers through with an expression best described as a scirk — somewhere between a scowl and a smirk, permanently set, deeply unwelcoming.

His numerous wrinkles do not suggest wisdom so much as accumulated grievance. He is the sort of man children are warned about before they ever meet him.

## Personality

Volkar is a misanthrope in the truest sense — not because he hates people, but because he has spent decades preferring the company of gears, solder, and silence. He is terse, prickly, and has no patience for social niceties. He will tell you exactly what he thinks, and what he thinks is usually unflattering.

But underneath the crust lies something more complicated. Volkar is a man shaped by grief and guilt — grief for the wife he lost, guilt for the daughter he failed to raise. He does not talk about either. He works instead, and has worked so long and so well that his craft is the most respected thing about him in the kula, even if his person is the most avoided.

He was a classic introvert long before loss made him a recluse. He was always more comfortable with mechanisms than people — always more certain of what he could fix than what he could say.

## Character History

Volkar grew up in the Southern Kula with a gift for tinkering that showed itself early and never left. Shy and self-contained, he spent his formative years perfecting his craft rather than cultivating friendships — until, somehow, an irrepressibly warm and social woman decided he was worth the effort. Their relationship was a study in opposites: she brought him out of his shell enough to function, and he grounded her with quiet steadiness. He loved her in the way that introverts love — completely, without announcement, and without any real preparation for what her absence would mean.

When she was killed — in an incident that also cost him his left horn — he was left with a newborn daughter and no idea how to be a father. He tried, in the way a man tries when he is drowning: badly, and with increasing distance. The kula did what kulas do — they stepped in, surrounded the child with warmth and community, and raised her where he could not. His daughter survived. Volkar retreated further inward.

The daughter eventually became an adult, and the estrangement calcified into something neither of them has found a way to name. He loves her. He has never adequately shown it.

## Current Status

For the past ten years, Volkar has been the primary caretaker of his granddaughter, [[Ariesa Lightbringer|Ariesa]] — passed to him not through choice, exactly, but through the consensus of the kula that she was too wild for anyone else's patience. What was meant as a last resort has quietly become the most important thing in Volkar's remaining years.

Ariesa has thawed something in him. She reminds him of her grandmother — the energy, the fearlessness, the refusal to treat his scirk as a stop sign. He will not say this aloud. He may not even fully admit it to himself. But he takes better care of her than he took care of her mother, and some part of him hopes that counts for something.

He remains the Southern Kula's master tinker, his skills unquestioned and his company avoided. *"Behave or I'll tell Volkar"* is a threat tiefling parents deploy to reliable effect.

## Combat Style

Volkar does not fight. He builds things that make fighting unnecessary — or considerably more dangerous for the other party.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Ariesa Lightbringer]]** – His granddaughter and, unexpectedly, his second chance. She irritates him constantly. He would do anything for her.

**His Daughter** – Estranged. He failed her when she needed him most, and both of them carry that. He loves her. He doesn't know how to say it.

**The Southern Kula** – They respect his work and keep their distance. He prefers it that way. Mostly.

## Secrets

He keeps a small portrait of his wife hidden in the lining of his tool belt. He has not shown it to anyone — not even Ariesa. But sometimes, when she laughs a certain way, he touches the spot where it sits.
