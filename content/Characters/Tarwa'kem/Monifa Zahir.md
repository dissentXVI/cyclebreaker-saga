**Title**: Youngest Daughter of House Zahir

**Allegiance**: [[House Zahir]]

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Female

**Age**: 12

**Eyes**: Brown

**Hair**: Honey blond, often braided or pinned up

**Attire**: Custom-tailored noblewear in golds, creams, and reds, always adorned with subtle healing charms and crafted embellishments

**Home**: [[Providence]], [[Tarwa'kem]]

## Appearance

Monifa Zahir is a delicate but radiant presence — her build is petite, her features a soft blend of her parents'. Despite her illness, she carries herself with quiet poise, aided by a walking staff designed more like a royal accessory than a crutch. Her honey-blond hair, unusual for a Zahir, gives her an ethereal presence in the halls of [[Providence]]. Her clothes, though beautiful, are chosen for comfort and lined with medicinal silk. She is always watched over, never alone.

## Personality

Monifa is wise beyond her years. Soft-spoken but keenly perceptive, she often sees through adult posturing and responds with knowing calm. While she has moments of frustration and sadness — usually when her condition flares up — she remains the cherished heart of her family. She listens more than she speaks, and when she does speak, everyone listens. Monifa adores stories, especially tales of ancient heroes and faraway lands, and she clings to hope with a tenacity that defies her failing body.

## Character History

Monifa was born during a period of rising fortune for the Zahir family, but her health has always been fragile. Over the years, her family spared no expense seeking a cure, from ancestral remedies to imported treatments, yet nothing has alleviated her suffering. She is often confined to her chambers or the family gardens, her body frail and easily exhausted. Despite this, she remains close to her sisters — particularly [[Laena Zahir|Laena]], who always made time to read to her, play gentle games, or simply sit beside her in silence.

## Current Status

Monifa's condition is worsening. The finest healers in [[Tarwa'kem]] and beyond have been consulted, but none have diagnosed or reversed her affliction. She grows increasingly tired each day, and while her mind remains sharp, her strength fades. She is aware that [[Laena Zahir|Laena]] is gone — she was never fooled by the "study abroad" story — but she doesn't speak of it, choosing instead to trust in her sister's promise to return.
