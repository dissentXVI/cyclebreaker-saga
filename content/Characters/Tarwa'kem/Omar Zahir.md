**Title**: Patriarch of House Zahir

**Allegiance**: [[House Zahir]]

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Early 70s

**Eyes**: Brown

**Hair**: Silver-white, full beard, kept short and clean

**Attire**: Modest noble robes in crimson and ivory with understated gold, always featuring an amulet of balanced scales or a closed coin pouch

**Home**: [[Providence]], [[Tarwa'kem]]

## Appearance

Omar Zahir is an image of aged dignity — tall for his generation, square-shouldered, and still possessing the sharp gaze of a man who once dealt in danger. His smile is disarming and warm, but his posture is calculated. Every line on his face is a roadmap of decisions made in secret. Though officially retired from public life, he remains a respected elder, his counsel sought by family and allies alike.

## Personality

Omar is quiet, deliberate, and deeply introspective. He lives with gentle humor and grandfatherly grace. His greatest pride is his family's elevation. Omar believes deeply in legacy — not merely bloodline, but what one leaves behind in deeds, memory, and coin.

## Character History

It was Omar who first lifted the Zahir name from the merchant class to nobility. The circumstances surrounding this rise are spoken of with awe — his medicinal practice soared in popularity in [[Tarwa'kem]] and beyond. Since then, Omar has largely retreated from public view, choosing instead to guide the family from within. He taught his son [[Oman Zahir|Oman]] the importance of measured ambition, and he keeps a watchful, if distant, eye on his grandchildren.

Omar once took [[Laena Zahir|Laena]], his eldest granddaughter, to the temple of [[Siren]] in secret. It was a rare moment of vulnerability from him — speaking to her not of piety, but of legacy and the two inevitabilities of life: death, and inheritance.

## Current Status

Omar resides in quiet semi-retirement in the Zahir estate. He walks the gardens, reads daily reports, and offers counsel only when asked — but when he speaks, the room listens. He remains an influential figure among elder nobles and respected even by his rivals. While his public role has diminished, his influence within the family is as strong as ever.
