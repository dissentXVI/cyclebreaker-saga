**Title**: Sultan of [[Tarwa'kem]]

**Allegiance**: [[The Karo'kem Dynasty]], [[Tarwa'kem]]

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: *Deceased; would have been mid-40s at time of death*

**Eyes**: Dark brown

**Hair**: Black, close-cropped, with the beginnings of silver at the temples

**Attire**: Royal vestments in deep gold and midnight blue, worked with solar motifs and ancestral symbols in lapis thread; he favored cleaner, less embellished cuts than ceremony demanded, but never abandoned the dynasty's colors

**Home**: [[Royal Palace]], [[Kemtabi Shelf]], [[Tarwa'kem]]

## Appearance

Shakir Karo'kem was a man of compact, grounded presence — not imposing in the way that power often performs itself, but steady in a way that commanded rooms without demanding them. He had his daughter's dark eyes and, according to those who knew them both, her directness of expression. He was rarely seen in full ceremonial dress outside of formal occasions, preferring attire that allowed him to move through the city without theater. He visited the [[Ash Ward]] and the [[Shai Fields]] regularly, and the people who saw him there remember a sultan who looked like he actually wanted to be there.

## Personality

Shakir was pragmatic, warm, and sharper than he let on. He governed through relationship rather than decree where possible, preferring to understand the interests of those he negotiated with — including [[the Monetarist Council]] — before moving against them. He was not naive about the council's ambitions, but he believed that durable authority required buy-in rather than suppression. His elevation of [[Omar Zahir]] as a trusted ally and frequent Palace guest was characteristic of this approach: he built loyalty personally, one relationship at a time.

He was known for his humor, his patience, and an occasional stubbornness that his advisors learned to read as a signal that a decision had already been made.

## Character History

Shakir ascended to the sultanate in his late twenties, inheriting a city whose council had grown considerably more powerful during his predecessor's long illness. His early reign was spent carefully reclaiming ground — not through confrontation, but through the slow accumulation of personal credibility and popular affection that made removing him politically costly. By his mid-thirties he had stabilized the relationship between the throne and the council into something workable, if never fully comfortable.

His marriage produced one child: [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]], born in 4003 ApN. [[Nena Karo'kem|Nena]]'s death in childbirth devastated him. He raised [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] with deliberate attention, aware that she would inherit whatever he built — and whatever he failed to protect.

He was assassinated in 4008 ApN, five years after [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]]'s birth. The official account attributes his death to Vociran operatives, a conclusion that served several interests and was never seriously investigated. [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] was five years old.

## Current Status

Deceased (4008 ApN). His memory is honored throughout [[Tarwa'kem]], particularly in the lower districts where his visits and reforms left lasting impressions. [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] governs in conscious continuation of his approach, though the political landscape he left her is considerably more dangerous than the one he inherited.

## Notable Relationships

[[Neema Karo'kem]] – His daughter and sole heir. He shaped her directly — her instinct to visit the lower city, her belief that a ruler must be seen, her certainty that the council's authority is not the same as legitimate authority, all of it traceable to him.

[[Omar Zahir]] – A trusted ally and personal friend. [[Shakir Karo'kem|Shakir]] gave [[Omar Zahir|Omar]] his own rooms in the [[Royal Palace]], so frequently was he in residence. Their relationship was genuine and is remembered warmly by both families — making what followed considerably darker.

[[The Monetarist Council]] – A necessary friction. Shakir managed the council effectively without ever fully trusting it, and his death removed the only figure capable of holding it in check.

## Secrets

Sultan Shakir was not killed by Vocirans. He was poisoned by [[Amasis Ramses]] using a method derived from the Zahir family's own poisoning tradition — the same craft that [[Omar Zahir]] had long practiced in service to the crown, now turned against it. Whether [[Omar Zahir|Omar]] ever learned the truth, and what it cost him if he did, has never surfaced. The knowledge sits buried beneath thirteen years of council governance and a narrative that suited everyone who benefited from Shakir's absence.
