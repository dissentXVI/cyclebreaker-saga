**Title**: General of the [[Gilded Legion]]; Member of the [[Monetarist Council]]

**Allegiance**: Sultana [[Neema Karo'kem]], the [[Gilded Legion]], and the D'yadyan diaspora

**Race**: Human (D'yadyan)

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Late 40s

**Eyes**: Brown

**Hair**: Silver-streaked blonde, long and often tied back

**Attire**: Heavy ceremonial armor of brass and red lacquer, engraved with stylized sunbursts and arena motifs; off-duty, he favors simple desert-worn fabrics and military sashes

**Home**: Official residence near the [[Kemtabi Shelf]]; visits [[Misrata]] often in secret

## Appearance

Tarik cuts a commanding figure — broad-shouldered, weathered, and ever-alert. His presence is forged from battle cheers and bloodied sand. Scars lace his arms and back like the chapters of a warrior's memoir, but his poise is refined, grounded in honor. His armor is polished, but his hands are calloused. He walks like a man used to carrying more than his weight.

## Personality

Loyal. Grounded. Reluctantly political. Tarik does not seek titles or accolades, only purpose. He is loyal to [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] not because of politics, but because she believed in him when no one else did. He speaks plainly, prefers action over posturing, and treats his soldiers like brothers and sisters in arms.

He dislikes the scheming of the [[Monetarist Council]], but recognizes the power of position. His presence is a symbol — that the lowborn can rise, and the forgotten still have value. Despite his fame, he remains humble, often uncomfortable with praise and more at ease in a barracks than a ballroom.

## Character History

Tarik Tazi was once a refugee — one of many displaced [[D'yadya|D'yadyans]] who fled the Siege of the White Sands. Arriving in [[Tarwa'kem]] with nothing, he found work as a pit fighter in [[Meninos' Theater]], where his strength, honor, and silent rage made him a legend. Over time, his arena victories gave him not just fame, but wealth and influence.

Among his earliest admirers was Sultana [[Neema Karo'kem]], then a young heir to the throne. She saw in him not just entertainment — but potential. With her backing, he turned his prize money into assets, and eventually purchased a seat on the [[Monetarist Council]], shocking the elites.

When Neema rose to power, she named him General of the [[Gilded Legion]]. Though many scoffed at a former gladiator leading the military, Tarik quickly silenced them with discipline, integrity, and victories in training reforms. He remains deeply respected by soldiers, and quietly feared by nobles.

## Current Status

Tarik serves as both General and councilor, walking the line between soldier and statesman. He commands the [[Gilded Legion]], [[Tarwa'kem]]'s standing military force, and advises [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] on matters of security and civil unrest. He has taken a special interest in maintaining order around [[Misrata]], defending the D'yadyan community from exploitation — particularly from figures like [[Ngor Azizi]].

Though not a politician by nature, he uses his position carefully — favoring bold truths over quiet compromise. He is the only one who knows of the Sultana's plan to send adventurer envoys on her behalf.

## Combat Style

Tarik fights with measured brutality. He rarely strikes first, preferring to let opponents reveal themselves. His arena background honed his instinct for reading movement, exploiting overextension, and striking precisely when it matters most. He is not flashy — his style is about presence, pressure, and commanding space.

In battle, he becomes an anchor — holding lines, protecting allies, and smashing through confusion with clean, devastating blows. He plants his feet like a wall, letting enemies crash against him. The longer the fight, the more dangerous he becomes.

## Notable Relationships

[[Neema Karo'kem]] – His closest bond. She gave him a future; he would lay down his life for her without hesitation. Their trust is absolute.

[[Ngor Azizi]] – Barely concealed hatred. Tarik despises Azizi's exploitation of D'yadyan labor and has considered confronting him directly.

[[Amasis Ramses]] – Wary respect. They rarely interact, but Tarik recognizes his cleverness and watches him like a hawk.

The [[Gilded Legion]] – Tarik treats his soldiers with dignity. Many come from low or refugee origins, and see him as proof they matter.

## Secrets

Has funded multiple quiet initiatives to place skilled D'yadyans into trade schools and temples under assumed names.

Has started to question whether the Sultana is truly safe — too many eyes watch her, even within the council.
