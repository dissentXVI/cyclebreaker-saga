**Title**: Owner of the [[Kustara Consortium]]

**Allegiance**: Himself, the Consortium, and the unrelenting expansion of profit

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Early 60s

**Eyes**: Deep brown

**Hair**: Salt-and-pepper, cropped close; thick beard

**Attire**: Lavish kaftans and industrial-embroidered robes layered with gold insignia of his enterprises; his chestpiece always bears the mark of the Consortium

**Home**: [[Providence]], [[Tarwa'kem]]

## Appearance

Ngor Azizi is a mountain of a man — both physically and politically. His presence demands attention, with broad shoulders, a barrel chest, and layers of gold that rival any royal display. His voice carries like a merchant's auction bell: loud, confident, and impossible to ignore. He favors rich reds and blacks, and walks with the stride of a man who has never been told "no" without consequences.

## Personality

Brash. Strategic. Ruthlessly ambitious. Azizi embodies the industrialist class in its rawest form. He believes wealth is the only measure of worth and sees the city-state as a chessboard — and himself as the only player who understands the game. Where [[Amasis Ramses]] is quiet and precise, Azizi is loud and blunt, a man who announces his threats before making good on them. He surrounds himself with opportunists and bulldozes through bureaucracy with charisma and coin.

## Character History

Ngor Azizi built the Kustara Mining Company from a modest desert quarry into one of [[Tarwa'kem]]'s largest sources of raw material. When he realized his influence could exceed mere industry, he expanded — most recently forming the Kustaran Logging Company, securing rights to ancient woodlands near [[D'yadya|D'yadyan]] territory.

He has frequently been accused of exploiting refugee labor, violating environmental treaties, and bribing city officials — none of which have ever been proven. His open disdain for the [[Monetarist Council]]'s old guard has made him a disruptive force, yet his wealth keeps him immune to reprimand. He views the council as a battlefield, and [[Amasis Ramses]] as the only true rival worth his time.

## Current Status

Azizi continues to expand the Consortium's reach while lobbying aggressively for trade rights, military contracts, and expansion into the [[Ash Ward]]. He publicly opposes Sultana [[Neema Karo'kem]]'s policies, including her embargo of [[Vociterra]], arguing they harm "Tarwa'kem's economic sovereignty." He has gained significant influence among middle-tier merchants and industrial guilds, though the elite remain wary of his vulgarity and volatility.
