**Title**: Sultana-Consort of [[Tarwa'kem]]

**Allegiance**: [[The Karo'kem Dynasty]]

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Female

**Age**: *Deceased; would have been early 40s*

**Eyes**: Dark brown

**Hair**: Black, thick, worn long

**Attire**: Layered robes in midnight blue and warm gold, embroidered with solar and ancestral motifs in the Karo'kem tradition; she wore the dynasty's colors with the ease of someone who had earned them rather than inherited them, and favored adornments of hammered gold over finery

**Home**: [[Royal Palace]], [[Kemtabi Shelf]], [[Tarwa'kem]]

## Appearance

Nena Karo'kem is known primarily through portraits commissioned before her death and the memories of those who served the Palace during her years there. By those accounts she was striking — confident in bearing, unhurried in movement, with the kind of presence that suggested she had decided long ago not to make herself smaller for anyone's comfort. [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] is said to resemble her.

## Personality

By all surviving accounts, Nena was formidable. She was intelligent, politically attentive, and not inclined toward the decorative role that a sultan's consort was expected to occupy. She involved herself in civic matters, formed her own opinions on council affairs, and made those opinions known to [[Shakir Karo'kem|Shakir]] — and, occasionally, to others. Those who served under her describe a woman of warmth and exacting standards in equal measure. Those who found her inconvenient describe her less generously, though they describe her less often.

## Character History

Nena came from a respected Tarwa'kin family with long civic ties and married Sultan [[Shakir Karo'kem|Shakir]] in her mid-twenties. Their partnership was by most accounts a genuine one — she was not a figure of ceremony but of function, her influence on the Sultan's governance real if informal. She was pregnant with [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] when she died in 4003 ApN.

The official record lists her cause of death as complications in childbirth. The midwife present that night was paid to ensure she bled out. Who paid the midwife, and on whose instruction, has never been established. The question was not pursued. [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] survived.

## Current Status

Deceased (4003 ApN). Her image is maintained in the [[Royal Palace]] and appears on certain civic commemorations. [[Shakir Karo'kem|Shakir]] never remarried. [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] did not grow up with stories about her mother so much as silences where those stories should have been — a shape of absence that she has never quite filled in.

## Notable Relationships

[[Shakir Karo'kem]] – Her husband. Their partnership was genuine, and his grief at her death was, by every account, total.

[[Neema Karo'kem]] – Her daughter, whom she never met. [[Neema Karo'kem|Neema]] carries more of her than she knows.

## Secrets

The circumstances of Nena's death were not accidental. The midwife was paid to ensure she did not survive the birth. Whether this was connected to the same interests that would later arrange [[Shakir Karo'kem|Shakir]]'s assassination — or represented a different threat entirely — remains unknown.
