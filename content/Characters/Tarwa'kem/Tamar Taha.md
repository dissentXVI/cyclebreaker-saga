**Title**: Former Fiancé of Laena Zahir; Contractor under Amasis Ramses

**Allegiance**: House Taha (tenuously), [[Amasis Ramses]] (reluctantly)

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Mid-20s

**Eyes**: Brown

**Hair**: Light brown, tightly curled and well-kept; short beard cropped close

**Attire**: Once the height of noble fashion — white and gold robes with turquoise inlays and lion-etched jewelry — but now slightly weathered at the edges; often seen without a proper escort

**Home**: [[Tarwa'kem]]

## Appearance

Tamar is handsome, polished, and visibly uncomfortable with his fall from grace. His clothes are still expensive, but no longer pristine. His jewelry is real, but increasingly modest. He walks like someone trying not to look desperate — and failing. There is a tightness around his smile that wasn't there before, and his once confident posture has started to fray into tension.

## Personality

Charming but volatile. Tamar grew up entitled, surrounded by wealth and used to winning social games with ease. But after losing [[Laena Zahir|Laena]] and gambling away more than just coin, he has become defensive, bitter, and full of unresolved spite. While he knows he is partly to blame, he cannot stop fixating on others — particularly Laena — for where he ended up.

That said, Tamar is no fool. He is quick-witted, skilled at reading people, and can still flash a disarming smile when it suits him. But behind that charm is a man who knows he is one misstep away from irrelevance — and deeply resents it.

## Character History

Born into a well-established noble family, Tamar was raised with expectations of comfort and influence. His betrothal to [[Laena Zahir]] was meant to be mutually beneficial: her rising family would gain legitimacy, and his house would align with one of [[Tarwa'kem]]'s most promising medical dynasties.

But Tamar's gambling habits — always whispered about but never confronted — worsened in his early twenties. A string of bad debts and political embarrassments strained the engagement. When [[Laena Zahir|Laena]] left in pursuit of a cure for her sister, the marriage was quietly annulled. Without that alliance, Tamar's creditors closed in.

To survive, he accepted a position under [[Amasis Ramses]], performing advisory and courier work — though he suspects Ramses enjoys the humiliation of it.

## Current Status

Tamar now works quietly in [[Amasis Ramses|Ramses]]' network, managing correspondence, negotiating with minor nobles, and occasionally serving as a presentable face for parties the Monetarist prefers not to attend himself. Though outwardly functional, Tamar is seething — he considers this chapter of his life temporary and is already scheming for a comeback.

He keeps track of [[Laena Zahir|Laena]]'s rumored movements, telling himself it is for closure. In truth, he does not know if he wants revenge, redemption, or something else entirely.

[[Laena Zahir]] – His former fiancée. Tamar still burns with resentment and confusion. He hasn't forgiven her for leaving — though a part of him suspects he never deserved her to begin with.

[[Amasis Ramses]] – Employer. Tamar owes him more than just coin, and Ramses never lets him forget it. Their relationship is coldly transactional, with an undercurrent of mockery.

[[Khafra]] – Tamar once tried to match wits with Khafra at a gala. It did not end well. They have avoided each other ever since.

## Secrets

Believes one of Ramses' accountants is intentionally keeping him in debt to control him. He hasn't confronted it — yet.

Still has [[Laena Zahir|Laena]]'s engagement token, kept in a drawer beneath his desk. Sometimes he stares at it when drunk.
