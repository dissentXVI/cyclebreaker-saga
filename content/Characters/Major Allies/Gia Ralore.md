## Appearance

Gia Ralore cuts a striking figure: sun-bronzed, muscular, and unflinchingly poised. Her hair is pulled back in a utilitarian ponytail, strands often matted with sweat and sand from the canyon heat. Her eyes — a fierce amber — scan every room like a battlefield, weighing each person by their stance, not their words. Her combat garb is piecemeal but functional: armored bracers, flexible sandals, and a crimson scarf often drawn to shield her face from grit — or from recognition. Scars mark her arms like tattoos, each one earned rather than regretted.

## Personality

Gia is bold, headstrong, and fiercely loyal — to her people, her sister, and the idea that D'yadya still belongs to D'yadyans. While not naturally political, she is passionate and outspoken, often acting on instinct before consensus. She doesn't trust easily, but once her loyalty is earned, it's unshakable. Beneath her brashness lies a deep well of grief and frustration — grief for the homeland she watched erode under Vociran boots, and frustration at how little the rest of the world seems to care.

She struggles to reconcile her ideals with her actions, but when it's time to move, she doesn't hesitate.

## Character History

Born Jeah al-Ralori, Gia grew up in the last generation of D'yadyans to know peace — or at least, to believe in it. Her family's roots ran deep in the White Sands, with her older sister Nahja already serving as a scout for the growing resistance. When the Vociran Empire invaded, seizing control of trade routes and crushing local militias, the al-Ralori name became synonymous with quiet defiance.

But quiet wasn't enough for Jeah.

After a failed protest raid led to the execution of several resistance leaders — including her mentor — she vanished into the canyons, emerging years later under the name Gia Ralore: a fighter and rebel trained in guerrilla strikes and hand-to-hand combat. She became a symbol among scattered resistance cells — not a leader, but an example. Someone who didn't wait for permission to fight back.

Now, she operates from D'yadyil, working beneath the Vociran surveillance net, training new recruits, and preparing for a pushback that may never come. She doesn't care. She'll be ready anyway.

## Current Status

Gia is currently stationed in D'yadyil, helping coordinate underground resistance efforts. Though not officially affiliated with any formal chain of command, her name is spoken with equal parts respect and fear among insurgent circles and imperial patrol logs. Her current work includes training young fighters, smuggling supplies, and sabotaging imperial signal posts.

She keeps her true identity guarded, even among allies — unsure how many of them would stand beside Jeah, rather than the myth of Gia. Only her sister Nahja knows the truth, and even she's begun calling her by her chosen name.

## Combat Style

Gia fights with speed, precision, and raw kinetic power. A trained monk, she relies on hand-to-hand strikes, joint manipulation, and agile movement to dismantle opponents before they can react. Her fighting style is honed from a mix of D'yadyan martial traditions and practical battlefield adaptation — striking weak points, disarming enemies, and overwhelming targets before reinforcements arrive.

She carries no weapons — her fists, feet, and instinct are more than enough. Her strength lies not just in skill, but in relentless willpower. She doesn't just fight to win — she fights so others don't have to.

## Notable Relationships

**Nahja al-Ralori** – Her older sister and the only person who still calls her Jeah. Nahja serves as her anchor and sometimes her conscience.

**The D'yadyan Resistance** – Gia is a respected fighter among many cells, though she refuses formal leadership roles. Her reputation often precedes her.
