**Title**: Heir of the Lael'Nathor Line, Steward of the Cosmic Pillar

**Allegiance**: None (formerly [[Shurevaire]])

**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: 17

**Eyes**: Gold, formerly green

**Hair**: Coiled black curls worn loose, with a braid tied behind one ear; often tousled by travel or study

**Attire**: Practical green tunic with forest-toned layering, accented by a leather harness for scrolls and satchels; subtle arcane stitching woven beneath the seams

**Home**: Unknown (formerly [[Shurevaire]])

**Status**: Presumed lost — merged with the [[The Cosmic Pillar|Watcher of the Lake]]

## Appearance

Reyn'lael Joran stood at the cusp of youth and purpose — lean and agile, with the quiet grace of someone who moved like he belonged in the wild, but thought like he belonged in books. His dark skin glowed warmly in natural light, and his eyes, one deep green and one gold shimmered with curiosity and buried resolve. Once [[Gothmog Gansukh]], performed his ritual Reyn's other eye turned gold. Coiled black curls framed a thoughtful face, usually marked by a faint, cautious smile that deepened when he was caught mid-thought. A simple earring glinted in one ear — a keepsake from the past, or perhaps a gesture toward a future he was still trying to define. 

He wore a ranger's green — not by trade, but for practical travel — with leather harnesses for books and scrolls rather than blades. Stray grasses and herbs often clung to him, not from neglect, but from immersion: Reyn hadn't just walked through the world — he had lived it.

## Personality

Reyn was a romantic at heart — enchanted by stories, shaped by epics, and desperate to live one of his own. He believed in heroism, legacy, and the idea that knowledge could not only unlock the past but guide the future. That belief made him earnest, overeager, and sometimes naïve. But beneath the idealism was an intense emotional core — loyal to his friends, burdened by loss, and terrified that his destiny would prove more curse than gift. After entering the [[The Cosmic Pillar|Watcher]], his demeanor changed. He smiled less. Asked more. And when the time came to act, he did so with clarity.

## Character History

Reyn'lael Joran was born into the fading bloodline of Lael'Nathor, the once-great rulers of the [[Navarresean See|Navarresean Empire]]. His village — a quiet remnant of that lineage — was destroyed while he was still a child. Orphaned and alone, he was taken in by scholars in [[Shurevaire]], where he found refuge in tomes, ruins, and the stories of ancient kings and heroic deeds. His enthusiasm for lost history eventually caught the attention of [[Layla Basura]], who secured him a place on a secret expedition to the ruins of the [[The Cosmic Pillar|Watcher of the Lake]].

Accompanied by [[Layla Basura]], the cantankerous [[Erros Opars|Professor Erros Opars]], and Aspiria's Chosen, Reyn became the key to unlocking the tower's sealed gates — something no one before had achieved. It was his bloodline that the Watcher recognized. But what awaited him inside was not the legacy he had hoped to reclaim. It was the truth — and betrayal.

Within the Watcher, Reyn was confronted with the cruelties of the Navarresean Empire: a history of conquest, enslavement, and arrogance written in stone and memory. The heroic image he had once clung to crumbled before him, replaced by the weight of his ancestors' sins.

Then [[Layla Basura]] betrayed him. She stabbed Reyn, believing his blood alone would allow her to seize control of the throne. [[Erros Opars]] followed suit, using the stolen remains of Duisaratte — the broken staff of Archmage Velethoui — in an attempt to harness the Watcher's arcane systems. Instead, he tore a rift into the unknowable, summoning the Empty: a monstrous entity devoid of pneuma, a fragment of what existed before the gods.

Aspiria's Chosen fought to protect Reyn, escorting his failing body to the heart of the tower — the Throne. In his final conscious act, Reyn took his rightful place. The Watcher accepted him. He merged with it. He banished the Empty. And then, with one last breath of agency, he teleported the party and Duisaratte to safety, sealing the Watcher — and himself — from the world. Shortly after the party was ejected, the Watcher of the Lake disappeared with Reyn inside.

## Current Status

Reyn is presumed lost along with the [[The Cosmic Pillar|Watcher of the Lake]], which vanished from the dried lake bed where it once stood. Only faint pneuma traces remained — and the breathstone beneath it, which occasionally pulses in strange patterns.

Some believed he died. Others whisper he became the tower itself — an echo-bound guardian tethered to a relic too powerful to exist in the world freely. What is certain is this: he sacrificed everything to save the others.

## Combat Style

Reyn fought like a rogue with a scholar's instinct — quick, clever, and never where enemies expected. He used a shortblade and hand crossbow, pairing them with bursts of minor spellcraft: illusions, blinding flashes, and pneuma-based tricks meant to disorient. His movements were sharp, his strategies improvised, and his focus precise. He never fought for domination. He fought to survive — and to protect.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Layla Basura]]** – Once his mentor, later his betrayer. Reyn never spoke harshly of her, even after the wound. That silence said more than words.

**[[Erros Opars]]** – A figure of both intimidation and fascination. Reyn admired his mind until the moment he saw what it could destroy.

**[[Aspiria's Chosen]]** – They were the first real heroes Reyn met — and the last ones he saved. He trusted them when everything else collapsed.
