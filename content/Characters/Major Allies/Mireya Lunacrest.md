**Title**: Proprietor of the Sailor's Whimsy; Unofficial Mayor of [[Laconia]]

**Allegiance**: Independent ([[Laconia]])

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Female

**Age**: 16

**Eyes**: Striking ice blue

**Hair**: Long, curly hair dyed black

**Attire**: Modest work dress with a fitted corset, clean apron, and a pendant that once belonged to her mother

**Home**: The Sailor's Whimsy, [[Laconia]]

## Appearance

Mireya Lunacrest carries herself with soft strength — composed, approachable, but never passive. Her fair skin and bright, soulful eyes often catch travelers off-guard, especially when paired with the quiet authority she holds in her posture. Her long red curls are usually dyed black and tied loosely or pushed back with a scarf, more out of habit than vanity. Though young, she moves like someone much older — someone who's learned the weight of responsibility through living it, not choosing it.

## Personality

Mireya is gentle, principled, and unshakably kind — not because the world has been good to her, but because she refuses to let it make her cruel. She listens more than she speaks and leads not through command, but through compassion and consistency. Her strength lies in presence: in being the one who remains when others falter. She is prone to doubt, carries more than she admits, and has not fully learned how to ask for help — but she believes deeply in others. Sometimes too deeply.

## Character History

Born elsewhere and raised in [[Laconia]], Mireya was the adopted child of two beloved innkeepers who ran the Sailor's Whimsy, once the heart of the village's harbor. When the last Cataclysm struck, her parents were among the many killed — victims of an event that left scars on both the land and the soul. At only sixteen, Mireya inherited the inn, its debts, and the unspoken role of emotional anchor for those who remained.

With the quiet support of [[Jendrick Ironbrook]], her parents' dearest friend, she refused to abandon the Whimsy, choosing instead to rebuild what she could — one room, one ledger, one loyal patron at a time. Though competition from the Lionstooth Inn siphoned off business before it was abandoned during [[Dagozog]]'s attack, Mireya kept the Whimsy alive through grit, kindness, and a quiet understanding that healing takes time.

## Current Status

In the aftermath of [[Dagozog]]'s attack on [[Laconia]], something shifted. With no formal leadership left standing and a community in shock, people began looking to whoever remained calm — and Mireya was there. She organized shelter. She kept the Whimsy's doors open when others closed theirs. She mediated disputes, rationed resources, and sat with the grieving long after others had gone home.

No election was held. No title was formally conferred. But Laconia began calling her Mayor anyway, and she didn't correct them. She's not sure she should.

She still runs the Sailor's Whimsy — still rebuilds it, still tends it — but the weight on her shoulders has grown considerably. [[Zeyn Choukri]] coordinates militia and security; she handles the rest. Her role is unofficial and unrecognized by the city-states, but in [[Laconia]], it is entirely real.

She carries the responsibility with the same quiet stubbornness she's always carried everything else. She just doesn't sleep as much as she used to.

## Combat Style

Mireya is not trained for war, but her courage runs deep. She stands her ground when others run and rallies those who falter. If pressed into danger, she uses a light crossbow and improvised cover — trained quietly by [[Jendrick Ironbrook]]. What she lacks in martial skill, she makes up for in unshakable presence. Her leadership is instinctual, her courage contagious. More than once, others have fought harder simply because she stood nearby.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Jendrick Ironbrook]]** – Her protector, confidant, and family in every way that matters. Their loyalty is wordless and absolute.

**[[Zeyn Choukri]]** – An unlikely but functional partnership. Zeyn handles the blade and the boots; Mireya handles the people. She finds his pragmatism grounding. He finds her idealism... complicated. But neither would deny the other has earned their place.

**Aspiria's Chosen** – She offered them lodging in exchange for a favor and ended up trusting them more than she expected. They remind her that even ordinary people can do extraordinary things.

## Secrets

Since the Cataclysm, Mireya has experienced brief, haunting visions — fragments of the past she did not live, felt as though through someone else's skin. A bloodstained hallway. A dragon's breath over ruins. A hand reaching, then fading into mist. These flashes arrive unbidden, often tied to strong emotions or ancient places.

She now hears a voice — quiet at first, now unmistakable — speaking in dreams, in moments between breaths. It calls itself Aspiria, the will of the world. It does not command. It invites. And it tells her that she, like the others, carries the Blessing of Light — the power to witness what came before and survive what is coming. She has not told anyone. Not even [[Jendrick Ironbrook|Jendrick]].
