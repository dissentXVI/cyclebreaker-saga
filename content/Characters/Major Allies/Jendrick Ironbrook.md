**Title**: Bouncer and Handyman of the Sailor's Whimsy

**Allegiance**: [[Mireya Lunacrest]]

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Early 30s

**Eyes**: Pale blue

**Hair**: Black, tousled and clean-shaven

**Attire**: Reinforced travel leathers worn beneath a weathered jacket, suitable for both tavern work and unexpected trouble

**Home**: The Sailor's Whimsy, [[Laconia]]

## Appearance

Jendrick Ironbrook is tall, broad-shouldered, and perpetually in motion — a man made for narrow alleys, shifting crates, and breaking up bar fights before they start. His dark hair is usually windswept, his clothes clean but unpretentious. A casual smile rests naturally on his face, often hiding deeper weariness. Though built more for speed than brute force, there's an unmistakable weight to his movements — the kind honed by years of surviving scrapes most men wouldn't walk away from. He looks like someone who's talked his way out of as many problems as he's fought through.

## Personality

Easygoing, flirtatious, and quick with a joke, Jendrick projects the persona of a man who doesn't take life too seriously. But beneath the charm lies a current of guilt and protectiveness that runs deep. He deflects vulnerability with humor and handles pain with silence. He is fiercely loyal, especially to Mireya, and will cross any line to keep those he cares about safe. While not formally educated, Jendrick has sharp instincts and a good heart — even if he's convinced he doesn't deserve either.

## Character History

Jendrick grew up rough in the backstreets of [[Avosa Tesota]], running messages and running from trouble. He found his way to the Sailor's Whimsy in his late teens and quickly became a fixture: part bouncer, part repairman, part older brother to the little red-headed girl at the Whimsy. His life changed irrevocably during the previous Cataclysm, when his closest friends — including Mireya's parents — died in a disaster he believes he could have prevented. He wasn't there when it happened, and that absence has haunted him ever since.

Mireya became his responsibility, whether she asked for it or not. He's stuck close to her ever since, quietly guarding her while insisting she's the one running things. The Whimsy is her bar. The protection? That's just his penance.

## Current Status

Jendrick continues to consider the Sailor's Whimsy his home, but he hasn't been behind the bar in weeks. Though he had thrown himself into rebuilding the tavern after [[Dagozog]]'s attack — carpenter by day, quiet sentinel by night — he stepped away when [[Sylviane Arsenault]] announced her intent to investigate the Sticaean Abyss. She wasn't going to wait, and Jendrick knew too well what happened to people who went into dangerous places alone.

He volunteered to accompany her, though it was clear she would have gone without him. Now, with hammer left behind and blade quietly sharpened, he walks the crumbling caverns beside Sylviane, watchful as ever. This time, he doesn't intend to be too late.

## Combat Style

Jendrick fights like a man who knows the price of losing. He uses misdirection, speed, and blunt force to take control of a fight quickly. His style mixes street brawling with calculated strikes — practical, dirty, and efficient. He favors short blades, reinforced gloves, and always keeps something sharp tucked in his boot. He rarely kills unless absolutely necessary, but he never pulls a punch when someone he loves is at risk.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Mireya Lunacrest]]** – His found-sister and dearest friend. He's devoted to her safety, even when she frustrates or baffles him. She's the only one who sees past his self-deprecating mask.

**Locals of [[Laconia]]** – Jendrick is quietly beloved by the neighborhood — especially the old, the drunk, and the small children who think he's some kind of hero. He insists they're wrong.

## Secrets

Jendrick believes he could have saved Mireya's parents the day they died — and that his failure doomed her to a harder life. What he's never told anyone is that he was meant to be with them that day, but chose to stay behind for selfish reasons he won't admit, even to himself.

He keeps a locket belonging to Mireya's mother hidden in his room, and sometimes, when no one's watching, he talks to it like she's still listening.
