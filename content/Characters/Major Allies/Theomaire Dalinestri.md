**Title**: Arcane Theorist

**Allegiance**: [[Shurevaire]] (independent fellowship)

**Race**: Half-Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: 24

**Eyes**: Slate gray

**Hair**: Dark brown, swept back with a slight wave

**Attire**: Deep blue scholar's cloak embroidered with gold sigils, high collar and wide sleeves tailored for movement and concealment; always carries a leather-bound book and a fountain quill

**Home**: [[Laconia]] (The Registrar's Office)

## Appearance

Theomaire Dalinestri presents a refined, almost monastic appearance — robes impeccably maintained, posture composed, and expression always several thoughts deep. His dark hair is neatly combed back, his face clean-shaven, and his round spectacles lend him the look of someone who not only reads extensively, but corrects footnotes in the margins. He speaks with a calm, deliberate cadence — not out of arrogance, but as if choosing every word with surgical care. Though not imposing in stature, his presence is commanding in silence.

## Personality

Reserved, introspective, and exacting, Theomaire is a man who prefers precision over bravado. He dislikes rash action, especially in matters of arcane or historical consequence. He is deeply principled, though often reticent to express those principles until asked directly — and even then, he answers with caution. Beneath the scholarly detachment lies a sensitive soul, one who has experienced profound isolation and now guards his emotions with an academic's discipline.

He is quietly loyal to those he considers worth his time — a rare distinction — and shows affection not through warmth, but through meticulous care, subtle gestures, and unspoken presence.

## Character History

Theomaire hails from a minor elven house in [[Aqualonde]], one whose name has long faded from political favor but still clings to tradition. He was best known in academic circles for his research into sub-pneumeric resonance theory — the idea that pneuma echoes can persist in physical structures long after life has departed. His work explored how ruined places "remember" events through magical imprint, and how such memories might be reconstructed or unraveled through careful magical tuning. His brilliance, however, often came hand-in-hand with social distance — peers found him aloof, overly exacting, and frustratingly enigmatic.

It was [[Ylaena Sharie]], another brilliant outcast, who first pulled him from isolation. Through her, he met [[Gothmog Gansukh]], a relationship that began with studied observation and slowly warmed into something deeper. The three formed a close-knit circle: outcasts bound not by similarity, but by the rare safety of being understood.

## Current Status

Theomaire resides in [[Laconia]], keeping quarters at The Registrar's Office and serving as a liaison between local scholars and the distant Collegiate Archive. While he avoids direct conflict, he remains informed on adventurer activity and occasionally offers quiet insight into relic behavior, pneuma instability, or magical anomalies in the region.

He does not travel with the party, but he stays close to their orbit — particularly to [[Gothmog Gansukh]]. He considers their relationship new and unpredictable, which is to say: terrifying. And quietly sacred.

## Combat Style

Theomaire avoids confrontation whenever possible. When forced into combat, he favors illusion, divination, and escape-oriented magic — veils of mist, false sound signatures, mental misdirection, and spatial redirection glyphs. He does not wield power to defeat, but to disarm, outwit, or vanish. His preferred spells allow him to sow confusion while observing safely from the edges — a ghost of the battlefield, rather than a presence within it.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Gothmog Gansukh]]** – His partner, though the term feels embarrassingly small. Theomaire is still learning how to love openly. Gothmog makes that easier.

**[[Ylaena Sharie]]** – The catalyst for his personal growth. He would quietly dismantle kingdoms for her, if she asked nicely.

**[[Diazana Trubough]]** – Professional admiration. Theomaire once critiqued one of her theories in a footnote, and she still hasn't let it go.
