**Title**: Scholaris Medius, Registrar for Archaeological Expeditions

**Allegiance**: [[Shurevaire]]

**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: 38

**Eyes**: Grey, steely beneath his glasses

**Hair**: Short, messy, and dark brown

**Attire**: Utilitarian academic robes layered with reinforced fieldwear; always carries a note satchel, quill kit, and weathered field journal

**Home**: [[Laconia]] (Registrar's Offices)

## Appearance

Yenden Clearlade has the unassuming look of a man more at home in a library alcove than on a battlefield. His stocky build and soft frame are those of a dedicated scholar, shaped by years of late nights, cold mornings, and too many hours hunched over parchment. His short, tousled hair is always a little out of place, and his round glasses ride low on his nose when he's focused. His pale complexion and tired eyes hint at long days and longer worries, but his smile — warm, worn, and deeply genuine — has a way of disarming tension. He moves like someone who is perpetually mid-thought.

## Personality

Yenden is humble, methodical, and unfailingly kind. He believes in the power of knowledge, but also in the responsibility that comes with it. While not socially adept, he makes up for it with sincerity and a willingness to listen. He is a diplomat among academics — the steady hand that balances ambition, ego, and theory with practicality and empathy.

Though he rarely raises his voice, Yenden is no pushover. When challenged, he anchors himself in facts, history, and moral clarity. He has no patience for cruelty masquerading as intellect, and little tolerance for those who prioritize legacy over truth. At his core, he is a servant of understanding — and a quiet guardian of those who seek it with honest hearts.

## Character History

Yenden hails from a long line of Shurevarian scholars and archivists, but never rested on the laurels of pedigree. A diligent student of Halinori history and applied pneumacraft, he earned his Scholaris Medius designation after completing his first circle of accreditation, studying under none other than Archmage Velethoui Arsenault. That mentorship — and the grief of Velethoui's loss — defined his approach to scholarship: careful, collaborative, and deeply ethical.

Among his closest colleagues is [[Diazana Trubough]], with whom he has cultivated a respectful, sometimes challenging, partnership. Their teaching styles clash as often as they align, but their mutual respect runs deep. Together, they foster open forums, shared research initiatives, and student-driven field projects.

Yenden relocated to [[Laconia]] after the emergence of the [[Watcher of the Lake]], accepting a position as Registrar for Archaeological Expeditions. There, he balances administration, teaching, and field supervision — and does his best to keep eccentric colleagues and rogue adventurers from blowing up half the continent.

## Current Status

Yenden currently oversees expedition logistics and research approvals in and around [[Laconia]], helping manage academic access to unstable ruins and pre-historic sites. While not directly involved in high-risk adventures, he remains a trusted advisor to several groups — including Aspiria's Chosen — and serves as a bridge between the academic community and those who work outside it.

His evenings are spent cataloging expedition findings, responding to letters from his younger students, and occasionally sharing tea with [[Diazana Trubough]] when she remembers to stop moving for five minutes. He continues to research the implications of pneuma imbalance and residual echoes around the Sticaean Abyss, though his true concern remains unspoken: what happens if history begins to repeat itself so soon.

## Combat Style

Yenden is not a combatant by training or inclination. However, when absolutely necessary, he uses precise, non-lethal applications of pneumacraft — field-grade stasis runes, suppression glyphs, and force-buffer charms — to buy time or protect others. His greatest strength lies in field support: assessing ancient hazards, diagnosing pneuma corruption, or neutralizing unstable relics before someone else touches them and bursts into flame.

He prefers to stand behind those braver than himself — but if no one else will, he'll step forward.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Diazana Trubough]]** – Longtime colleague and occasional rival. He grounds her when she spirals; she challenges him when he stalls. They trust each other completely.

**[[Ylaena Sharie]]** – A former student who became something far more important. Her friendship helped him feel less alone. He quietly sees her as family.

**[[Gothmog Gansukh]]** – Yenden admires his courage, complexity, and conviction. Their bond is still forming, but Yenden is already emotionally invested — more than he knows how to handle.

**[[Reyn'lael Joran]]** – A student he never got to teach. Yenden carries guilt for not reaching out sooner — and hope that Reyn may yet return.
