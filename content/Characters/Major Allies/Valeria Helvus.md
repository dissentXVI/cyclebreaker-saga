**Title**: Former Imperial Engineer

**Allegiance**: None (formerly [[Vociterra]])

**Race**: Human (Vociran)

**Gender**: Female

**Age**: 29

**Eyes**: Blue

**Hair**: Dark violet, worn in two braided buns

**Attire**: Reinforced flight leathers in purple, patched with civilian repairs; fingerless gloves, brass goggles, and a set of tools always at her belt

**Home**: Currently staying in [[Tarwa'kem]]

## Appearance

Valeria Helvus wears her past as plainly as her leathers — a mix of imperial precision and frontier practicality. Her violet hair is tied back in twin braids, her round glasses smudged with graphite more often than not. She carries herself with quiet confidence, often found perched on scaffolding or halfway inside a propulsion core mid-repair. Her incisors, slightly elongated like all Vocirans, give her a faintly lupine edge when she smiles — a trait she usually hides behind bandanas, scarves, or careful silence in public. Her eyes miss nothing.

## Personality

Friendly but guarded, Valeria is the sort of person who keeps her hands busy to keep her mind from spiraling. She's curious, quick-witted, and remarkably adaptable — a survivor of a regime she no longer believes in. Though she smiles easily and chats with warmth, there's a constant tension behind her eyes, like a spring held just shy of release.

She avoids conflict whenever possible, not out of fear, but principle. A lifelong pacifist, she believes technology should elevate, not destroy — and she's never forgiven herself for what the Empire asked her to build.

## Character History

Valeria Helvus was born and raised in [[Vociterra]], trained from childhood in the Empire's most prestigious engineering academies. Her aptitude for vulcaneum machinery and aerial propulsion systems quickly set her apart from her peers, earning her the rank of lut Helvus — a fully recognized imperial engineer. She worked in advanced design for years, building and refining Vociran prototypes used for transport, surveillance, and — though she tried not to admit it — warfare.

Over time, whispers began to circulate through her division: of a secret initiative tied to Halinor, of weapons that didn't just kill, but altered. She was never told the specifics. But she knew enough. And she knew she couldn't live with it.

So she ran.

She hijacked a classified airship — a prototype she'd helped design — and fled west across the sea. She didn't make it far. The ship crash-landed in the forests beneath the Amrakebir Mountains, not far from [[Laconia]]. Flames lit the night sky as her damaged vessel struck the trees. It was there Aspiria's Chosen found her, wounded but conscious, unwilling to give her name at first, and even more unwilling to lie.

She told them she came to warn them. That [[Vociterra]] was planning something. That they didn't have much time.

## Current Status

Valeria now resides in [[Tarwa'kem]], having accompanied the party to deliver her warning to Sultana [[Neema Karo'kem]]. Though the message was heard, the real work is just beginning. Valeria has since thrown herself into designing a new airship for the party, repurposing what salvageable parts she could recover from her wreck and sourcing materials through Aspiria's Chosen throughout their journey.

She also continues her investigation into the Empire's larger plans — piecing together old logs, predictive engineering models, and scraps of imperial theory. She knows she's being hunted, but not urgently. The Empire thinks she's irrelevant.

She intends to prove otherwise.

## Combat Style

Valeria is not a traditional combatant — and she certainly isn't a soldier. She relies entirely on her engineering ingenuity and field improvisation. When forced into conflict, she uses non-lethal tools and repurposed devices salvaged from her imperial training: arc-spark tools that discharge stunning bursts, signal jammers to disable pneuma-based tech, smoke canisters for cover, and collapsible grappling systems for quick escapes or repositioning.

She avoids direct confrontation whenever possible, preferring sabotage, misdirection, or disabling an enemy's equipment before the fight begins. She is a support specialist in the field, never the spearpoint — but she ensures the spear flies true.

## Notable Relationships

**Sultana [[Neema Karo'kem]]** – Someone she expected to distrust her. The Sultana's willingness to listen gave Valeria hope — and a reason to stay.

**Aspiria's Chosen** – The first people to offer her trust instead of shackles. She hasn't said it aloud, but she would follow them anywhere.

## Secrets

Valeria has never told the party who her father is — not because she's ashamed, but because she knows what it would mean. Tribunis Faustus tes Helvus, Prime Engineer of the [[Vociran Empire]], is not just a senior member of the Tribunii under Legatus Quietus — he is also the chief architect behind many of the Empire's most devastating projects. Brilliant, ruthless, and politically entrenched, Faustus once taught Valeria everything she knows.
