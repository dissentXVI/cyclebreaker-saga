**Title**: Former Tengushin of the Shanyu's Guard

**Allegiance**: [[Ilsong]]

**Race**: Orc

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: 50

**Eyes**: Ember-red, weathered but unyielding

**Hair**: Black streaked with gray, pulled back in a warrior's knot with a braided beard

**Attire**: Faded, well-worn cloth in piecemeal fashion — remnants of old Ilsong style patched together with whatever came to hand; a carefully kept flask; an unseen but immaculately maintained blade

**Home**: Wandering exile, most recently seen near the edges of the Tatarr'khan steppes

## Appearance

A hulking figure carved from time and war, Tsuga stands like a wall that once protected kings. His skin is weathered green, lined with scars — not all from battle. His eyes burn not with anger, but with the quiet fire of regret.

He wears the remnants of his old station like a burden: layered armor dulled by age and pulled from many different places as one piece is replaced by cheaply-obtained pieces when necessary. Only the flask he carries seems to be given any care — but hardly anyone ever sees the blade he keeps well-honed and oiled.

## Personality

Tsuga is the embodiment of old honor — battered but not bent. Once stoic and disciplined, he has grown distant, his warmth buried beneath self-imposed disgrace. He sees himself as a failure, and cannot bear the kindness of those who would disagree.

He drinks more than he should. He speaks less than he used to. And when he does speak, his words carry weight like stone. Yet despite the pain, he remains a warrior of principle — loyal to death, and still watching the horizon for a sign that his oath might yet be redeemed.

## Character History

Tsuga Mirai was once the Tengushin — the supreme commander of the Shanyu's royal guard, charged with defending the sovereign of [[Ilsong]] and his family. Fiercely loyal, revered as a legend in his time, Tsuga trained countless warriors — including his own son, [[Gothmog Gansukh]], and the deadly [[Araki Runa]], who fought from the shadows where he stood in the light.

But when [[Vociterra]] invaded, Tsuga failed. The Shanyu was slain. The palace burned. And the young prince, [[Amano Kioshi]], was presumed dead.

Tsuga escaped with his life, barely — but not his honor. Believing both the prince and his fellow guards lost, he vanished into the wild steppes of [[Tatarr'kha]]. There, among dust and strangers, he drinks in silence and dreams of war drums he cannot answer.

Unbeknownst to him, [[Amano Kioshi]] lives.

Unbeknownst to him, [[Araki Runa]] still searches.

And somewhere... his son [[Gothmog Gansukh]] still hopes.

## Current Status

Tsuga wanders the borderlands under false names, never staying long. He works as a caravan guard, sells his sword to protect others, but avoids anything that might earn him renown. If recognized, he claims it was a different Tsuga. One who died with the palace.

[[Araki Runa]] has found him more than once, begging him to return. He refuses — not out of cowardice, but because he believes he is a curse. A sword that failed to strike. A wall that let the fire in.

Still... he keeps his armor oiled. He sharpens his blade. And some nights, he stares at the moon and whispers the prince's name.

## Combat Style

Tsuga is a master of Raiun-no-Kata — the "Stormcloud Forms," a high-guard martial tradition emphasizing overwhelming defense, explosive counterstrikes, and immovable positioning. Where others dance, Tsuga endures. Where others strike, Tsuga ends.

He wields a massive zanbato-like cleaver, often strapped across his back. Despite his age, he is still deadly — perhaps even more so now, with nothing left to protect but memory.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Amano Kioshi]]** – The prince he swore to protect. Believed dead. His greatest shame. If he learned Amano still lived, it might kill him — or bring him back to life.

**[[Araki Runa]]** – Once his peer and ally, now his conscience in flesh. She still believes. He cannot forgive himself. Their reunion is often bitter — but never truly cold.

**[[Gothmog Gansukh]]** – His son. Tsuga assumes Gothmog died defending the palace.
