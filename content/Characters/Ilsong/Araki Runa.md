**Title**: The Black Whisper

**Allegiance**: Formerly Ilsong Reclamation Front; now operates alone

**Race**: Orc

**Gender**: Female

**Age**: 32

**Eyes**: Ember-red

**Hair**: Jet black, swept into a high, braided ponytail

**Attire**: Matte-black lamellar armor with silent-fastened plates; bone-threaded necklace of the old warden clans; twin daggers concealed in cross-scabbards

**Home**: Born in Shilu'an Province, [[Ilsong]] (now Vociran-occupied)

## Appearance

Runa carries the discipline of a royal guard turned ghost — rigid stance, deadly grace, and eyes that cut deeper than blades. Her pale green skin bears war freckles and faded ink sigils of her service. She favors blackened, silent armor built for infiltration, and her expression is a constant mask of control. Her ember-red eyes burn low and steady, and her high ponytail is bound with the same knot used by the ancient palace sentinels. She speaks softly, moves quieter, and rarely smiles.

## Personality

Quiet, fierce, and unwavering, Runa serves not for glory but for the soul of her homeland. Her loyalty is unshakeable — not to symbols or thrones, but to the promise she made as a royal protector: [[Ilsong]] endures.

She believes in strategy over speeches, action over ceremony. While others grieve, she sharpens. While others flee, she waits. Her belief in the prince's survival is what keeps her blade steady — even as her last comrade drinks himself toward oblivion.

## Character History

Araki Runa was once one of the elite, handpicked by [[Tsuga Mirai]] to train and serve in the Shield of the Rising Moon — the royal guard of [[Ilsong]]. While [[Tsuga Mirai|Tsuga]] was the shield in front, Runa was to be the blade in the dark: a covert assassin, saboteur, and whisper-gatherer for the crown.

She was seven when the Vocirans razed Shoran. The prince, [[Amano Kioshi]], fell. The palace burned. The guard broke.

Unlike many, Runa survived — wounded, hunted, and burning with purpose. She continued operating in secret alongside [[Tsuga Mirai|Tsuga]], who fell into despair and drink, haunted by the belief he failed his sworn duty. Runa never abandoned him — but she no longer waits for his approval.

Years later, when a young rebel called Shinsei appeared, Runa recognized the tactics, the cadence, the breath. She never confirmed it aloud — but in her heart, she knew: the prince lives.

After the Ilsong Liberation Front was crushed at [[Shuvalan Temple]], Runa once again slipped into the shadows. She now moves between provinces and ports, seeking whispers, allies, and evidence to bring back not a crown, but a future.

When the Front fell at Shuvalan Temple, Runa barely escaped. Those who did not, she still carries in her memory.

## Current Status

Runa is currently traveling beyond the borders of [[Eldamar]] under false names, gathering information and testing the waters for rekindled resistance. She still reports sporadically to [[Tsuga Mirai]] — now a broken relic of the guard — but makes her own decisions.

She believes [[Amano Kioshi]] — [[Amano Kioshi|Shinsei]] — survived the fall of the Liberation Front. And she is determined to find him.

## Combat Style

Runa wields twin dusksteel daggers, enchanted to suppress sound within a short radius. Her martial style — Whisperblade Tactics — emphasizes precision, agility, and lethal quietude. Where Tsuga's strength was honor and resilience, hers is surprise and finality.

She does not posture, does not warn. She strikes once. And only once.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Tsuga Mirai]]** – Her former commander. Once an unshakable pillar, now a man drinking himself into nothing. She still protects him — though he doesn't see it.

**[[Amano Kioshi]]** – Her prince. She has never stopped believing he lives. She has killed to protect his memory. She would die to see him return.
