**Title**: Shanyu of [[Ilsong]]

**Allegiance**: [[Ilsong]]

**Race**: Orc

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Early 40s at time of death

**Eyes**: Deep green

**Hair**: Black, worn long and loose in peace, bound in a warrior's knot for war

**Attire**: Ceremonial lamellar armor of lacquered black and deep crimson; the Kioshi seal — a cresting wave above a drawn blade — embossed at the pauldron

**Home**: Shoran, [[Ilsong]]

**Status**: Deceased — slain during the Fall of Shoran

## Appearance

Yurei Kioshi carried himself with the kind of ease that belongs only to those who have never needed to perform authority. He was tall and broad-shouldered, his green skin weathered by decades of campaign and court in equal measure. His face was not young even in his prime — lined early by responsibility, by grief, by the particular exhaustion of a man who could not stop thinking about his people even when he slept.

In ceremony, he wore the full regalia of the Kioshi line. In private, he often shed the armor entirely, preferring plain robes and bare feet in the gardens of Shoran. Those who served him long enough came to understand that the man in the garden was not less the Shanyu — he was more of him.

## Personality

Yurei was patient in the way that only deeply principled men can be — not because he lacked fire, but because he understood that fire spent unwisely is ash. He was measured, warm, and possessed of a dry humor he deployed rarely and to great effect. He listened more than he spoke. When he did speak, people stopped what they were doing.

He did not believe in cruelty. He did not believe in glory as an end in itself. He believed in [[Ilsong]] — not as an abstraction, but as the sum of the people in it: the farmers and the sword-saints, the river traders and the palace guards, the children who had never known anything but occupation.

That belief is what made him dangerous. And what made him loved.

He also knew — in the quiet part of himself he showed only to [[Tsuga Mirai]] and, in rare moments, to his son [[Amano Kioshi|Amano]] — that he was afraid. Not of dying. Of dying before Ilsong was free.

## Character History

Yurei inherited the Kioshi seat young, following his father's death during an early border conflict with Vociran-aligned factions pressing east. He was not yet thirty. What he inherited was not a kingdom at peace — it was a kingdom watching the horizon.

He spent the years before the invasion building. He reinforced the Shield of the Rising Moon, elevated [[Tsuga Mirai]] to Tengushin, modernized the river defenses, and cultivated diplomatic ties with neighboring peoples. He was not preparing for war with any certainty. He was preparing because he was the kind of man who understood that hope and preparation were not opposites.

When Vociterra came, it came faster and harder than anyone had modeled. The capital's outer defenses held for three days. The river fell on the fourth. Yurei made the decision that would define — and divide — his legacy: he surrendered Shoran rather than see it burned to ash around its people.

The Vociran administration installed him as a nominal deputy — a familiar face behind which the occupation could present a civilized surface. Yurei accepted the role. He wore their courtesies. He attended their ceremonies. And in the hours between, he met in darkened rooms with [[Tsuga Mirai]], with commanders of the old guard, with the architects of what would become the Ilsong Liberation Front, laying plans in careful silence.

He waited because the moment had to be right. The moment came — or seemed to — when word reached Shoran that Vociterra was fracturing from within, its resources split across too many fronts. Yurei made his move. The Liberation Front rose openly. The palace was briefly reclaimed. For three days, the Kioshi banner flew again over Shoran.

It was not enough. The fracture in Vociterra closed faster than his intelligence had predicted. Reinforcements arrived. The battle that followed was not a battle — it was a correction. In the final hours, as the palace fell room by room, Yurei sent [[Amano Kioshi|Amano]] out through the eastern passage with a trusted retainer and a single instruction: *live*.

He did not ask [[Tsuga Mirai]] to follow. He told him to.

Tsuga did not obey.

What followed between them in those last hours is known only to the men who were there, most of whom did not survive. What is known is that Yurei faced [[Caedus seq Magnus]]'s vanguard in the throne room of Shoran — alone, in full regalia, with Ilsong's ancestral blade drawn — and bought time he no longer needed for a son who was already gone.

He was not defeated quickly. That, too, is known.

## Legacy

Yurei Kioshi is remembered differently depending on who remembers him.

To the Vociran administration, he is a cautionary tale about managed populations and insufficient surveillance. To the diaspora, he is a martyr — the Last Shanyu, the man who bled for every hour [[Amano Kioshi|Amano]] needed to escape. To [[Tsuga Mirai]], he is a wound that never closed, a name whispered at the moon's edge on sleepless nights. To [[Amano Kioshi|Amano]] himself, he is everything the name Shinsei was invented to outrun — and everything that makes outrunning it impossible.

The cresting wave above the drawn blade is still worn in secret by those who remember what Ilsong was. It is a dangerous thing to display.

Some wear it anyway.

## Combat Style

Yurei was considered one of the finest swordsmen of his generation — a practitioner of Tenkū-no-Tachi, the "Heavenward Draw," a Kioshi-lineage form emphasizing wide, decisive strikes built around reading an opponent's breath rather than their blade. He trained daily without exception and sparred often with [[Tsuga Mirai]], whose Raiun-no-Kata made him the only consistent challenge Yurei could find in the palace.

Their sparring sessions were, by all accounts, as much conversation as combat.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Tsuga Mirai]]** – There is no adequate word for what Tsuga was to Yurei. Sworn protector, yes. Commander of his guard, yes. But also the man Yurei trusted with his doubts when the Shanyu could not afford to have any in public. They had known each other since Tsuga's first posting to the palace, when Yurei was barely old enough to hold court. Decades later, Yurei's last order to Tsuga was to live. That Tsuga is still alive, and still drinking, and still unable to forgive himself, would have broken Yurei's heart.

**[[Amano Kioshi]]** – His only son. Yurei saw in Amano everything he had hoped and more — the warmth, the principle, the idealism still intact. He also saw the weight of what he was asking the boy to carry, and it cost him sleep he never recovered. His final act was to give Amano time. He hopes it was enough.
