**Home**: [[Ilsong]]

## Appearance

Amano's youth is worn thin by grief and exile. Though only twenty-five, his sun-scarred features and weary eyes give the impression of someone far older. His face is lean, his shoulders squared from discipline, but his posture often dips when unobserved. His topknot is simple and unadorned. His armor, scorched and stripped of sigils, hints at royalty only to those who know what to look for.

## Personality

Still idealistic at heart, Amano now buries his hope beneath layers of self-blame. He is soft-spoken, humble, and introspective — a young man trying to reconcile the promise of his birth with the weight of failure. He seeks no crown, no title — only atonement.

He remains deeply principled and gentle with those in pain. But beneath that gentleness lies a reservoir of fury, turned inward.

## Character History

As the only son of Shanyu [[Yurei Kioshi]], Amano was raised within the sacred halls of Shoran — the imperial capital of Ilsong. Trained in diplomacy, history, and the Qingshido sword forms, he excelled under immense pressure, but never forgot to laugh with his childhood companion: [[Gothmog Gansukh]], son of [[Tsuga Mirai]], commander of the Shield of the Rising Moon — sworn guardians of the royal line.

At six, Amano witnessed the fall of his kingdom. The Vociran invasion came with fire and steel. His father was slain; the palace fell. Only a loyal retainer's sacrifice allowed Amano to escape the capital under cover of night.

By seventeen, he had taken the name Shinsei the Gray, and began rallying exiles, scholars, and warrior-priests into a rebellion — the Ilsong Liberation Front. It was bold, righteous... and doomed. At nineteen, it was betrayed. The siege of Shuvalan Temple ended in slaughter. Hundreds died under his banner.

Amano disappeared. He fled west, to the tribal steppes of [[Tatarr'kha]], where his name meant nothing. There, he learned silence.

## Current Status

Amano now wanders the windswept wilds of [[Tatarr'kha]], living among orcish tribes and forgotten mystics. Known only as Shinsei, he works as a healer, hunter, and unassuming sword-hand. His title, lineage, and purpose remain buried — spoken only to the wind.

But fate moves. And whispers travel.

## Combat Style

Amano's blade, Windsunder, remains hidden beneath his robes. A gift of the Amano line, it bears stormcall glyphs and the names of every emperor before him — and now, the names of those who died under his command.

He practices Qingshido, the Sword Way of Breath, with solemn grace: counter-based, fluid, and defensive until the killing stroke. His style mirrors the steppes — open, unpredictable, and unrelenting when provoked.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Gothmog Gansukh]]** – Once his dearest friend. Each believes the other died in the fall. Their reunion, when it comes, will shake the past loose.

**[[Tsuga Mirai]]** – Amano believes the man died saving him. He wears the crescent-threaded sash Tsuga wrapped around his waist as a child.
