**Title**: Scholar; former Shurevairean academic

**Allegiance**: None (formerly [[Shurevaire]])

**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Elderly

**Eyes**: Dark, sharp, perpetually unimpressed

**Hair**: White, loosely curled, thinning at the crown

**Attire**: Red academic robes with a green cloak bearing gold pauldrons and a chain clasp; round wire-framed spectacles

**Home**: Formerly [[Shurevaire]]

**Status**: Deceased — corrupted into a shade by the Empty; destroyed by [[Aspiria's Chosen]] in the throne room of the [[Watcher of the Lake]]

## Appearance

Erros Opars was an elderly elf worn by decades of study and — one suspects — by decades of being correct when no one would listen. His face was deeply lined, his expression set in a permanent scowl that communicated not cruelty but an exhaustion with the general inadequacy of others. He wore round wire spectacles perched on a prominent nose, and his white curls rose in disarray above a red and gold academic uniform that spoke of a Shurevairean rank he'd outgrown and never quite shed. In motion he was brisk and dismissive; at rest he radiated the particular intensity of a man who believes his time is being wasted.

## Personality

Erros was brilliant, contemptuous, and almost entirely without social grace — not from ignorance but from a long-ago decision that other people's feelings were an inconvenient friction. He treated his colleagues as tools, his expedition members as obstacles, and Reyn'lael Joran as a key to be inserted into a lock. The tragedy of Erros is not that he was malevolent, but that he was incurious about the cost of the things he wanted. He could identify a breathstone's resonance frequency and remain entirely unaware that the mines where it was quarried were built on the suffering of enslaved tieflings. History, for Erros, was a puzzle box. He never quite grasped that it was also a record of pain.

He was not without intelligence about people — he recognized [[Reyn'lael Joran|Reyn]]'s potential immediately, understood how to leverage deference from the party when necessary, and knew exactly how far he could push before consequences arrived. He simply weighed those consequences as inconveniences rather than reckonings.

## Character History

Erros spent the better part of his long life studying the [[Navarresean Empire]] — its pneuma-craft, its architectural legacy, the breathstone systems underlying the [[Watcher of the Lake]]. He believed, with the certainty of a man who has been right before, that the Tower held secrets that would "restructure the very foundation of pneuma and pneuma-craft" as Halinor understood it. The expedition was his life's work made actionable — unofficial, unsanctioned by [[Shurevaire]]'s university, run on his own terms because institutions move too slowly for the discoveries he intended to make.

He recruited [[Layla Basura]] as a field companion and co-organizer, and through her recruited [[Reyn'lael Joran]] — a young scholar whose bloodline Erros had identified as Navarresean royal, the specific key the Tower required. He did not tell Reyn this in full. He presented the expedition as an educational opportunity and let the boy's enthusiasm do the rest.

Inside the Tower, Erros was precisely as useful and precisely as insufferable as expected. His knowledge of Navarresean systems proved genuinely valuable. His treatment of the party and of Reyn proved consistently corrosive. When Reyn leveraged his indispensability to demand Erros treat the group with basic respect, Erros complied because the arithmetic required it. He did not change. He adapted.

In the Tower's throne room, Erros acted independently of [[Layla Basura]]'s own betrayal, though both chose the same catastrophic moment. Using the stolen half of [[Velethoui]]'s shattered staff as a pneumatic battery, he attempted to open a portal to the [[The Empty|Empty]] — not out of malice, but out of the particular arrogance of a scholar who believed that any power, properly channeled, could be made to yield knowledge. He was wrong. The Empty does not yield knowledge. It consumes. The rift he tore corrupted Erros on contact, transforming him into a shade — a hollow, void-touched remnant of himself. Aspiria's Chosen destroyed him in the throne room. He did not survive to see what Reyn did next.

## Combat Style

Erros was not a fighter. His magic was academic in orientation — analytical, pneuma-theory-driven, best suited to identification, warding, and large-area effects rather than direct combat. In the Tower he contributed meaningfully when the threat was elemental or structural. In personal confrontation he was largely useless, which he considered beneath his concern. As a shade, whatever tactical judgment he once possessed was lost.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Reyn'lael Joran]]** – A key, then an obstacle, then something Erros never quite had the framework to name. Reyn admired his mind until he saw what it could destroy. Erros never admired Reyn's courage, because he never learned to see courage as a variable worth measuring.

**[[Layla Basura]]** – A colleague and field partner. He trusted her competence. He did not know she had her own agenda in the Tower, and would have found it irrelevant had he known — his agenda took precedence. They betrayed the party independently, at the same moment, for entirely different reasons, without coordination.

**Aspiria's Chosen** – He viewed them as hired muscle with occasional usefulness. The party mostly viewed him as a necessary inconvenience. Both assessments were partially correct.

## Secrets

Erros had long suspected that the [[The Empty|Empty]] — the void phenomenon documented in older Navarresean records as something Empress [[Amastacia Lael'Nathor]] had torn through from the astral sea — could be communed with rather than merely survived. He was wrong about this in every particular. The Empty is not a mind. It is an appetite. His final act was the proof of that thesis, administered at fatal cost.
