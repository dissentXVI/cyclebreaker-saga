**Attire**: Purple and gold academic vest over a cream high-collared shirt; gold hoop earrings; a small gold cross pendant at her throat

**Home**: Formerly [[Shurevaire]]

**Status**: At large — escaped after her betrayal in the [[Watcher of the Lake]]; whereabouts unknown

## Appearance

Layla Basura presented herself with quiet authority — composed, precise, and seemingly always slightly ahead of the conversation. Her dark skin and sharp brown eyes gave nothing away that she didn't intend to give. She dressed in the understated academic fashion of [[Shurevaire]], functional but well-made, with gold earrings and a small cross pendant that suggested either genuine faith or a very carefully curated impression of it. She was not imposing. She was the kind of person you trusted because nothing about her seemed to want anything.

## Personality

Layla was intelligent, patient, and almost entirely self-contained. She spoke in the measured cadences of someone who had learned to let others fill silences. She was genuinely perceptive about people — she identified [[Reyn'lael Joran]]'s potential early, managed [[Erros Opars]]'s ego effectively, and calibrated her warmth toward the party with enough sincerity that her betrayal registered as a wound to their judgment rather than simply a tactical failure. Whether that warmth was performance from the beginning or something she later chose to discard in favor of ambition is a question that may never be fully answered. Reyn never spoke harshly of her after the stabbing. That silence is its own kind of evidence.

She was not cruel. She simply decided, at some point, that what she wanted was worth taking — and that the people who would be hurt by that decision were a variable, not a concern.

## Character History

Layla was a Shurevairean scholar with a genuine expertise in Navarresean history and pneuma-craft, and a professional relationship with [[Erros Opars]] that predated the expedition. She served as his field partner and co-organizer for the unofficial journey to the [[Watcher of the Lake]], and it was her initiative that brought [[Reyn'lael Joran]] into the project. She had recognized Reyn's bloodline — or at minimum, his usefulness — before anyone formally introduced him to Erros.

She sat beside Reyn on the boat to Elesatha and watched him suppress his excitement about the sea air. She heard him promise he wouldn't be a disappointment. She knew, or suspected, what she was planning to do. She vouched for him anyway.

Inside the Tower she was professionally valuable: knowledgeable, calm under pressure, and apparently loyal. She helped stabilize the group's situation on the platform hub after the fire and ice breathstones were recovered. She contributed to the library arc. She built enough of a relationship with [[Laena Zahir]] that the betrayal registered to Laena not merely as a tactical failure but as an indictment of her own judgment.

In the Tower's throne room, Layla stabbed Reyn. Her reasoning was straightforward: she believed that Navarresean royal blood, used without Reyn's consent or consciousness, would allow her to seize control of the throne and direct the Tower's vast pneumatic systems for her own ends. She did not coordinate with [[Erros Opars]], who was acting on his own catastrophically misguided agenda in the same moment. She simply saw the opportunity and took it.

She was wrong about the throne requiring only blood. The Tower accepted Reyn himself — his will, his sacrifice, his choice. Layla's plan failed. She fled in the chaos and escaped before the Tower disappeared.

She has not been found.

## Combat Style

Layla fought as a bard — supportive, unpredictable, and most effective at the edges of a fight where her voice and her blade could do work without drawing attention. She used her magic to sustain allies, destabilize enemies, and on one significant occasion, keep [[Gothmog]] alive when he was dying in the forge level of the Tower. That she spent that credibility and then stabbed the boy she'd mentored says something about her sense of when a debt is paid.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Reyn'lael Joran]]** – She recruited him, mentored him, and gave him the first real opportunity of his life. Then she stabbed him when the moment came. He never spoke harshly of her. She presumably knows this, or would, if she thought about it.

**[[Erros Opars]]** – A professional partnership built on mutual usefulness. She managed his ego with practiced efficiency. She did not tell him she had her own agenda in the Tower, because she didn't need to and because he would have found it irrelevant anyway. They betrayed the party independently, simultaneously, for entirely different reasons.

**[[Laena Zahir]]** – Laena believed they were building a genuine connection during the Tower expedition. The betrayal cost Laena something beyond trust — it cost her confidence in her own ability to read people. Laena killed Layla during the throne room escape. She still thinks about it.

**Aspiria's Chosen** – She used them well while she needed them. When she no longer needed them, she made her move. She was aware, at minimum, of their capabilities — which is why she moved when they were most compromised and least able to stop her.

## Secrets

What Layla intended to do with the [[Watcher of the Lake]] if her plan had succeeded has not been established. The Tower's power is vast — twelve breathstones, the pneuma of enslaved souls, architectural systems reaching to the center of the world. A scholar with access to all of that and no royal bloodline obligation tying her to its function could have directed it toward almost anything. What she wanted it for badly enough to sacrifice everything she had built is a question that remains open — and one she may eventually have to answer.
