> "To ignore the plight of those one might conceivably save is not wisdom — it is indolence."

**Title**: Archmage of [[Shurevaire]]; Speaker of the Pneumeric Concord

**Allegiance**: [[Shurevaire]] (independent in conscience)

**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Elderly at time of death

**Eyes**: Grey-green, steady and unhurried

**Hair**: White, long and worn back; beard kept with the careful precision of a man who has better things to think about but does it anyway

**Attire**: Layered scholarly armor of teal and dark gold, practical beneath its elegance; always carried Duisaratte, his ironwood staff wound in breathstone thread

**Home**: [[Shurevaire]]

**Status**: Deceased — sacrificed at [[Sticaea Lake]] during the [[Trembling Foundations]]

## Appearance

Velethoui Arsenault was a figure of contained enormity — not imposing in the way of warriors, but in the way of mountains. Aged and weathered, his face was deeply lined but held no weakness in it, only the particular gravity of a man who has spent decades carrying things others set down. His white hair was worn long, swept back from a broad forehead, and his grey-green eyes moved slowly and saw everything.

He was rarely without Duisaratte, the ironwood staff he had carried for so long it seemed less a tool than an extension of himself. He was also, those close to him knew, rarely without something sweet in his pocket — a small and endearing contradiction in a man otherwise defined by discipline and consequence.

## Personality

Velethoui was warm without being soft, principled without being rigid, and patient in the way that only people who have genuinely considered the alternatives can be. He believed that knowledge without application was vanity, and that the choice to observe suffering rather than interrupt it was not wisdom — it was cowardice dressed in academic robes.

This belief put him at odds with [[Shurevaire]]'s dominant culture of neutrality and preservation. He did not argue loudly. He simply acted, and accepted the institutional cost of acting without apology. His relationship with Shurevaire's Council of Deans grew increasingly strained in his later years, not because he ceased to love his home, but because he refused to let love become an excuse for inaction.

He was beloved by his students — [[Diazana Trubough]] and [[Yenden Clearlade]] among them — not because he was easy, but because he was honest, and because his faith in them was the kind that asked something of them in return. He also had a weakness for tinkering with magicked artifacts that his more serious colleagues found undignified, and a well-documented fondness for sugared pastries that he never once tried to explain.

His grandchildren, [[Sydnea Arsenault|Sydnea]] and [[Sylviane Arsenault|Sylviane]], were his great delight and his great worry in equal measure — two brilliant, reckless young people who reminded him, often uncomfortably, of himself.

## Character History

Velethoui rose through [[Shurevaire]]'s academic hierarchy on the strength of a mind that outpaced its peers in both speed and rigor. His early work on pneuma resonance theory and spellweaving application became foundational texts. His founding of what would become the Pneumeric Concord gave institutional shape to the belief that scholarship had an obligation to the world beyond its own walls.

But it was his defiance of Shurevaire's isolationism that defined his later years. When the political climate between [[Vociterra]] and the other nations of [[Halinor]] began to destabilize, Velethoui and his students — including [[Diazana Trubough|Diazana]] and [[Yenden Clearlade|Yenden]] — brought their concerns before the Council of Deans, urging cooperation over withdrawal. The Council dismissed them. Yenden was reassigned to containment work at the Watcher of the Lake. Diazana was stripped of her duties. Velethoui carried the weight of those consequences without complaint.

He continued to act in the ways he could. He mentored, corresponded, and watched. And when the situation at Sticaea Lake erupted — when Cassius tes Amata's forbidden rite tore open the earth and the shade of [[Krisnarax]] began to rise in full — Velethoui moved.

He arrived at Sticaea Lake as Aqualondian and Tarwa'kin forces converged on the site. The damage had already been done: the land torn open, the lake draining into what would become the Sticaean Abyss, the breathstone tower rising from the lakebed. Krisnarax's shade was not yet fully unbound. Velethoui used the last of his life and the full weight of his knowledge to rebind it — driving the shade into the depths of the Abyss and sealing it there. The Trembling Foundations did not stop entirely, but they stopped short of catastrophe.

Those who witnessed his sacrifice have described it differently depending on who they are. To the soldiers present, it was a blinding column of light. To his students, it was the silence afterward. Duisaratte was found shattered in the crater where he had stood, and was recovered by Aspiria's Chosen and eventually entrusted to Diazana — who has been trying to understand it ever since.

## Legacy

Velethoui Arsenault is remembered publicly as the Archmage who gave his life to prevent the Trembling Foundations from consuming Halinor. Monuments have been discussed. His name is spoken with reverence in every city-state that felt the earth shake that day.

Within [[Shurevaire]], his memory is more complicated. The University honors him in the way institutions honor people who proved them wrong — carefully, from a distance, with the more inconvenient parts quietly omitted. His final expedition is not fully discussed. The disagreements are not archived. The students he championed who were stripped of their posts are not mentioned alongside his name.

His grandchildren are carrying his questions into a world that is still learning to ask them. His staff waits, broken, in the hands of the woman who loved him most honestly. And somewhere beneath the Sticaean Abyss, the thing he gave everything to contain still pulses in the dark.

## Combat Style

Velethoui was not a battle-mage by inclination, but by necessity he had become one of the most formidable practitioners of applied pneumacraft alive. His spellwork was architectural in quality — precise, layered, and built to last. He favored containment, binding, and large-scale stabilization over offensive casting. Duisaratte amplified and focused his pneuma with a resonance built over decades of use, making the staff as much a part of his casting as his own hands.

He fought like a man who understood the cost of every spell he cast. Nothing was wasted. Nothing was reckless. And when the moment required everything, he gave it without flinching.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Diazana Trubough]]** – His most irreverent and most devoted student. She frustrated him regularly and impressed him constantly. He trusted her with Duisaratte's legacy not because she was the safest choice, but because she was the right one.

**[[Yenden Clearlade]]** – His most careful student and, in many ways, his truest inheritor. Yenden carries his approach to scholarship — ethical, collaborative, patient — more faithfully than he may realize.

**[[Sydnea Arsenault]] & [[Sylviane Arsenault]]** – His grandchildren. He gave them matching halves of a larger grimoire before his final expedition, a quiet instruction that they would only understand in time: that they needed each other more than either of them knew.

**[[Shurevaire]]** – His home and his greatest frustration. He never stopped loving it. He never stopped disagreeing with it.
