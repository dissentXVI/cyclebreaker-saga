**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Young adult

**Eyes**: Brown, guarded

**Hair**: Dark brown, short and unstyled

**Attire**: Plain farmhand clothing — heavy wool sweater, worn scarf; nothing that marks him as anything other than what he claims to be

**Home:** [[Snowfinch Rest]] / Marveux Farms, [[Carinthia]]

## Appearance

Jorren has his brother's features softened by years of outdoor labour and sharpened by something harder to name. He dresses like what he is — a farmhand — and has clearly worked at looking unremarkable. He has a noticeable limp, the result of an injury sustained during the wagon raid he helped plan, that he has been unable to entirely hide. His arms are usually crossed, and his expression is that of someone calculating how much the person in front of him already knows.

## Personality

Quiet, guarded, and more principled than his choices suggest. Jorren didn't join the Icehearts out of cruelty or chaos — he joined because he believes in something, and he was willing to risk everything for it. He is not reckless; he planned the Heartroot diversion carefully, forging his brother's handwriting in the supply ledger and covering his tracks with the patience of someone who understood the stakes.

When pressed or cornered, he runs rather than fights. Not cowardice — calculation. He knows what the Inquisition does to people like him.

## Narrative Relevance

Jorren is the key figure in the wagon raid investigation at [[Snowfinch Rest]]. He diverted a large supply of Heartroot Coagulant to the [[Icehearts]] by forging entries in his brother [[Tristan Halvich]]'s supply ledger. His limp, the blood trail back to town, the hidden map beneath the floorboards of his room, and the matching handwriting on the original requisition form are the chain of evidence that leads the players to him — and through him, to the Iceheart hideout near the frozen lake. His brother's arrest by the Inquisition is a direct consequence of his actions, a weight Jorren will carry regardless of how events resolve.
