**Race**: Human

**Gender**: Female

**Age**: Late 40s

**Eyes**: One sharp blue; one slightly glazed

**Hair**: Copper-red, pulled loosely back

**Attire**: Sturdy work shirt, heavy brown apron, sleeves always rolled up

**Home:** [[The Pale Roost]], [[Snowfinch Rest]]

## Appearance

Greta is broad-shouldered and solidly built, the kind of woman who looks like she could carry a full keg and an argument at the same time — and has. Her copper hair is usually pinned up in a loose, practical knot that loses the battle with gravity by midday. One eye is sharp and blue; the other carries a faint glaze, the remnant of an old injury she's never explained and no one has been brave enough to ask about. She is usually found behind the bar or leaning over the stove, ladle in hand, looking entirely at home.

## Personality

Blunt, pragmatic, and capable of warmth when she decides you've earned it. Greta has the vocabulary of a sailor and the intuition of someone who has been reading people across a bar counter for twenty years. She can play the fool with convincing ease when it suits her — a slack-jawed smile, a shrug, a refilled cup — but she is no fool, and anyone who mistakes her for one tends to regret it.

She hates the Inquisition with a quiet, deeply-held conviction she would never say out loud. She expresses it instead in small, satisfying ways: their ale comes out a little slow, their stew runs a little thin, and somehow there is never quite enough of the good bread when they come calling.

She doesn't care for small talk. She cares deeply for a good story.

## Narrative Relevance

Alongside her husband [[Tomas Wundt]], Greta runs the [[The Pale Roost]] and serves as one of [[Bernard Snowtree]]'s most trusted sources of local information. She is the one who hears things — who sat with whom, who left before dawn, who looked nervous when they shouldn't have. For the players, Greta is a potential ally who rewards directness and a decent yarn; she has no patience for evasion but plenty for someone willing to be honest over a bowl of stew.
