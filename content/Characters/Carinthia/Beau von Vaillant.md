**Title**: Lord Beau of [[House Vaillant]]

**Allegiance**: Carinthia (High House Vaillant)

**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Early-20s

**Eyes**: Emerald Green

**Hair**: Tousled black curls

**Attire**: Red and gold regalia with fur trim and layered jewelry

**Home**: [[Vaillant Hall]], [[Heldenheim]]

## Appearance

Beau von Vaillant is a vision of charming mischief wrapped in noble regalia. With tousled curls, vivid green eyes, and a disarming smile, he looks less like a soldier and more like a romantic hero sprung from a bard’s ballad. His cloak — red with gold trim — is always fashionably draped, his collar perpetually crooked, and his gloves mysteriously absent. He carries a ceremonial sword, though it’s often overshadowed by the gleam of his rings or the glint in his eyes.

He moves like someone who knows he’s being watched — and enjoys it immensely.

## Personality

Beau is flirtatious, clever, and dangerously observant. He disarms with charm, distracts with wit, and deflects with ease. Behind his playful exterior is a mind far sharper than many expect — he is excellent at uncovering secrets hidden beneath laughter or flattery.

He has a genuine love for people and attention, but struggles to focus on his noble responsibilities. Not out of malice or laziness — simply because he often feels more alive in a tavern booth or a masked gala than at a war council.

## Character History

Born the second son of Lord [[Gerard von Vaillant]], Beau was raised in the same halls and taught the same lessons as his older brother Matthias — but responded to them with very different instincts. Where Matthias internalized duty and discipline, Beau discovered loopholes and social nuance.

Though trained in swordplay and rhetoric, Beau rarely excelled in either arena by conventional standards. Instead, he flourished in courtly intrigue, diplomacy, and the manipulation of reputation. He was once sent to [[Avosa Tesota]] as part of a "cultural exchange" and returned with both fluent Low Avosan and a merchant’s daughter smuggled in a diplomatic carriage.

He holds no formal military rank beyond a ceremonial officer’s commission, though he occasionally rides as a liaison or envoy under his brother’s or [[Adrien Frost]]’s direction. He’s most often seen where politics, scandal, or celebration coincide.

## Current Status

Beau currently resides in [[City-State]], splitting his time between courtly obligations, unofficial diplomatic work, and charming his way into the city’s restricted archives and noble salons. He is technically attached to [[Carinthia]]’s war effort as an auxiliary, but his real strength lies in information gathering and morale-boosting — often of the wine and whispered variety.

He knows of [[Bernard Snowtree]]’s true parentage, though he handles it with rare discretion. He respects Bernard more than he lets on and has subtly shielded him from gossip when needed.

## Combat Style

Beau carries a blade and knows how to use it, but he prefers to avoid violence whenever possible. If forced into a fight, he fights with grace and quick reflexes — more duelist than soldier.

## Notable Relationships

[[Matthias von Vaillant]] – His older brother, and someone he both admires and teases relentlessly. Though their approaches to life differ greatly, the bond between them is solid. Beau often serves as Matthias’s voice of levity, and Matthias serves as Beau’s conscience when needed.

[[Bernard Snowtree]] – Beau discovered the truth long ago and has never spoken of it publicly. He respects Bernard deeply and is one of the few nobles to treat him as a full equal — sometimes with brotherly jest, sometimes with quiet solidarity.

[[Adrien Frost]] – Beau finds [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]] fascinating: noble, tragic, unreadable. He flirts with him more than is wise, perhaps to hide his own genuine respect. He often runs errands for [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]] unofficially, usually involving people, places, or parties others wouldn’t dare touch.

Lord [[Gerard von Vaillant]] – Beau loves his father, though their relationship is strained. Gerard sees Beau’s potential, but struggles to accept his flamboyant nature. Beau, for his part, tries to live up to his father’s legacy — just on his own terms.
