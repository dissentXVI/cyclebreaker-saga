**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Young adult

**Eyes**: Brown, steady

**Hair**: Light brown, shoulder-length, neatly kept

**Attire**: Snowfinch Guard uniform — scale armor beneath a blue cloak, belted with military precision

**Home:** [[Snowfinch Rest]], [[Carinthia]]

## Appearance

Tristan carries himself with the careful posture of a man who takes his post seriously. His guard uniform is always in order, his cloak fastened correctly, his belt buckled tight. There is nothing flashy about him — he looks exactly like what he is: a young elf who has decided that doing his job well is the most important thing he can offer. He has his brother's dark colouring but none of his brother's restlessness.

## Personality

By-the-book, precise, and quietly proud of it. Tristan folds his socks by regulation — the town gossips are not wrong about this. He has little patience for shortcuts, corners cut, or paperwork filed incorrectly. He is not cold, exactly, but he is formal in a way that keeps most people at a comfortable distance.

He loves his brother Jorren with a loyalty that runs deeper than his rules. When faced with evidence of what Jorren had done, his first instinct was not to condemn him but to tell the players to help him run. That instinct cost Tristan considerably — he was arrested by the Inquisition on suspicion of complicity and held until Lord Commander [[Adrien Frost]] intervened to exonerate him.

## Narrative Relevance

Tristan's forged requisition ledger is a key clue in the supply chain investigation at [[Snowfinch Rest]] — the angular handwriting in one entry belongs to [[Jorren Halvich]], not him. He is a useful contact for the players afterward: he knows the Snowfinch supply lines in detail, and he owes them something he won't easily say aloud. He has a complicated relationship with [[Bernard Snowtree]], who trusted him before events overtook them both.
