**Title**: Archbishop of [[Carinthia]]

**Allegiance**: [[The Light of Alophia]]

**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Late 100s (appears early 70s by human standards)

**Eyes**: Pale Blue

**Hair**: White, close-cropped

**Attire**: White and gold ecclesiastical robes with layered vestments

**Home**: [[Cathedral of the Sacred Light]], [[Heldenheim]]


## Appearance

Aedelweg von Achaard is always seen in full ceremonial regalia — robes of ivory thread, gold trim, and embroidered verses from the Testament of [[Alophia]]. His face is long and stern, like a figure carved from stone. He stands tall for his age, with angular features, sunken cheeks, and ears as sharp as his rhetoric. His presence dominates whatever room he enters, whether pulpit, council chamber, or throne of judgment.

## Personality

Archbishop Aedelweg is shrewd, calculating, and unflinchingly devout. He believes deeply in divine providence — and equally in the necessity of order and sacrifice. He presents himself as a spiritual father to the nation, but behind closed doors, he is a master of statecraft and subtle coercion.

He values results over sentiment and sees himself as the steward of Carinthia's divine destiny, even if that means bloodying his hands. He rarely raises his voice, yet commands silence with a glance. To his allies, he is a bulwark of faith. To his enemies — or even some close to him — he is as cold as carved ivory.

## Character History

Aedelweg von Achaard was born into a noble bloodline known for its theological scholarship. From a young age, he was groomed for the mantle of spiritual authority. By his first century, he had already penned three canonical commentaries on [[Alophia]]'s doctrine and purged a cult from within the inner sanctum of the Church itself.

His rise to Archbishop was swift and uncontested — not only due to his brilliance and conviction but also because he quietly orchestrated the disgrace or disappearance of all serious rivals. As Archbishop, he centralized power within the Church, expanding the reach of the Inquisition and tightening ecclesiastical control over the nobility.

## Current Status

Aedelweg von Achaard is the supreme spiritual authority in Carinthia and its High Theocrat — de facto head of state when the Church asserts its dominance. From the towering [[Cathedral of the Sacred Light]] in [[Heldenheim|Carinthia]], he directs [[The Inquisition]], oversees the [[The Dawnbound|Dawnbound]], and shapes the will of the Four High Houses through pressure, prophecy, and fear.

He remains wary of [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]]'s growing popularity and has increased oversight over the Templars, embedding inquisitors into every major campaign under the guise of "spiritual guidance."

His personal guards, the [[Dawnbound]], are loyal beyond question, and the nobility of House Achaard obey his will with religious fervor.

## Combat Style

Aedelweg's command of holy scripture is legendary. He often speaks in layered references to divine law, turning sermons into veiled threats and blessings into chains. He rarely invokes miracles — preferring to rule through policy, prophecy, and punishment — but his power as a high cleric is genuine.

It is rumored that his personal relic, The Dawnbrand Medallion, allows him to channel Alophia's will directly into wards, bindings, and curses — though none who have witnessed its use have lived to confirm it.

## Notable Relationships

[[Adrien Frost]] – Their relationship is icy and formal, bound by duty but fractured by pride and unspoken resentment.

[[Sabine von Achaard]] and [[Martin von Achaard]] – His legitimate children, raised within the Church's doctrine and loyal to its cause. Both serve in high-ranking positions — one in the priesthood, the other in the Inquisition — and act as Aedelweg's instruments of will across the realm.

[[The Inquisition]] – Aedelweg's favored blade. He has expanded their influence under the justification of heresy prevention, using them to root out dissent and enforce loyalty. The Inquisition fears him as much as they revere him.
