**Title**: Lord of [[House Vaillant]]

**Allegiance**: [[Carinthia]] (Secular and Noble Authority)

**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Late 80s

**Eyes**: Grey

**Hair**: Black streaked with silver, neatly combed

**Attire**: Crimson velvet cloak with black fur trim and carved armor underneath

**Home**: [[Vaillant Hall]], [[Heldenheim]]

## Appearance

Lord Gerard cuts a striking figure — a man of stately bearing, draped in red and black with a silver brooch in the shape of a twelve-pointed star. His eyes are sharp with experience, and his expression rarely softens in public. His clothing is noble yet practical, worn to endure Heldenheim’s harsh winters and the colder winds of politics. He keeps his beard trimmed and his posture upright, like a knight who never let go of the battlefield. He walks with a rimewood cane, a constant reminder of the day he lost his wife.

## Personality

Gerard von Vaillant is principled, honorable, and iron-willed. Though his methods are strict, his sense of justice is genuine. He believes in the sacred duty of the nobility to protect [[Carinthia]] — not to rule from towers, but to stand shoulder-to-shoulder with its soldiers and people.

He speaks little but listens closely, weighing his words like iron. In private, he can be warm and deeply loyal, especially to those who share his sense of duty. But betrayal — particularly of Carinthia’s ideals — is something he does not forgive.

Unlike many of his peers, Gerard despises vanity and favors action over rhetoric. His strength lies not in political scheming, but in reliability, discipline, and a long memory

## Character History

Gerard von Vaillant was born during the last major dragon incursion and raised during the fragile peace that followed. His early years were spent under the weight of expectation — trained in tactics, governance, and command from the moment he could read.

He served in the border skirmishes of the northern range and was eventually named Commander of the [[Crimson Wall]], Carinthia’s oldest military bastion. There, he earned the loyalty of his bannermen and the respect of the [[High Houses]] through grit and tactical brilliance.

When his father passed, Gerard inherited [[House Vaillant]] and shifted it from traditional isolationism to active cooperation with the secular Templar command — even if it meant enduring the Church’s oversight.

He is the only noble to have stood beside every Lord Commander for the last century, offering counsel not as a puppet master, but as a swordarm of Carinthia itself.

## Current Status

Lord Gerard is one of the most respected secular voices in Carinthia and a pillar of the High Council. Though his formal duties lie in overseeing the realm’s military infrastructure, he acts as both advisor and counterweight to [[Adrien Frost|Lord Commander Adrien Frost]].

Their bond is one of shared ideals and mutual respect — Gerard was one of the few nobles to vote in favor of [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]]’s appointment, despite the controversy of his bloodline.

As the [[Wyrmsfall War]] escalates, Gerard remains in [[City-State]] to manage supply lines, train new Templar officers, and guard the political flanks of the war effort. He regularly intervenes to keep the Inquisition’s reach from undermining strategic defense and is not afraid to oppose [[Archbishop Aedelweg]] — though always with measured words.

## Combat Style

Though he no longer rides to war, Gerard was once a renowned battlefield commander. In his prime, he led pikewall formations and heavy infantry with unmatched precision. He now trains elite commanders in his doctrine of "strength through restraint" — winning not through brute force, but unshakable discipline.

His ceremonial weapon, a greatsword named Red Oath, is said to have slain three dragons in the old war and is now kept in his family’s warhall.

## Notable Relationships

[[Adrien Frost]] – Gerard sees [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]] as the future of Carinthia. He respects the young commander’s integrity and skill, and protects him politically when needed.

[[Aedelweg von Achaard|Archbishop Aedelweg von Achaard]] – Their relationship is professional but wary. Gerard tolerates the Archbishop’s rule for the sake of unity, but privately disapproves of his manipulations and theological excesses. He views Aedelweg’s refusal to acknowledge [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]] as both cowardly and dangerous.

Secrets

[[Bernard Snowtree]] – Gerard’s illegitimate son by a lowborn woman. Though he has never formally acknowledged Bernard, he watches his career with interest and quietly ensures he has the resources needed. His pride prevents open recognition, but his care manifests in small, unseen acts.
