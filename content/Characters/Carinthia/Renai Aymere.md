**Alias**: The Ice Witch

## Appearance

Renai Aymere wears no crown, but her bearing is regal and resolute. Her garments, stitched in deep blue and icy white, are neither noble nor common — they are symbolic. The fur lining evokes warmth, but her presence is cold. Her expression is calm, distant, and severe, as if her thoughts dwell in memories no one else remembers. The wind often follows her, and her longbow — adorned with bone and glass — is always close at hand.

## Personality

Renai is serene in the way that glaciers are: beautiful, patient, and capable of catastrophic power. She speaks in soft tones even when addressing enemies, and rarely raises her voice — unless invoking her so-called divine purpose. Her charisma is undeniable, and those who speak with her often leave questioning their own beliefs.

She believes herself to be the reincarnation of Saint Iseult, the controversial elven saint who once brokered peace with dragons. Unlike the Light of Alophia, which views Saint Iseult’s legacy with discomfort, Renai embraces it fully — seeing herself as a vessel through which the cycle of war can finally end.

To her followers, she is a prophet. To her enemies, a heretic. To herself, she is simply fulfilling a memory reborn.

## Character History

Renai’s early life is a mystery. No noble house claims her, and Church records contain no mention of her before a failed inquisition sweep in the Vale of Spires nearly a decade ago. The survivors of that mission whispered of a woman who called frost to her fingertips and spoke in the ancient tongue of dragons.

Since then, Renai Aymere has led the Icehearts, a cult that operates in the remote regions of Carinthia. Comprised of outcasts, deserters, and believers in draconic reconciliation, the Icehearts reject both the Light of Alophia and the authority of the High Houses. They believe that peace with Wodr’s Brood is possible — not through steel, but through understanding.

Renai preaches that she remembers her past life as Saint Iseult, and that Alophia has betrayed her original teachings. Her message has found fertile ground in the cold, neglected corners of the realm.

## Current Status

Renai has been seen intermittently across the Free Den and Frostmere, with confirmed sightings near Snowfinch Rest, where her cult has disrupted supply lines.

Her activities have triggered multiple Templar deployments and Inquisition warrants. While many within the Church call for her death, [[Adrien Frost|Lord Commander Frost]] has urged restraint, citing the strategic risk of martyring her. The truth of her past, her power, and her intentions remains a growing mystery that gnaws at Carinthia’s fragile stability.

## Combat Style

Renai is a powerful mage specializing in frost and wind magic. When she invokes her Blessing of Light, she transforms into Saint Iseult herself - a paragon of her at least. Her spells are elegant and symbolic — often mimicking natural phenomena like snowfall, crystal bloom, or glacial rupture. She is also a master archer, able to strike from impossible distances.

Her rituals often involve invocation of Saint Iseult’s name and draconic prayers. Some claim she has spoken directly with Wodr’s children, though the truth remains unverified.

## Notable Relationships

[[Adrien Frost]] – While they have never met directly, Renai speaks of [[Adrien Frost|Adrien]] with cautious respect. She views him as a tragic figure — torn between duty and clarity — and believes he could one day join her cause if he lets go of Alophia’s chains.

[[The Inquisition]] – Her most vocal and dangerous enemies. The Inquisition has labeled her an apostate, blasphemer, and threat to national security. Renai, in turn, refers to them as “fire-drunk zealots who mistake ash for purity.”

[[The Icehearts]] – Her loyal cult. They see her as a miracle returned — a living saint. Most are former soldiers, refugees, and those broken by war. With Renai, they find not just purpose, but peace.

Secrets

Renai believes herself to be the reincarnation of the saint, and her entire worldview is shaped by this conviction. She claims to remember Iseult’s thoughts, feelings, and dreams — and holds Alophia’s Church accountable for silencing her voice.
