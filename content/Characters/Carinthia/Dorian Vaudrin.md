**Title**: High Inquisitor

**Allegiance**: [[The Light of Alophia]]

**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Late 90s

**Eyes**: Piercing yellow

**Hair**: None

**Attire**: gray cloak with silver scripture, reinforced black leather armor

**Home**: [[Cathedral of the Sacred Light]], [[Heldenheim]]

## Appearance

Dorian Vaudrin cuts an imposing figure even in the grandest chapel. Tall, rail-thin, and severe, he wears his authority like a blade at the throat. His skin is stretched tight over sharp elven features, his face marred by a deep scar that runs across one cheek like a carved sentence. His bald head gleams beneath candlelight, and his pointed ears leave no doubt of his Carinthian lineage.

His unblinking yellow eyes radiate judgment — the kind that has no need for second chances. He dresses in a high-collared gray cloak stitched with silver scripture, bearing a pendant of Alophia’s twelve-pointed star over his chest. The silver is worn with age but polished with near-religious precision. Beneath the cloak, his armor is not meant for open battle, but for domination: black leather reinforced with etched steel, festooned with symbols of divine authority. It is the regalia of a man who wages war not on fields, but in confession chambers.

## Personality

Dorian Vaudrin is cruel, calculated, and absolutely unshakable. He sees mercy as a form of spiritual negligence and believes fear is the most honest form of reverence. His faith is not warm or aspirational—it is cold, ordered, and immutable.

Dorian rarely speaks directly, preferring to control conversations through silence, implication, and the weight of his gaze. He relishes the power of unease, forcing others to reveal more than they intend by saying nothing at all. He is a master of psychological pressure and theological guilt.

His loyalty to doctrine is not just strong—it is absolute. He does not question the Church because he sees himself as its natural hand. In his view, only weakness breeds rebellion, and only fire cleanses doubt.

## Character History

Born to a minor elven house in [[City-State]] loyal to the Archdiocese, Dorian was groomed for religious service from an early age. He showed an early aptitude for doctrinal law and a talent for discerning lies—traits that propelled him into the Inquisition’s inner circles.

During the [[Vardane Purge]], he led a sweeping investigation that exposed three heretical clerics and sentenced dozens to death. His methods were brutal, but effective, and earned him both fear and favor within the Church. He was eventually appointed High Inquisitor of the Northern Watch, charged with overseeing heretical threats along Carinthia’s icy borders.

## Current Status

Dorian commands the Inquisition’s presence in [[Snowfinch Rest]], tasked with purging the Iceheart cult and suppressing local instability. Though he tolerates [[Bernard Snowtree|Captain Bernard Snowtree's]] jurisdiction, he clearly views the Templar’s methods as weak-willed and indulgent.

Dorian is known to have sanctioned burnings within the chapel crypt to extract truth from suspects, though the Church officially denies such rumors. He monitors his own agents for signs of moral deviation, including his subordinate, [[Alsten Duvaine]]—whom he sees as a useful, if blunt, instrument.

## Combat Style

Dorian does not seek combat—but when pressed, he fights with precision and cruelty. His preferred weapon is a ceremonial estoc blessed for piercing magical wards and the flesh of the damned. He is a master of binding rites, pain-driven confession magic, and silent intimidation.

His true strength lies in psychological warfare—most of his enemies are defeated before the first blow lands.

## Notable Relationships

[[Aedelweg von Achaard|Archbishop Aedelweg von Achaard]] – The only man Dorian truly serves. He views the Archbishop not just as the voice of Alophia, but as the last line against the world’s moral decay.

[[Alsten Duvaine]] – A functional but flawed tool. Dorian tolerates his ambition and charm, but watches him carefully for signs of corruption or disobedience.

[[Bernard Snowtree]] – A well-meaning fool. Dorian sees Bernard’s compassion as a liability and would remove him if not for political restraint.
