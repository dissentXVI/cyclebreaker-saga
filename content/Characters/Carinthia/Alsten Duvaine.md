**Title**: Confessor

**Allegiance**: [[The Light of Alophia]]

**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Late 30s

**Eyes**: Storm blue

**Hair**: Black, tousled and swept back with precise care

**Attire**: Tailored gray cloak trimmed in gold, polished boots, pristine leathers beneath

**Home**: [[Cathedral of the Sacred Light]], [[Heldenheim]]

## Appearance

Alsten Duvaine looks more like a noble courtier than an inquisitor—elegant, handsome, and perfectly composed. He wears the red and gold of the Light’s Inquisition, but with refinement: his cloak is custom-tailored, his boots always polished, his armor beneath untouched by ash or blood. He carries no visible weapon, though a discerning eye might catch the glint of a gilded dagger nestled under one sleeve—more ornament than threat… until it’s not.

He stands with the posture of a man used to being watched, every movement deliberate, every word weighed.

## Personality

Where others in the Inquisition dominate through fear, Alsten disarms with a smile. He is charming, ruthless, and quietly ambitious—never openly disloyal, but never truly loyal either. He mirrors those he speaks to: gentle to the fearful, cruel with the cruel, devout with the pious, and pragmatic among soldiers.

He collects secrets, names, and favors like coins in a noble’s ledger, always investing in future advantage. Every conversation is a transaction. His loyalty to the Church is a performance; his only true allegiance is to himself.

## Character History

Born in the lower quarters of [[City-State]], the bastard son of a dockworker and a transient scribe, Alsten clawed his way into the Church through a mix of brilliance and calculation. A patron from [[House Bellefeuille]] secured his seminary education, though Alsten never forgot the cost of charity—or how to repay it in full.

He rose quickly through minor clerical ranks before catching the attention of High Inquisitor [[Dorian Vaudrin]]. Under Vaudrin's tutelage, he shed his humble origins and emerged as one of the Inquisition’s most promising—if controversial—confessors.

## Current Status

Now stationed at [[Snowfinch Rest]] under [[Dorian Vaudrin|Vaudrin]]’s authority, Alsten is investigating ties between the Iceheart cult and recent supply disruptions. He’s requested broader authority to act independently and has begun compiling dossiers on both civilian and military figures—including Captain Bernard Snowtree.

Though publicly cooperative, Alsten’s true goal is influence. He views the unrest in Snowfinch as an opportunity to rise.

## Combat Style

Alsten avoids brute force. He prefers surgical action, precise threats, and psychological leverage. His hidden dagger is blessed and enchanted to sever magical wards and bindings. When forced into combat, he aims for disabling strikes and swift conclusions—violence is simply a means to restore control.

His most dangerous weapon remains his tongue, sharpened by study and subtlety.

## Notable Relationships

[[Dorian Vaudrin]] – His mentor, and the only man whose approval Alsten still seeks. He respects Vaudrin’s power but quietly believes he’ll surpass him.

[[Bernard Snowtree]] – A frustrating obstacle. Alsten sees Bernard’s warmth as a liability and has begun gathering evidence to discredit him.

[[Adrien Frost]] – A symbol of secular interference. Alsten respects his presence, but believes the Templars should answer to the Church more directly.

Secrets

Alsten once forced a false confession from a frightened villager during his early trials, covering it up when the truth emerged. The man died by fire. Rather than risk his standing, Alsten falsified the records and burned the originals. He rarely dwells on it, but he’s never forgotten the feeling of being untouchable.
