**Title**: Lord Commander of the [[Knights Templar]] (Secular Arm of Carinthia)

**Allegiance**: [[Carinthia]]

**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Late 40s

**Eyes**: Piercing ice blue

**Hair**: Wavy black, shoulder-length

**Attire**: Ornate blue and gold plate with Carinthian etchings

**Home:** [[Skyward Keep]] [[Heldenheim]]

## Appearance

Adrien wears an elegant blue plate trimmed with gold filigree, adorned with a twelve-pointed star and motifs of wings and spears. The armor bears faint scorches from dragonfire, which he refuses to polish out — reminders of sacrifices made. He moves with quiet authority, his posture noble but never haughty. In battle, he dons a full-faced helmet etched with silver wings to hide his features and represent Carinthia itself rather than any one man.

## Personality

Adrien Frost is measured, diplomatic, and principled. Though unfailingly polite and composed, he speaks with the weight of command and conviction. He believes in the strength of strategy tempered by compassion, and refuses to let zealotry dictate his actions. His loyalty is to the people of Carinthia — not to power, nor to blind faith.

He is known to carry grief privately. Every soldier lost under his watch leaves a mark. Yet he never wavers. His presence steadies the morale of both commoners and nobles alike, and he is seen by many as the living standard of Carinthian resolve.

## Character History

Adrien was born to a minor noble house along Carinthia’s harsh eastern borderlands. Or so the official record states.

His mother was a handmaiden at the Cathedral of [[Saint Carine]] — quietly sent away before her pregnancy showed. Adrien was placed with a loyal foster family and raised away from the capital.

As a young officer, Adrien earned his first real recognition at the Siege of [[Lichtendorn]], where he and fewer than a hundred Templars held the high pass against wyverns for seven days. His tactics — brutal in execution but humane in purpose — earned him battlefield renown and noble patronage.

When the previous Lord Commander fell in a failed northern crusade, Adrien was chosen by the Council of the [[High Houses]]. Though his lineage was modest by Carinthian standards, his record and character made the choice clear. His appointment marked a shift in the Templars — from dogmatic enforcers to disciplined guardians of the people.

## Current Status

Adrien currently commands in the [[Wyrmsfall War]], Carinthia’s desperate struggle against Wodr’s Brood — a horde of vengeful dragons led by the ancient wyrm [[Wodr]]. As Lord Commander, Adrien oversees all military actions north of Heldenheim. From forward bastions along [[Kiergan Ridge]] to remote signal towers in the Maiden’s Covert, his orders shape the defense of the realm.

The [[Sebastien Cellier|Lightwarden]], Carinthia’s sacred dragon-slayer, is not merely a soldier, but a living weapon — bound by ancient rites to the will of the Lord Commander. Chosen by divine omen and clad in arcane relics, the [[Sebastien Cellier|Lightwarden]] is said to channel the very essence of dragonkind in order to destroy it.

The [[Sebastien Cellier|Lightwarden]] reports directly to Adrien alone, and is deployed only in the most desperate conflicts, such as when Wodr’s eldest spawn breached the skies above the [[Brannoch’s Peaks]]. Adrien guards the [[Sebastien Cellier|Lightwarden]]’s true role fiercely — not just as a symbol of hope, but as a last resort when conventional armies break.

Combat Style

Adrien is a master of battlefield tactics and spear-based combat. His signature weapon, Vigil’s End, is a glaive of quicksilver-steeped steel, forged in the old style and blessed in ceremony but built for real bloodshed. In close quarters, he fights with a longsword.

He favors positional advantage and defensive resilience, drawing out foes and then collapsing on them with precise counterattacks. His use of elite formations and tactical retreats has preserved thousands of lives

## Notable Relationships

[[Bernard Snowtree]] – Adrien holds Bernard in high esteem, recognizing his strength, loyalty, and moral clarity in the defense of Snowfinch Rest. Though technically a bastard, Bernard has Adrien’s full trust and political backing.

[[Aedelweg von Achaard|Archbishop Aedelweg von Achaard]] – Their alliance is cold and formal. Adrien respects the Archbishop’s role, but resents his manipulation of the Church.

## Secrets

In truth, he is the illegitimate son of [[Aedelweg von Achaard|Archbishop Aedelweg von Achaard]], a secret known only to a scarce few.
