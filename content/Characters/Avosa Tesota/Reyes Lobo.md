**Title:** Warden of the Gragato Rangers  

**Allegiance:** [[Avosa Tesota]]  

**Race:** Elf  

**Gender:** Male  

**Age:** Appears mid-30s (actual age 120)  

**Eyes:** Amber-green with faint gold rings like sunlight through leaves  

**Hair:** Deep brown, long and loosely braided with jungle vines and feathers  

**Attire:** Lightweight elven armor of leather and steel, forest-green cloak, travel-worn boots, and a silver fang clasp; carries a curved machete and a vinewood bow

**Home:** Ranger’s Hold, [[Gragato Jungle]]  
## Appearance

Reyes Lobo looks carved from the forest itself—lithe, sun-bronzed, and weathered by endless patrols beneath the Gragato canopy. His amber-green eyes gleam like sunlight through moss, while a streak of green-gold pigment runs across his face from temple to cheek, marking him as a sworn warden.  

His ears are tattooed along the edges with geometric tribal sigils, each representing a hunt, treaty, or oath fulfilled. The designs shimmer faintly when he channels pneuma. His armor bears scratches and stains of a hundred storms, more practical than decorative, and his posture is ever watchful—like a bow always half-drawn.

## Personality

Reyes is calm, deliberate, and difficult to read—his words chosen like arrows, each meant to land true. He believes the jungle is a covenant, not a possession, and treats even predators with measured respect. Though sworn to [[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]] and Avosa’s Congress, his true loyalty lies with the land itself.  

He laughs rarely, but when he does, it’s genuine and bright—a sound that startles more than comforts. He has the discipline of a soldier, the patience of a hunter, and the quiet reverence of a priest. In the city, his silence unnerves bureaucrats; in the wild, it is a language older than law.
## Character History

Born in the river village of Liraché on the edge of the Gragato, Reyes was the son of an elven herbalist and an elf navigator. From his mother he inherited knowledge of the tides and storms; from his father, the songs of roots and stones.  

When Avosa’s early expansion brought conflict to the jungle, Reyes swore the **Fruitbound Oath** alongside the gnoll tribes, sharing blood and fruit. That vow bound him to the protection of both the wilds and the city that depended upon them.  

He rose through the ranks of the Gragato Rangers, tempering idealism with experience. His reputation for fairness—and for surviving places no one else returned from—earned him command before his first century.
## Current Status

He has begun quietly coordinating with [[Corella Marante|Admiral Marante]] and [[Isaacs Cabres]], seeking proof that [[Serpiosa]]’s growing slave trade may be tied to this resurgence of primal magic. Though he prefers to act unseen, the jungle’s unrest may soon force his hand.

## Combat Style

Reyes wields a curved elven machete, forged from enchanted vine-steel, and a recurved bow. His fighting style is a fusion of motion and instinct—he flows through combat like water through reeds, striking only when escape is impossible.  

He summons forest spirits in the form of translucent beasts—jaguars of mist, serpents of smoke, and owls of light—to harry and distract his foes. When wounded, the vines around him tighten, forming a living shield until his breath returns.

## Notable Relationships

- **[[Corella Marante |Admiral Corella Marante]]** – Mutual admiration and shared pragmatism; she sees him as the conscience of Avosa.  
- **[[Isaacs Cabres]]** – Respectful but wary; bureaucracy irritates him, though Isaacs’s cleverness earns his trust.  
- **[[Solana Varros]]** – A former adversary whose rhetoric he distrusts. He fears her return to unchecked piracy would shatter the fragile peace with the gnoll tribes.
- **Gnoll Chieftess Mak’ara** – His oldest ally; they share a bond deeper than treaty or tongue.
