## Appearance

Corella Marante commands attention like a thunderhead cresting the horizon. Their undercut is severe — shaved clean on one side, while the other cascades in a wild plume of violet curls streaked with indigo. Their skin is sun-kissed and saltworn, pierced with precision: golden studs adorn their nose, lips, and jawline, catching light like sea-glass in stormlight. A regal forehead pendant crowns their presence, and ruby-drop earrings sway with each measured tilt of their head.

They wear the high-collared, gold-epauletted admiral’s coat of Avosa’s Navy — crimson and obsidian, lined with brass stitchwork and ceremonial braids. Beneath the collar gleams a chain of maritime merit and a crimson gem knotted in storm-etched gold.

Their gaze is unsparing — oceanic blue, rimmed in salt and stubbornness. They do not blink unless it serves a point. Every inch of their posture exudes command — the kind earned not through election, but by leading men into fire and bringing them back home.
## Personality

Corella is charisma weaponized. They command not just with tactics, but with presence — a voice that can hush a hall or rouse a fleet. Witty, unflinching, and sometimes ruthless, they believe in Avosa’s independence with religious fervor, but resents institutions that would ossify it into tyranny.

Though they were born into wealth, Corella has long shed the silk of aristocracy for the salt and leather of the sea. They respect cleverness, boldness, and results — not pedigrees.
To allies, they are a vibrant force of inspiration. To rivals, a storm that announces itself with a smile.
## Character History

Corella Marante was born on the sun-drenched isle of [[Ceralde]], one of the wealthier holdings in the [[Aflado Gulf]]. Their family were pearl magnates and spice barons — nobles in all but name, with a lineage steeped in trade, artifice, and quiet influence among several Fleet Captains and maritime guilds. From a young age, Corella was groomed to inherit a fleet of merchant schooners and a seat in the [[House of Maritime Shares]].

But wealth was never enough.

At sixteen, they vanished from their family estate, leaving only a letter and an empty mooring. By the time they resurfaced, they had already sailed under four false names, challenged a Vociran corsair to single combat (and won), and led a mutiny against a corrupt blockade officer. The life of adventure suited their — salt, steel, and sovereignty.

In the harbor taverns of [[Avosa Tesota]], they met a young bureaucrat named [[Isaacs Cabres]]. He bought them a drink they didn’t need and asked them a question no one else had: “What happens after the storm?” They liked him instantly.

Over the next two decades, they rose together — Corella the stormfront, [[Isaacs]] the harbormaster behind the scenes. Their bond turned heads and turned tides. When the time came to elect a new Admiral, it was [[Isaacs]] who pushed their nomination across the finish line.

They have held the title ever since, and their Banner has not faltered once.
## Current Status

As Admiral of [[The Congress of Captains]], Corella Marante walks the highwire between liberty and law. Their greatest challenge now lies not in foreign waters — but in the shadows of their own coast.

Rumors have reached their desk that [[Serpiosa]], a semi-autonomous outlaw settlement nestled in [[the Gragato Jungle]], has begun trafficking in slaves — a crime explicitly forbidden under Avosan Charter Law. More disturbing are the whispers that the victims are not humans, but members of the local gnolls, long believed protected under Avosa’s Treaty of Kinship.

Marante has responded with quiet fury. They dispatched bounty hunters and covert agents to infiltrate the town, expose the ringleaders, and shut down the trade. But most who go either vanish or return empty-handed, spooked by the depth of corruption and the wilderness that swallows trails.

Though they longs to send the Admiral’s Banner to raze the operation, they hold back — knowing that to send official forces into [[Serpiosa]] without congressional approval could trigger a fracturing of the peninsula’s delicate web of free cities and banner towns. They cannot risk open conflict among their own people.

Marante has grown wary of intelligence leaks, unsure which threads of [[the Red Net]] still answer to the Banner.
## Combat Style

Corella is a master duelist and battlefield tactician. In personal combat, they favor elegant saberplay enhanced by pneumatic pistols and quickburst magic relics passed down by pirate queens of old. Their movements are efficient, theatrical, and precise — every strike meant to dazzle and dominate.
