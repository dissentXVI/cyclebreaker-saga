**Race**: True Elf (Navarresean)

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Unknown — a veteran battlefield commander during the First Celestial Campaign

**Eyes**: Dark brown — steady and direct, soldier's eyes

**Hair**: Black, close-cropped; a man who kept everything about himself functional

**Attire**: Military dress — well-maintained campaign armor with minimal ornamentation, the gear of someone who wore it because it worked rather than because it impressed. A sword at his hip that looked used.

**Home:** [[the Cosmic Pillar]]

## Appearance

Zumrah was built for a battlefield rather than a court, and looked it. Broad-shouldered, close-cropped dark hair, the kind of steady, unhurried presence that soldiers read as reliable. Not decorated or ceremonial — his armor bore the marks of campaigns rather than craftsmen. He stood slightly apart from the Blessed in every portrait that included him, as though the court's habits had never quite taken hold.

## Personality

Zumrah was defined by loyalty. His dedication plaque in the Garden of Asj'Norriah, placed in the stead of his sworn liege, is the clearest window into his character: a fearsome battlefield commander who made his dedication about the emperor rather than himself, who fed his soldiers Titan's Reach to make them stronger, and who encoded the flower's properties in a soldier's rhyme so his men could remember it in the field.

He believed in the people under his command in the practical, unglamorous way of someone who has watched them bleed. His later role as guard to [[Caelum Aes'Thiir]] suggests the emperor trusted him with something more personal than the campaign — which, given Aes'Thiir's character, says something about the kind of man Zumrah was.

Whether he shared his emperor's indifference to the costs of the First Celestial Campaign, or simply kept his doubts to himself the way soldiers often do, the record does not say.

## Narrative Relevance

Zumrah appears in the Garden of Asj'Norriah as the dedicant of the [[Titan's Reach]] plaque — standing in for [[Caelum Aes'Thiir]] as an act of loyalty that outlasted both of them. The rhyme he left is the only surviving record of his voice:

*By ones and threes, fives for sure*
*Titan's blood shall e'er endure*
*But hand in hand or twice more*
*Titan's foes dashed to the floor*

He is otherwise a footnote in the imperial record — the kind of man empires are built on and rarely bother to remember.
