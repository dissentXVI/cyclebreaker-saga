**Race**: True Elf (Navarresean)

**Gender**: Female

**Age**: Unknown — ruled for twenty years before her body began to fail; her soul began decaying before her body did

**Eyes**: Gold-green, sharp and appraising

**Hair**: Long, straight, pale blonde

**Attire**: Purple and gold armored imperial dress; a heavy gold crown set with amethyst; amethyst drop earrings. Everything she wore was built to be looked at.

**Home:** [[The Cosmic Pillar]]; later [[Taras'Nula]] (where her body was transferred)

## Appearance

Amastacia was striking in the way that power sometimes makes a person striking — not warmth, but certainty. Long pale blonde hair falling straight over gold-trimmed shoulders. Gold-green eyes that look not quite at you but slightly past you, measuring. Her crown is heavy, ornate, studded with amethyst, and she wears it like she was born with it on. Her expression in portraiture is the same in every surviving image: composed, slightly elevated, the look of a woman who has already decided she is correct. Pointed ears mark her True Elf lineage clearly. There is nothing soft in her bearing.

## Personality

Amastacia Lael'Nathor was a woman of formidable will and catastrophic judgment. She assassinated her husband [[Thallan Lael'Nathor]] — whom she viewed as weak — to seize the throne, then ruled with an iron fist, reigniting wars and rooting out real and imagined threats with equal ferocity. She was not without capability; she was without restraint.

Her relationship with Dr. [[Thalaniel Shamashur]] became the defining feature of her later reign — a partnership that began as political utility and deepened into something more troubling as her ethical limits dissolved. When accusations reached her that Shamashur was conducting experiments on True Elves within the tower, she showed no surprise. Her mage Alyndra, who brought the accusations directly, noted the absence of concern as clearly as the absence of denial.

As her body and soul began to decay simultaneously, her focus narrowed entirely to survival. She charged Shamashur with cloning her — with making her permanent. The work was not finished in time. The clone he eventually produced was grotesque, and her son [[Thallan Lael'Nathor II]] was sickened by it rather than relieved.

Her body was transferred to [[Taras'Nula]], where it remains.

## Narrative Relevance

Amastacia is the empire's final expression of its own worst logic: Life pneuma as personal immortality, power as self-preservation, the resources of an entire civilization bent toward one person's refusal to die. Her body in Taras'Nula is an unresolved thread — not definitively gone, not definitively retrievable.

Her son's breakdown and the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]] are direct consequences of what she became. The devoted son who wrote her garden plaque — defending her, grieving her, naming the princess's rest vine in her honor — is [[Thallan Lael'Nathor II]].
