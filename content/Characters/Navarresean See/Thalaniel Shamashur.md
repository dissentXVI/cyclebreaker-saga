**Race**: True Elf (Navarresean)

**Age**: Unknown — centuries old by the empire's end; his current whereabouts and state are unknown

**Gender**: Male

**Eyes**: Pale, obscured behind tinted green-lensed glasses — a detail that may be affectation, may be necessity

**Hair**: Grey, slicked back close to the skull

**Attire**: Black scholar's robes trimmed with gold filigree; hanging chains and pendants of various lengths draped across his chest and sleeves — instruments, perhaps, or trophies, or both. Nothing about his dress is accidental.

**Home:** [[the Cosmic Pillar]]; later [[Taras'Nula]]

## Appearance

Shamashur is gaunt in the way of someone who has simply stopped being interested in the physical maintenance of himself — all his attention pointed elsewhere. Sharp-featured, grey-haired, standing with his arms raised and hands open in the posture of a man mid-experiment rather than mid-conversation. The tinted glasses obscure whatever his eyes might give away. His robes are expensive and meticulously maintained, which is its own kind of statement: the work matters; the body wearing the work is incidental.

He does not look like a monster. He looks like a scholar. These are not mutually exclusive things.

## Personality

Shamashur experienced himself as a performer — and not merely a performer, but the director, maestro, and sole genius of a grand production that the rest of the world was too small-minded to appreciate. His written records frame everything in theatrical terms: experiments are performances, subjects are actors, setbacks are dramatic obstacles on the road to a finale the ages will remember. His subjects' fear and suffering he described as "exquisite preludes to applause." He killed his own research assistants after each project phase to maintain secrecy and recorded this in the same dry administrative tone he used for [[breathstone]] calibration results.

This was not affectation layered over competence — the competence was genuine. His biomantic work represented real breakthroughs, and he knew it, and his ego had expanded to fill every inch of the space that knowledge created. He dismissed [[Alaric Vyrne]] as a timid failure while mining his notes for useful ideas, credited [[Allythoia]] as a rare intellectual equal primarily because she told him he was brilliant, and treated [[Thallan Lael'Nathor II]] with barely-concealed contempt, routing around him through Allythoia when the prince's interference threatened to slow the work.

He had no ethical framework that functioned as a constraint. He had an aesthetic one: crude methods were beneath him, dwarven necromancy was an offense to proper arcane discipline, and suffering that served no experimental purpose was simply untidy. Suffering that did serve a purpose was, in his own words, encouraging.

His commission to clone [[Amastacia Lael'Nathor]] he understood as his masterpiece — the act that would make his name permanent. When he succeeded in producing a clone, the result was hollow enough that Thallan II was sickened rather than relieved. Whether Shamashur experienced this as failure or simply as an unsatisfying final scene before moving on to the next production is unrecorded. He was gone before the empire fell.

He also maintained the Life pneuma suspension machinery used to keep [[Alyndra]] alive for over two thousand years — not as an act of mercy, but as an ongoing resource.

## Narrative Relevance

Shamashur's work is one of the threads that did not end with the empire. His whereabouts during the fall of the [[Navarresean See]] and the chaos of the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]] are unaccounted for — he was gone before the empire collapsed, and no record places him among those who fled, survived, or perished. What became of him afterward, and whether the work he began in [[Taras'Nula]] continued in some form, remains an open question.

He should not be assumed dead.
