**Race**: True Elf (Navarresean)

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Unknown

**Eyes**: Gold — deep-set and unreadable, as with all of the Navarresean royal line

**Hair**: Dark brown, worn long and loose, swept back from a sharp-featured face

**Attire**: Deep purple cloak trimmed with gold filigree over dark fitted robes; a silver and violet mage's staff topped with a star-shaped gem. No crown — the title of High Magister carried its own authority, and Nox understood that.

**Home:** [[The Cosmic Pillar]]

## Appearance

Nox does not look like a founder. He looks like a man who has already decided something and is waiting for the world to catch up. Tall and lean, dark hair loose past his shoulders, deep-set gold eyes that give nothing away. He holds his staff the way a soldier holds a weapon — with casual familiarity rather than ceremony. The gold halo of light behind him in his portrait is almost certainly a later addition, the kind of retroactive sanctification that empires apply to their origins. The man himself looks like he needed none of it. His expression is not warm. It is certain.

## Personality

By all accounts a visionary of genuine intellectual force — the kind of figure who could draw disparate, quarrelsome scholars into a unified purpose through sheer conviction. Whether that conviction was selfless or self-serving is a matter of historiographical debate that the empire itself had little interest in settling. The Navarresean See needed a founding myth, and Nox became one.

He is described in later texts as a man of patience, wisdom, and a certain imperious certainty that his project was not merely good but cosmically necessary. The dissenting voices of later eras suggest this certainty was the empire's first and most durable inheritance from him.

## Narrative Relevance

Nox is the ideological origin point of the [[Navarresean See]] — the man who first wove together elven scholarly ambition, pneumatic philosophy, and theocratic governance into a single self-justifying system. The Holy Six Elements, the concept of Life as supreme, the divine mandate to expand and civilize: all of it flows from the convocation he called at the dawn of the Second Radiant Age.
