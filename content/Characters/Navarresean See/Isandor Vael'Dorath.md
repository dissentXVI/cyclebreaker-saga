**Race**: True Elf (Navarresean)

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Unknown — a veteran general before becoming emperor; his shade projects a figure in its prime

**Eyes**: Gold in life; the shade that walked the Tower burned with hollow green light

**Hair**: White, worn long — likely silver-white even before age

**Attire**: In life: dark, etched battle armor with intricate knotwork and a high collar — functional but not plain, the armor of a man who understood that presence on a battlefield was its own weapon. The shade that the party encountered in the Tower wore the same silhouette, trailing spectral green fire.

**Home:** [[the Cosmic Pillar]]

## Appearance

The shade of Vael'Dorath that lingered in [[the Cosmic Pillar]] was a tall figure in dark engraved armor, white hair streaming loose in a wind that wasn't there, green light burning where eyes should have been. Translucent. Purposeful. Still dangerous.

In life, he was likely not so different — minus the fire. White hair worn long, the kind of angular face that looks like it was made for command, gold eyes and armor he never took off unless he had to. A general's bearing that never quite became a ruler's ease. He would have been the kind of man people stepped back from in a corridor without being sure why.

## Personality

A soldier's soldier elevated to emperor by his own battlefield victories. Vael'Dorath was not a philosopher or a diplomat — he was a general who understood the world in terms of enemies to be defeated and territories to be held. He expanded the empire eastward into dwarven lands without hesitation, and when the campaign proved more costly than expected, his answer was not restraint but escalation.

He is remembered within Navarresean historiography as a hero: Wyrmslayer, first of the Holinesses, the emperor who concluded the [[The First Celestial Campaign|First Celestial Campaign]] in glory.

## Narrative Relevance

His shade was encountered by [[Aspiria's Chosen]] within the Tower — a remnant of the empire's most aggressive era, still standing guard over something it no longer had any right to protect.

Every tiefling in Aspiria traces their existence, however distantly, to the decision he made to solve a labor shortage with a manufactured people.
