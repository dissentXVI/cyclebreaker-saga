**Race**: True Elf (Navarresean)

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Young — still addressed as prince during his mother's decline

**Eyes**: Gold

**Hair**: Golden blonde, shoulder-length, worn loose

**Attire**: Gold-etched pauldrons and breastplate over a purple cloak and tunic — imperial dress worn on a frame that looks like it hasn't yet settled into the weight of what it means

**Home:** [[the Cosmic Pillar]]

## Appearance

Thallan II was beautiful in the uncomplicated way of someone who has not yet been made ugly by what they've done — because he hadn't done it yet. Golden hair falling loose to his shoulders, gold eyes catching the light, cheekbones that belonged in a painting of a hero. His armor is fine, his purple cloak immaculate. He looks like the heir to something great. The expression is the tell: not pride, not confidence, but something strained behind the eyes — a young man already exhausted by what he is being asked to hold.

## Personality

Thallan II was sharper than his circumstances allowed him to be useful. His journals show a young man of genuine intelligence and dry, dark wit — someone who understood the empire's structural rot with uncomfortable clarity and found dark humor the only honest response to it. He described his own life as a comedy rather than a tragedy, and meant it: the irony of a dynasty built on the mastery of Life pneuma losing its empress to decay, of an empire of civilizing elves being stopped cold by "minute and disparate" dwarven tribes, of a prince told since birth he was a star looking out into the dark and finding the dark had won — he saw all of it, clearly, and laughed because the alternative was worse.

He loved his mother with a completeness that left him very little else. He sat outside her room listening to her breathe. He canceled councils to sit on the throne and pretend he could hear her voice in the wind. He kept her room unchanged because he could not bear to let go of the expectation of her return. When [[Thalaniel Shamashur|Shamashur]] produced the clone, he had been building toward hope for years. What he was presented with instead — hollow, wrong, quietly violent — broke something that did not recover.

He was not passive in the interim. He granted Shamashur access to the audience chamber to tap more directly into the tower's pneuma, authorized increasingly extreme experimental measures, and managed the political fallout of a collapsing empire with whatever mordant competence he could muster. He also, quietly, made contact with a formless entity that fed on pneuma and had no name — something his mother had apparently called into the tower before her decline. He named it Estelle. He fed it sparingly. He kept it contained, aware of what it was and what releasing it would mean, and held onto it as an option of last resort.

After the clone, the option became the plan.

His final act was not madness. It was a clear-eyed conclusion from someone who had spent years watching everything he understood as worth preserving come apart, reached the end of the last thing he had been holding on for, and decided that if the world could not sustain what it was supposed to be, it could start over. He turned the power of [[the Cosmic Pillar]] on the world and brought down the [[The Tempest of Madness|Tempest of Madness]].

## Narrative Relevance

Thallan II is the direct cause of the Third Cataclysm and the collapse of the [[Navarresean See]]. His action ended the empire, drowned the continent, and left the world to rebuild from scratch.

He is also the last of his line — until [[Reyn'lael Joran]], whose bloodline traces back to the Lael'Nathor dynasty. The throne of [[the Cosmic Pillar]] recognized Reyn as it once recognized Thallan II. What that continuity means, and what Thallan II's final act set in motion beyond the obvious devastation, remains an open question.
