**Race**: True Elf (Navarresean)

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Unknown

**Eyes**: Pale green — the kind that look most at home focused on something small and close rather than at a person

**Hair**: Brown, thinning, kept short and unremarkable; a man with no interest in how he appeared

**Attire**: Practical researcher's robes in muted grey-green, ink-stained at the cuffs; the dress of someone who works at a bench rather than in a court. No ornamentation.

**Home:** [[the Cosmic Pillar]]

## Appearance

Vyrne looked like what he was — a man of laboratories rather than courts. Slight, a little hunched at the shoulder from years bent over a workbench, hair that had long since stopped being a priority. His hands were always occupied, always reaching for something. Not imposing. Not memorable in a crowd. The kind of person you might not notice until you found out what he'd built.

## Personality

Alaric Vyrne was a scientist of genuine capability who accepted a commission he should have refused and spent the rest of his long life knowing it. The [[Infernal Hybridization Initiative]] was his project, carried out under [[Isandor Vael'Dorath]]'s orders to produce a resilient and subservient workforce for the Second Celestial Campaign's mines and labor needs.

The historical record notes that in his later years Vyrne retreated from public life and devoted himself to more benign scientific work, attempting to reconcile his past actions with his personal morals. What that reconciliation looked like, whether it ever succeeded, and whether he considered the people his work produced as people at all — none of this is recorded.

He is not remembered as a monster in Navarresean historiography. He is barely remembered at all, which is its own kind of verdict.

## Narrative Relevance

Vyrne's [[Infernal Hybridization Initiative]] is one of the Navarresean See's most consequential and least examined legacies — its full scope and what it produced extending far beyond what was ever publicly acknowledged within the empire.
