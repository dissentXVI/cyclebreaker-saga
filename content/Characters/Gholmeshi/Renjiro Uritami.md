**Title**: Vociran Cultural Attaché to the Ilsong Provisional Authority

**Allegiance**: [[Vociran Empire]]

**Race**: Orc

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: 33

**Eyes**: Pale crimson

**Hair**: Sleek black, shoulder-length, neatly tied at the nape

**Attire**: Vociran-trimmed formalwear with Gholmeshi accent layers — ceremonial shoulder cloak, silver-threaded sash, polished vambraces

**Home**: Kazhar, [[Gholmeshi]] (defector)

## Appearance

Renjiro radiates refinement and strategic charm. His jade-green skin is unmarred, his voice perfectly modulated — diplomatic, theatrical, never flustered. His crimson eyes are colder than Kaihana's, but more inviting at first glance.

He dresses in immaculate layers of Vociran officialwear touched with Gholmeshi symbolism — a calculated display of "unity." His smile is flawless. His bow, exact. His presence, disarming. And yet... the longer one listens, the more the rot beneath begins to show.

## Personality

Renjiro presents himself as a man of peace: measured, reform-minded, a bridge between cultures. He speaks of [[Gholmeshi]] heritage with warmth, of Ilsongese dignity with apparent respect, and of the Vociran Empire with the sober pragmatism of someone who has simply accepted the way the world works.

None of it is true.

Behind the diplomat's mask is a man whose entire existence is organized around a single, consuming fixation: [[Caedus seq Magnus]]. Not loyalty to Vociterra as an institution — to *him*. To be seen by him. To be useful to him. To be, someday, the kind of instrument that a man like Caedus bothers to remember.

Everything Renjiro does — every ceremony, every cultural directive, every carefully positioned public statement — is a performance staged for an audience of one. He is charming to guests because charm serves his image. He is flattering to superiors because flattery is infrastructure. And he is methodical in his manipulation of those around him — including his sister Kaihana — because tools must be kept sharp and pointed in the right direction.

He is not cruel for cruelty's sake. He is cruel because it is efficient. The distinction matters to him enormously.

## Character History

Renjiro was born the legitimate son and heir of the Uritami household in Kazhar — a family of modest but genuine standing. When the household's fortunes declined and a distant relative's orphaned daughter was taken in, Renjiro was young enough to absorb the lesson without examining it: that some people in a household have names, and some people have uses. [[Kaihana Uritami|Kaihana]] had uses. Renjiro had a name.

He was educated. He had opportunities. He watched what happened to Kaihana with the selective blindness of a child who understood, even then, that acknowledging it would cost him something. He told himself it was not his problem. Later, he told himself he was too young to intervene. Later still, he stopped telling himself anything at all, because the story had calcified into something he no longer needed to examine.

When the family married [[Kaihana Uritami|Kaihana]] away and then sold her to settle debts, Renjiro did not come. He was occupied with his studies. He was advancing. He was, by every measure that mattered to the people who kept such measures, doing well.

When the Vociran Empire moved on the region, Renjiro was among those caught in the early sweeps — not as a prisoner, but as a conscript candidate. Educated, multilingual, and possessed of the kind of bearing that colonial administrators find useful, he was assessed and redirected toward diplomatic training. He accepted without reluctance.

It was during a war council in Shoran, early in the occupation, that everything changed. Renjiro was a minor attendant — present to take notes, not to speak. [[Caedus seq Magnus]] entered the room, and in the space of a single hour, Renjiro's understanding of power was permanently restructured. He had never seen anything like it. Not strength — he had seen strength. What Caedus had was something Renjiro had no word for and has been trying to name ever since: a kind of absolute, crystalline certainty, the quality of a man who has never once doubted that the world should organize itself around him.

Renjiro left that room a different person. He began studying Caedus the way scholars study scripture — every recorded speech, every documented decision, every account of him from people who had stood in the same room. He modeled his own speech patterns, his posture, his silences. He became the most effective cultural liaison the Vociran administration had in the region, and he dedicated every commendation, every promotion, every small success to the project of becoming someone that Caedus might one day find useful enough to address directly.

He has met Caedus briefly, twice. He recounts both occasions in private like a man recounting visions.

## Current Status

Renjiro maintains a public profile as a reformist voice within the Vociran administration — a Gholmeshi face arguing, persuasively and with apparent sincerity, for cultural sensitivity in the occupation of Ilsong. Vociran officials find him useful. Ilsongese representatives find him almost believable. Neither group understands what he actually wants.

What he actually wants is to give Caedus a reason to look at him.

To that end, he has been quietly maneuvering around [[Kaihana Uritami]]. Publicly, he supports her — a loyal sibling, proud of her service to the Empire. Privately, he has been engineering circumstances designed to push her toward crisis: not out of hatred, but because Kaihana destabilized at a key moment would create exactly the kind of chaos that a sufficiently clever resolution might bring to someone's attention. He has already taken certain steps he does not discuss. He will take more if necessary.

The whispers of Shinsei circulating through the provinces have caught his attention. He does not yet know what to do with them. But he is paying very close attention.

## Combat Style

Renjiro favors Subtle Edge Doctrine, a Vociran ceremonial dueling art designed for showmanship and surprise. He carries a parrying fan in public and a concealed mono-blade beneath his sash — both legal under cultural exemption clauses he helped draft.

He views violence not as savagery, but as theater. And he performs beautifully.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Caedus seq Magnus]]** – The organizing principle of Renjiro's life. He has met him twice. He has been preparing for a third meeting for years. His private quarters contain records of Caedus's words and decisions, maintained with a devotion that has long since crossed the threshold from admiration into something else entirely.

**[[Kaihana Uritami]]** – His adoptive sister. He did not come when she needed him, and he has spent years constructing a narrative in which that was reasonable. He claims to support her. He is, in fact, using her — carefully, patiently, and with the particular cruelty of someone who tells himself he feels bad about it.
