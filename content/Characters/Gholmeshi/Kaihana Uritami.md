**Hair**: Ink-black, cascading in long waves adorned with fresh pink blossoms

**Attire**: Vociran diplomatic robes styled in Gholmeshi fashion — ivory, crimson, and rose — layered over reinforced silk; a necklace of carved amber at her collar

**Home**: Kazhar, [[Gholmeshi]]

## Appearance

Kaihana is the embodiment of elegance weaponized. Her jade-green skin is flawless, her black hair meticulously styled, and her crimson eyes betray not anger, but contempt held behind lacquered calm. Blossoms — always fresh, always pink — adorn her hair like satire.

Every inch of her presentation is calculated: the way she walks, the softness of her voice, the carefully polished accent. Even the fan she carries is part of her performance. She is not here to serve — she is here to be seen.

## Personality

Soft-spoken. Polite. Poisoned.

Kaihana speaks with the courtesy of a diplomat and the precision of a blade. She rarely raises her voice, yet every word drips with memory. The people of Ilsong whisper that she is cruel — but they do not understand the depths of her hatred. It is not directed at Vociterra. It never was.

It is directed at them.

She takes a specific, deliberate pleasure in her cruelty. When a Ilsongese noble bows before her — the same kind of man who once looked through her like glass — something in her settles, satisfied. She does not merely wield power. She savors it. Every act of public humiliation, every signed decree, every paper-thin smile withheld is payment on a debt long overdue.

She is not performing malice. She is enjoying it.

## Character History

Kaihana was not born into comfort. Her parents died when she was still young, and she was taken in by distant relatives in Kazhar — a transaction dressed as charity. What followed was not a childhood but an apprenticeship in invisibility: denied schooling, put to work, and treated as a burden the household was unfortunate enough to bear. When her relatives found use for her, it was the only use Gholmeshi society had long reserved for beautiful girls without money or name.

They married her away. Her husband was a minor noble of means and vices — a drunk, and worse. The years she spent under his roof were not spoken of afterward, not by her, and not by anyone who valued their comfort. When he died, the debts he left behind were settled in the oldest way: her relatives sold her to a pleasure house in the city's quieter quarters to clear what was owed. No one came to argue otherwise.

Within those walls, she learned a different kind of survival. She climbed — not through luck, but through absolute discipline, a flawless memory, and a hatred she kept banked low and burning. She became one of the establishment's most sought-after courtesans, not because she desired the work, but because mastery was the only currency available to her. By the time the Vociran Empire's forces moved on the border, she had already learned that grace was armor and that beauty, properly wielded, was a weapon.

When Vociran soldiers swept through the borderlands, Kaihana was among those caught in the net — catalogued, assessed, and brought before imperial administrators who dealt in useful people. It was not a random officer who saw her potential. Word of a displaced [[Gholmeshi]] courtesan with a command of Ilsongese custom, an immaculate manner, and a very particular relationship with her homeland reached [[Caedus seq Magnus]] through his intelligence apparatus. He summoned her.

What followed was not an interview. It was a single, direct question: what did she feel for the people of Ilsong?

She told him the truth.

That answer — unguarded, precise, and utterly without shame — was what elevated her. Not her beauty, not her training, not her survival. Her honesty about her hatred. Caedus installed her as Imperial Viceroy to the Ilsong Provisional Government: a [[Gholmeshi]] face on a Vociran occupation, a symbol of "civilized" administration that the empire could present to the world. She accepted without hesitation.

The people who once sneered now bow. The nobles who looked past her now beg for audience. And she answers them with paper-thin smiles, and makes them wait.

## Current Status

Kaihana is stationed in the former Ilsong capital of Shoran, now rebranded as a Vociran protectorate city. She leads reconstruction ceremonies, signs compliance decrees, and presides over public proceedings with the unhurried calm of someone who has nowhere else she would rather be.

She is universally feared among the occupied population. The resistance calls her a traitor. The Vocirans call her a success story. She calls herself Kaihana.

And yet, she listens. Whispers of a man named Shinsei have begun to surface across the provinces. The name stirs something — not loyalty, not hope. Something older. Something she buried a long time ago and is not certain she wants unearthed.

## Combat Style

Though rarely seen on the battlefield, Kaihana was once trained in Haizutsu-no-Mai, the "Dance of Withering Petals" — an assassin's art disguised as court movement. Her weapon of choice is a war-fan of crimson lacquer and hidden steel, and she carries poison pins in her sleeves.

She doesn't fight unless forced. But if you do force her... there is no second strike.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Renjiro Uritami]]** – Her brother. A devoted servant of the Vociran Empire and a mirror of everything she despises. They speak politely in public, but behind closed doors, their every word is a weapon. He sees her as damaged goods; she sees him as a traitor in finer robes. That he was not there — that he never came — is a wound she has not named aloud and never will.

**[[Caedus seq Magnus]]** – Her superior and the only person who has ever asked her the right question and listened to the answer. She treats him with perfect obedience. In private, she writes his name in fading ink every night and watches it vanish — a ritual she has never explained, even to herself.
