**Title**: Grand Artisan of [[The Loomhall]]

**Allegiance**: [[The Sunweavers Guild]] ([[Aqualonde]])

**Race**: Elf

**Gender**: Female

**Age**: Appears mid-40s

**Eyes**: Bronze

**Hair**: Deep mahogany, bound in intricate coils and thread

**Attire**: Layered robes of dyed flax and moonweave, embroidered with solar glyphs and golden latticework

**Home:** [[The Loomhall]], near the [[Lightreach Falls]] in Aqualonde

## Appearance

Maevienne is regal, composed, and carved from elegance. Her face carries the soft lines of maturity without the weight of age, and her eyes gleam like burnished coin beneath long lashes. Her hair is wrapped in spiraling coils, bound with silken cords dyed in the sunrise hues of her guild. Her robe hems shift in color under changing light, a trick of enchanted threadwork that few can replicate.

She often smells faintly of lavender oil, ink, and molten dye.
## Personality

Poised and principled, Maevienne is both an artist and a guardian of [[Aqualonde]]’s spiritual identity. Where others see weaving as utility or fashion, she sees it as a sacred act—binding pneuma into form, memory into material. She is exacting, reverent, and at times unyielding in her standards, believing that beauty and discipline must be interlaced to endure.

Maevienne is a quiet force in politics. She seldom raises her voice, but when she speaks, rooms still. She reveres [[Maylin Lurel|Maylin]] deeply, but believes [[Aqualonde]] must assert its cultural authority before foreign ideals unravel its foundation.

Her compassion is real—but it is often hidden behind protocol and poise.
## Character History

Maevienne was once a wanderer, traveling across [[Halinor]] as a journeyman artist and sacred tailor. She was present at the tail end of [[the Choral Accord]], where she helped sew the ceremonial banners for [[D’yadya]]. But when the Accord collapsed, she returned to [[Aqualonde]] disillusioned and refocused her efforts inward—founding what would become [[The Loomhall]], [[Aqualonde]]’s most prestigious artistic and ritual-weaving school.

Through decades of study, she refined her ability to imbue woven goods with memory and meaning, pioneering new forms of pneuma-threaded cloth that respond to touch, music, or light. Under her guidance, [[The Sunweavers Guild|the Sunweavers]] rose to prominence, blending artistry with spiritual function.

She was named Grand Artisan by unanimous acclaim—an honor that doubled as a responsibility she never asked for.
## Current Status

Maevienne now presides over [[The Loomhall]], overseeing both public works and private commissions from nobles, druids, and temple custodians. She recently completed a tapestry for the Temple of [[Demeria]] said to record [[Aqualonde]]’s entire founding myth—encoded not in image, but in vibration, only audible when given backing song by an  assembled choir.

She has voiced concern over growing Aqualondian dependence on outside trade, and quietly opposed other’s push for technological integration into crafting guilds.

She has also begun reviewing apprentices for a rumored [[Sunveil Ceremony]], a rite not performed since the Fourth Radiant Age.
