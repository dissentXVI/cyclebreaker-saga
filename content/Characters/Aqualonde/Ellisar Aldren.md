## Appearance

Ellisar is thin, pale, and disturbingly still for a child. He walks with an eerie sense of calm, his wide hazel eyes flicking toward every sound but never showing fear. His clothes are too large for him now, worn thin with travel, and he clutches a wooden whistle he no longer plays.

He often walks barefoot and hums broken versions of lullabies. Animals no longer approach him.
## Personality

Before: Energetic, clever, and full of endless questions. Fascinated by insects, birds, and stories. He looked up to his sister and adored his father. Often wandered off, but always found his way back.

After: Quiet. Passive. He mimics others’ movements like a puppet and only speaks when echoing someone else’s words. There are flickers of his old self—he sometimes hides shiny stones in tree hollows or arranges leaves into patterns.

If spoken to gently, he sometimes whispers, “Do I still belong to the woods?”
## Character History

Ellisar was always the one who strayed too close to strange mushrooms, who asked questions about magics and the gods, who believed too easily in stories. When his family entered the Fauconwood and strange mists began to rise, he was the first to vanish.
