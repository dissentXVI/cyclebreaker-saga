## Appearance

Ilyana carries herself with quiet strength. Her beauty is understated—sun-kissed and weathered by loss, but radiant in moments of purpose. Her cloak is threadbare, her gloves patched, yet she moves with the precision of a trained hunter and the care of a mother tending a wound.

Her eyes rarely look away when spoken to. She listens with her full self.

She carries a carved lantern etched with inititials of her husband and children—one for each. The light within it never dims.
## Personality

Wise, steady, and fiercely protective, [[Ilyana Aldren|Ilyana]] was never a warrior, but she has become something stronger: unbreakable. She endures without bitterness, mourns without paralysis, and fights without rage. Her strength is in presence, in refusal, in love that outlasts even death’s illusion.

To those who’ve seen her wandering the edge of [[The Fauconwood]], she is a ghost. To her children, she is a memory waiting to be proven real.
## Character History

Ilyana was born to a family of herbalists and married Elluin Aldren, a woodsman known for charting unwalked paths. They settled in Serinvale and raised two children, Selphie and Ellisar.

During a pilgrimage to one of [[Aqualonde]]’s elder glades, their wagon was ensnared by strange forces in the forest. [[Elluin Aldren|Elluin]] and [[Ellisar Aldren|Ellisar]] vanished into the mist. [[Selphie Aldren|Selphie]] was lured into a velvet carriage by a red-haired man with eyes like falling stars.

Ilyana tried… and failed to save them.

But she survived. And in that survival, she found a purpose: to bring her family home.
## Current Status

Ilyana has become a quiet legend in the southern woods of [[Aqualonde]]. She wanders with her lantern and walking staff, gathering rumors, memories, and sacred herbs. She speaks with anyone who will listen. She tells a tale of the creature in the woods that took her family and is determined to find them.

Some say she’s mad. Others believe she is the only one who sees clearly.
