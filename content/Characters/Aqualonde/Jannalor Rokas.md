**Title**: Grand Forgewright of [[The Moonblade Forge|the Moonblades]]

**Allegiance**: [[The Moonblade Forge]] ([[Aqualonde]])

**Race**: Male

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Appears mid-50s

**Eyes**: Pale gray

**Hair**: Silver-white, worn long and tied at the nape

**Attire**: Reinforced robes of nightsteel-thread, crescent-etched bracers, cloak clasped with a carved moonstone

**Home:** [[The Gloam Smithy]], a sacred smithy embedded in moonstone cliffs of north [[Aqualonde]]

## Appearance

Jannalor is austere and imposing, tall even by elven standards. His silver-white hair and weathered features lend him the air of a marble statue chipped by time. His eyes are cold, not cruel—like forged iron waiting for purpose. He wears a fusion of robe and armor: a blacksmith’s practicality over ceremonial polish. His skin is dotted with old forge scars and protective runes inked in starlight-blue.

He carries a crescent-forged glaive across his back and a blacksmith’s hammer at his hip. One builds. The other breaks.

## Personality

Stoic, blunt, and unshakably loyal, Jannalor speaks when there is need—and falls silent when words become noise. While others in [[Aqualonde]] craft through beauty or diplomacy, Jannalor forges through certainty. He believes that defense is not just preparation, but obligation. To protect art, peace, and dreams, someone must be willing to die with neither poetry nor applause.

Despite his demeanor, he is not without empathy. His bond to [[Aqualonde]] is paternal, deeply felt but rarely expressed. He teaches apprentices gently but firmly, and his most dangerous warriors are also his most thoughtful.

He distrusts sentimentality but honors it in others.
## Character History

Jannalor was born under a red moon—considered a bad omen by the old druids. As a child, he showed little interest in song or ritual, but displayed a deep connection to stone, fire, and weight. He apprenticed at [[The Gloam Smithy]] in his youth and never left, eventually claiming the title of Grand Forgewright, the first warrior-smith in centuries to do so.

He reforged the broken [[Brambleblades]]—[[Aqualonde]]’s defender corps—and devised the Breathfold armor pattern used by [[The Moonblade Forge|the Moonblades]] today. His focus on preparation, training, and infrastructure saved [[Aqualonde]] during [[the vineblight plague]], when his defensive wards kept the Elder Tree from collapse.

He has resisted all calls to run for higher office, claiming: “A sword does not govern. It protects the hand that does.”
## Current Status

Jannalor is expanding [[The Gloam Smithy]]’s capacity in secret, training a new generation of Moonblade tacticians in response to growing instability across [[Halinor]]. He has begun personally overseeing construction of an underground vault system meant to house relics, [[breathstone]] reserves, and sacred texts in case of siege.

He is also one of the few openly critical of Renzo’s proposed cultural exports and of [[Maylin Lurel|Maylin]]’s reluctance to militarize. He does not oppose them—but he prepares for their failure.
## Combat Style

Jannalor is a master of Elemental Steel, a Moonblade martial tradition that blends smithcraft with elemental pneuma. His glaive, Silent Crescent, can channel lunar energy into cutting arcs, magnetic bursts, or gravity pulses. He also carries forged moonstone spikes that act as temporary wards or arcane anchors when struck into the ground.

He fights with patience, dominance, and precision—holding chokepoints, covering allies, or drawing enemy focus. His presence on a battlefield changes the terrain itself, reshaping movement and flow like a forge shaping molten ore.
