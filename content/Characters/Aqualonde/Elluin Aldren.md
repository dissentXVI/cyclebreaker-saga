## Appearance

Elluin has the build of a ranger gone hollow—lean, capable, but moving as though underwater. His once-sharp eyes are fogged with obedience, and his posture has a quiet slouch, as if he’s constantly bracing against a weight he cannot name.

His clothing is neither elegant nor ragged—merely forgotten. His belt carries a blade he never draws, and his boots never leave prints, even in soft mud.

His face remains stoic. But when he hears his children’s name, he flinches.
## Personality

Before: Gentle, practical, and deeply rooted in family life. A calming presence and pillar of support for Ilyana. Loved old tales, woodworking, and forest-walking with his children.

After: Wordless. Watchful. Obedient. Speaks only when ordered and never uses his family’s names. However, he sometimes hums under his breath—snippets of lullabies or trail songs.

He has become a blade in a sheath of silence. But the blade remembers what it once protected.
## Character History

Elluin Aldren spent most of his life mapping trails between glades and groves throughout southern [[Aqualonde]]. He was known for his ability to read the forest like a book—its dangers, its moods, its shifts. He married Ilyana and raised two children, [[Selphie Aldren|Selphie]] and [[Ellisar Aldren|Ellisar]], in the peaceful village of [[Serinvale]].

During their final journey together, he was the first to notice something wrong in the trees. The vines shifted when no wind blew. The fog refused to burn off.

He vanished trying to protect his son.
