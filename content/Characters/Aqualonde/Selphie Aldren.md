## Appearance

Selphie’s once bright demeanor has dulled into a vacant, dreamlike presence. Her pale skin has taken on a cool, sun-starved tone, and her lips are often parted in silence, as if waiting for a command. A red ribbon once tied in her hair has frayed to threadbare strands, but she keeps it, idly wrapping it around her fingers.

She walks barefoot, even across thorns and roots, seemingly unfazed. Her movements are slow and graceful—too graceful. Like a marionette under perfect control.
## Personality

Before: Cheerful, brave, and inquisitive. Protective of her younger brother and deferential to her parents, though a streak of independence had begun to show.

After: Muted. Detached. She speaks only in hushed tones or poetic riddles, often echoing Sharl’s phrasing. She does not resist her orders, nor does she seem aware of the full truth. Occasionally, a flicker of emotion escapes her control—a tear, a twitch, a whispered name.
## Character History

Selphie was the first to step forward when her family’s wagon broke down in the forest. After her father and brother disappeared, she witnessed her mother be lured away by fae whispers—and was left alone until the arrival of a crimson-cloaked man with cerulean eyes.

What happened after is a mystery.
