**Title**: Emperor of Vociterra

**Allegiance**: [[Vociterra]]

**Race**: Human (Vociran)

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: 60

**Eyes**: Deep blue

**Hair**: Pale silver, long and immaculately kept

**Attire**: Imperial black-gold ceremonial armor adorned with leafwork and sigils of unification; crowned with the Flame of Magnityr, an imperial diadem wrought from vultek and polished obsidian

**Home**: The Imperial Citadel, [[Vociterra]]

## Appearance

Lucius sep Magnus stands like a monument — composed, dignified, and absolute.

He is tall and broad-shouldered, though the bulk of his strength lies in the presence he commands. His pale silver hair is grown long and brushed behind regal pauldrons, framing a face chiseled by time and rule. His skin is pale but not weak — hardened, like stone washed in snow. His eyes, a piercing blue, gleam with quiet calculation and impossible expectations.

His imperial armor is both art and message — blackened steel trimmed in deep gold, embossed with fractal sigils that signify unity, dominion, and unbreakable lineage. Every angle has been engineered to awe. Atop his brow rests the Flame of Magnityr, a flame-shaped crown of black-gold filigree bearing a vultek crystal core — symbol of his dynasty's rise.

Even still, his most terrifying feature is his silence.

## Personality

Lucius is not cruel. He simply no longer believes cruelty to be relevant.

To him, the age of mystics and gods is over — humanity must seize its own future, by industry, by reason, and by command. He does not govern through fire, but through pressure. His words are few, his laws absolute, and his ambitions measured not in borders, but in generations.

He believes in hierarchy as harmony, in conquest as curation. Empires, in his mind, are not built to dominate. They are built to elevate. And if elevation demands the erasure of lesser systems — faith, tradition, sorcery — then that is a mercy.

Lucius does not shout. He does not flinch. He does not kneel.

His will is policy. His shadow is law.

## Character History

Lucius sep Magnus rose from generalship to emperorship in a blaze of precision few historians truly understand. His grandfather, [[Meonus sep Magnus]], founded the Empire through revolution, force of will, and the ingenuity of man. Upon his death, a brief but violent civil conflict erupted as rival factions attempted to claim the throne.

It was Lucius's father, Terenus sep Magnus, who crushed the insurrection. With unwavering resolve, he subdued the dissenters and stabilized the empire — centralizing command, purging disloyal elements, and laying the bureaucratic and militaristic groundwork for the dynasty's continuation.

Lucius inherited a throne not won by blood, but solidified by discipline. And he would ensure it never cracked again.

## Current Status

Lucius sep Magnus remains firmly seated upon the imperial throne in [[Vociterra]], continuing his long-term strategy of continental consolidation. Though the fallout from [[Krisnarax]]'s awakening — the Trembling Foundations — temporarily slowed expansion, Lucius remains unmoved.

He dispatched [[Caedus seq Magnus]] to stabilize [[D'yadya]] and [[Gallus gen Quietus]] on a covert mission to Halinor.

Lucius's personal involvement in daily governance is minimal. He operates through executive decrees, technocrat councils, and the Throne Ministry, allowing his will to echo across the Empire like ripples from a dropped blade.

What he plans next is unknown. What he allows to happen is never by chance.

## Combat Style

Lucius no longer fights on the battlefield — but he has not forgotten how.

In his youth, he was trained in all forms of imperial martial discipline, favoring overwhelming force over finesse. His preferred weapon was — and remains — the Magnityr Mace: an imposing vultek-core war mace crafted of obsidian-forged alloy, crowned with a striking head shaped like a stylized flame.

The mace is not ceremonial. It is built to crush armor and dislocate joints, capable of emitting short-range kinetic pulses to stagger opponents. Lucius wielded it personally in the early years of his command, and its presence is still felt in imperial courts — mounted behind his throne, ready, unquestioned.

Though he now wages war through policy and machine, the mace serves as a reminder: the Emperor still remembers how to swing his own law.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Caedus seq Magnus]]** – His only son and heir. Lucius sees him as a perfected weapon, though he remains uncertain whether Caedus possesses vision — or merely instinct.

**[[Gallus gen Quietus]]** – One of Lucius's most loyal and decorated generals. Lucius respects Gallus deeply and sees him not just as a trusted commander, but as a brother.
