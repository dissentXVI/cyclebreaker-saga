**Title**: Tribunis Prima of the First Legion

**Allegiance**: [[Vociterra]]

**Race**: Human (Vociran)

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Early 50s

**Eyes**: Slate gray

**Hair**: Jet black, streaked with silver at the temples

**Attire**: Gold-bronze technician's plate over dark robes; reinforced mask with ocular enhancements; always equipped with specialized tools and diagnostic rods

**Home**: Patria Noster

## Appearance

Recius is not a soldier — he just happens to wear armor better than most of them. His lean frame is often swathed in gold-filamented plating fused with diagnostic coils and reinforced joints. He wears a modified Vociran technician helm, beaked and impassive, with retractable ocular lenses that click and whir with precision.

Despite the anonymity of the mask, his posture and gestures bleed personality: sharp, impatient, and performative. When unmasked, his eyes are cold slate, and his jet black hair — once combed fastidiously — now often rebels in unruly streaks of silver.

He walks like a man whose time is being wasted. Constantly.

## Personality

Recius met Helvus is a genius — and he knows it.

He is acerbic, exacting, and intolerant of incompetence. But beneath the biting sarcasm lies a mind of staggering scope: engineer, tactician, weapons theorist, and designer of multiple vultek-driven systems now standard across the Empire.

He resents authority, unless it's earned. He resents inefficiency, unless it's his. He resents praise, unless it's from someone he respects. That shortlist includes [[Gallus gen Quietus]] — and almost no one else.

And yet, he stays. Because what he builds matters. Because, secretly, he still believes the Empire can become something worthy of its potential.

## Character History

Recius was born to a minor artisan caste and rose through the Magistrate Academion like a comet — brilliant, incendiary, impossible to ignore. His thesis on vultek field stabilization rewrote three military doctrines overnight.

Rather than remain a cloistered scholar, he demanded field deployment. He fought not with blade or gun, but with engines, constructs, and rail-bound dread platforms. He developed the VORTAX armor reactor, the Iron Standard's chassis design, and experimental anti-pneuma shielding.

Eventually, he joined the First Legion, where his talents could operate on the cutting edge of imperial intervention.

He treats warzones like laboratories. Failure is merely delayed success.

## Current Status

Recius is currently deployed alongside [[Gallus gen Quietus]], [[Tribunis Ignia tes Metellus|Ignia tes Metellus]], and [[Tribunis Serena tes Paulus|Serena tes Paulus]] aboard the Iron Standard, en route to Halinor. His primary goal is to survey pneuma anomalies and develop countermeasures to any emergent Paragon activity.

Officially, he's a Tribunis. Unofficially, he's the brain in the room that nobody wants to acknowledge but everyone relies on.

## Combat Style

Recius avoids frontline combat, but he is far from defenseless. His primary tool is a multi-pronged baton crafted with vultek-threaded resonance filaments and conductive channels — functioning as a shock stave, a precision tool, and a command rod for his ravens.

Unlike Vociran technologists who favor synthetic constructs, Recius trains real birds — a pair of black-plumed ravens that obey his voice, gestures, and baton pulses. Through harmonic clicks and frequency shifts, he can direct them to scout, deliver tools, or harass targets mid-battle. He claims organic creatures have better survival instincts — and better judgment.

His personal armor houses compact field devices: energy dampeners, vultek micro-flares, and folding diagnostic arrays. But his greatest defense is always foresight.

Recius doesn't fight you. He lets the environment, the equipment, and the animals do it for him.

## Notable Relationships

**[[Gallus gen Quietus]]** – Their partnership spans decades. Gallus tolerates Recius's sarcasm, and Recius respects Gallus's competence. He claims Gallus is the only field commander who doesn't ruin his machines.

**[[Tribunis Ignia tes Metellus]]** – She doesn't understand him, and he rarely bothers to explain. But she protects him, and that's more valuable than most friendships.

**[[Tribunis Serena tes Paulus]]** – She unnerves him. Too precise. Too clean. He suspects she has surveillance notes on his dreams.
