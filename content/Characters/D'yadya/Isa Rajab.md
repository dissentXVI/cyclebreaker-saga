**Home**: [[D'yadya]]

## Appearance

Isa Rajab carries herself like a drawn blade — steady, dangerous, unapologetic. Her pale blonde hair contrasts with her deep brown skin, often tied back for battle but left loose during marches. Her face is marked by a D'yadyan tattoo: a traditional crest etched above and around her right eye, honoring her mother's lineage — a reminder of where she comes from, even if others say she's betrayed it.

Her Vociran uniform is standard issue, but altered — lighter for desert use, fitted for movement.

## Personality

Isa is ruthless, determined, and painfully clear-eyed. She doesn't believe in rescue. She doesn't believe in destiny. She believes in control — of one's body, one's reputation, one's future.

Branded a traitor by her own people and distrusted by her Vociran handlers, she's learned to live in that liminal space between two fires. She doesn't apologize for it. Her pride is cold-blooded, her ambition relentless, and her morality entirely situational. But she feels deeply — she just buries it beneath duty.

She's sarcastic, unflinching, and unnervingly calm when others break. Her loyalty lies not with nations, but with those who refuse to die quietly.

## Character History

Isa was born in [[A'Mukafa]], a salt-trading city of [[D'yadya]], to a devout priestess of [[Demeria]] and a traveling trader. She was only eight when [[D'yadya]] fell. In the aftermath, her father believed peace could be bought through submission. He collaborated with the Vociran occupiers, hoping obedience might spare their people. It didn't.

An angry mob of D'yadyans turned on them. Her father died shielding her from the stones.

By sixteen, Isa had grown weary of promises — promises of rescue, of resistance, of return. She wanted power. She wanted respect. And the Vociran Auxilia Program offered both: food, shelter, combat training — in exchange for loyalty. Isa accepted without hesitation.

She rose quickly. Her discipline, clarity under pressure, and battlefield efficiency marked her as a natural leader. She was granted the rank of [[Decana]], commanding a unit composed largely of other D'yadyan auxiliaries. She executed her orders without pause and made herself indispensable to Vociran command.

But the price was steep. Among the diaspora, her name became a curse. She was called traitor, butcher, collaborator — and worse. Isa bore the hatred in silence, her thoughts forever circling back to her father's blood on the sand, spilled not by their conquerors, but by their own. That was the moment she stopped believing in purity — of purpose, of loyalty, of survival.

Isa does not fight for Vociterra. She fights because no one else gave her the power to choose. And if she rises high enough, she intends to build something stronger than scraps — something her people won't have to beg for.

## Current Status

Isa is currently deployed in D'yadyan territory as part of a Vociran auxiliary unit assigned to route patrols and counterinsurgency response. She's quietly earned a reputation for tactical insight and exceptional close-quarters performance, especially in canyon or desert terrain.

## Combat Style

Isa favors brutal, efficient close-quarters tactics. Her fighting style mixes Vociran military drills with old D'yadyan brawling techniques — disabling joints, breaking rhythm, ending fights fast. She wields a short curved saber and keeps a dagger in her boot.

## Notable Relationships

**Vociran Commanders** – She follows orders without question but offers few smiles. Her superiors respect her results, even if they question her long-term loyalty.

**Fellow D'yadyans** – Most view her with contempt or fear. Isa doesn't argue with them. She just keeps surviving.
