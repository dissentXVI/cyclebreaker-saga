**Title**: Sheriff of [[Laconia]]

**Attire**: Sand-toned desert leathers with steel-clad shoulders, faded Tarwa'kem scarf

**Home**: [[D'yadya]]

## Appearance

Zeyn carries the desert with him — sun-darkened skin, wind-chapped lips, and a face carved in stillness. His hair, long and swept by the wind, tumbles over one shoulder like bleached flax. His armor is practical and ceremonial at once: the shoulderplates speak of a past in Tarwa'kem's army, while the leathers underneath speak to a present built in the dust. A storm-colored scarf from his homeland is always wrapped at his neck, the only indulgence he allows himself. His presence is calm, commanding — the kind that speaks softly and listens harder.

## Personality

Zeyn is controlled fire. Outwardly, he is calm, dutiful, and reserved — the sort of man who gives orders without raising his voice. But underneath simmers a lifetime of abandonment, betrayal, and loss. He believes in order, but not always in peace. His loyalty lies with his people — the D'yadyan diaspora scattered across a continent that has forgotten them. He respects competence, honors service, and distrusts idealism. What others see as grudges, he sees as ledgers waiting to be settled.

Zeyn's interactions with outsiders are civil but cautious. His respect for [[Mireya Lunacrest]] is growing, but complicated by her youth and optimism. His resentment toward [[Tarik Tazi]] — once a role model, now a symbol of surrender — is palpable in private moments.

## Character History

Zeyn was only eight when the white banners fell and D'yadya burned. He remembers little of the trade courts or salt caravans — only fire, screaming, and the long road east. Like many children, he ended up in the refugee camp across the Tefuti Stream, in what the Tarwa'kins derisively called The Sift. There, he learned to survive — not with blades at first, but with ration slips, clenched fists, and bitter truths.

When he came of age, Zeyn enlisted in the Gilded Legion, driven by a single hope: that [[Tarwa'kem]] still held plans to reclaim their lost homeland. So he trained. He trained like every dawn was a prelude to war. He rose swiftly through the ranks, earning respect for his discipline and command — and with each promotion, he believed he was one step closer to someone who could hear his cause. Someone who would care.

Instead, he was sidelined. [[Tarik Tazi]], once a fellow soldier and D'yadyan kin, was elevated to General. Zeyn was assigned to border patrol. Then, to silence. Then, to [[Laconia]].

Following the Trembling Foundations and the emergence of the [[Watcher of the Lake]], Laconia ballooned with refugees, scholars, and mercenaries. Zeyn was named Sheriff — a gesture by [[Tarik Tazi|Tarik]] to "keep order." The guards from [[Aqualonde]] and [[Avosa Tesota]] mocked his title.

When goblins and their summoned Paragon [[Dagozog]] attacked Laconia, Zeyn stood with Aspiria's Chosen in its defense. That act, and his actions since, earned him grudging respect. Now, he trains a militia composed of the displaced, the overlooked, and the determined. He keeps order in Laconia not by the grace of city-states, but by sheer will and a growing belief: if the world won't give D'yadyans a home, they'll carve one out themselves.

## Current Status

In the aftermath of [[Dagozog]]'s attack, [[Laconia]] needed more than a sheriff. [[Mireya Lunacrest]] stepped into the gap — unofficial, unelected, and entirely necessary — and Zeyn found himself working alongside her rather than parallel to her. The arrangement suits him better than he'll admit. She handles what he cannot: the grief, the trust, the fragile social fabric of a community still in shock. He handles what she cannot: the patrols, the weapons, the hard decisions made in the dark.

He continues building his militia, training fighters from among the displaced and the determined. Every day that passes without another attack is a day he uses to prepare for the next one.

## Combat Style

Zeyn fights with the precision of a tactician and the fury of a son of salt. He favors curved shortblades and close-quarters ambushes, often disabling foes with joint breaks, chokeholds, and disabling strikes rather than outright kills. His signature style blends traditional D'yadyan canyon skirmishing with militia-trained efficiency.

When leading others, he moves like a ghost in the sand — seen only when the trap closes. His greatest weapon isn't his blade. It's belief.

## Notable Relationships

**Aspiria's Chosen** – Tentative allies. Zeyn trusts action, not titles — but so far, they've earned his respect.

**[[Mireya Lunacrest]]** – Unofficial Mayor of [[Laconia]], a title the people gave her and she hasn't refused. Zeyn respects her resolve and cannot argue with her results — she held the community together after [[Dagozog]]'s attack in ways no blade could have. He finds her idealism naive and her youth concerning, but he has stopped pretending that makes her wrong. Their working arrangement is simple: she handles the people; he handles the threat. It functions better than either of them expected.

**[[Tarik Tazi]]** – Once a role model and fellow D'yadyan soldier. Now a symbol of surrender and political compromise. Zeyn's resentment runs deep, though he keeps it private.
