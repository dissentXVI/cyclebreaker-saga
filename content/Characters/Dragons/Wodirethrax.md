**Title**: The King of Terrors

**Allegiance**: Vengeance

**Race**: Silver Dragon

**Gender**: Male

**Age**: Ancient

**Eyes**: One molten golden eye

**Scales**: Gleaming silver, pitted and scarred, weathered by ice, ash, and war

**Home**: Wodr's Floe — a floating island of glacial ice that drifts along the eastern coast of [[Carinthia]]

## Appearance

Wodirethrax is an enormous and terrifying presence — a war-scarred silver dragon with battle-worn wings and jagged horns like blades of broken frost. His right eye socket is an open wound of magical ruin, blackened and runic. In the left gleams a golden eye — [[Durnirax]]'s own, given in sacrifice.

Despite his regal bearing, Wodr carries with him a sense of profound anguish and unrelenting rage. Frost clings to his breath, and where he lands, the sky stills in fear.

## Personality

Once noble and watchful, Wodr has become a cold and pitiless force — the embodiment of vengeance with the mind of a general and the heart of a grieving brother. He is driven not by hatred of mortals, but by an unyielding desire to punish betrayal, especially the Carinthians.

He mourns [[Skadraxes]] in silence, his fury sharpened by guilt for not protecting her. Though capable of eloquence and strategy, his emotions smolder behind every word. Only [[Durnirax]] has ever managed to calm him — and even that came at a cost.

## Character History

Wodirethrax arrived in northern Halinor with [[Skadraxes]] and [[Durnirax]] after hatching. He watched as mortals changed — some revering their kind, others conspiring in fear.

[[Skadraxes]], ever friendly, bonded with the elves of [[Carinthia]]. She was betrayed — lured under false pretenses by [[Archbishop Renard Louis]] and slain in cold blood. Her eyes were stolen and consumed in a dark rite that birthed a new Paragon and empowered the Dawnbound and the Archbishop himself. The [[The Light of Alophia|Light of Alophia]] claimed she had attacked unprovoked.

Wodr's war began that day. In his first assault, he fought [[Archbishop Renard Louis|Louis]] and the [[The Dawnbound|Dawnbound]], only to lose his own eyes in battle to Ser Thibault Achaard. Near death, Wodr fled to his brother, [[Durnirax]], who — against every instinct — gave up his own left eye to save him.

With one eye returned and his grief reforged into icy fury, Wodr swore to carry on the war his sister died trying to prevent.

## Current Status

Wodr wages a relentless war from his frozen throne — the drifting fortress of Wodr's Floe, a glacial citadel that haunts the eastern skies of Halinor like a spectral omen. His campaign is not one of conquest, but of retribution. Every strike is a dirge for [[Skadraxes]]. Every village burned, every knight slain, is part of a grief-forged vengeance that knows no rest.

To his brood, he is the King of Terrors — not because he commands with cruelty, but because he inspires with pain. His fury has shaped a generation of dragons into warriors who believe that peace with mortals is a lie. He speaks often of betrayal and justice, rallying his kin with visions of a world where the skies no longer weep for the slain.

He does not attack blindly. His fury is cold, calculating. He studies his enemies. He knows their prayers. He learns their weaknesses. And when he strikes, it is not simply to kill — it is to make them remember why dragons were once revered as gods.

Few in Carinthia speak his name without trembling. For Wodr is not a tyrant to be reasoned with — he is vengeance given wing, and the skies have not known peace since his sister fell.
