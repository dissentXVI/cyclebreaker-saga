**Title**: The Progenitor

**Gender**: Male

**Scales**: Platinum-white with streaks of luminous breathstone along the spine and underbelly

**Home**: Unknown

## Appearance

Ymirethor is ancient majesty incarnate. His wings span the sky, his neck arcs like a cathedral vault, and his breath steams not with fire, but the glow of creation. Platinum scales reflect not just light, but memory, myth, and meaning. Each beat of his wings distorts the air like a bell tolling across time.

His eyes carry aeons — watching, judging, and sometimes... mourning.

## Personality

Ymirethor is a father, philosopher, and survivor. Where his children burn with passion or vengeance, he seeks balance. He speaks rarely but with deep clarity, favoring long silences over rushed conclusions. He mourns what was lost on Thyraxia and fears for what might be lost again on Aspiria.

Yet beneath that solemnity lies a fire of protective love — and when stirred, he will rise.

## Character History

Ymirethor was the progenitor of the First Brood, one of the few dragons to survive the fall of Thyraxia, their original homeworld. The planet was razed by the [[Telothians]] — synthetic beings of cold precision. Ymirethor escaped with a clutch of eggs — his children — and fled across the stars.

When he arrived on a still-young world called Neumeris, the planet's living soul — [[Aspiria]] — welcomed him. She offered refuge, not just for his brood, but for his legacy.

In gratitude, Ymirethor taught the early peoples Higher Magic, laying the foundation for arcane knowledge across Halinor. He calmed the raging heat of the Salamandron's Famine, a cataclysm of sun-scorched lands and failed rains.

## Current Status

Ymirethor has not been seen for centuries. His last known act was guiding his children toward opposite corners of the world, seeking balance across the winds of fate. Some believe he now slumbers deep within the Creation's Maw, beyond mortal reach. Others claim he has become spirit alone — a guardian of thought, no longer bound to scale or bone.
