## Appearance

Psyronos is the most visibly mortal of the Archorans. His garments are pristine but worn — a once-ceremonial uniform now softened by centuries of travel and regret. His face is half-hidden behind a sculpted mask, and his cloak clasps with a golden ring like a broken crown.

Unlike his brethren, he walks with visible weight. He is composed, but never cold — and there is a terrible kindness in how he watches others, like one who already knows their grief.

When he speaks, the world seems to pause — not out of fear, but respect.

## Personality

Psyronos is reflective, erudite, and unfathomably sad. He remembers the world that was — before the rise of gods, before Aspiria was rewritten by false virtues and forgotten wars. He does not wish to destroy. He simply cannot bear what remains.

Where [[Kyrichos]] is sharp and [[Diatitos]] remote, Psyronos is personal. He knows names. He tells stories. He will sit with the dying and speak to them in their own tongue. He does not deceive — he simply does not stop you from believing what you need.

But when he acts, there is no mercy.

## Secrets

> [!dm] DM Only — Not Revealed to Players
> Psyronos is [[Meonus sep Magnus]] — or rather, Meonus Magnus was Psyronos. At some point before the founding of [[Vociterra]], he assumed the identity of Meonus and lived among the people enslaved by the Zhikk, guiding the development of what would become the Vociran Empire. The empire that [[Lucius sep Magnus]] and [[Caedus seq Magnus]] now rule — and that [[Vociterra]] worships as the product of pure human will — was founded and shaped by one of the oldest beings in Aspiria, for purposes that have nothing to do with mankind's liberation. Everything the Vocirans believe about their founding mythology is built on this lie. The entry in [[Characters/Vociterra/Meonus sep Magnus.md]] reflects only what is publicly known.
