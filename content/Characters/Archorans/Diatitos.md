## Appearance

Diatitos is veiled in deliberate anonymity. His helm, smooth and faceless, suggests neither age nor identity. He wears layered robes woven with symbols predating any known script — angular constellations, recursive diagrams, and silent equations etched in breathstone thread.

No skin is visible. His voice, when heard, is low and resonant — as if echoing from a deeper reality. There is no warmth in him, but neither is there cruelty. He moves like a thought given shape.

## Personality

Diatitos is serene, impartial, and implacable — a vessel for something older than memory. His presence is not a choice but a commission, and he carries out his duties with quiet finality.

He does not see himself as judge, enemy, or savior — merely the Emissary, ensuring the structure of the world does not fall beyond repair. Emotion is irrelevant to his purpose, but not absent; he simply locks it behind duty.

Where others rage or lament, Diatitos remains. Not to interfere — only to observe and restore when the pattern demands it.

## Character History

In the era before gods, Diatitos was chosen as the Emissary — a role not of power, but of intercession. He walked between titans, between empires, between truth and its cost. His voice settled disputes that could have ended worlds.

When the [[Predecessors]] were overthrown and the world rewritten, he did not resist. He recorded. He endured. Of the surviving Archorans, only Diatitos still believes that peace — true peace — may one day be possible again... if balance is restored.
